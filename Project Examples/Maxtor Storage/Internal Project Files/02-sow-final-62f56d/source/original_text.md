STATEMENT OF WORK

BACKGROUND AND OBJECTIVES
Aberdeen will assist Maxtor in planning a comprehensive marketing program for its new, moderately priced, high-MTBF ATA MaXLine? disk drives.  The MaXLine products were quietly announced on September 9, 2002, and the market development campaign was initiated thereafter.

A fundamental element of the MaXLine program is to define a new market segment in the storage hierarchy between disk and tape storage systems.  Today, this segment is typically referred to as "active archiving" or "rapid/instant archiving."  Maxtor's objective is to gain visibility as the leading supplier in this crossover market, while also creating demand and recognition for this new market category.  Aberdeen Group, as an independent market research firm, has a worldview of storage hierarchy, and actively communicates with clients and the IT world in general through its research portal, www.aberdeen.com.  Aberdeen believes that Maxtor's technology enabler, the MaXLine class of disk drives, can be communicated as an important ingredient in an active archiving initiative at enterprise IT organizations.

There are two key objectives of the program beyond building recognition for this new information storage technology segment.  First, Maxtor wants to avoid cannibalizing its existing, higher margin SCSI disk drive product families when it offers lower priced ATA drives with solid performance characteristics as a replacement for tape.  Maxtor's OEMs feel the same about their SCSI profit pools.   And second, Maxtor wants to blunt the efforts of competitors that may have different technology portfolios and competing market development agendas.

Aberdeen believes that it can provide assistance to Maxtor in the creation and roll out of these interrelated marketing initiatives.  This Statement of Work outlines several core components of a go-to-market consulting engagement, as well as briefly describing several other optional services that might be incorporated into the project as work orders at a later date.
PROJECT APPROACH
In order to create interest in this market, position Maxtor as a market leader, and accelerate rates of adoption, Aberdeen will provide a combination of independent market research and a resulting executive briefing white paper, which will quickly help the Maxtor sales force and channel partners to build market momentum.

PROJECT SCOPE AND DELIVERABLES
Task 1		Market Research and Segment Definition
Task 1a	Aberdeen will assemble its existing storage research, including data and analysis of the storage pyramid, industry trends and customer buying patterns.  Aberdeen has a well-developed storage model, illustrated below.  MaXLine disk drives fit into the emerging cost-effective storage class.
Aberdeen's Storage Pyramid Explained

Task 1b	 Aberdeen will develop a survey instrument to test the market receptivity to cost-effective "instant access archiving" disk drives at U.S. enterprises.  Aberdeen will review the survey instrument with Maxtor.
Task 1c	Aberdeen will conduct a telephone field survey across a wide variety of vertical markets and enterprise sizes using the survey instrument, with some of the interviews being face-to-face with IT managers responsible for storage architecture, costs, and business issues.  Aberdeen will complete a minimum of 75 interviews.  This number of interviews is not statistically valid, but it typically gives us a high level of confidence that we have surfaced the key issues and market findings across a variety of vertical and size market "cells".

Aberdeen will also interview storage product managers at the top computer companies about their plans and opinions about what Aberdeen calls cost-effective disks in the Aberdeen storage pyramid, specifically about the concept of instant-access archiving.  Aberdeen's discussions will not deliberately focus on Maxtor or MaXLine, but obviously it could illustrate with MaXLine.

As a result of this Task 1c, Aberdeen will have a good market sense of the needs surrounding the concept of instant-access archiving.  Aberdeen will also be able to create positioning and messaging which reacts to the market's needs.  
The deliverables from this Task 1c are two-fold: the survey results and analysis report, and an outline for an Aberdeen white paper.  Aberdeen will complete these tasks in accordance with the following schedule:

Task	Completion Date
Conduct Interviews	April 25, 2003
Tabulate and Deliver Survey Results and Analysis Report	May 2, 2003
Deliver White Paper Outline	May 2, 2003

Task 2		Market Education
Task 2a	Aberdeen will meet with Maxtor's executives in California to review the survey results and the white-paper outline.  This meeting will be a workshop, where Aberdeen and Maxtor exchange information and ideas.  It gives Maxtor the opportunity to drill-down interactively on the survey information, so that Maxtor might benefit in tangible ways from what Aberdeen learns in the field.  In addition, Maxtor can influence the emphases and organization of the white paper outline.

The deliverables from the meeting are an updated outline for the executive white paper, which will be delivered to Maxtor within 3 days after the executive briefing.

Task 2b	An Aberdeen Executive White Paper provides an interpretation and analysis of industry trends, technologies, and methodologies - with an emphasis on the business implications for end-user organizations.  An Executive White Paper describes and analyzes a key IT issue and how a specific supplier responds to it - in this case, MaXLine and instant-access archiving.  Aberdeen publishes an Executive White Paper when an in-depth analysis is required for a significant issue that is being vigorously debated within the industry.

1. Aberdeen will write a 10-20 page Executive White Paper using the outline developed in Task 2a.  The intended audience are IT executives and enterprise executives who will have to buy-in on the concept of low-cost storage that provides near instant access with near-archival longevity, e.g., MaXLine.  

2. Maxtor will have the opportunity to review the draft in a two-week review cycle.  Aberdeen will conduct a review session, if necessary, to discuss Maxtor's aggregated comments, and will then make appropriate changes.  

3. Aberdeen will deliver a copy of its final version of the Executive White Paper to Maxtor within 3 days after the review session in hard copy and in .pdf and Microsoft Word form.  Aberdeen hereby provides to Maxtor the exclusive, unrestricted, royalty-free, worldwide and perpetual license to copy, publish, distribute, display, prepare derivative work based thereon, and merge into other material, whether in hard copy or electronic form and on any media, the Executive White Paper and any other Aberdeen publications produced under this Statement of Work.   The foregoing exclusivity shall apply to Aberdeen as well as any and all of its outlets, customers, licensees and distribution channels, other than Maxtor.
4. During the sixty day period after receipt of the final version of the Executive White Paper, Maxtor will conduct a review of the White Paper and consider the appropriate manner of dissemination of such material.  At its discretion, Maxtor may elect to waive by written notice to Aberdeen the exclusivity granted to Maxtor in Item (3) above. 

5. Subject to Maxtor's waiver of its exclusivity (as referenced in Item (4) above), Aberdeen will (i) publish the Executive White Paper, (ii) deliver 1,000 copies to Maxtor (iii) post the White Paper on its web site; (iv) promote the new White paper and research report in its customary fashion on its Home page and at its Storage sub-website; and (v) the White Paper will be pushed to the Aberdeen Storage and Platforms practices' newsletter opt-in clients as an Adobe .pdf.

6.  For a period of six months after first posting, Aberdeen will provide download demographics information to Maxtor on those individuals who choose to download the White Paper.  Because Aberdeen will demand that readers register at Aberdeen.com prior to being allowed to have the free White Paper - Aberdeen's standard practice - Maxtor will receive a sales lead flow, including the identity and other available information with regard to such visitors.

Task 2c	Aberdeen will host a webcast, advertised as "sponsored by Maxtor", which reviews issues in the storage market as discussed in the Executive White Paper, and specifically addresses the opportunity for using ATA disk drives in a near-line environment.  The webcast will provide an opportunity for Maxtor executives to participate in a panel discussion.  It will be another chance for Maxtor's sales team to point prospects toward an educational forum on the subject.  A recording of the webcast and its presentation materials will be Web-posted to garner further downloads, while pointing to the growing Aberdeen research on the subject.  Aberdeen will regularly provide to Maxtor the identity and other available information with regard to visitors who register or download the webcast.

Task 2d	Aberdeen will provide at least four briefings for Maxtor executives after publication of the Executive White Paper in which Aberdeen will provide a review of its assessment of the development of this new "Near-line" market category for disk drives, market trends and industry reactions.
Optional tasks include engaging with Aberdeen to discuss opportunities for outreach to various standards groups, trade shows and even direct customer sales calls as another method of promoting the technology and gaining endorsements for the segment.  Also, Aberdeen can advise Maxtor on potential software partnerships that will provide additional industry acceptance.  Aberdeen would do this at our volume-customer per diem rates.
Finally, Aberdeen offers a substantial number of sales-training curriculum development services, under our eLearning division banner.  This division, located in Fort Collins, Colorado provides web-based, action-oriented, rapid response content for sales and channel partner education.  Their offerings will be detailed in a separate proposal.
COST AND DURATION
Task 1 will be carried out over approximately five weeks - and is dependent on holiday issues.  Aberdeen is prepared to start work on this project immediately.  Aberdeen's professional services fee for Task 1 is $25,000, including days of consulting, survey field research, and preparation and delivery of a report and presentation, but excluding travel and lodging expenses.  This will be invoiced at upon delivery of the survey instrument and payable within thirty days after delivery of the White Paper outline and receipt of invoice.
Task 2 will also be carried out over six weeks.  Aberdeen's professional services fee for Task 2 is $35,000.  Task 2 will be invoiced at one-half upon completion of the Task 2a workshop and one-half submission of the Task 2b first draft.  

Aberdeen's fees do not include travel and direct expenses, which will be billed separately at cost in the month they occur and must be approved in advance by Maxtor.  Aberdeen expects that this project will require some travel-related expenses for field face-to-face interviews and the Task 2a California workshop.  Aberdeen recommends establishing a budget of $6,000.  Fees also do not include teleconferencing charges for the task 2c web cast, which cannot be estimated more closely at this point than $3,000 for 100 participants.
