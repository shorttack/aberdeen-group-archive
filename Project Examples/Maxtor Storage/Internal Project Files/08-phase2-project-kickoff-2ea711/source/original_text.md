## Original Document Text

November 25, 2003

Aberdeen-Maxtor Phase 2 - Pools of Storage Market Development

Scope
This project supports Maxtor's immediate and long-term Pools of Storage/midline outreach goals:
    1. Gain internal consensus on Pools of Storage/midline market messaging in the bigger picture of ILM. 
    2. Establish the strategic and tactical foundations upon which to build out subsequent Pools of Storage/midline project initiatives (e.g., "Phase 3")
    3. Provide the tools to validate the new efficiencies.

Objective
The objective is to sponsor an industry dialogue about a better way to better organize Enterprise Data into four unique "Pools of Storage" reducing capital costs, improving return on capital, aligning more closely to business policy and insuring the synchronized expansion of storage systems with storage growth requirements.  If ILM is "getting control over storage", then Pools of Storage is the economic and flexibility "best approach".

The program will identify and validate ways to introduce and reinforce a lexicon for the four "Pools of Storage" so the industry can have a clear and concise dialogue on products, software and solutions that fit into each pool.  Those titles are:
    - On-line  - Transactional Processing	
    - Mid-line - Fixed Content 	
    - Near-Line - Disk Backup & Staging & Spooling from disk to tape	
    - Off-line - Tape Archive

In synchronization with the external program, Maxtor will align specific Maxtor hard disk drive brands with these Pools of Storage, thereby empowering Maxtor to have a significant voice in the next generation of enterprise storage - Serial Storage in a Box.

Maxtor's Business Strategy
Maxtor and Aberdeen have mutually identified the following as imperative to successfully implementing "Pools of Storage" and specifically launching a new storage category of midline storage within a year:
    - All players in the Serial Storage ecosystem adopt Maxtor's "Pools" lexicon.
    - Technology and business buyers alike must recognize that Midline solutions fill a vacuum for fixed content with an economic and scaleable set of products
    - Maxtor must prevent the misperception that Pools of Storage means hierarchical storage management.  Pools of Storage is a set of storage systems that can adapt to a company's business objectives and policies for data management and should be an integral part of enterprise Information Lifecycle Management.
    - Enterprise customers must feel the TCO pain of storing fixed content on highly priced drives that are designed for speed instead of capacity. 
    - An endorsement for Pools of Storage is required from industry publications in the US and Europe and from at least four Fortune 500 company CIOs.

Approach: Synchronize, Validate, Evangelize the Pools of Storage Messaging
Maxtor wishes to produce a series of industry outreach programs to create a dialogue on organizing enterprise storage into four pools.   Each pool reflects are unique and specific type of usage of storage.  Each pool has a unique value to the enterprise, each pool has a specific best use of capital and labor, and each has its own set of business risks.  Therefore, each pool should be constructed with different set of components designed to be the most economic and efficient for that specified pools. 

To sponsor a dialogue, introduce a lexicon and align Maxtor products to Pools of Storage, a set of clear, crisp and customer oriented messages must be developed and tested.  These messages will be incorporated into Maxtor's marketing properties, offered to Maxtor's partners and become part of our pitch to analyst, press and customers.

1.  Synchronizing Maxtor's Product Groups With a Go-to-Market Messaging Strategy
In order to align the various Maxtor product and marketing constituencies, a "Messaging Workshop" must be conducted with Maxtor executives, product marketers, partners and customers.  The result will be to create a full set of communications that should resonate with all stake holders.

2. Test the Messages
Aberdeen needs to evangelize the messaging to corporations around the world.  Partners who will be producing the components of Serial Storage in a Box must adopt the messaging so customers hear the same words from all vendors, press, analyst and peers.  But marketing science suggests the way to avoid a "ready, fire, aim" situation is for Aberdeen Group to test the messages developed in the Messaging Strategy phase on prospective buyers and channel partners.  Aberdeen will create a messaging survey and conduct qualitative and quantitative field research which tests the attractiveness and resonance of the proposed Pools of Storage messages.  We will also include limited questions on likely buying behavior.

3.  Evangelize the Pools of Storage Program with Tested Messages
In concert with Maxtor's PR department and Aberdeen's outside PR counsel, Aberdeen will evangelize the lexicon and messages of Pools of Storage.  While the scope of our efforts is flexible, we envision a program containing elements of:
    - Press outreach promoting our research about Pools of Storage in the U.S. and Europe
    - Presentations to industry trade associations and trade shows
    - Presentations to user storage groups (e.g., SNIA)
    - Market awareness building through publications and online seminar(s)
    - Value measurement through interactive tools which demonstrate best practices, ROI, and TCO savings through the use of a comprehensive enterprise Pools of Storage program.
    - Face-to-face interviews with early adopters of Pools of Storage and Storage in a Box products and publication of best practices in storage pools.
    - Prepare a plan to create a web site Center of Excellence on Pools of Storage subject, where user managers can learn and model the savings, learn best practices, and serve as a one-top-shop for the Pools of Storage subject for the industry.

Project Plan
This program establishes the foundation of a larger market outreach roadmap. The three keys to this program are: 

    1. Midline Messaging that will speed communication and yield data that can be leveraged across a roadmap to launch a new category. The messages will be market tested and valid.  This front-end investment will strengthen internal consensus on Maxtor's messaging and result in cost-savings for all subsequent Midline and Serial Storage in a Box outreach program elements.

    2. Value-based decision tools that can be published to public web sites to validate efficiencies in use of capital and labor. 

    3. Evangelism and Outreach services to spread the Pools of Storage program to industry and user groups and the press.

Task Three: Message Testing
    - Develop survey instrument
    - Recruit survey population and face-to-face schedule
    - Conduct 100-250 Internet surveys and 5-8 face-to-face or telephone interviews
    - Analyze results versus proposed Pools of Storage messages
    - Analyze Pools of Storage buying intentions
    - Where appropriate, merge this survey with the April 2003 Aberdeen survey results on midline storage
    - Prepare a report on market survey intelligence and Aberdeen's recommend approach to the market
    - Conduct a telephone workshop with Maxtor to re-align proposed messages with market intelligence
    - Collateral: Prepare a short white paper for use as a Pools of Storage talking piece with the press and as the first evangelization collateral.

Task Four: Evangelization
Press tour of the east-coast, west-coast and two cities in England and continental Europe to evangelize the Pools of Storage white paper, coordinated by Aberdeen's PR counsel

Prepare a detailed plan, schedule and budget for a sustained program throughout 2004 to build market awareness and cohesion around the Pools of Storage concept and messages.
