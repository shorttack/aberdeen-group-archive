Appendix A: Key Pools of Storage Concepts
Key Definitions
    - Information Lifecycle Management (ILM)-manages "content" through different stages of its life using some degree of policy-driven software automation
        ? Assign data to the least expensive storage device possible without compromising service
        ? Transfer data to different storage resources as it ages and access needs change

    - Pools of Storage-a pool is a homogeneous group of storage hardware that meets service level requirements for access to data with a certain level of capital and management intensity; a pool of storage is a target of the ILM process

    - Midline Storage-high capacity disk storage that has lower price per gigabyte as well as relatively lower performance than higher performance disk storage; suitable for low I/O duty applications

Appendix B: The Foundations of Messaging
Storage Buying Decision Justification
    - Fit within existing budget constraints-to get done what it needs to get done without running over budget (TCO)
    - Net positive savings to IT-enables IT to do more with less (ROI)
    - Benefits to end-users that exceed IT costs-IT provides benefits to the enterprise as a whole rather than to itself (ROI and business value)
    - Competitive advantage-provide value to the enterprise externally as well as internally (business value)
Business Value of Storage Decisions
    - Economic value (best use of capital) of hardware based in the utility of the data at each stage of ILM.
    - Reducing capital costs, improving return on capital, aligning more closely to business policy, and insuring the synchronized expansion of storage systems with storage growth requirements are some of the goals.
    - Not only improving TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) - a cost efficiency argument - and ROI (Return on Investment) - a cost benefits argument, but also business value - an advantage to the business argument

Strategy-What we are trying to do?
    - Take market share away from competitors
        ? Maxtor must be externally focused and focused on the competition
        ? Maxtor must force competitors to give it market share
    - Establish a basis for sustainable competitive advantage
        ? Maxtor must compete other than on the basis of price

Positioning -Where we want to fit?
Positioning against competitors
    - Seagate Barracuda 7200.7 Product Family
        ? Seagate wants to protect its dominance in the FC disk market; it will be reluctant to compete aggressively in the midline market
    - Western Digital Caviar Product Family
        ? WD does not have extensive experience in selling to "business"; it sells products, not solutions
    - Hitachi Deskstar 7K520 Series
        ? Hitachi Global Storage Strategies focuses on the "right product at the right time"; as with WD it has a product and technology focus rather than a business solutions focus

Messaging-What we need to communicate that resonates with potential customers.

    - Buying proposition

    - Case study

Understanding the Buyer

Messaging Basics
