RAMP Interview Notes — Maxtor Midline Storage Project
=====================================================

This subdirectory contains the complete RAMP (Rapid Accurate Market Prediction)
study materials from Aberdeen Group's 2003 market validation project for Maxtor
Corporation's proposed "midline" disk storage product (MaXLine).

The RAMP process was Aberdeen Group's proprietary methodology for testing market
readiness before a product launch. It combined structured telephone surveys with
in-depth face-to-face interviews at enterprise customer sites.

Authors: Peter S. Kastner and David Hill, Aberdeen Group
Date: February–May 2003
Client: Maxtor Corporation

Prescience Assessment
---------------------
Overall: HIGH to VERY HIGH (8-9/10)

Key predictions that proved correct:
1. "Midline" as complementary (not substitute) storage positioning succeeded
   exactly as recommended — ATA/SATA nearline became standard enterprise tier
2. Storage vendor revenue shortfall from growth-below-forecast drove SATA
   tier creation — vendors needed new disk use cases to maintain shipments
3. Disk-based backup/VTL market materialized as predicted (multi-billion
   scale by 2004-2010)
4. ISV partnership requirement for policy-based storage migration proved
   necessary (Symantec, NetApp, IBM TSM)
5. Administrative cost > hardware cost as adoption barrier presaged the
   entire storage automation market and ultimately cloud/hyperconverged
6. Healthcare IT chaos preventing "storage utility" proved prescient —
   EHR migration, HIPAA storage requirements, regional fragmentation all
   played out as documented
7. Financial sector as early adopter despite SLAs proved correct

Kastner Note: I co-authored this study with David Hill and attended several
of the face-to-face interviews. The RAMP methodology was designed to give
clients like Maxtor actionable go/no-go intelligence before committing to
product launch. This study directly influenced Maxtor's MaXLine product
positioning and go-to-market strategy.
