Q10: Disk Storage In Use As Opposed to 
Available But Unused Storage
Finding: The survey refutes the often-made claim that only a 
small portion of the available disk space is actually used.  
65% say storage is used 60% or more.
Implications:
1) Any further consolidation will not free up large amounts of 
unused storage for other tasks
2) Continued growth in storage demand would lead to the 
need for additional disk storage.
Conclusion: Demands that can use ATA disk cannot be 
satisfied with repurposed existing disks.

Capacity Breakdown by Major OS
Mainframe: Over half said that mainframe storage was at least 33% 
of their storage, but about 21% said that it represented more than 
66% of their storage.  
Windows: By contrast, nearly 82% said that Windows storage was at 
least 33% of their storage, and nearly 35% said that it represented 
70% or more of their storage.  
Unix/Linux: Nearly 50% said that Unix/Linux storage was at least 
33% of their storage and only about 15% reported that it 
represented 66% or more of their storage.  
Conclusion: The mainframe is not dead and Windows is benefiting 
at the expense of Unix/Linux.

Q12: Storage Growth in the Next 12 Months
Finding: Storage growth, while still healthy, is a lot 
less than predictions.  Only about half plan to 
increase storage.
Implication: Since storage prices are likely to fall faster 
than storage growth, an overall revenue shortfall for the 
hardware portion of the storage industry is foreseen.
Conclusion: Storage system vendors are going to seek 
new uses for disk, such as ATA disk, as they try to take 
market share from their competitors to keep up unit 
shipments, if not revenue.

Q15: Online Disk and Tape Copies
Finding: The majority of enterprises have a moderate number 
of online and tape copies, but about a third have a larger 
number of copies.
Implication: A requirement for high availability, the difficulty in 
changing existing processes, or simple mismanagement may 
be responsible for extra copies.
Conclusion: The third that have a lot of copies might be a 
good candidate for a solution that could either reduce the 
number of copies (tape) or the cost of a copy (disk).

Q19: IT Willingness to Accept More Cost Effective 
Higher Capacity Disks vs. Current Capacity Disks
Finding: Higher capacity disks are more acceptable to IT than 
current capacity disks with a lesser cost reduction. 
Implication: IT managers weigh discount performance 
differences and weigh capacity and % of cost reduction 
higher than slight difference in availability.
Conclusion: Maxtor may very well be successful in selling 
complementary online storage whereas Western Digital may 
have greater difficulty in selling substitute storage.

Q27: Backups as a Pain Point
Findings: Backup windows are still a major problem, but 
backup job failures are not a problem to nearly 75% of the 
respondents.  Respondents are split in half on the ability to 
restore all critical data every day and whether the 
management of backup is a burden.  Nearly 2/3 said that the 
time to restore critical data is longer than they would like. 
Implication: The backup/restore process is still a problem.
Conclusion: If ATA disk can play a role (say disk-based 
backup), then it may stand a good chance of acceptance – if 
admin costs stay steady.
