Aberdeen Group Disk Storage Usage Questionnaire
Part One: Survey Questions
Q1: Which of the following general operating systems does your organization use, and of those, which are you directly responsible for?
    a) Mainframes
    b) Windows
    c) Unix, Linux, open systems
Q2: How much total internal and external disk arrays do you have available (in GB or TB)? (For servers, not PC or workstations)

Q3: What percent of this disk storage is actually in use? (As opposed to available storage)

Q4.1	Mainframe
    a) What is your current storage capacity for mainframes as a percentage of total storage?
    b) What percent of your mainframe storage is currently associated with the following types of storage? Internal direct-attached storage/External direct attached storage/ Storage Area Network/Network attached Storage.
    c) How much do you think that your mainframe storage will increase in the next 12 months (in % terms)?
    d) At a maximum, how full do you want your mainframe storage to become (in % terms)?

Q4.2	Windows Servers
    a) What is your current storage capacity for Windows as a percentage of total storage?
    b) What percent of your Windows storage is currently associated with the following types of storage? Internal direct-attached storage/External direct attached storage/ Storage Area Network/Network attached Storage.
    c) How much do you think that your Windows storage will increase in the next 12 months (in % terms)?
    d) At a maximum, how full do you want your Windows storage to become (in % terms)?

Q4.3	Unix, Linux "Open Systems"
    a) What is your current storage capacity for Unix as a percentage of total storage?
    b) What percent of your Unix storage is currently associated with the following types of storage? Internal direct-attached storage/External direct attached storage/ Storage Area Network/Network attached Storage.
    c) How much do you think that your Unix storage will increase in the next 12 months (in % terms)?
    d) At a maximum, how full do you want your Unix storage to become (in % terms)?

Q5:  How many full copies of currently available online data do you keep on:
    a) Online disk storage (not counting the original as a copy)
    b) Tape storage (where each generation of tape is a copy)

Q6: Do you have some data that is effectively read only or seldom written, such as closed transaction logs or old e-mail, but cannot yet be archived to tape?

Q7: What kind of applications fall under this category?

Q8: What percent of total storage is this?
Q9: If you could move some or all of this read-only data to a costly disk technology, what would you be willing to trade off? Please rate on a scale of 1 to 7, where 1 represents "unwilling to tradeoff", and 7 represents "sign me up today".  
    a) Higher capacity disks with slightly less performance and availability, but cost deduced on the order of 50% .
    b) Current disk sizes and performance, but slightly less availability, but cost reduction on the order of 30%.
    c) Current disk size,  performance and availability characteristics, but cost reduction on the order of 30%.

Q10: Has your current storage supplier discussed their low-cost disk storage options with you?  (Supplier name?)

Q11: If yes, do you have plans to purchase this option in the next 12 months? Please rate on a scale of 1 to 7, where 1 represents Not at all and 7 represents all ready have.

Q12: How often do you have to restore one or more volumes, due to an online data storage problem? 
    a) Once a week
    b) Once a month
    c) Once a quarter
    d) Once every two quarters
    e) Once a year
    f) Less than once a year

Q14: Do you have any single applications with very large non-volatile/static data, such as video data, images, data warehouse detailed data, and old e-mails? (Get application description)

Q15: Please describe the disk technology that is used with your open systems disk arrays.
    a) What percentage of storage is SCSI-based? What size SCSI drives do you use and what are their rotational speeds?
    b) What percentage of storage is FC-based? What size FC drives do you use and what are their rotational speeds?
    c) What percentage of storage is ATA-based? What size ATA drives do you use and what are their rotational speeds?

Q16: Do you have any issues with your backup/restore process?  Please choose all that apply.  
    a) When offline backups are used, we can have a problem with the length of the backup window.
    b) Backup jobs fail to run to completion at an unacceptable rate.
    c) We cannot guarantee that all critical data can be restored on a given day.
    d) The operational management required to manage the backup process is a burden and a management concern.
    e) The time to restore critical data is longer than we would like.

Q17: What do these terms mean to you?
    a) Active archiving
    b) Online archiving
    c) Mezzanine storage
    d) Nearline storage
    e) Low-cost storage

### Abstract

Refined second version of the Aberdeen Group telephone survey questionnaire for the Maxtor RAMP project. Streamlines the v1 instrument to 17 survey questions and a demographic section, with key enhancements: per-OS storage breakdowns (Q4.1-Q4.3) for mainframe / Windows / Unix separately, introduction of a 1-7 Likert willingness-to-tradeoff scale (Q9) testing three distinct cost-performance scenarios (50% cost reduction with lower availability; 30% with less availability; 30% with same availability), vendor engagement probing (Q10-Q11), restore frequency measurement (Q12), static data application identification (Q14), and a refined terminology awareness battery testing five terms. The instrument was used to collect data from approximately 70 enterprise IT decision-makers.
