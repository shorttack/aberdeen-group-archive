Stephen DiFranco, CMO at Maxtor, wanted to create a new storage category in 2003,
which we eventually named Midline Storage, to consume more of the new SATA drives
coming off his factories and into consumer PCs. The target market was enterprise.
DiFranco came to Aberdeen for two reasons: we had created the CRM market around
Siebel quite successfully, and he wanted to work with principal Peter Kastner.

This directory contains project files for key internal communications, external
client communications, market-facing white papers, and the details of the Rapid
Accurate Market Prediction surveys that shaped the decisions and positioning.
Finally, we developed a Flash tool that allowed users to create midline pools of
storage demonstration and explanation.

Mind you, creating a new market category is difficult and expensive. The project
took almost a year and was not cheap. There were significant client issues because
Maxtor was a disk drive OEM that could not understand why all this marketing money
was necessary!

So if you want to create a new technology market category, think again. The chances
of failure are high. Then delve into this cache of history.

     -- Peter S. Kastner, April 2026
