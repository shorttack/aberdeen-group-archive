Company Facts

Snap Appliance is the number one, volume leading provider of network
attached storage (NAS) solutions from the workgroup to the enterprise. The
company has pioneered the workgroup NAS market and has an installed
customer base of over 130,000 units. The first Snap Servers were launched
by Meridian Data in 1998 and the company was acquired by storage giant
Quantum in 2000.  Original plans to take the company public were shelved
and, in 2002, Snap Appliance was reborn as a private company.

Markets
Snap Appliance, Inc.  sells  its  products  exclusively  through  sells  its
products  through  manufacturers,  distributors  and  resellers   in   North

Products and Services
IT professionals are increasingly vocal about the urgent  need  to  keep  up
with storage requirements,  discover  low-cost  alternatives,  and  to  have
products that are simple  to  install,  use  and  upgrade.   Snap  Appliance
provides  instant  and  cost-effective   relief   of   storage   constraints
experienced by companies that haveexperiencing massive  amounts  of  growing
data bydatadata growth by providing storage that  integrates  seamlessly  in
heterogeneous  environments.   These  characteristics  make   Snap   Servers
particularly  attractive  to  small  and   medium   businesses   (SMB)   and
enterprises that have minimal or no IT staffing.

Snap  storage  appliances  use  inexpensive  ATA  disks  in  a  variety   of
redundancy configurations.  The company has creatively covered all the  mid-
line storage categories envisaged by Aberdeen: inexpensive staging  of  data
from SCSI production systems prior to tape archival  backup;  fixed  content
for inexpensive long-term storage;  light-  and  moderate-duty  applications
where  storage  consolidation  leads  to  cost-of-ownership  benefits;  data
replication; and, PC client storage backup.

Workgroup products are  targeted  at  1-50+  users  for  NAS.   An  included
DataKeeper™ provides automated, scheduled  client  backup.   Three  products
cover desktop and 1U rack mounted storage housing 1-4 disks.

The Snap Server 1100 is the world’s best selling NAS server, in part due  to
its capabilities as a capable and portable file  server.   The  Snap  Server
2200 offers larger storage capacity along with RAID 1 data protection.

The Snap Server 4100 offers entry-level RAID 5 in  a  slim,  rack  mountable
chassis.

The Snap Server 4100 offers entry-level RAID 5 in  a  slim,  rack  mountable
chassis.  Th The Snap Server 4200 and 4500 departmental products  are  built
around 4 ATA-100 disks, with total capacity of 320 GB to 1 TB.  The 1U rack-
mounted appliances come with dual gigabit-Ethernet adapters.  All the  usual
RAID options are available for performance  and  high  availability.   Every
Snap Server 4500 can support up to two Snap Disk  10  expansion  arrays  for
easy to manage, best-in-class storage expansion of up to 3TB of raw  storage
capacity.

The enterprise-class SnapServer 14000 comes in 1.44 TB, 2.16 TB, and 3.0  TB
flavors by spreading data across 12 disks.  It  fits  in  a  3U  rack  slot.
There is support for the ADS and NIS  storage  security  protocols  used  in
sophisticated Unix and Windows datacenters.

There  are  a  number  of  utility  products  from   Snap,   which   improve
manageability through heterogeneous storage movement,  synchronization,  and
migration.   An  unlimited  user  license  of  DataKeeper™  desktop   backup
software for Windows clients is  included  with  every  Snap  Server.   Also
available is Server-to-Server  Sync  replication  software  for  replication
between Snap Servers.  The company develops and supports its  own  operating
systems for the all Snap Server appliances.  The workgroup products  utilize
the SnapOS operating system,  leveraging  field-hardened  functionality  and
ease of use.  The departmental and enterprise Snap Servers  use  the  Linux-
based GuardianOS adding innovative features, such as snapshots  and  Windows
ADS — plus the same ease of use  Snap  Appliance  customers  have  grown  to
expect in workgroup models.

Snap Appliance also offers a wide variety of comprehensive service  programs
for all Snap Servers  to  meet  the  service  and  support  needs  of  their
customers.

### Abstract

This Aberdeen Group SnapShot profiles Snap Appliance Inc., the self-described number-one volume NAS provider, which was reborn as a private company in 2002 after being spun out of Quantum. The profile details Snap's product line spanning workgroup to enterprise NAS using inexpensive ATA disks, and explicitly maps Snap's coverage of Aberdeen's midline storage categories including staging, fixed content, consolidation, replication, and PC backup. The document positions Snap as a key validation of Aberdeen's midline disk storage thesis.
