## _**InSight:**_ 

## _**Microsoft Corporation**_ 

**Upgrading to Windows XP: A Long and Winding Road** 

## **November 2001** 

With the long-anticipated launch of Microsoft's Windows XP operating system - backed by a $200 million marketing program - technologists may be tempted to upgrade their existing machines. With an XP upgrade, users will get the enterprise stability and speed of Windows 2000 while enjoying a raft of new features supporting audio and visual digital media and networking. 

However, our experiences with upgrading PCs to Windows XP - documented in this _InSight_ - make us recommend such an upgrade only if it is performed by a good technician. Most consumers and small businesses that want or need the excellent capabilities of Windows XP should opt for a factory-installed version on a new PC - which, costing around $1,000, should be a bargain. 

_**Peter Kastner**_
