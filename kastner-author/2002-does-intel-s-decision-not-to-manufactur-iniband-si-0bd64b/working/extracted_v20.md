## _**InSight:**_ 

**Does Intel's Decision Not To Manufacture InfiniBand Silicon Damage the Initiative?** 

## **August 2002** 

InfiniBand, an important next-generation data-center I/O (input/output) initiative supported by the IT (Information Technology) industry, is scheduled for production launch late this year. But with the launch date moving closer, strong supporter Intel recently canceled plans to supply silicon for InfiniBand interface adapters. While many industry observers believe Intel's decision will cause InfiniBand to stall, Aberdeen holds a different - and more positiveview. This Aberdeen InSight explores the rationale behind Intel's decision and explains how the move is good for Intel, and - contrary to other opinions - how it is good for the InfiniBand ecosystem. 

_**Russ Craig and Peter Kastner**_
