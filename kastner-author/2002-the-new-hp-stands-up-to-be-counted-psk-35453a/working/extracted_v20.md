## _**InSight:**_ 

## **The New HP Stands Up to be Counted** 

## **November 15, 2002** 

With Michael Capellas' resignation as president of Hewlett-Packard, the stock market pounded HPQ for an 11% loss on the day. As much as we admire Capellas, his departure is not worth an 11% hit on HP's long-term prospects. Mindful of upcoming quarterly results that will be less than stellar, we are more bullish on the overall HP trajectory than we were after the merger was announced 14 months ago. It may take awhile for the market to catch up to the scenario described here. And execution is what it's all about. 

_**Peter Kastner**_
