## _**InSight**_ 

**Wi-Fi's New Standard: 802.11a Will Offer the Best Bandwidth for the Buck** 

## **January 2002** 

Wireless home networking via 802.11b, also known as "Wi-Fi," is quickly becoming a reality. Price-points have come down; the technology now delivers reliable, high-bandwidth wireless access; and vendors are steadily improving the installation experience. However, a new flavor of Wi-Fi, called 802.11a -incompatible with 802.11b - presents a compelling alternative for consumers seeking to install a wireless home network in the coming months. 

This Aberdeen _InSight_ helps consumers plan for 802.11a, outlines technology benefits and costs, and highlights an opportunity for smart hardware vendors to deliver tomorrow's standard today.
