### LaGrande Technology -- A Proposal

**Purpose**

The purpose of this proposal is to open a discussion with Intel which would lead to one or more market research projects over the two-plus year period leading up to the market introduction of LaGrande Technology (LT). This is not a firm proposal.

**Aberdeen Points of View (POVs)**

Aberdeen believes, and seeks to prove through qualitative and quantitative market research, that a definable and addressable market for LT in the consumer segment will exist at the time of LT introduction. Today, Intel's market research suggests the consumer market should not be directly addressed, and plans are to initially under-play the consumer segment and concentrate on LT in business. Our POVs are:

1. **LT Platform**: LaGrande has no value without software applications that can exploit it. Likewise, the software applications possess no value unless the hardware exists. Hence, LT Platform = LT + OS + applications.
2. Consumers include business workers. Business adoption of LT will drive some consumer adoption.
3. Highest volumes for the LT Platforms will come from consumers demanding privacy and identity protection.
4. Next largest volumes for LT Platforms will come from business-to-consumer value chains.
5. Lowest volumes for LT Platforms will come from Business-to-Business value chains.
6. To maximize volumes: hardware and software must be ubiquitous. Revenue models may be different for consumers and businesses.

**Value to Intel**

Proving that addressable consumer and business markets for LT Platforms exists will expand the TAM, with plenty of time to go to market. Aberdeen suspects the WW consumer market for LT in 2006 is at or above 10M units, assuming a $100 PC retail uplift for a consumer LT platform.

**Basis for Aberdeen Point of View**

During the past year, we surveyed consumers on an on-going basis about identity theft and privacy concerns when consumers are connected to the Internet. We also conducted numerous research interviews with businesses having financial exposure due to credit and identity fraud.

Aberdeen has attended NDA and public discussions on LT on at least six occasions over the last 18 months. In addition, we are in NDA discussions with Microsoft and other Trusted Computing Group members on related technologies. We have built our private opinions on a limited view based on research conducted in Europe and North America, and well as Intel's (and other TCG members) plans, but with enough information for us to believe Intel is not seriously considering a valuable opportunity.

**What Needs to be Proved and Tested?**

1. Global demographics of likely LT buyers (young/old, take work home, entrepreneurial, etc.) and market segmentation
2. Early adopter drivers such as ID theft fear, work at home, virus scares, intra-family privacy, online trust, personal records, business-to-consumer applications, business-to-business applications
3. Applications or activities for which privacy and protection are desired. What are the winning components of an LT Platform?
4. Understand the consumer mix of computing between secure and ordinary. Do consumers want a secure version of a 2006 home media PC or a corporate LT machine dressed for home?
5. Will consumers pay, and how much would consumers pay?
6. Understand the psycho-behavior attributes of privacy by culture and country. What protection do consumers seek and what is a perceived turn-off (e.g., processor ID). What key words, messages, and examples are turn-ons and turn-offs?
7. Which distribution channels will maximize reach to consumers and businesses, and what's in it for them?
8. How does business brand and trust impact consumer behavior relative to LT Platforms?
9. What will businesses and consumers purchase? Product feature set.
10. Who are considered trusted suppliers of secure, private computing?
11. Changes in buyer attitudes -- measured over time

**Methodology**

In broad terms, we propose three phases to this research:

1. **Consumer-based qualitative research**: focus groups and individual direct interviews (IDIs) in countries representing a proxy for WW computing markets. We suggest US, Canada, Japan, Germany, and UK. France or Italy could substitute for Germany. Mexico could be added for a Latin America surrogate. We believe China is a special (political) case and should not be included.

   Phase 1 would shape the subsequent research by narrowing the options of "what needs to be answered and how". The focus groups and the IDIs provide baseline data for establishing what needs to be surveyed and tracked in subsequent phases.

2. **Business-based qualitative research**: Qualitative in-depth interviews in countries representing a proxy for WW computing markets. (We would survey the same countries as the consumer qualitative research.)

3. **Consumer-based quantitative research**: focused on attitudinal scope of value propositions, channels, solutions, and pricing.

4. **Consumer Tracking Study**: every six months, conduct survey of the Phase 1 quantitative respondents to look for changes. Aberdeen's thesis is that consumer attitudes on privacy and security are evolving with experience (i.e., BLASTER virus). Therefore, these attitude changes must modify go-to-market plans and messages.

5. **Localized Quantitative Surveys**: from the qualitative phase 1, develop aided and unaided surveys of attitudes and buying intentions, and drive localized surveys in major global markets. The results and subsequent analysis will provide the actionable data on which to develop consumer LT go to market plans.

**Methodology & Time Line**

Phase 1 -- starting in Q4-03, design the focus group guides, line up the consumer-based focus groups and the business-based IDIs by country, and initiate scheduling. Due to holidays in US and Europe, actual focus groups may start in Q1-04, depending on project start date. Estimated completion of Phase 1 is 90-120 days after start. Deliverable is a report to Intel management on overall recommendations, recommended quantitative surveys, detailed focus group and IDI findings, and a one-day workshop where Aberdeen and Intel read the focus-group tea leaves and shape Phases 2 and 3.

Phase 2 -- Develop the survey instrument for the initial and tracking surveys and execute every six months. Analyze and aggregate responses along with recommended actions in a report to Intel management.

Phase 3 -- Assumption is that consumer LT will be adopted first in developed countries. Prepare and execute localized surveys in major IT markets: US, Japan, Germany, UK, France, China (special case), Italy, Canada, Brazil and Mexico. These 10 countries represent 74% of IT spending (source: Aberdeen) and are a proxy for developed countries.

- Analyze and determine local consumer LT Platform market opportunities, segments, key turn-on and turn-off messages, and high-level go to market strategies.
- Aggregate WW responses for global market opportunity, segmentation, positioning, messages, and strategy.

### Abstract

A proposal from Aberdeen Group EVP Peter S. Kastner to Intel Corporation arguing that the consumer market for Intel's LaGrande Technology (LT) represents a significant untapped opportunity exceeding $150M in annual revenue. The proposal outlines a three-phase multinational research methodology spanning qualitative focus groups, tracking surveys, and localized quantitative surveys across 5-10 countries. Aberdeen contends that Intel's plan to focus LT exclusively on business markets overlooks consumer demand driven by identity theft fears, virus scares, online trust concerns, and intra-family privacy needs. The proposal estimates 10M+ consumer units by 2006 at $100 retail uplift per unit.
