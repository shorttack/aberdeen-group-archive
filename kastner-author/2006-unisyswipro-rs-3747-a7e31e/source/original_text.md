
Can Unisys-Wipro Alliance Cover All Outsourcing Bases? 
Market Event 
 
Unisys Corp. and Wipro Technologies have linked up in an alliance that can service the IT outsourcing customer both on-site and offshore. Recent Aberdeen research shows that the typical outsourcing buyer can gain advantages on two fronts, rather than on one at the expense of another: the cost savings of offshore work coupled and the added customer care of working with someone close by from the outsourcing provider�s team. This partnership between two key players in the IT outsourcing arena has the potential to do just that and possibly more.

Customers of both vendors cited cost savings and the freedom for internal IT to focus on more strategic work as key benefits in outsourcing ADM work. However, Wipro may bring an added benefit to the table: Half of its customers that were represented in the survey cited improved version control and lifecycle management as a benefit in outsourcing application work. Anything that can simplify IT operations can pay off in indirect savings and efficiency. 
Further, most Unisys customers said a key response to challenges in outsourcing relationships is implementing new or revised vendor management processes. By comparison, most Wipro clients did not cite process changes as a response to challenges. 
Most companies in the Outsourcing Application Development and Maintenance survey said vendor management costs and effort were higher than they had expected, highlighting the special operational challenges that working with third-party providers in the delivery of critical business functions can pose. Further, a majority of survey respondents for a previous report, The Business Value of IT Outsourcing, indicated they dedicated more management time and resources to help overcome initial challenges in their outsourcing relationships (Figure 1), while 30% tinkered with vendor management processes.

Aberdeen Conclusions and Recommendations 
The Wipro-Unisys alliance combines cost savings with tighter customer focus. As Wipro�s subcontractor, Unisys will provide Wipro clients with local support services, including desktop and server maintenance, desk-side support; equipment installs, moves, adds and changes (IMACs); and tape exchanges at data centers. Wipro, meanwhile, will handle remote services, including remote server management and service desk/help desk support. 
The alliance has already won a major deal with a U.S.-based Fortune 500 company, according to a statement from Unisys: It will be a subcontractor to Wipro in providing managed services, while both companies will support more than 20,000 of the company�s employees in the U.S., Canada, and the U.K.
