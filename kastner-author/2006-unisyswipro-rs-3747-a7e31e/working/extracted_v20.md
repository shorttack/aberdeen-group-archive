## **Can Unisys-Wipro Alliance Cover All Outsourcing Bases?** 

## **Market Event** 

Unisys Corp. and Wipro Technologies have linked up in an alliance that can service the IT outsourcing customer both on-site and offshore. Recent Aberdeen research shows that the typical outsourcing buyer can gain advantages on two fronts, rather than on one at the expense of another: the _**cost savings**_ of offshore work coupled and the _**added customer care**_ of working with someone close by from the outsourcing provider’s team. This partnership between two key players in the IT outsourcing arena has the potential to do just that and possibly more. 

## **Aberdeen Analysis** 

Cheaper labor and high quality have combined to form the foundation of India’s rise as an information technology powerhouse over the last decade. So, little wonder that respondents to Aberdeen’s recent benchmark survey on _Outsourcing Application Development and Maintenance_ whose companies outsource work to that country cited cost savings as the chief driver behind their application outsourcing strategies. 

Wipro is one of the top three Indian providers and – not surprisingly – its customers’ focus on cost savings was more than evident in the survey. But at the same time, most of those customers cited communication or cultural “gaps” with the provider as a top challenge in their outsourcing relationships. ## **Aberdeen Conclusions and Recommendations** 

The Wipro-Unisys alliance combines cost savings with tighter customer focus. As Wipro’s subcontractor, Unisys will provide Wipro clients with local support services, including desktop and server maintenance, desk-side support; equipment installs, moves, adds and changes (IMACs); and tape exchanges at data centers. Wipro, meanwhile, will handle remote services, including remote server management and service desk/help desk support. 

The alliance has already won a major deal with a U.S.-based Fortune 500 company, according to a statement from Unisys: It will be a subcontractor to Wipro in providing managed services, while both companies will support more than 20,000 of the company’s employees in the U.S., Canada, and the U.K.
