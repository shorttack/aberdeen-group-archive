Aberdeen Group Point of View
ILM is a classic “Great New Idea”, with all the challenges and opportunities:
Opportunity: automating data life-cycle policies and related storage management has great potential business value
ILM Timeframe: the next decade.  (See systems management in 1990s)
Winners: None yet.  Opening gun just went off.  EMC making waves.
Immediate services opportunity
Conclusion:  ILM will come to be the big storage-related market battle of the decade  whether called ILM or not.
Concept clarity:  what is ILM?
Definition:  too vague, but vagueness gives the ability to morph the concept over time as products mature 
Vendors fight with their vision and products as the “true believers” of ILM.  (See Unix market)
Big concept short on solutions
No one can deliver on the ILM vision anytime soon
Challenge is to line up a product & service train with immediate payback yet integrates a solution over time … without creating jaded and disgruntled customers
Lack of visible business value
Looks like “more IT infrastructure” with few tangible business benefits to non-IT execs
Likely cause of impeded growth in ILM market, especially early
