Digital Consumer Electronics and Information Technology
    • Intel announced last night a $400M expected shortfall in Q2 revenue, driven largely by slow microprocessor sales, with Europe being the new soft spot.
    • Today, the stock market tanked on Intel’s news because semiconductors are widely viewed as the building blocks of technology, and hence the sector which will lead high tech out of a two-year recession.
    • Two weeks ago, Intel reduced PC desktop and laptop chip prices aggressively.  We believe the reasons for doing this are:
        ◦ Manufacturing capacity at 300 cm wafers and .13 micron allow for more chips at much lower cost per chip
        ◦ Advanced Micro Devices’ market share inroads to 22% of the Intel-compatible microprocessor market is causing Intel to be very competitively focused on price and performance
        ◦ At higher clock speeds (now at 2.566 GHz), Intel’s P4 performs better versus AMD than Intel does at lower clock speeds — going for technology competitive advantage versus AMD’s lower price per chip
        ◦ Seasonal factors, namely preparing the market for the big Q3 (school) and Q4 (Christmas) buying seasons.
    • Aberdeen’s retail PC tracking research (Caroline Kastner), has noted a number of changes in the past month:
        ◦ More hardware for the same dollars
            ▪ Faster microprocessors due to Intel price reductions
            ▪ Lower disk drive costs due to IBM inventory dumping
    • But as the CPU speeds increase, memory and GB in the HDD offered decreases.
    • Across the board, manufacturers are offering less memory for the same price point, but compensating by offering higher-end features like an upgraded optical drives or better monitors.
    • Retailers, like BestBuy and Circuit City, offers haven’t changed considerably in the past three weeks because many of their offers are already rock bottom ($375 off eMachines online prices). 
        ◦ With PC sales down as much as 22% this quarter and Intel having reduced its processor prices May 26th, manufacturers and retailers are offering some of the best deals ever to entice consumers.
        ◦ HP, Sony, Dell, Gateway, Compaq, and eMachines all offer free shipping, a $60-$110 dollar value, on popular models.
        ◦ At the beginning of May, companies like Gateway advertised free HDD upgrade up to 80GB, now popular deals include DVD-ROM or CD-RW upgrades or $100 mail-in rebate.
        ◦ PC manufacturers and retailers are pushing PC bundles that include a monitor and sometimes a printer.  Especially noticeable are HP and Compaq who formerly emphasized CPUs alone.
        ◦ Gateway
            ▪ With the introduction of the 1.7GHz Intel Celeron processor, Gateway has replaced the 1.3GHz Celeron Processor in its 300S and 300X model with a 1.7GHz for the same price.  Gateway also has an attractive bundle with a flat panel display.
            ▪ Gateway has also begun to boost its models with P4 2.0AGHz processors to 2.26GHz for the same cost.
        ◦ Dell
            ▪ Dell’s has replaced its 4400 line with the 4500s that offer faster P4 processors, but less GB in the HDD.
            ▪ Meanwhile, Dell souped up its midrange and high end of the new 4500S line that launched with all 1.7GHZ Celeron processors to P4 1.8 and 2.0GHz processors for the same price.
