﻿Peter Kastner’s Wish List







Photo


HP Scanjet 5500c	


I used to do a lot of photography and that means I have boxes of photos, slides and negatives.  This handy scanner will bulk load old-style photo media and add it to my growing digital archive.  The way I figure it, if I have to handle every negative and photo, the project will never get done.  The Scanjet 5500c might add just the right assist to get this project going.

Dell Axim x5 Pocket PC


 
With Outlook 2002 inside, this powerful pocket pc gets the job done without breaking the bank.  Just imagine, I can delete all my spam from my Inbox on the train into work!  Then use Avantgo to read my cached web pages on all the latest overnight technology news.  With a D-Link DCF-660w 802.11 wireless compact flash adapter installed, I can roam the office and be synched all the time.

Viewsonic NextVision N6

While my smug colleagues are lusting after the arguably luscious Samsung 42” plasma high-definition TV (HDTV), I’m solving the problem with the Viewsonic NextVision N6.  The brand new N6, which retails for $xxx, plugs into your PC display monitor.  Voila!  A “personal HDTV”.  Since decent new PC monitors are $200 and up, and used monitors are so plentiful that they’re an environmental hazard, this penurious New Englander thinks the N6 could be just the gadget to bring PHDTV to the home office.  Oh, and the N6 also handles multiple digital video and audio sources.  Now, if I can only hide that new remote control before my wife sees it and goes ballistic…

Gateway Profile 4 XL

With a tip of the hat to Apple, Gateway has crafted one neat all-in-one.  The PC’s chassis and peripherals are snugly fit below a 17” LCD flat panel monitor.  Customize it with Intel’s hyperthreading 3.06Ghz microprocessor, and you’ll have a desktop that doesn’t take a lot of real estate.  More great industrial designs such as the Profile 4 will help get Gateway back on track to better fiscal health.  And the Profile 4 will make a great platform for Microsoft’s media center version of Windows XP.

nVidia GeForce FX
screen shot  which I cannot paste ftp://press:pressaccess1@press.nvidia.com/GeForce_FX/board_shots/The_Goods.jpg


Unless Santa sends me beta silicon, I doubt I’ll get nVidia’s new GeForce FX graphics card under the tree for Christmas.  Even so, a rain check will do.  In case you haven’t noticed, the rate of improvement in graphics processors has been stunning this year, as ATI and nVidia innovate and compete.  GeForce FX takes the battle to a new level, with hardware that drives high frames-per-second on multiple monitors with near cinema quality — all generated on the fly with a powerful engine and lightening-fast memory.  When the games get tuned for this baby, you’ll really get sucked into the game.  Meanwhile, the GeForce FX will give Microsoft developers plenty of horsepower to use as the platform for the next Windows’ version with three-dimensional support in the user interface.

Davis Instruments Vantage Pro Plus


Weather and climate have interested me since I was ten years old.  No more wet and dry sling psychrometers needed with the Davis Vantage Pro Plus.  The outside unit sits in a place where the weather is, wirelessly reporting every few seconds on a dozen wind, temperature, and precipitation metrics.  The inside unit shown in the photo has a graphical display and keeps a history and trends.  But the neat ability to automatically transfer the data points into a PC for long-term storage and analysis make for a perfect micro-climate measurement machine.  Just think, in 30 years I’ll know whether I live in climate zone 6B or 7A — assuming global warming hasn’t changed the whole map in the meantime.
