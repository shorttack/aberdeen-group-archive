## Abstract

A 2026 retrospective memoir comparing Peter Kastner's 2001-2004 prescience track record across two Kastner-authored methodologies: the Maxtor RAMP structured-interview corpus (39 studies, 2-5 year enterprise horizons) and the DCT weekly consumer PC retail tracker (75 studies, days-to-weeks tactical horizons). Written after the Phase 3 backfill of 105 DCT DEFERRED observations, this memoir argues that the methodology differences — not analyst skill differences — drive the gap in study-level prescience scoring, and shows that at the observation level both corpuses post strong records.
