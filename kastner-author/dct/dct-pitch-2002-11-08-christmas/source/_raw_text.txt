Draft Pitch Letter 

Dear _______________:

Dreaming of a Digital Christmas?

	If so, great deals abound.   

Technology improvements and market forces have resulted in an unprecedented array of low-priced, high-powered consumer technology products for shoppers to chose from this holiday season.  

According to Aberdeen Group, consumers who a year or two ago may not have considered buying a hot new PDA, digital camera, or DVD player due to high prices, will now find retail shelves stocked full of relatively inexpensive, highly useable devices with superior technology.

Here's what's in store:  

DVD: Four years ago, DVD players cost $500, $600, even $700 - well above the mainstream price point of about $300.  Now DVD players sell for as little as $50, thanks to a new wave of manufacturers that obtain reference designs - basically a standard blueprint - for the machines and manufacture them in low-cost locations. 

Digital Cameras: These will be one of the hottest-selling items this year, thanks largely to falling prices for small cameras and the availability of high-quality, low-cost color digital printers.  Prices range from $99; $300 buys a 2 MB or 3 MB model with a 2x or 3x optical zoom.  

Personal Digital Assistants: PDA processing power and memory have continued to increase, to the point where top-of-the-line models now contain 64MB of memory and 32MB of program space.  The last generation of high-performance PDA priced in the $500-$550 range.  Today, economies of scale and competitive pressures have reduced prices to $200 to $300 for a very well equipped device. And as PDA ownership grows, low-cost wireless data services are emerging through service providers.  For $10 a month, unlimited data access can be added to a wireless service.

Personal Computers: Consumers buying PCs can now get much more for their dollar than they could even a few months ago - a result of falling chip prices and competitive pressures.   $900 buys a Pentium 2.4GHz machine with half a gigabyte of RAM memory and a monitor and printer.   Consumers can also obtain a highly functional PC with a monitor for around $500 - a price-value proposition that would have been unheard of even a year ago.  

Flat Panel (LCD) Monitors: Another hot product this holiday season will be flat-panel PC monitors, which are also falling drastically in price.   A 15-inch LCD monitor recently priced at $249.  A few months ago that would have been an unheard-of price.  $500 buys a 17-inch TV/PC monitor, which is the equivalent of an 18-inch CRT monitor.

Home Networking: Devices that enable consumers to create a zone for wireless connectivity inside their house or even across their property are becoming increasingly popular.  Eighteen months ago, an access point and an adapter card for a laptop cost about $500.  Now the same technology can be purchased for about $200.  
Stocking Stuffers: High-tech stocking stuffers abound this year too, at the $50 and under price point.  A USB-memory device can store much more than a floppy, and can fit on a key chain.  Figure $1-2 a megabyte in 16-, 32-, and 64-megabyte sizes.  Or how about a latte with email?  T-Mobile sells 300 minutes of Internet access for $50, which you can use at over 1,500 Starbucks stores or at hotels and airports.  
Holiday Greetings: Even holiday greetings will go digital this year.  With $50 software like Pinnacle Express, digital photos and videos can be quickly and creatively crafted into a photo album on a CD-ROM or DVD.  This year, at least, the novelty will be fresh, and far-away friends can see much more of the family than writing can convey.
