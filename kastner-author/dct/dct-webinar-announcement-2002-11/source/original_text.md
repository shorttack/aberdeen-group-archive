## Original Document Text

﻿Webinar
	Digital Consumer Technology: The Revolution Has Started	 
Date and time:	11/01/2002,11:30 am
Eastern, USA & Canada (DST) = GMT -04:00	
Presenters:	Peter Kastner, Executive Vice President and Chief Research Officer, Russ Craig, Research Director, Semiconductors & Isaac Ro, Research Associate, Emerging Technologies Intelligence Group 	
	Duration:	1 hr including Q&A
	Description:	There is much activity in the Digital Consumer Technology market arena as the boundaries between previously separate product categories begin to blur, bringing non-traditional competitors in contact with each other and newly emergent firms. In this webinar, we will discuss three areas of emerging DCT products: 

· Mobile Convergence Devices 

· Home Media Servers 

· Home Networking Challenges 

Despite the slower than expected emergence of third generation wireless networks, mobile devices are rapidly diversifying, with convergent voice and data enabled wireless devices being the hottest segment of this emerging market. Aberdeen will highlight key supply-side market dynamics against this backdrop. 

Home media servers have begun to appear, but approaches are diverse. The Aberdeen DCT practice will discuss the major variations now available in the global market, and the pros and cons of each. This will be followed by an Aberdeen view of what is required to make the media server segment into a mainstream product category. 

The third discussion topic focuses on home networking, the acronym soup that is inundating consumers, what is really required to serve the networked DCT home of the future, and why all current solutions are unsatisfactory. We close with an illustrative integrated architecture that points the way to future possibilities of a more satisfactory product offering.
