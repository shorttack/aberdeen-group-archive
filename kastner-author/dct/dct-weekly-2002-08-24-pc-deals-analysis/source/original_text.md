This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deal Trends
With three weeks to go in the back-to-school promotion season, the market is in the midst of adding enormous technology bang for the buyer's buck.  Pentium 4 2.53 GHz high-end microprocessors, too expensive for PC Deals' mass-market focus to date, exploded onto our radar screen, while 2.26 GHz machines are now under $900.
Led by Compaq, this week's deals include a 2.0A GHz Pentium 4 with monitor for under $750, and a 2.26 GHz machine with monitor for under $800.  We think HP, which lost market share to Dell last quarter and which will announce Q2 earnings this week, is out to get that market share back with outstanding PC deals.
Pentium and Athlon chips in the 1.8 GHz range are disappearing, supplanted by 2 GHz and up as the baseline for machines over $900.  Even Intel Celeron chips at 1.2-1.4 MHz, common until now in machines up to $1,000, are being pushed down to the lowest of our nine price bands.  And it's not just the microprocessor chips - configurations are getting richer in memory and peripherals.  
The segments improving the least are the low end, which we define as $600 and under, and high-performance Pentium machines made with RDRAM.  Low end machines are not changing their price points so much as upgrading technology while keeping the prices largely constant.  RDRAM machines are hundreds of dollars more than their DDR SDRAM equivalents, signaling to us that consumers will soon need to learn about the idiosyncrasies of double data rate (DDR) memory.
This week, buyers should try to buy a customized machine.  The reason is two-fold.  First, the technology is moving much faster at this time through the online, custom arms of Dell and HP/Compaq through stores, particularly Best Buy.  Second, this is a slow week running up to Labor Day, so Comp-USA and other retailers have dropped circulars this week, effectively benching themselves in the PC deal game.
We expect next Sunday's circulars to be full of deals as the post-Labor Day, end-of-summer arrives in earnest.  Also, Intel is expected to announce a new high end chip soon, which will undoubtedly unleash a further cascade of good deals this fall.
Buying Tips
Dell is offering optical and PC monitor upgrades this week while charging the regular $99 for shipping.  We had the most luck configuring the Dimension 4500, a flexible model with numerous processor and peripheral options.  The 4500 is the Dell machine to configure in the $1,000 - $1,500 range.  The 8200, which uses RDRAM, costs about $200 more than comparable DDR configurations with the 4500.
When considering Gateway PCs, it is a good idea to shop as a "small and mid-size business." Selecting that option means you can view all of Gateway's models. This week, several of Gateway's business models allow you to upgrade to Windows XP Professional for $30, an outstanding value.  Last but not least, check out the new all-in-one machines: LCD flat-panel monitor houses the Pentium 4 machine, ala Macintosh.  Worth checking out.
Best Buy leads the retailers this week, as CompUSA lacks deals.  Best Buy store custom configuration stations have some outstanding advertised deals on Compaq machines.  However, outside the advertised specials, the custom configuration options include many older configurations at less attractive prices.  Before placing that custom order, take a walk down the aisles and check out the current offerings.
Current Deals
Compaq Online offers CD-ROM upgrades, memory-speed upgrade, free shipping, and a $50 in mail-in rebate or back-to-school rebate on "build-your-own" machines, such as the Presario 8000T and 8000Zs in this week's PC Deals, at Compaq Online.
Dell configures an optical drive upgrade or a second optical drive.  Free upgrades to a 19" flat screen monitor are also common.
eMachines Online offers either $100 or $175 off a PC and monitor bundle. Although not advertised openly on their website, eMachines also offers free shipping, a $90 savings. Circuit City, CompUSA, and BestBuy offer excellent bundled deals on eMachines. 
Gateway has new deals and lower prices, along with a new look for Gateway's homepage. Check out the 300L, 500L, and 700L specials for excellent pre-configured machines.  Note that the 500L Special does not include a monitor. Other deals include XP Pro upgrades for $30 and free CD-ROM drive upgrades. 
HP Online continues to advertise free shipping on all purchases over $250. HP Online encourages buying bundled PCs, with its best rebates of up to $725 requiring the purchase of a PC, monitor, and HP accessories. Rebates from HP's website include $100 off the Pavilion 502n, Pavilion 512n, and customized AMD Athlon PCs. Retailers frequently discount the Pavilion 502n and 512n, so the best deals on these PCs are usually found in weekly sales circulars of the major chains - and tracked here in PC Deals.
Sony continues to offer $50 or $100 rebates on its machines, as well as free shipping on select PCs. Customizing a Sony PC yields the best deal on a Sony without monitor. By doing this, you can either upgrade to an 80 GB hard drive for free or get an additional $50 to $100 off the advertised rebated price. If you already have a monitor, national retailers advertise weekly bundled Sony PCs that include a monitor and printer at better prices than buying all the components from Sony directly.
Why the Winners Won
EEMachines is back on top of the $550 price point. BestBuy continues to offer the best prices on eMachines' T1220 and T1440 models. All the machines in this price range include a printer this week. 
At the $750 price point, last week's winner, the eMachines T1860 with an Athlon 1800+ 1.53 GHz processor sold out. The top three winner's this week are all new.  The customized Compaq 6000T at Best Buy has a Pentium 2.0A and a monitor, a great value for under $750.  The HP Pavilion 522n has a slower microprocessor, but a larger hard drive and better opticals - but no monitor.  The new eMachines T1862 uses a Celeron 1.8, putting it behind the two Pentium 4 machines.
For under $800, the customized Compaq 6000T at Best Buy has a fast 2.26 GHz Pentium 4 with the latest 533 MHz front-side bus, a monitor and printer.  This is our PC Deals "buy of the week" - the first time we have seen Intel's latest technology at such a low price point.  Also, the Compaq 6000T could win this week's $1,000 category too!
At $1000, EMachines' T2080 offers a low price with great specs in an Athlon XP, making it the continued winner in the $1000 range. If you are interested in a Pentium 4 machine, then Dell's 4500S should be considered. 
Last week's $1,100 winners, the Compaq Online's Presario 8000Z and Compaq's retail machine, the Presario 6027US, lose out this week at a Dell 4500S with a fast 2.4 GHz processor and a 19" flat screen monitor.
At $1,200, it's a toss-up between HP'S Pavilion 762n from BestBuy and Compaq's 8000T.  The Compaq lacks a monitor.  Third place Dell 4500 has a fast 2.4 GHz processor but less memory and a smaller hard drive than the top two.  In this category, it's worth checking specs and configuring carefully this week.
$1,350 now buys Intel's top of the line (for another week) 2.53 GHz microprocessor from Compaq online or Dell.  Our rating system puts the Compaq ahead, but a smart shopper would check them both out.
Those with $1,550 to spend should start in the $1,350 category and accessorize, accessorize, accessorize. 
The above $1,550 price category looks haggard.  The top machines have fast RDRAM, but the fastest microprocessors with DDR memory are now at lower price points.  Aberdeen Group will consider reordering our price points after Labor Day.
