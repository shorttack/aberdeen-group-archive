PC Deal Script:  August 27th
Brian:  What's the big picture?
Peter: With three weeks to go in the back-to-school promotion season, the market is in the midst of adding enormous technology bang for the buyer's buck.  Pentium 4 2.53 GHz high-end microprocessors, too expensive for PC Deals' mass-market focus to date, exploded onto our radar screen, while 2.26 GHz machines are now under $900.  All this at www.aberdeen.com/pcdeals.
Brian:  Who are the big movers?
Peter: Led by Compaq, this week's deals include a 2.0A GHz Pentium 4 with monitor for under $750, and a 2.26 GHz machine with monitor for under $800.  We think HP, which lost market share to Dell last quarter and which will announce Q2 earnings this week, is out to get that market share back with outstanding PC deals.
Brian:  What technology is changing?
Peter: Pentium and Athlon chips in the 1.8 GHz range are disappearing, supplanted by 2 GHz and up as the baseline for machines over $900.  Even Intel Celeron chips at 1.2-1.4 MHz, common until now in machines up to $1,000, are being pushed down to the lowest of our nine price bands.  And configurations overall are getting richer in memory and peripherals.  
Brian: Is everything changing at the same rate, or are some areas behaving differently?
Peter:  The segments improving the least are the low end, which we define as $600 and under, and also high-performance Pentium machines made with RDRAM.  Low end machines are not changing their price points so much as upgrading technology while keeping the prices largely constant.  RDRAM machines are hundreds of dollars more than their DDR SDRAM equivalents, signaling to us that consumers will soon need to learn about the idiosyncrasies of different speeds of double data rate (DDR) memory.
Brian:  How should PC buyers hunt for a good PC deal this week?
Peter: This week, buyers should try to buy a customized machine.  The reason is two-fold.  First, the technology is moving much faster at this time through the online, custom arms of Dell as well as HP/Compaq through stores, particularly Best Buy.  Second, this is a slow week running up to Labor Day, so CompUSA and other retailers have dropped circulars this week, effectively benching themselves in the PC deal game.
Brian: What news does Aberdeen see coming this week?
Peter: We expect next Sunday's circulars to be full of deals as the post-Labor Day, end-of-summer arrives in earnest.  Also, Intel is expected to announce a new high end 2.8 GHz chip, which will undoubtedly unleash a further cascade of lower chip prices and good deals this fall.  Second, Gateway announced yesterday its new all-in-one machines: an LCD flat-panel monitor houses the Pentium 4 machine, ala Apple Macintosh.  The dream machine with a 17" monitor at about $1,900 is worth checking out.
Brian:  Any machines stand out this week?
Peter: For under $800, the customized Compaq 6000T at Best Buy has a fast 2.26 GHz Pentium 4 with the latest 533 MHz front-side bus, a monitor and printer.  This is our PC Deals "buy of the week" - the first time we have seen Intel's latest technology at such a low price point.  Also, the Compaq 6000T could win this week's $1,000 category too!
Last week's $1,100 winners, the Compaq Online's Presario 8000Z and Compaq's retail machine, the Presario 6027US, lose out this week at a Dell 4500S with a fast 2.4 GHz processor and a 19" flat screen monitor.
Third, $1,350 now buys Intel's top of the line (for another week) 2.53 GHz microprocessor from Compaq online or Dell.  Our rating system puts the Compaq ahead, but a smart shopper would check them both out.
Those with $1,550 to spend should start in the $1,350 category and accessorize, accessorize, accessorize. 
Brian:  Thanks, Peter.  That was Peter Kastner of Aberdeen Group, www.aberdeen.com/pcdeals.
