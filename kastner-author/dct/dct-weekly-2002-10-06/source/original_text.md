This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deal Trends
After two slow weeks after the end of the school buying season, this week sees a major rotation in the retail channel.  As a result, caveat emptor!  Buyers will want to make sure they get the latest technology at a good price, and not last summer's leftover inventory clearance.
eMachines, HP, and Sony all delivered new models to retailers.  These machines include the price cuts and processors from Intel's August announcements, albeit nine weeks after the announcement leaked.  You'll see several new winners in the price-value classes at $1,200 and under.
Most of the machines above $550 come with DVD-ROM and CR-RW.  That means you can watch DVDs on your computer as well as rip and burn CDs.  DVD burners are still a $300-and-up add-on that won't come down in price until next year.
The Pentium III-based Celerons at speeds up to 1.4GHz are now clearance items.  The low end is populated by the Pentium 4-based Celerons at 1.8-2.0 GHz.
There are more Athlon XP configurations to choose from, which should please struggling AMD.  However, chips at 2400+ or better are not making it into top-tier systems.  We included an $1800 Alienware computer this week as an indicator of AMD high-end price-to-value at this time.  Nor do we expect the recently announced 2800+ to be seen into retail systems at price points we cover until next year.
This Week's Buying Tips
First and foremost, carry a copy of PC Deals with you to a retail store.  That way, you won't be confused by the model overlap - the leftovers are competing with the new good stuff.  And unlike clothing, old computers stay at their last list price until the last one sells to an unknowing sucker customer.
Second, this week is a lead-in to Columbus Day weekend.  We think there will be more deals - and certainly more circulars to study - next weekend.
eMachines just announced their fourth quarter line-up.  Because the configurations won't change for 90 days, the best technology values are now, early in the quarter while lowest prices are late in the quarter.  EMachines takes the blue ribbon in the three lowest-price categories we cover.
HP finally has Pentium 4 machines above 2.2 GHz at retail.  HP is mum, but it looks like the Compaq Presario brand is exiting the consumer channel.  All the new machines at retail bear the HP corporate name - but not Pavilion.  Circuit City has the best HP deals this week.
Sony's new lineup is priced online in the $1,500-$2,000 range.  That's well above the competition in price - and the competition has caught up or surpassed Sony in the media department.  Look for models starting with PCV-RZxxx.  The PCV-RXxxx models are obsolescent but still in stores.
Gateway is seldom a category winner based on the value-price methodology that we use [URL LINK].  We see Gateway moving more to a high-end niche, with custom loaded computers a mainstay.  This may be just the deal for some.  For instance, Gateway is a bit off the fastest/biggest specs with the 500X or 700X, but the machines come with LCD monitors, a desktop space-saver.
Dell's 4500 continues to be a category killer - infinitely configurable from $900 to $1,600, and always competitive.  The Dell 8200 is less attractive.  A top-of-the-line 4500 with a Pentium 4 at 2.8GHz and 512 MB DDR SDRAM costs just under $1,400 today.  In an 8200 with RDRAM, the identical configuration's price balloons to $1,669 - a $270 premium for RDRAM which is pure profit for Dell.  The smart way to buy an 8200 is with 256 MB of RDRAM, with an immediate upgrade that's not bought from Dell.
Spotlight

Microsoft is now in the home networking hardware business, selling 802.11b WiFi access points and network adapters for desktops and laptops.  The immediate need for these products is damage control.  Windows XP has some support for WiFi, but the connection experience leaves a lot to be desired.  Worse, many other WiFi companies use inexpensive reference designs, with little thought to consumer usability.  Our home experience has been, well, simply deplorable.  Microsoft's products have installation wizards aimed at mere-mortal consumers, not Information Technology geeks.  We hope to review these products later this fall.
Current Deals
Compaq Online gives a 120 GB hard drive for the price of an 80 GB one when customizing an 8000 series Presario.  There is also an opportunity to save up to $100 on a second optical drive.   
Dell offers 19" monitor, optical, and warranty upgrades this week.  Also cash in on $50 off all Dimension desktops.  
EMachines Online offers either $100 or $175 off a PC and monitor bundle. Although not advertised openly on their website, eMachines also offers free shipping, a $90 savings. Circuit City, CompUSA, and BestBuy offer excellent bundled deals on eMachines. 
Gateway offers specials on various models in each of its lines.  Look for free memory and hard drive upgrades, CD-ROM upgrades, or Microsoft Windows XP Pro as a free upgrade. 
Why the Winners Won
With the expiration of Gateway's 300Lspecial, eMachines wins the $550 price point with its new T1840.  
At the $750 price point, the new eMachines T2085 is a powerful Athlon 2000+ machine with both a DVD-RW and DVD. 
The eMachine T2200 also wins the $900 category with Circuit City's deal.  This machine has a flat screen monitor, and a speedy Athlon XP 2200+ processor.  For just under $900, the Dimension 4500 offers a great machine with a 19" monitor.
The new HP 523n at $950 this week at BestBuy, wins the $1000 category with an attractive PC, monitor, and printer bundle.
Dell takes the $1100 price point, as well, with a machine that is just a speed-bump slower than of the winning $1200 machine.  With a 2.4 GHz Pentium 4 processor, 512 MB DDR memory, a 19" monitor, and an 80 GB hard drive, you can download like crazy.  
At $1,200, HP's new 753n machine is a great deal at BestBuy that includes a printer.  
$1,350 now buys a 2.66 GHz processor at Dell Online, while under $1,400 buys a top-of-the-line 2.8 GHz Pentium 4.  In the $1,200 - $1,500 price band, Dell beats all competitors with lower prices for faster processors, and without skimping on peripherals.  Dell takes the $1,550 category too.
Dell takes the over-$1,550 category by a whisker over Compaq based on the free 19" monitor, and the better performance of the Dell 8200's RDRAM.
