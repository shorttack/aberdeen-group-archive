This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deal Trends
The retail ad circulars are back after a two-week hiatus! No notable price cuts emerged this week, although many price increases appeared.  Retailers and manufacturers should be gearing up for the holiday season soon, so look for better deals in the future.  But caveat emptor as many PCs are already marked at extremely low prices, as compared to when the market was booming.  Manufacturers and retailers may not be able to drop prices drastically lower because memory and disk drive component prices have been firming lately - at least until Intel cuts prices in two weeks.  Our star-watching suggests a holiday PC buyer should wait until Thanksgiving for best deals.  And we have no worries about supply!  It's been a tough year.
Compaq custom configurations are gone, as www.athome.compaq.com no longer exists  However, going to that web page will redirect you to HP's Online retail site, www.hpshopping.com, where you can buy Compaq Presario 6000 series models.  Although this is the first time you can buy Compaq's retail models direct from HP-Compaq Online - retail models were previously only available in stores like BestBuy or online retail sites) - the option to "build your own Compaq" seems to have disappeared.  This is quite a loss for the consumer as customized Compaq PCs offered great value, and often won several price points in weekly PC Deals' evaluations.
PC Deals was a bit surprised to find that HP has integrated Compaq's website into it's home site.  HP has been slow to fully mesh with Compaq, and it previously appeared that Compaq would maintain it's own online site and customer service/ordering process.  We expect the Compaq brand to focus more on business and home bread-and-butter productivity, while the HP PC brand emphasizes performance and multimedia capabilities.  A new line-up is due in mid-2003.
Last week was not a great week to buy a Dell Dimension 2300, but it was a great week to buy Dell's middle to higher-end models.  This week Dell's reversed the trend.  The 2300 has a great price tag, but other models experienced price hikes.  PC Deals' highest-end Dell, the Dimension 8250 with a 2.8 GHz processor and RDRAM, costs over $300 more this week than last.  And the new-in-October 8250 is more expensive than the 8200 predecessor.  What's with the rising Dell pricing, Dude?  While Dell still wins several PC Deals price points, this is not the week to purchase a higher-end system.
This Week's Buying Tips
Best Buy is offering $100 - $150 rebate on all computer packages.  Also, no interest for 12 months on computer packages.
Compaq online no longer has its own website.  HP has consolidated Compaq's computers into its website, making www.hpshopping.com the retail site for both HP and Compaq PCs.  As far as PC Deals can tell, the option to customize a Compaq PC no longer exists.  But you can now buy Compaq retail PCs from HP, who is advertising up to $250 off Presario PC and monitor bundles.
You can save an extra $200 at CompUSA this week.  Simply trade in a 300MHz or faster Windows based desktop at the time of purchasing your new machine to cash in on the savings.  $300 on a notebook with a 166 MHz trade-in or better.  Also, there's a $249 special on a 15" LCD monitor - the best ever national retail deal we can recall.  At prices like this, it's no wonder that LCD display sales dollars (but not units) now exceed CRT displays.
Dell's deals include $50 off the Dimension 2300 and free shipping on all Dimension models.  
There was a noticeable lack of eMachine's retail deals this week.  Neither BestBuy nor Circuit City advertised any of the many eMachine's models they carry.  Hopefully the great deals will return next week.
The HP media center PCs have just arrived in stores.  These feature a video home theater in a PC, using new Microsoft extensions to Windows XP.  Try $1700 with a 17" CRT at CompUSA or $2000 at Best Buy with a 15" LCD display.  Great for an all-in-one dorm, apartment, or bedroom solution to TV, stereo, radio, and PC - there's even a remote control.
OfficeMax has a specially configured HP 513xb, featuring an AMD Athlon XP 1700+, 256MB SDRAM, 40GB disk, CD-RW, and a 17" monitor for $700.  Add an HP 3820 printer for $100.
Sony Online offers a plethora of models not seen in retail stores, although many of them are higher end.  You can now customize a Sony Viao PC starting at $699.  And Sony offers $100 off all models as well as one cent shipping, making their deals competitive with BestBuy and Circuit City Online.
