This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deal Trends
AMD ruled the retail ads this week with an abundance of Athlon machines at great prices.  AMD Athlon PCs offer competitive machines that cost less than their Intel counterparts.  Winning the $600, $750, and $900 category with processors that far exceed their Intel Celeron competitors, consider an AMD machine when looking for a great basic home PC.  EMachines, Compaq, and Sony offer some of the best configured AMD Athlon machines.
If you can afford the jump from the $900 to the $1050 price point, your $150 will go a long way.  $900 gets you a fast AMD Athlon machine, but a little more money gets you an Intel P4 2.53GHz without a monitor or a 2.4GHz machine with a monitor.  The $1050 price point is really PC Deal's sweet spot, as the $1200 range stagnates at P4 2.4GHz machines and you don't see a whole lot more bang for your buck until the $1400 realm.
Price fluctuations continue in both the retail and manufacturer channels.  Dell's prices vary from week to week, in some cases by over $100.  Retailers, like BestBuy and Circuit City, have not been steady in their pricing either.  PC Deals predicts that prices will drop, or the consumer will get more for the same price, as the holiday season nears.  But this has been a tough year for the PC market and their pricing has been volatile.  Conclusion: if you think you've found a hot deal, don't hesitate because offers are changing quickly.
Manufacturers and retailers continue to push extras like printers, digital cameras, and scanners because these products continue to sell well while PC sales fall.  So if you're thinking about getting a higher quality color photo printer, you can probably find a brand new model for the same price, or less, than what you paid for your old printer.  So, while it's a great time to upgrade or add new accessories, we don't suggest buying these items from online manufacturers.  Online shipping costs and generally higher prices at manufacturers' sites mean that retail chains really corner this space, especially with bundled PC deals that include a printer, etc. 
This Week's Buying Tips
Best Buy advertised its Sony Creation Station this week.  As an incentive to buy a customized Sony PC, BestBuy offers a free 64MB nVidia GeForce4 MX440 graphics cards with any customized Sony Viao machine.  BestBuy's prices on these customized machines are competitive with the Sony retail models it sells.  But PC Deals did not include this week's advertised BestBuy customized Sony PC because it does not include a monitor or printer for a price very similar to the BestBuy's Sony PCV-RXA842.  Also, PC Deals is unsure whether customizing a PC at BestBuy or other retail stores incurs shipping costs.
There continues to be no advertised retail bundled deals on eMachine's T1840 and T1742 models.  Circuit City does have a bundled deal on the T2200 that includes a flat screen monitor.  Here at PC Deals we're stumped as to this sudden halt in continuous great deals on the lower end eMachine's models.  Hopefully great deals will return as the holiday season approaches.
OfficeMax will save you $50 when buying a customized HP machine with an Intel P4 processor of 2.0GHz or higher.
Office Depot serves up a similar deal with $100 savings on Compaq 8000T customized PCs with Intel P4 processors of 2.6GHz or higher.  Clearly, this means that it is still possible to customize Compaq PCs, although this is no longer an option through HP-Compaq Online.  
Dell's deals include free CD-Burner and hard drive upgrades.  Again, this week there emerges a huge discrepancy between price changes in the Dimension 2300 and 4550.  The Dimension 2300 with a Celeron 1.8GHz processor jumped from $709 to $848 this week.  This suggests that Dell is collecting the price of its "free" upgrades somewhere else in its configuration of its low-end model.  The 2300 with a P4 2.0GHz processor maintained last week's price.  Then surprisingly, all Dimension 4550 machines decreased in cost by about $50.  This means Dell inflated last week's prices on its Dimension 4550 machines.
Last week Gateway pointed out our unconscious exclusion of its 300S PC.  Gateway offers $100 off its 300S machine, making this model very competitive with the $600 price point.  Note that the 300S does not come standard with a monitor, but you can add a 17" monitor for $120.  PC Deals noted good prices for upgrading the processor (up to a P4 1.8), hard drive, and memory.  
Perhaps this was just a fluke, but when PC Deals tried multiple times to access HP's Online site via www.hpshopping.com the page would not open.  We finally succeeded by entering www.shopping.hp.com.  Good rebates can be found on HP and Compaq bundles, but remember retail chains usually factor these rebates into their deals as well.  
Sony Online offers a plethora of models not seen in retail stores at competitive prices, although many of them are higher-end.  Sony continues to focus on media capabilities, but top-of-the-line processors, etc. are not available.  You can now customize a Sony Viao PC starting at $699.  And Sony offers $100 off all models as well as one cent shipping on all products, making their deals competitive with BestBuy and Circuit City Online.
Customize a Sony Viao PC and get an 80GB hard drive for the price of a 60GB one.  Sony Online also offers $50 offer a 120GB or 160GB hard drive.  Ground shipping for a customized model is free.  But beware, now that Sony allows for full-scale customization watch out for automatically added extras that can add hundreds to the cost of your PC.  For example, the RZ series customizable machines costing over $1,500 include a Sony Giga Pocket Personal Video Recorder; choosing to exclude this feature saves you $220.
