This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deals Trends

Amidst the weekend of turkey, stuffing, and crazed holiday shopping, the deals to pounce on arrived.  In fact, the weekly offers arrived Thanksgiving Day amidst the department store flyers and announcements of holiday savings.  

These huge post-Thanksgiving Day offers mean the best bargains we have seen in six months, but also mean more work for the consumer to track more sources than usual. 

Staples and CompUSA advertised prices that beat the usual retail leaders, Best Buy and Circuit City.  Consumers will have to browse a larger selection of vendors to find the best offers.  Don't fall into the trap of buying from merchants with great prices on non-PC items who've now entered the PC market (remember Costco's seemingly good deal on Dell's Dimension 2300 that was always $50 more than Dell Online's prices).  Amazon.com sells HP's Pavilion 513n for $879, which is way over the prices of PC merchants like Best Buy and Circuit City, and included here.

Dell's Dimension 2350 arrived.  Faster DDR memory and a new chipset replace the SDRAM in the now-obsolescent 2300.  Unfortunately, as with the earlier 4550 and 8250 machines, you only get half the memory in a 2350 as in the 2300 for the same cost.

Celerons are disappearing fast from retail machines.  We expect the Pentium 4 2.0 GHz, which was introduced just a little over a year ago, at the $750 level very soon competing with AMD Athlon's.
    
This Week's Buying Tips
Radio Shack entered PC Deals winner's circle this week with its exclusive Presario 6331RSH for $750, an attractive bundle indeed.
Bundled deals on eMachine's T1742 and T1840 are back.  Best Buy bundles these machines at $400 and $500, respectively.  Look for more eMachine's models this week, including the T4060 with a P4 2.0A GHz processor.
www.circuitcity.com and www.bestbuy.com matched prices on multiple PCs this week.  But Circuit City Online has an edge if you're looking for a machine that costs over $1,000: Circuit City will send a $100 gift card after receiving your mail-in rebate forms.  Expect more incentives to appear from these competitors as we near the holiday.  
Best Buy has free shipping on its customized kiosk PCs.  Also look for great financing options from retailers, like no interest for a year!
Dell offers free shipping on all models and instant cash back on the Dimension 8250 and 4550.  $50 mail-in rebate on the brand new Dimension 2350 makes this new series comparable in price to the now gone 2300 series (but with less memory).  Also, if you have the Axim PDA in mind for under someone's tree, order soon - the ship date is December 18th and slipping.
Gateway continues to offer specials on individual machines.  The 500S is back to containing a 2.0A GHz P4 processor after a brief upgrade to a 2.4 GHz one.  
HP Online offers free shipping and continues to offer bundled deals on select models.
Sony Online offers a plethora of models not seen in retail stores at competitive prices, although many of them are higher-end.  Sony continues to focus on media capabilities, but top-of-the-line processors, etc. are not available.  You can now customize a Sony Viao PC starting at $699.  And Sony offers $100 off all models as well as one cent shipping on all products, making their deals competitive with BestBuy and Circuit City Online.  Sony Online offers a free Epson C42UX printer with any Viao desktop purchase through December 31st, making a bundled deal direct from Sony more reasonable.
Customize a Sony Viao PC and get an 80GB hard drive for the price of a 60GB one.  Sony Online also offers $50 offer a 120GB or 160GB hard drive.  Ground shipping for a customized model is free.  But beware, now that Sony allows for full-scale customization watch out for automatically added extras that can add hundreds to the cost of your PC.  For example, the RZ series customizable machines costing over $1,500 include a Sony Giga Pocket Personal Video Recorder; choosing to exclude this feature saves you $220.
