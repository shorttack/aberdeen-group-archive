This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deals Trends

Our secret shoppers say eMachine's and Sony PC are the hot ticket at retail this week.  

EMachines, a consistent performer in the under-$1000 PC Deals prices bands, wins on - ta, dah - low price, according to store managers.  No need for a lot of analysis on that observation, but we'll point out again that PC demand is price elastic, just like the economists tells us.

Sony is doing well for a different reason: consumers are falling for the Memory Stick ploy by buying PCs with media readers that are compatible with Sony's proprietary flash memory, which also fits in digital cameras and camcorders.  As a result, Sony is benefiting at the same time consumers are locking themselves into Sony's memory form factor.

HP, which has been picking up market share against both eMachines and Sony must wonder what it's doing wrong.  We rate the Compaq 6310US along with the Gateway 300L as price-value leaders in the under-$600 class this week, beating eMachines.  But the HP machine lacks a monitor and printer, while eMachines bundles are a ready-to-go out-of-the-box holiday gift.  On the high-end where HP competes with Sony for media-intensive configurations, HP would be wise to add a memory media center in lieu of a floppy disk.  That way, HP machines can read Sony Memory Sticks as well as the other common types: Secure Digital, Compact Flash, SmartMedia and MultiMedia.  The HP Pavilion 893 Media Center PC already has a memory media center.

Gateway, as noted above, is in the deals business in a big way this week.  The company offers the first two-fer that we have seen.  Yes, two for the price of one.  Buy a richly configured 700L at $3,400 with dual hard drives, a 19" LCD flat panel,  and a top-of-the-line Pentium 4 at 3.06 GHz - and get a Celeron-based 300L second machine for free (monitor sold separately).

Dell continues to raise prices, this week on mid-range 4550s and high-end 8240s.
    
This Week's Buying Tips
Radio Shack dropped its PC Deals winner's circle position this week by retiring the attractive Presario 6331RSH for $750.
Bundled eMachines are prevalent this week, after a hiatus of some weeks.  Both BestBuy and Circuit City are appealing to shoppers wanting maximum bang-for-the-buck.
Circuit City will send a $100 gift card after receiving your mail-in rebate forms.  Expect more incentives to appear from retailers as we near the holiday.  Also look for great financing options from retailers - like no 0% interest for a year!
This week, all of Dell's deals end Tuesday.  These offers include free shipping on all Dimension models, as well as $100 and $200 off the 4550 and 8250, respectively.  The Dimension 2350 dropped this week into the under-$900 class, but it has yet to be anywhere near the PC Deals value winner of its model 2300 predecessor.  The price for all Dimensions 4550s went up for the second week in a row, some by over $100.  This is not the best week to buy a 4550.  Our Dimension 8250 reference machine with a Pentium 4 2.8 GHz went up in price from $1,808 to $2,157.  That's a 19% jump in a week!  Overall, Dell has raised prices aggressively this fall, while continuing - to date at least - to gain market share.  That's good for Dell stockholders but not so good for customers.
Gateway continues to offer specials on individual machines.  PC Deals always configures Gateway PCs in the "At Work, Small Office" department because more models are offered.  This week, Gateway has "Triple Double", a deal doubling the warranty, hard drive size, and memory for free.  Translation: good deal, indeed.
At hp.com, we configured a 3.06 GHz Pentium 4  for $1,858.  The deal includes a $150 rebate on processors at 2.66 GHz or higher, a CD-RW upgrade for $10, and a faster 7200 rpm hard drive.  For $740 more, you can get a Dell 8250 that includes a 15" flat panel display instead of the HP MX70 monitor.  That would make the Dell 8250 very unattractive.
Sony Online offers a plethora of models not seen in retail stores at competitive prices, although many of them are higher-end.  Sony continues to focus on media capabilities, but top-of-the-line processors, etc. are not available.  You can now customize a Sony Viao RZ series PC starting at $700.  And Sony offers $100 off all models as well as one cent shipping on all products, making their deals competitive with BestBuy and Circuit City Online.  Sony Online offers a free Epson C42UX printer with any Viao desktop purchase through December 31st, making a bundled deal direct from Sony more reasonable.
Regional chains are worth checking out online before rushing off to the local mall.  IN the west, Fry's Electronics is a household name.  In the east, Microcenter is building traffic and offering attractive prices on both pre-configured machines and bundles with monitor and printer.

Last but not least, trim the tree with all the peripherals and software that line the shelves and have been holiday priced.  There are too many great deals to mention.

Deals of the Week

The $800 bundled eMachines T2085 at BestBuy has an AMD Athlon 2400+ processor and 512 MB of DDR memory, DVD and CD-RW, a flat screen monitor and a Lexmark printer.  

Also at BestBuy is the HP Pavilion 523n, an Athlon 2200+ machine with HP peripherals, this deal is $850.

Another BestBuy deal at $1,400 is the HP Pavilion 753n.  This machine comes with a Pentium 4 at 2.53 GHz, all the usual peripherals, and a 17" HP LCD flat panel monitor.  This beats the configurations at $1,600, so it's a very good deal.
