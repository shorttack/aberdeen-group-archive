This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deals Trends

Good news and bad news this week, PC shoppers.  The good news is that Dell has partially reduced its recent price hikes, although we're far from returning to Dell's best deals before Thanksgiving; the bad news is that there are a pitiful number of retail deals.  PC Deals expects mid-week flyers to appear in the paper advertising the beginning of post-Christmas sales.  

Sony is doing well because consumers are falling for the Memory Stick ploy by buying PCs with media readers that are compatible with Sony's proprietary flash memory, which also fits in digital cameras and camcorders.  As a result, Sony is benefiting at the same time consumers are locking themselves into Sony's memory form factor.  

Of course, Sony is not the only one to use this tactic.  Canon sells one of the smallest digital photo printers that only works with its line of Canon digital cameras.  If you want instant photos on the go, this is a great idea.  HP offers a larger compact printer line that works with any digital media card.

Another item of note is that during this holiday season many retailers offer "best-price guarantees" that mean BestBuy and Circuit City are having a run for their money.  Blockbuster guarantees its movie prices and Target does the same for its electronics.  This means you don't have to shop around as carefully for the best deal on many popular media items. 
    
This Week's Buying Tips
Strategy: buy a newspaper on Christmas day and check out the post-Christmas PC deals.  We expect the best opportunities to score will be Thursday through Saturday.  The day after Thanksgiving had the best deals of the year.  Given the so-so retail performance this holiday season, we expect excellent bargains for those who wait until the 26th.

Circuit City offers a $100 gift card on AMD desktops and notebooks while Best Buy has no interest until 2004 on $299 purchases and up.  Other BestBuy incentives include $200 savings when you buy any Pentium 4 or AMD Athlon computer and a digital camera, camcorder, or gaming console.  Sorry, but you missed the HP 503n pre-Christmas bundle at BestBuy for only $550.  Today, it's $699 through Christmas.  Look for a repeat after Christmas.

Dell's free shipping and memory upgrade isn't enough to bring prices down to where they were previously, but this week does look a lot better than last week.  For example, the Dell Dimension 4550 with a 2.4 GHz Pentium 4 is $149 less expensive than last week, coming in just under $1,200.

Staples will let you upgrade a PC purchase to Windows XP Pro for $25.

OfficeMax has the Verbatim DVD+RW/R and CD-RW drive for $200, about $100 less than the going price for DVD writers.

CircuitCity, unfortunately, ended its great deal on a Sony RXA-842 PC at only $750 Saturday.  This Athlon 2400+ machines features 512 MB of DDR memory, 80 GB hard drive, and DVD/CD-RW.  This is the kind of deal we expect right after Christmas.
