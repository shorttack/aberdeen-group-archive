This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deals Trends

You may have already read that this year's holiday season's sales yielded the lowest rate of growth in decades.  Frankly, PC Deals is not surprised.  As we've been reporting, the holiday PC deals have been disappointing, especially from usual deal winners like Dell.

Our hypothesis that immediate post-Christmas flyers would reveal price cuts did not pan out.  While small electronics, like DVD players, continue to sell well, PCs are not flying off the shelves, but retailers continue to resist further price decreases.  It is taking noticeably longer for the prices of Intel P4 2.8GHz machines - no longer the fastest Pentium 4 - to fall than did 2.53GHz PCs' prices when that chip was second best.  Consumer hesitance to buy more expensive items or replace their 2-year old machines means it's going to be a disappointing quarter for many manufacturers and retailers.

So how are retailers and manufacturers reacting to the dip in PC sales?  This week's PC Deals reveals no consistent trends, but several companies, like Dell, have reduced their prices.  Even with several machines dropping into lower price points (many because they are end of season stock like the eMachines T2085 at BestBuy), this week presented the largest price hike recorded in PC Deal's history.  Last week, HP's 573n sold at Staples for $900.  Loaded with an AMD Athlon 2600+ and 512MB, the 573n is a top performer.  But this week, expect to pay $1,418 at HP Online!  Obviously, last week was the time to buy this great machine. 

On a totally different note, Circuit City Online features customer reviews on all its products.  While this may not seem newsworthy, these reviews offer unbiased critiques that steered one PC Deals writer clear of several electronics choices.  BestBuy Online, on the other hand, often lacks relevant product information such as laptops' weights.  Aberdeen's PC Deals likes all the brands we follow, but checking out reviews on specific models at CNet, Circuit City Online, or Dealtime.com can reveal pros and cons that only purchasers would know.

This Week's Buying Tips

BestBuy is the last online retailer to offer free shipping on all items.  Circuit City offers in-store pick up that negates shipping charges (BestBuy does this as well, if you demand it and the manager accommodates you), but expect to pay at all other sites from Compusa.com to Amazon.com.  One perk of in-store pick up is that you can open and examine your purchase at the retailer and return it immediately if there are any obvious defects.  And the store will install upgrades for you.  Otherwise, BestBuy's free shipping offer saves you at least $20 on ground shipping.

Check out online BestBuy's price on eMachine's T2085 and Circuit City's price on Compaq's 6350US.  Both great deals, the kind we expected to see after Christmas, that these retailers are trying to move as new models come in.

Dell offers $100 off all Dimension 8250 and 4550 models, as well as CD-RW and hard drive upgrades on all models.  These offers brought prices down, except for the 2350s which have a $30 price increase.

Gateway's prices have remained practically stagnant for the past few weeks.  Even though the prices aren't falling, we continue to be impressed with Gateway's pricing on flat screen monitors.
