This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deals Trends and Commentary
If you thought that Gateway's strategy of adding computer peripherals and electronics products to its PC and notebook line just applies to the 200+ Gateway stores, guess again.  The Gateway logo is showing up more and more at online storefronts as PC Deals searches for deals and best prices.  First, you'll find Gateway brand monitors for sale from several online stores we recognize.  This indicates Gateway is OEMing product into the distribution channel.  Perhaps more interesting from a competitive standpoint is seeing Gateway as the preferred supplier for, say, Samsung LCD monitors at Yahoo Shopping.  This move takes some pressure off of Gateway's core PC business and puts the company in a food-fight with online merchants such as cdw.com and PCconnection.  Gateway has a national brand, TV advertising, and a long list of small and medium business customers.  Thus, we think the analysts on Wall Street will be pleased when they catch on.  So far, Gateway is showing up on our radar screen for peripheral best prices more often than arch-rival Dell.  So far, all this effort has yet to show a profit.  The Wall Street Journal on January 6th reports the company will reorganize and restructure.

In the smoke of war, it's often difficult to see what's happening.  We could be wrong, but it looks to us like AMD's worst nightmare is occurring: the attack of the Intel Celerons.  Our lowest price category, at $600, has been a toehold for AMD recently in the low-end of the value segment.  Now it looks like it's back in the hands of Celerons.  And after a raft of post-Thanksgiving winning PC deals that covered the gamut of AMD's Athlon XP processors, the $900 price category is loaded with Pentium 4s.  We are not privy to inside PC microprocessor pricing information.  It just looks to us like Intel is winning some big orders that are now showing up on our price-value charts.  Our notebook research is also turning up more Celeron-based machines.

The monster that won't go away for AMD is gigahertz.  In this case, the new Celeron 2.0 and 2.2 GHz microprocessors are clocked to match the gigahertz equivalency of the AMD Athlon XP 2000+ and 2200+, respectively.  We'll say it again: computer performance is not based on gigahertz alone.  In particular, an Athlon 2000+ will run rings around a Celeron 2.0.  But this week, for instance, Celerons lead in the $600 class and decent Pentium 4's start at under $900.  This leaves AMD the winner in only the $750 class, which is under-populated with choices.

This Week's Buying Tips

The last week of December was an online week.  By that we mean that the best deals were available at online sites, such as shopping.hp.com and circuitcity.com.  On reason is the lack of ink that retailers gave to PCs in their end-of-year, post-Christmas advertising splurge.  Another may be the desire to clean out inventory in the warehouses.  In any event, PC buying in the Internet age should be approached just like automobile buying.  Arm yourself with the facts on the Net before making the trip to the store.

EMachines
EMachines unveiled two of its four new desktop models.  BestBuy already has advertised deals on the new T2042 and T1842.  On its website, eMachines promises the T2260 and T2460 are coming soon, and these models are already priced with the low costs we've come to expect from this company.  Caveat emptor - the T1842 is NOT a souped-up version of its predecessor, the T1840.  Now the low-end of the eMachine's line, the T1842 boasts a lower price than the T1840, but also lesser opticals - only a CD-ROM instead of a CD-RW and DVD-ROM.  No need to despair at this change because the T2042 with its slightly faster processor contains the CD-RW and DVD-ROM that the T1842 lacks.
Dell offers $50, $100, and $150 instant Online savings on the Dimension 2350, 4550, and 8250, respectively.  A free memory upgrade extends to all these models.  But these offers do not equal savings on any Dell PCs this week.

Dell
This is not the week to find the best deals on a 2350 from Dell.  For more than you would have paid last week you no longer get a DVD-ROM.  

Prices on the bread-and-butter, mid-line Dimension 4550 line rose $80 to $100 this week, bringing Dell's prices back to their Christmas time high levels.  When PC Deals configured a Dimension 4550 with 120 GB hard drive and the remaining specs matching the winning Dell in the $1,400 price category on Friday afternoon, we could have purchased the machine for $1,204 (another $50 less than last week's PC Deals price).  January 5th, this same PC configuration - even with a smaller 80 GB hard drive - costs $1,362, an alarming price hike in two day's time.  

The 8250 with a 2.8 GHz processor rose in price $100, and its 3.06 GHz counterpart rose over $160.
 
Gateway
Gateway has changed its offers this week.  The best deal in the 300 line is no longer the 300L Special, but the 300S Value.  The 300S Value has a slightly higher price tag, but includes a CD-RW.  The Gateway 500L Special no longer has a $100 mail-in rebate so that it now costs $80 more than last week.  All other models we cover remained the same.

Sony
As happens from time to time, the best deals on Sony PCs can be found at Sony's own site.  Sony Online offers free overnight shipping on all Viao desktops as well as $100 mail-in rebates.  Sony has severely cut back on its models compared with the RX model line in October, but the RX850 and RX860 are still available, although no national retailers sell the RX850 online.

On the whole, Sony's retail presence has dropped over the past six weeks.

HP and Compaq
HP Online has the best deals on many of PC Deals' HP and Compaq machines this week, partly because of the lack of advertised deals but also because HP's former mail-in rebate has expired so that Online retailers currently lack a discount on HP and Compaq PCs.  Well, HP will give you $50 off on all its PCs, but you'll still have to pay $30 shipping.  Not the greatest time to buy a HP-Compaq PC since there is little competition for the best deals.
