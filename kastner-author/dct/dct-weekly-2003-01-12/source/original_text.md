This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deals Trends and Commentary

This Week's Buying Tips

The weekly deals are back!  But don't expect to save on pricier systems; this week is all about low-to-mid-range PCs.  Witness the explosion of PC options in the $600 and $900 price categories.  EMachines and Compaq are the brands filling these ranges with excellent deals, but don't miss the $900 VPR Matrix 3110, which stands out as the overall winner of PC Deals this week.  AMD Athlon machines also make a strong reappearance this week.  As always, PC Deals suggests that consumers consider the great value of Athlon machines especially from (yep you guessed it) eMachines and Compaq.  

For those of you looking to spend a little more mullah, Dell leads the pack although PC Deals is disappointed in how much it still costs for a loaded P4 2.8 GHz machine from any dealer.  Costs are down this week so this is the time to buy.

BestBuy
BestBuy is your eMachines source, offering three of the four eMachine's PCs in PC Deals this week.  Note that BestBuy is not offering printers in any of their bundles this week, but that does mean another $25 off the price tag of many PCs.  BestBuy also sells our winning deal of the week: the VPR Matrix 3110.  With a 2.66 GHz P4 processor, 512 DDR, an 80 GB hard drive, and a monitor all for under $900, we are impressed.  Get it fast as this is only a weekly offer.
  
BestBuy.com still offers free shipping and guarantees that your big screen TV will arrive in time for the big game.

CompUSA
CompUSA had a lot of deals this week exclusively on HP and Compaq machines.  Especially consider Compaq's 6370US for $1,300.

EMachines
All four of eMachine's models are in stores or at eMachines.com now.  This brand now has four machines out for under $750.  We're especially impressed with the T2460 for around $660 with a monitor at BestBuy stores.

Dell
Dell bounced back from last week's price hikes.  Online deals include free shipping, CD-ROM upgrades, and hard drive upgrades on all Dimension models, as well as $100 rebate on the 8250.  Compared with last week, you'll save over $70 on the Dimension 2350 with a 1.8 GHz P4 processor and over $180 on the P4 2.0 GHz Dimension 4550.  

If you're interested in the Dimension 8250 with a processor over 2.4 GHz consider how much more you will be paying than the same configuration of the Dimension 4550.  There is a $60 difference between a 2.4 GHz, 60 GB, 512 MB system with the same opticals and monitor when customizing a 8250 v. a 4550.  The higher grade RDRAM memory in the 8250 accounts for its slightly higher price tag as supposed to the 4550's DDR grade memory.  But the same configuration with a 2.8 GHz processor reveals a $420 price discrepancy between the Dimension 8250 and 4550.  Why this huge cost difference?  We have no good explanation.    
 
Gateway
For a change, PC Deals configured all Gateway PCs under the "Home" section of gateway.com.  While the trend of Gateway lacking PCs in the $1,000-$1,300 price ranges continued in the "Home" section, the deals were better and you'll find some lower price tags or more features for your money.

Enjoy free shipping on all PCs - a huge deal for Gateway.

Sony
As happens from time to time, the best deals on Sony PCs can be found at Sony's own site.  Sony Online offers free overnight shipping on all Viao desktops as well as $100 mail-in rebates.  Sony has severely cut back on its models compared with the RX model line in October, but the RX850 and RX860 are still available, although no national retailers sell the RX850 online.

On the whole, Sony's retail presence has dropped over the past six weeks.

HP and Compaq
HP Online continues to be competitive with retail deals, but the resurgence of weekly offers means that you're best bundled deals are at stores like CompUSA.  HP Online does offer free shipping as well $50 off select models and $100 off customized PCs.
