This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deals Trends and Commentary
It's getting on to February.  Intel has not changed microprocessor prices since November.  As a result, we have not seen much of a downward trend in high-end configurations, such as the 2.8 GHz machines.  We expect Intel to follow its traditional pattern and reduce prices next month.  A faster chip with HyperThreading should take the crown from the reigning champ, the 3.06 GHz.  In this shuffle down the food chain, the venerable 2.0A GHz is likely to become obsolescent, with the 2.4 GHz taking up the bottom of the P4 line.

In October, HP got first crack at the Microsoft Media Center extensions to Windows XP.  They sold out.  Today, Gateway, ViewSonic and Alienware all offer these all-media-in-one-box PCs.  While HP uses the traditional tower form-factor, we'd like to mention the Alienware Navigator series.  Based on the compact Shuttle platform, the Navigator has a footprint of only 7.4"x7.88"x11.82".  It comes in stereo-component black, and should fit right in with a rack of media components.  Filled with top-end components, this mighty-mouse is no performance slouch.  Who says the PC can't fit in the living room?

This Week's Buying Tips

BestBuy
BestBuy is your eMachines source, offering three of the four eMachine's PCs in PC Deals this week.  With the reintroduction of printers into its bundled deals this week, BestBuy's prices went up around $25.  While BestBuy's VPR Matrix FT3110 is no longer a bundled deal and has moved into a higher price point, there's a new great VPR Matrix machine, the FT3110.  We're impressed by a PC with a 2.8 GHz Pentium 4 processor, 512 DDR, a 100 GB hard drive, and a 15" LCD flat panel monitor for under $1,400.  Judging by the brief one-week offer on last week's VPR Matrix machine, you should grab this deal now.
  
BestBuy.com still offers free shipping.

Circuit City
All Circuit City AMD Athlon bundled deals include $100 gift cards.

Circuit City no longer sells any Sony PCs on its website. 

CompUSA
CompUSA continues to have a plethora of deals on HP and Compaq machines.  Especially consider Compaq's 6430US and 6350US bundled offers both for under $1,050.  They also offer 18 moth no-interest financing.

CompUSA Online offers one cent shipping.

EMachines
All four of eMachine's models are in stores or at eMachines.com now.  This brand now has four machines out for under $900.  We're especially impressed with the T2460 for around $650 with a monitor and printer at BestBuy stores.

Dell
Dell's Online deals include free shipping, CD-ROM upgrades, and hard drive upgrades on all Dimension models, as well as $100 rebate.  Dimension 2350 prices went down dramatically, but there were a few minor price increases on some other Dimension models.  
 
Gateway
Gateway's free shipping offer is over, and consequently Gateway prices are back up.  While continuing to impress with its monitor prices, Gateway's offers on memory, processors, and hard drive upgrades often fall short of other competitors' pricing.

Gateway has returned the two-fer promo.  Buy a 700XL for $3499 and get a free 300S or $399 off a second machine.  A similar deal exists on the 600XL notebook.  This deal is getting national TV promotion.

Sony
Sony Online offers free overnight shipping on all Viao desktops as well as $100 mail-in rebates.  Sony has severely cut back on its models compared with the RX model line in October, but the RX850 and RX860 are still available, although no national retailers sell the RX850 online.

On the whole, Sony's retail presence has dropped over the past six weeks.

HP and Compaq
HP Online continues to be competitive with retail deals, but the resurgence of weekly offers means that you're best bundled deals are at stores like CompUSA.  HP Online does offer free shipping as well $50 off select models and $100 off customized PCs.

Walmart
Has a new HP Pavilion 553w-b PC with 15-inch flat-panel LCD Monitor for $998.  This is a good deal in the $1000 price category this week, but no better than Dell's Dimension 2350 deal last week.
