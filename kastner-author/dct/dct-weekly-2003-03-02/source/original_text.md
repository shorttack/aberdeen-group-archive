This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deals Trends and Commentary

Here Today, Gone Tomorrow
We do a lot of online shopping in order to bring our PC Deals to you.  Unfortunately, we can't be at the console 24x7, which is what really is necessary to keep tabs on this industry.  Think airline ticket prices.  A deal for a minute or an hour and then gone into the electronic ether of lost opportunities.  This commentary comes to mind after we configured a Dell 4550 with a 2.8 GHz Pentium 4, 256MB  fast DDR memory, 60GB hard drive, DVD and CD-RW, and a free 17" monitor.  All for $928 plus shipping.  As careful readers will agree, a Pentium 4 system with a 2.8 GHz processor for under a thousand dollars is hundreds under the market - a real PC deal.  But that one's long gone, as Dell has the fastest changing prices of the major online brands.

So add PC pricing as an idle time Internet activity in addition to eBay trolling, Wall Street watching, and real-time sports.  You just might find the deal of your dreams.  If you do, please tell us about it at pcdeals@aberdeen.com.

The World if Flat - Flat Panels, That Is
We've mentioned this recently, but we'll say it again because PC Deals is so impressed with the pricing of LCD flat-panel monitors.  National retailers and online manufacturers alike offer extraordinarily affordable flat panel monitor options.  We've noticed 15" LCD monitors for under $200 in BestBuy's flyers the past few week's.  And Dell or Gateway Online will hook you up with a new 15" flat panel monitor for just $50 more than a traditional 17" monitor.

As 15" monitor prices fall, 17" prices come down into the attainable range.  Pre-holidays 2002, a 17" monitor cost around $700, and some ran into the $1,000 price range with TV options.  Now you can buy a 17" flat panel for under $500, often $400, the former price tag of a 15" LCD monitor.  

19" and up remain luxury items in the flat panel world, but their prices are also decreasing.  And note that flat panel monitors are equivalent to a CRT monitor one inch larger: a 17" flat panel is equivalent to an 18" monitor.

Printers
We've also noted the large selection of high quality color printers that can be purchased for under $200.  This includes a couple all-in-one printers, as well.  HP and Epson lead the pack in these affordable high-resolution color printers, but Lexmark and Canon offer options as well.  If you compare the stats on a $200 printer with those of a typical $79-99 printer, you'll find that the extra $100 gets you a lot of bang for your buck, especially in the photo quality of the printer.  

Before you choose any printer, price the ink cartridges because you'll be buying them for years to come.  Certain brands charge a lot more for their ink than others.  Also, for better color resolution, look for a printer that has separate ink cartridges for each color.  Lastly, printers designed for small businesses tend to have larger - hence more economical - ink cartridges.

All-in-one printer market leader Hewlett-Packard rolls out a new line today.  The top-of-the-line 7140 costs $699 MSRP.  It features dual paper trays, duplex printing, an internal JetDirect network interface, and 802.11 WiFi support.  The 7130 at $499 and the 7110 at $399 have fewer features.  For homes and small office/home office, the new 1210 may fit the bill.  This $149 printer/copier/scanner offers excellent 4800 dpi printing and scanning quality in a very small package.  Check it out if you are desk real-estate constrained!
 
This Week's Buying Tips

BestBuy
Get $100 mail-in rebate for all computer packages (bundled deals) this week at BestBuy stores.  
  
BestBuy.com still offers free shipping and, now, no financing for 24 months.

Circuit City
This week, get $100-$200 off all Pentium 4 processor systems and no interest for 12 months at Circuit City.

Circuitcity.com has reintroduced free shipping for a limited time on all its online items.  

CompUSA
CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them, like the HP 763n in this week's PC Deals.  This means you can get great deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  But this can also mean that you might pay almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; so, it's important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal.  One cent shipping is back at CompUSA.com.  

EMachines
EMachines has new models out.  Check out BestBuy's bundled deals on these new machines.

Dell
Dell's online deals include $50 instant savings on the 4550 and $150 savings on the 8250 series, free memory upgrades, and, for the first time that we can remember, monitor upgrades.  These deals caused Dell's prices to go down.  

Gateway
Gateway offers free shipping again.  Gateway revamped its website and has severely cut back on PC models for the home.  The 300 S Value no longer exists; in fact, the 300 series now starts at $799, instead of last week's $399.  

Gateway.com continues its offer of free shipping.

Sony
Sony Online has introduced new models at competitive prices.  It appears that prices have risen slightly this week, but there are still great deals to be found.  Right now, get free shipping on all Viao desktops, including customized models.  

HP and Compaq
HP Online continues to be competitive with retail deals, but the resurgence of weekly offers means that your best bundled deals are at stores like CompUSA.  HP Online does offer free shipping as well $1 CD-RW and $99 17" monitors when purchasing a customized PC.
