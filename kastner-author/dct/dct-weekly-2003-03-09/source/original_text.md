This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deals Trends and Commentary

Looks like Compaq is putting out the best mid-range and high-end deals.  With winners in the $900, $1050, $1200, and $1400 categories this week - and a decent showing at $600 and $750 - it looks like HP's branding of Compaq Presario's for consumers as "best value" machines is paying off.

By far, the best deal of the week is the Compaq 6000T at BestBuy's configuration station.  For $1,369.99 (maybe plus shipping, which is negotiable), you'll get 512MB of DDR memory, an 80 GB hard drive, the usual DVD and CD-RW, a 17" monitor and the requisite HP printer.  Oh, and the processor is Intel's top-of-the-line 3.06 GHz Pentium 4 with hyper-threading.  This is the first time we've seen this microprocessor anywhere near the price - check out the HT section of the Deals listing to see the rest of the market.  If you lust for a fast machine that will still be useful 3-5 years from now, beef up the graphics card and bring your credit card to BestBuy this week.

With 3.06 GHz Pentium 4's at $1,400, the market is in a stage of Pentium price compression.  A Dell 2350 with a 2.0A GHz microprocessor goes for $809, and a 2.66 GHz Compaq 6470Nx leads the $1,050 price category.  That means a modest increase in price buys a whole lot more in components.  This situation also opens up some breathing space for AMD Athlon XP's, which have reappeared at faster clock rates in the past couple of months.

If you can wait, expect price drops over the next month at retail as Intel's price cuts work their way through the - now much shorter - supply chain.
 
This Week's Buying Tips

BestBuy
Get $100 mail-in rebate for all computer packages (bundled deals) this week at BestBuy stores.  
  
BestBuy.com still offers free shipping and, now, no financing for 24 months.

Circuit City
This week, get $100-$200 off all Pentium 4 processor systems and no interest for 12 months at Circuit City.

Circuitcity.com has reintroduced free shipping for a limited time on all its online items.  

CompUSA
CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them, like the HP 763n in this week's PC Deals.  This means you can get great deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  But this can also mean that you might pay almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; so, it's important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal.  

EMachines
EMachines has new models out.  Check out BestBuy's bundled deals on these new machines.

Dell
Dell's online deals include $1000 instant savings on the 8250 and $50 savings on the 4550 series, free CD-RW in second bay, and, for the first time that we can remember, speaker upgrades.  

It's no longer an option to put a 2.4 GHz P4 processor in an Dimension 4550, meaning that a P4 2.53 GHz processor is now the bottom of the line.

Gateway
Gateway offers free shipping again.  Gateway revamped its website and has severely cut back on PC models for the home.  The 300 S Value no longer exists; in fact, the 300 series now starts at $799, instead of previous weeks' $399.  

Gateway.com continues its offer of free shipping.

Sony
Sony Online has introduced new models at competitive prices.  It appears that prices have risen slightly this week, but there are still great deals to be found.  Right now, get free shipping on all Viao desktops, including customized models.  

HP and Compaq
HP Online continues to be competitive with retail deals, but the resurgence of weekly offers means that you're best bundled deals are at stores like CompUSA.  HP Online does offer free shipping as well $1 CD-RW and $99 17" monitors when purchasing a customized PC.
