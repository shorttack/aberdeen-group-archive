This Week's Analysis of PC Deals
PC Deals Trends and Commentary

In PC Deals, the $1200 and $1400 price categories, previously hot spots for deals, have diminished to sparse pickings.  Concurrently, a new group of systems has boomed in the over $1600 range.  What does this all mean?  Well, if $1200-$1400 was your allotted budget for a new PC you're probably going to want to consider cheaper and/or more expensive deals.  At $1,600 and up, machines are usually targeted at media-centric users and gamers.

Recently, they were some great deals at Dell's SMB (small business) site that undercut the deals of Dell's home PCs.  Lesson: check your configuration at both sites because sometimes SMB can save you some serious cash.  However, the great deals on the Dell 4550 at under $900 are gone.

New this week is HP's second generation media-oriented desktop, featuring Microsoft's media center edition of Windows XP.  Starting at $1,700, the Pavilion 864n comes with a 2.66 GHz Pentium 4, 512 MB DDR memory, a 160 GB hard drive, DVD+RW/CD-RW combo, Creative labs Audigy sound, personal video recorder software, and an nVidia GeForce 4 MX440 graphics card.  There's a deal on the 864n at BestBuy this week.

This Week's Buying Tips

BestBuy
Save $100 on all Celeron and Athlon packages, as well as no interest for 24 months when purchasing with your BestBuy credit card.  BestBuy has a bundled deal on the new HP Media Center 864n that includes a 15" LCD monitor and Klipsch speakers for $2149.97.
  
BestBuy.com still offers free shipping.

Circuit City
This week, save $100 to $300 on all PC packages and pay no interest for 12 months when buying with your Circuitcity charge card.
Circuitcity.com has reintroduced free shipping for a limited time on all its online items.
  
CompUSA
This week, CompUSA did not include a weekly flyer.  CompUSA.com has some excellent deals on HP and Compaq pre-configured machines this week.

CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them.  This means you can get great deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  But this can also trap you into paying almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; so, it's important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal.  

EMachines
Emachines.com carries four of its models: the T1842, T2042, T2260, and T2460.  But BestBuy and CircuitCity stores always carry at least some of these models in great bundled deals so that PC Deals usually quotes the retailers' prices.  Also note that between BestBuy.com and Circuitcity.com two more eMachines models are available.  BestBuy carries the T4510 and CircuitCity has the T4480, both for under $900 in this week's PC Deals.

Dell
Dell's online deals include $150 instant savings on 8250 series and $50 off the 4550 line, free shipping, and free memory upgrade to 256 MB.  The memory upgrade offer is a poor deal in the 4550 and especially the 8250 where consumers should be configuring mid-to-upper range models that contain specs and software that will require more than 256 MB of memory.

Dell.com also offers a free printer, scanner, or digital camera this week.  But accepting this offer adds shipping fees, so PC Deals did not factor any of these free peripherals into its configurations.

Gateway
Gateway.com has finally joined the free shipping bandwagon for good.  At least that's what we're inferring from its new "Shipping always included" label under the price of any model you configure.  While this makes our job easier is determining the prices for PC Deals, this also tells consumers exactly what they're paying without having to checkout the contents of their carts and add $100 shipping per PC with monitor.  Bravo Gateway!
Sony
Sony.com offers a plethora of models right now, although, like eMachines, the best deals usually come from bundled retail offers.  The PCV-RS100 has disappeared, but for less than $100 more, the new PCV-RS220 offers a much faster processor and DVD+RW/+R capabilities.

HP and Compaq
HP Online offers competitive prices on its retail models, although national retailers always have the better bundled deals.  PC Deals added several new HP Media Center models to its spreadsheet this week.
