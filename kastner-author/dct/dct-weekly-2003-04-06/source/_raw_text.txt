This Week's Analysis of PC Deals

PC Deals Trends and Commentary

Feeling like your wallet is lighter than it used to be?  Don't rule out being able to afford a new PC.  This week, PC Deals displays 20 desktops that are all under $900, and 9 of these come in at under $600.  

EMachines, HP, and Compaq have the $900 and lower price categories stocked - and not just with Intel Celeron machines, as would have been the case 6 months ago.  Intel Pentium 4 2.4GHz and even a 2.53 GHz machine from Compaq rule the $900 range, but AMD Athlon machines are the real deals in these lower price points.  BestBuy.com offers Compaq's 6430NX for $800.  This machine packs an Athlon 2600+ processor, 512 MB of DDR memory, 120 GB hard drive, and DVD-ROM/CD-RW drives.  Compare this with Compaq's 6450NX, also at Best Buy Online, which contains a P4 2.53 processor that is slightly less powerful than the Athlon 2600+, but this PC costs $100 more.  What are we driving at?  AMD Athlon processors are less expensive than comparable Intel P4 processors so that you, the consumer, pay less overall or get better specs at the same price.

This Week's Buying Tips 

Best Buy
BestBuy.com still offers free shipping.  This week solidified a pattern we've noticed that Best Buy almost consistently offers the lowest prices on Sony PCs.  This week, Best Buy leads the market on all Sony models, except that RS220 that is only available at Sony.com.  Keep in mind that many of the Best Buy prices on Sony models include mail-in rebates. 

Circuit City
This week, save $100-$210 on all desktops and laptops, and pay no interest for 12 months when buying with your Circuit City charge card.

Circuitcity.com has reintroduced free shipping for a limited time on all its online items.
  
CompUSA
CompUSA is featuring the new Compaq s3200nx and s3300nx machines in bundles at $750 and $950 respectively.  The s3200nx is also available this week without a monitor at Circuitcity.com and other online locations.  The HP media center 854n with a 15" LCD flat panel is $150 off at $1,700.  

CompUSA is offering twelve months interest free on purchases of $599 and up this week.

CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them.  This means you can get better deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  But this can also trap you into paying almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; so, it's important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal.  This week's closeout is the Compaq 6450NX with a 17" monitor at $1,000.

eMachines
EMachines has once again revamped its line of models.  If you were thinking about buying a T2460, T2042, or T1842, you're now out of luck because these models are gone from all the usual sources we check.  But never fear because true to precedent eMachine's newest models launched at the same price points as its former brethren. Circuit City and Best Buy sell a couple models not available at eMAchines.com that are equally worth considering.  Bundled deals from these national retailers continue to supply the best bang for your buck.  

Dell
Dell's online deals include $50 and $150 off the 4550 and 8250 series, respectively.  Free CD-RW upgrade in second bay has returned for all models, as well as free shipping.  Unfortunately, these offers did not help maintain last week's excellent pricing at Dell.com.  The 4550 and 8250 lines rose dramatically in price this week.  But we've consistently seen price fluctuations at Dell so wait until next week and chances there will be better deals.

Gateway
Gateway.com has finally joined the free shipping bandwagon for good.  At least that's what we're inferring from its new "Shipping always included" label under the price of any model you configure.  While this makes our job easier is determining the prices for PC Deals, this also tells consumers exactly what they're paying without having to checkout the contents of their carts and add $100 shipping per PC with monitor.  Bravo Gateway!
Sony
Sony.com offers a plethora of models right now, although, like eMachines, the best deals usually come from bundled retail offers.  The PCV-RS100 has disappeared, but for less than $100 more, the new PCV-RS220 offers a much faster processor and DVD+RW/+R capabilities.  BestBuy.com advertises "pre-ordering" for the Sony PCV-RS210 for $849.99, a model not available at Sony's website.  We've included this model in PC Deals this week.

HP and Compaq
HP Online offers competitive prices on its retail models, although national retailers always have the better bundled deals.  PC Deals customized a Compaq 8000 series PC at http://www.hpshopping.com/ with a 3.06GHz processor, expecting this desktop to be lower in price than its HP counterpart.  We were wrong.  The Compaq model with specs matching the 700 series HP 3.06 GHz P4 cost over $300 more than the HP.  This is why you still don't see a Compaq PC in the P4 HT technology price range.
