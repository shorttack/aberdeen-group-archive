This Week's Analysis of PC Deals

PC Deals Trends and Commentary

Spring Cleaning
It's spring.  Dell is cutting prices.  All is status quo?  That would be the wrong analysis, in our humble opinion.  

If you are thinking Dell, this would be the week to act on the current product lineup.  Dell's quarter ends April 30th, so now is the time for the company to turn chips into (your) cash.  Over the past year, our observations are that the deals actually worsen in the last couple of weeks of Dell's fiscal quarter.  Act now.

On the other hand, if you are not yet quite at the buying point with Dell or any other PC supplier, a time out for a week might reveal a whole new spring lineup of Intel microprocessors and chipsets - and wholesale price cuts on today's chips.  The new Intel line sports an 800 MHz bus, a faster data highway than the 533 MHz bus found in the 2.53, 2.8, and 3.06 GHz Intel Pentium 4s.  The chipset also supports dual data paths to DDR memory.  Thus, a new 3.0 GHz Pentium 4 with an 800 MHz bus will outperform the 3.06 GHz with a 533 MHz bus.  Upshot?  The heretofore king-of-the-Pentium-hill 3.06 GHz microprocessor will drop in price.  And Dell over the weekend has already started dropping prices for the microprocessors most likely to move down the food chain.  Dell is helping Intel clean out the winter merchandise, so to speak, in a spring sale.  And we all benefit.

Intel's spring rollout comes in two phases.  The first is the 875P chipset - formerly code-named Canterwood - arriving this week.  The 875P is the chip that does all the 800 MHz bus magic.  Along with the 875P comes the Pentium 4 at 3.0 GHz.  The rest of the lineup of new Pentium 4s, which also include an 800 MHz bus and Hyper Threading support, arrive with the Springdale chipset later this quarter.

Handicapping the Spring Intel Lineup
Believe me, we know you are confused, dear readers.  We need a cheat sheet too.  Performance-oriented buyers with $1,000 or more to spend should look for the following attributes in the new Intel line:
    • 800 MHz bus
    • Support for Hyper Threading
    • 875P Canterwood is value-oriented but high performing
    • 865 Springdale is aimed at business PC markets, high performance.

Pentium 4s with the above features are expected at speeds from 2.4 GHz on up, with the new king-of-the-hill 3.2 GHz microprocessor out by the fourth of July.

The new chipsets and 800 MHz processors will bring a period of stability to the PC market, with fewer new introductions to roil the market.  PC manufacturers can lower costs by spreading design over more units.  Consumers are likely to see more stable prices - and PC makers might see larger profit margins.  As a result of this stability, we at PC Deals are going to shift more of our attention to the business PC markets.  More on that later this month.

This Week's Buying Tips 

Best Buy
At Best Buy stores this week, save $150 by mail on all notebooks, enjoy no interest for 12 months on PC bundles, and take advantage of free shipping on creation station PCs.  

BestBuy.com still offers free shipping.
  
Circuit City
Receive a $100 gift card with any PC purchase at Circuit City stores.

Circuitcity.com has reintroduced free shipping on all its online items.
  
CompUSA
Get a free HP 3425 printer after rebates with any computer & monitor bundle.  PC Deals included this free printer offer in the "Printer" column for all CompUSA bundled deals.  Check out the specs on the Sony PCV-RS220.  A very good deal.

CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them.  This means you can get better deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  But this can also trap you into paying almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; so, it's important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal.  

Dell
Wow!  This is the week to purchase a PC from Dell.com.  Every PC we configure at Dell noticeably dropped in price.  We're talking a price decrease of $200 for the 4550 series, and almost $300 for the 8250 line.  This is all due to Dell's current deals that include free CD-RW in second bay, free hard drive upgrade, and free shipping on the 4550 and 8250 series.  On top of those offers, Dell.com advertises $50, $200, and $350 mail-in rebates for online purchases of the 2350, 4550, and 8250, respectively.  

PC Deals has published for almost a year and we've never seen Dell discount its prices this drastically.  As Dell's prices have fluctuated upward since the winter holiday season, we suggest snatching these prices up now because chances are Dell will have changed its prices by next Sunday.

Dell has launched new 15" and 17" LCD flat panel monitors.  When customizing a 4550 PC, you can choose between the older and newer models of these monitors; but, when customizing an 8250 PC, your only option is the buy the newer, and more expensive, flat panel monitor. 

eMachines
EMachines has once again revamped its line of models.  If you were thinking about buying a T2460, T2042, or T1842, you're now out of luck because these models are gone from all the usual sources we check.  But never fear because true to precedent eMachine's newest models launched at the same price points as its former brethren. Circuit City and Best Buy sell a couple of models not available at eMachines.com that are equally worth considering.  Bundled deals from these national retailers continue to supply the best bang for your buck.  

Gateway
Gateway.com has finally joined the free shipping bandwagon for good.  At least that's what we're inferring from its new "Shipping always included" label under the price of any model you configure.  While this makes our job easier is determining the prices for PC Deals, this also tells consumers exactly what they're paying without having to checkout the contents of their carts and add $100 shipping per PC with monitor.  Bravo Gateway!
Sony
Sony.com offers a plethora of models right now, although, like eMachines, the best deals usually come from bundled retail offers.  

That being said, if you're interested in a PCV-RZ series Sony PC, then you'll want to customize one at Sony.com.  The savings speak for themselves.  In the P4 HT category of PC Deals, compare the PCV-RZ22G with the PCV-RZ series PC we customized at Sony's site and you'll notice that our customized PC costs $330 less and comes with a larger 200 GB hard drive.  Customizing a PC at Sony Online allows you to eliminate options, such as the "Giga Pocket" Personal Video Recorder, that come standard with retail models.

HP and Compaq
HP Online offers competitive prices on its retail models, although national retailers always have the better bundled deals.  PC Deals customized a Compaq 8000 series PC at hp.com with a 3.06GHz processor, expecting this desktop to be lower in price than its HP counterpart.  We were wrong.  The Compaq model with specs matching the 700 series HP 3.06 GHz P4 cost over $300 more than the HP.  This is why you still don't see a Compaq PC in the P4 HT technology price range.

But, investigations into how well hp.com's prices on unbundled Compaq PCs compare with national retailers reveals that HP Online (at least for now) undercut Best Buy and Circuit City by over $30 on some models.  This is due to $50 mail-in rebates on all Compaq PCs and free shipping on all orders over $250 at hp.com.
