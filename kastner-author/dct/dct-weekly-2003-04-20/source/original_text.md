This Week's Analysis of PC Deals

PC Deals Trends and Commentary

Last week, while gathering data for PC Deals, a window popped open at Dell's site inviting us to join "Dell Direct Deals."  We begrudgingly entered our email, expecting for Dell to hound us with advertisements.  But we were pleasantly wrong on that account.  This past Thursday Dell emailed us an virtual coupon for $50 off any Dell Home System over $999, redeemable by typing the appropriate code in at checkout.  And don't worry, Dell promises that you can combine this coupon with other current offers.  The only catch is that these coupons expire rather quickly - ours expires Tuesday April 22 at 11pm, but we'll be expecting another great offer soon.

PC Deals won't include Dell Direct Deals in its weekly pricing of Dell PCs, but we will inform everyone of what our current coupon offer is.  To subscribe to receive Dell Direct Deals, click here.

And surprise, surprise.  While looking over Dell's offers, we stumbled on the deal of the week.  Hurry because the free shipping ends soon, and we'd be surprised to see Dell keep this deal going for more than a few days.  For the first time we can remember - and that goes back a year of PC Deals research - Dell wins the PC Deals "Value $600" price category with a $559 Pentium 4 beauty.  We seldom see a Pentium 4 at under $600, and most machines lack a monitor.  Dell is blowing away the competition this week with a 2.4 GHz Pentium 4, 256 MB DDR memory, 30 GB value hard drive, a DVD-ROM, and a 17" monitor.  Check out the specs in the PC Deals spreadsheet.  We recommend you splurge and upgrade to a faster hard drive and throw in a CD-RW.  You won't be able to build this yourself for anywhere near Dell's price, which reflects a $150 mail-in rebate coupon.

This Week's Buying Tips 

BestBuy
At Best Buy stores this week, save $100 with a rebate on all Intel packages.  No interest for 12 months continues.

BestBuy.com still offers free shipping.
  
Circuit City
Receive a $100 gift card with any desktop or notebook purchase at Circuit City stores.  Circuit City also offers no interest for 12 months.

Circuitcity.com has reintroduced free shipping on all its online items.
  
CompUSA
No weekly advertisements appeared in this week's Boston Globe or Atlanta Journal-Constitution.

CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them.  This means you can get better deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  But this can also trap you into paying almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; so, it's important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal.  

Dell
While last week Dell offered great deals on all its models and price points, this week Dell is pushing Pentium 4 3.06 Hyper-Threading processors.  If you customize a 4550, 8250, or the new 8300 Dimension lines you'll get an additional $200 off with a mail-in rebate.  Otherwise, enjoy free shipping, memory upgrades, and new free upgrades in all but the 2350 of a DVD+RW/+R in the second CD bay.  Take advantage of $150 instant savings Online on the 8250 or $50 instant savings on the 4550. 

EMachines
EMachines has once again revamped its line of models.  If you were thinking about buying a T2460, T2042, or T1842, you're now out of luck because these models are gone from all the usual sources we check.  But never fear because true to precedent eMachine's newest models launched at the same price points as its former brethren. Circuit City and Best Buy sell a couple models not available at eMAchines.com that are equally worth considering.  Bundled deals from these national retailers continue to supply the best bang for your buck.  

Gateway
Gateway.com has finally joined the free shipping bandwagon for good.  At least that's what we're inferring from its new "Shipping always included" label under the price of any model you configure.  While this makes our job easier is determining the prices for PC Deals, this also tells consumers exactly what they're paying without having to checkout the contents of their carts and add $100 shipping per PC with monitor.  Bravo Gateway!
Sony
Sony.com offers a plethora of models right now, although, like eMachines, the best deals usually come from bundled retail offers.  

That being said, if you're interested in a PCV-RZ series Sony PC, then you'll want to customize one at Sony.com.  The savings speak for themselves.  In the P4 HT category of PC Deals, compare the PCV-RZ22G with the PCV-RZ series PC we customized at Sony's site and you'll notice that our customized PC costs $330 less and comes with a larger 200 GB hard drive.  Customizing a PC at Sony Online allows you to eliminate options, such as the "Giga Pocket" Personal Video Recorder, that come standard with retail models.

HP and Compaq
HP Online offers competitive prices on its retail models, although national retailers always have the better bundled deals.  PC Deals customized a Compaq 8000 series PC at http://www.hpshopping.com/ with a 3.06GHz processor, expecting this desktop to be lower in price than its HP counterpart.  We were wrong.  The Compaq model with specs matching the 700 series HP 3.06 GHz P4 cost over $300 more than the HP.  This is why you still don't see a Compaq PC in the P4 HT technology price range.

But, investigations into how well http://www.hpshopping.com's prices on unbundled Compaq PCs compare with national retailers reveals that HP Online (at least for now) undercut Best Buy and Circuit City by over $30 on some models.  This is due to $50 mail-in rebates on all Compaq PCs and free shipping on all orders over $250 at hpshopping.com.  Best Buy matched some of Compaq's prices this week, but there are still a few models that are hands down a better deal at www.hpshopping.com.
