This Week's Analysis of PC Deals

PC Deals Trends and Commentary

As we stated last week in this column, prices on PCs with Intel Pentium 4 Hyper-Threading technology have been falling into quite affordable price points.  In response to these price decreases PC Deals has decided to eliminate the separate Hyper-Threading (HT) category at the bottom of our weekly spreadsheet.  From now on we will place HT machines along side "regular" P4 machines and compare all PCs side-by-side by price.  We will continue to differentiate between HT Intel processors and standard P4 machines by adding an "HT" under the "processor" heading of PC Deals' spreadsheets.

As tempting as a fully loaded top-of-the-line P4 HT computer with a flat panel monitor is (and these machines are very tempting), you, as the consumer, need to ask yourself what you will be using your new PC for.  The majority of households in the United States currently own a PC or laptop.  This means most PC customers are buying at least their second PC as a replacement for or supplement to their current computer system.  And most of these buyers are not using their PCs for serious gaming or movie making.

To put things into perspective, PC Deals is currently being written on a two year old Dell Latitude LS laptop that has a Pentium 3 mobile processor, just over a 9 GB hard drive, and way under 128 MB of SDRAM memory.  And with such small specs compared with a desktop, I am able to store over 1,200 MP3s, all PC Deals written so far along with all other word documents, several downloaded games, and at least 50 digital photos in jpg format.  Oh, and I still have 2 GB free on my hard drive.  All this information isn't supposed to encourage you to buy a lightweight laptop, although we love laptops too; it's meant to show how little space and memory is actually necessary for the average, or even above average, computer user.

Now, if you take a look at any of the PCs in the under $600 category, you'll notice they all come with specs well above those of my laptop - not surprising as PCs are much larger than laptops - and all these machines cost less than most 17-19" flat panel monitors.  So if you're looking for a PC that will allow you to surf the web, download music, movies, and games, store digital photos, and use word processing or any other Microsoft applications, you really don't need to consider machines with high price tags.  You can always add a DVD burner or flat panel monitor later, and probably at a better price for you.

As we're discussing the merits of the lower-end PCs in PC Deals, we shouldn't neglect to mention that eMachines consistently impresses us with the amount of machine for what you pay.  But caveat emptor; in the past month, we've noted that bundled eMachines deals at Best Buy and Circuit City stores often have price tags below the retail price for the CPU alone.  This mean we encourage consumers to wait for bundled deals, although this may mean dealing with mail-in rebates. 

This Week's Buying Tips 

BestBuy
At Best Buy stores this week, save $100 to $150 with Best Buy's mail-in rebate on all computer packages.  Also receive no interest financing for 18 months on all these CPU bundles.

BestBuy.com still offers free shipping and no interest until January 2004.
  
Circuit City
Save up to $350 on all Desktop Computer Packages and enjoy no interest for 12 months.

Circuitcity.com has free shipping on all its online items.
  
CompUSA
No weekly advertisements appeared in this week's Boston Globe or Atlanta Journal-Constitution.

CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them.  This means you can get better deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  But this can also trap you into paying almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; so, it's important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal. 

CompUSA.com offers one cent shipping for a limited time.  This week Compusa.com introduced "In-store pickup" for items purchased online.  Circuitcity.com and BestBuy.com have offered this feature for some time. 

Dell
This week's deals at Dell for the 2350 include a free CD burner in the second bay, a free upgrade to a 60 GB hard drive, and a free upgrade to 256 MB of DDR memory.  Dell offers free shipping, hard drive upgrades, and CD burners in the second bay on all its other models. 

Our most recent Dell Direct Deals coupon gives us free shipping, but as that is currently one of the deals at Dell.com this is not a very helpful coupon.

EMachines
EMachines has once again revamped its line of models.  If you were thinking about buying a T2460, T2042, or T1842, you're now out of luck because these models are gone from all the usual sources we check.  But never fear because true to precedent eMachine's newest models launched at the same price points as its former brethren. Circuit City and Best Buy sell a couple models not available at eMachines.com that are equally worth considering.  Bundled deals from these national retailers continue to supply the best bang for your buck.  

Gateway
Receive the Sims basic software package for free this week at Gateway Online.
Gateway.com has finally joined the free shipping bandwagon for good.  At least that's what we're inferring from its new "Shipping always included" label under the price of any model you configure.  While this makes our job easier is determining the prices for PC Deals, this also tells consumers exactly what they're paying without having to checkout the contents of their carts and add $100 shipping per PC with monitor.  Bravo Gateway!
Sony
Sony.com offers a plethora of models right now, although, like eMachines, the best deals usually come from bundled retail offers.  Receive free shipping, and a free Epson C42UX printer after mail-in rebate at Sony Online this week. 

Customizing a PC at Sony Online allows you to eliminate options, such as the "Giga Pocket" Personal Video Recorder, that come standard with retail models.

HP and Compaq
HP Online offers competitive prices on its retail models, although national retailers always have the better bundled deals.  PC Deals customized a Compaq 8000 series PC at http://www.hpshopping.com/ with a 3.06GHz processor, expecting this desktop to be lower in price than its HP counterpart.  We were wrong.  The Compaq model with specs matching the 700 series HP 3.06 GHz P4 cost over $300 more than the HP.  This is why you still don't see a Compaq PC in the P4 HT technology price range.

But, investigations into how well http://www.hpshopping.com's prices on unbundled Compaq PCs compare with national retailers reveals that HP Online (at least for now) undercut Best Buy and Circuit City by over $30 on some models.  This is due to $50 mail-in rebates on all Compaq PCs and free shipping on all orders over $250 at hpshopping.com.  Best Buy matched some of Compaq's prices this week, but there are still a few models that are hands down a better deal at www.hpshopping.com.
