This Week's Analysis of PC Deals

PC Deals Trends and Commentary

The retail world calls this time of year the "Dads and Grads" season.  It's the last holiday on which retailers count to get them through the slow summer months until "Back to School" shopping frenzy beings in August.  

Surprisingly (to us, anyways), retailers do not seem to be giving desktops much attention.  Laptops are the favored gift for father's day and recent high school graduates.  Although desktops are our game, we can't deny the appeal of the newest laptops.  We especially like the Pentium M processor included in Centrino notebooks.

But don't be swayed into thinking that excellently priced desktops do not exist this week.  If you have the space and don't need a portable computer, desktops will always give you more bang for your buck.  We continue to be impressed with the prices on Compaq's S4000 series of PCs.  Take a look at the S4000Z with an Athlon 2800+ processor that wins this week's $900 price range, or, in the $1050 price category, the S4000T that wins because of its large hard drive and monitor for an startlingly low price. 

Compaq Floods the Low End
Compaq's newest S4000 series now includes four new lines beyond the launch of the S4000T that we discussed last week, and all can be found at the Compaq section of www.hpshopping.com.  As usual, the S4000NX is the retail version - NX always denotes retail machine when dealing with Compaqs; while only one model exists so far, the S4300NX available at Circuit City, we expect many more shortly.  The S4000Z offers customized high-end AMD Athlon processors.  

Generally, Compaq only offers up to the three variations on a make that we've already discussed: the retail version ending in NX (S4000NX), the customized Intel Pentium 4 model (now with HT) that ends with T (S4000T), and finally the customized AMD Athlon line that ends with a Z (S4000Z).  Unique to this new S4000 series are the S4000V and S4000J.  While this seems like an awful lot of lines all varying by a letter or so, we see what Compaq is trying to achieve.  The S4000V and S4000J are customized lower-end versions of the S4000T and S4000Z.  Now that Intel has both HT and non-HT processors on the market, and AMD makes at least 6 commonly used Athlon XP processor, it makes sense to separate all these processors into two categories: old vs. new or lower-end vs. higher-end.

HP also launched a new desktop model, replacing its Pavilion 800 line.  The A250 series finally allows consumers the ability to customize an Intel P4 HT machine; this clearly makes the A250 the high-end of HP's desktop line-up.

In summary, the HP and Compaq custom-configured systems we built at hpshopping.com are many of the best deals this week.  A year ago, HP was very sensitive to protecting its retailers.  Today, price-conscious buyers can get the configuration they want online.

This Week's Buying Tips 

BestBuy
At Best Buy stores this week, save $150 with Best Buy's mail-in rebate on all computer packages.  Also receive no interest financing for 18 months on all these CPU bundles.

BestBuy.com still offers free shipping and no interest until January 2004.
  
Circuit City
Save up to $380 on select Desktop Computer Packages and enjoy no interest for 12 months.

Circuitcity.com has free shipping on all its online items.
  
CompUSA
CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them.  This means you can get better deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  But this can also trap you into paying almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; so, it's important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal. 

Dell
The Dimension 4500 line has disappeared as expected with the 4600 and 4600C models taking its place.

Dell's deals include free memory and second optical drive upgrades.  This means that in most models you can get a free DVD+RW/+R drive in the second bay.  Unfortunately, free shipping is not one of this week's offers. 

eMachines
EMachines has once again revamped its line of models.  EMachine's newest models launched at the same price points as its former brethren. Circuit City and Best Buy sell a couple models not available at eMachines.com that are equally worth considering.  Bundled deals from these national retailers continue to supply the best bang for your buck.  

Gateway
Gateway.com seems to change its online graphics and layout almost weekly.  While these frequent changes are initially confusing, Gateway does endeavor to make its website as user-friendly as possible.  One hint for potential customers is to always customize Gateway's PCs, especially when considering a high-end (700 series) model.  We find that Gateway's ready-to-buy configurations often throw in extras that jack the prices up.
Gateway.com has finally joined the free shipping bandwagon for good.  At least that's what we're inferring from its new "Shipping always included" label under the price of any model you configure.  While this makes our job easier is determining the prices for PC Deals, this also tells consumers exactly what they're paying without having to checkout the contents of their carts and add $100 shipping per PC with monitor.  Bravo Gateway!
Sony
Sony.com offers a plethora of models right now, although, like eMachines, the best deals usually come from bundled retail offers.  Receive free shipping, and a free Epson C42UX printer after mail-in rebate at Sony Online this week. 

Customizing a PC at Sony Online allows you to eliminate options, such as the "Giga Pocket" Personal Video Recorder, that come standard with retail models.

HP and Compaq
HP Online offers competitive prices on its retail models, although national retailers usually carry well-priced bundled deals.  For a few weeks www.hpshopping.com had the best prices on both HP and Compaq PCs.  But now that HP is phasing out models like Compaq's 3000 series, it's anyone's guess as to who will have the lowest price each week.  Expect to see many of HP-Compaq's current models disappear from online sites except for vendors like http://www.compusa.com/ or http://www.pcconnection.com/ that carry overstock of outdated desktop models.

Get free shipping on all HP and Compaq PCs.  Also, the Compaq portion of hpshopping.com offers a $50 mail-in rebate on all Compaq Presario desktops and $50 mail-in rebate on all monitors purchased with desktops.
