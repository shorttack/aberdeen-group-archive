This Week's Analysis of PC Deals

PC Deals Trends and Commentary

Not all that much to comment on this week.  With Father's Day past, the best deals are dwindling in number.  We're not sure whether the normal lull in sales until back-to-school season starts in a month will mean retailers will lower prices or whether there will be fewer deals.  We're betting on fewer outstanding bargains.

That having been said, many of the brands we follow have launched new models or lines which means that this week and next will be prime time to get rock-bottom prices on the older PC models.  Circuitcity.com has already begun this process.  They have the lowest prices on some HP, Sony, and eMachines models.  We also recommend going directly to national retailers because they might have in-store specials not advertised online. 

Not sure if you need a new PC?  Think about upgrading your PC peripherals by adding a flat panel monitor, a color printer, or digital camera.  Manufacturers have all jumped on the trend that your space is at a premium so if you haven't looked at printers, cameras, and monitors in awhile you're in for quite a surprise.  Canon and Nikon make very compact digital cameras.  HP, in particular, has launched numerous printers that make its models from three years ago seem gigantic: Think photo printers that are smaller than a standard sheet of paper or all-in-one printer, scanner, copiers that compete in size with normal flat-bed scanners.  How about a monitor that's only 1.5" think?  All these items can be found at the very affordable price of under $300. 

This Week's Buying Tips 

BestBuy
At Best Buy stores this week, save $100 with Best Buy's mail-in rebate on all computer packages.  Also receive no interest financing for 18 months on all these CPU bundles.

BestBuy.com still offers free shipping and no interest until January 2004.
  
Circuit City
Save up to $350 on select clearance Desktop Computer Packages and enjoy no interest for 12 months.

Circuitcity.com has free shipping on all its online items.  Circuit City's online site is this week's place to go for the best deals on older models from eMachines, HP, and Sony.  Many of these models are clearance items, but with prices like $369.99 for HP's 505n you should give these PCs a look.
  
CompUSA
CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them.  This means you can get better deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  But this can also trap you into paying almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; so, it's important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal. 

Dell
Dell's deals include free shipping on all models and free hard drive upgrades.  Also look for $50 off instantly on the 4600 line and $100 the 8300 and XPS models.  Dell has new 15" and 17" flat panel monitors.  This means you can buy the older E151FPb 15" LCD monitor at a bargain price.

eMachines
EMachines has once again revamped its line of models.  EMachine's newest models launched at the same price points as its former brethren. Circuit City and Best Buy sell a couple models not available at eMachines.com that are equally worth considering.  Bundled deals from these national retailers continue to supply the best bang for your buck.  

Gateway
Gateway.com seems to change its online graphics and layout almost weekly.  While these frequent changes are initially confusing, Gateway does endeavor to make its website as user-friendly as possible.  One hint for potential customers is to always customize Gateway's PCs, especially when considering a high-end (700 series) model.  We find that Gateway's ready-to-buy configurations often throw in extras that jack up its prices.
Sony
Sony.com offers a plethora of models right now, although, like eMachines, the best deals usually come from bundled retail offers.  Receive free shipping, and a free Epson C42UX printer after mail-in rebate at Sony Online this week.  Sony's newest addition to the RZ line is the PCV-RZ32G for $1299.99 and can only be found at Sony's online site. 

Customizing a PC at Sony Online allows you to eliminate options, such as the "Giga Pocket" Personal Video Recorder, that come standard with retail models.

HP and Compaq
HP Online offers competitive prices on its retail models, although national retailers usually carry well-priced bundled deals.  For a few weeks www.hpshopping.com had the best prices on both HP and Compaq PCs.  But now that HP is phasing out models like Compaq's 3000 series, it's anyone's guess as to who will have the lowest price each week.  Expect to see many of HP-Compaq's current models disappear from online sites except for vendors like http://www.compusa.com/ or http://www.pcconnection.com/ that carry overstock of outdated desktop models.

Get free shipping on all HP and Compaq PCs.  Also, the Compaq portion of hpshopping.com offers a $50 mail-in rebate on all Compaq Presario desktops and $50 mail-in rebate on all monitors purchased with desktops.
