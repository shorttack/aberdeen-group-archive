This Week's Analysis of PC Deals

PC Deals Trends and Commentary

June 18th, HP announced it's newest addition to its desktop line-up, the m200 series.  This Media Center PC series has both retail and online customized versions, starting at under $999.  Customizing a m200 series desktop will allow consumers to choose from the full range of Intel's Hyper-Threading (HT) processors, including the brand new 3.2 GHz processor.

As the name suggests, HP's Media Center PCs are geared towards consumers who want a range of digital entertainment options and who may already have invested in peripherals like a DVD writer.  Updating its Media Center line is a smart move by HP, as the current 800n series of Media Center PCs does not offer consumers the latest technology. 

Retail versions, priced from $1,200, will be available in mid-July and online, customized desktops will be available as soon as July 9th.  Until that time, we recommend customizing a PC from the new A250n series if you want an HP PC with Intel's latest HT technology.

We suggested last week that the coming month would be a desktop deals drought.  Judging by this week that may not be the case.  Dell's prices, especially on its Dimension 4600 line decreased by over $100 this week and we have yet to see a rise in customized PC prices from Gateway, HP, or Compaq.

Even retail deals were not as sparse as we predicted, although many of those deals focused on Sony desktops.  As manufacturers and retailers alike struggle for sales, this might be the summer to pick up some great deals.

This Week's Buying Tips 

BestBuy
At Best Buy stores this week, save $100 with Best Buy's mail-in rebate on all computer packages.  Also receive no interest financing for 18 months on all these CPU bundles.

BestBuy.com still offers free shipping and no interest until January 2004.
  
Circuit City
Save up to $450 on select Desktop and Laptop Computer Packages and enjoy no interest for 12 months.

Circuitcity.com has free shipping on all its online items.  Circuit City's online site was the place to go for the lowest prices on clearance desktops last week.  Those deals have vanished and Circuit City's online site had very few of the best deals on any PCs this week.
   
CompUSA
CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them.  This means you can get better deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  But this can also trap you into paying almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; so, it's important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal. 

Dell
Dell's deals include a free memory upgrade on all models and free CD-RW in the second bay.  Also look for $50 mail-in rebates on the Dimension 2350 and $100 mail-in rebates on all other models, but shipping is not free this week.  Dell's current deals caused prices on the customized 4600 models we show to drop noticeable and we recommend buying this week.  You can also now customize a high-end PC with the latest Hyper-Threading Intel processor with 3.2 GHz, but selecting this option will cost you $290 more than the 3.0 GHz processor.  For that reason, we have not added any 3.2 GHz machines to PC Deals yet.

eMachines
Circuit City and Best Buy sell a couple models not available at eMachines.com that PC Deals covers.  Bundled deals from these national retailers continue to supply the best bang for your buck on eMachines desktops.  

Gateway
Gateway's prices on the PCs we customize at www.gateway.com have not changed over the past few weeks.  While this makes it easy to predict the price of a Gateway PC, it also means that Gateway is not lowering its prices as newer technology emerges.  Shipping is always included in the prices shown at Gateway's site.
Sony
Sony.com offers a plethora of models right now, although, like eMachines, the best deals usually come from bundled retail offers.  Receive free shipping, and a free Epson C42UX printer after mail-in rebate at Sony Online this week.  Sony's newest addition to the RZ line is the PCV-RZ34G for $1599.99 and can only be found at Sony's online site. 

Customizing a PC at Sony Online allows you to eliminate options, such as the "Giga Pocket" Personal Video Recorder, that come standard with retail models.

HP and Compaq
HP Online offers competitive prices on its retail models, although national retailers usually carry well-priced bundled deals.  For a few weeks www.hpshopping.com had the best prices on both HP and Compaq PCs.  But now that HP is phasing out models like Compaq's 3000 series, it's anyone's guess as to who will have the lowest price each week.  Expect to see many of HP-Compaq's current models disappear from online sites except for vendors like http://www.compusa.com/ or http://www.pcconnection.com/ that carry overstock of outdated desktop models.

Get free shipping on all HP and Compaq PCs priced over $250.  Also, the Compaq portion of hpshopping.com offers a $50 mail-in rebate on all Compaq Presario desktops and $50 mail-in rebate on all monitors purchased with desktops.
