This Week's Analysis of PC Deals

PC Deals Trends and Commentary

Nothing very exciting happening in desktop deals this week.  Dell has replaced its Dimension 2350 with the newer 2400, but we have yet to notice any difference in these models other than a price drop.

EMachines' and HP's newest desktops dominate the weekly deals flyers from Best Buy and Circuit City this week.  And we're expecting more HP Media Center PCs to appear in these flyers in the coming weeks.

Excellent prices can be found on clearance HP-Compaq desktops, but do your research before buying one of these PCs.  We've seen out-dated models from several manufacturers at Best Buy stores, etc. selling for close to their original market price.  We're certainly not saying to stay away from purchasing an older PC model, but you don't want to pay almost as much, if not more, than you would for the new model.

This Week's Buying Tips 

BestBuy
Best Buy has revamped its website completely.  You'll find the link to its "weekly ad" at the bottom, center of the home page along with the "store locator" link.  When viewing desktop computers, the site works pretty much the same as previously, although Best Buy still has to work out some kinks, like having the machines appear in alphabetical order when you browse them by brand name.  We also noted that BestBuy.com is carrying many low-end Compaq and HP machines with skus that we can't find anywhere else.  This makes us wary of purchasing anything from BestBuy.com until they work out all the details of redoing their site.

Best Buy's weekly ad shows bundled deals on two of eMachines' and HP's new PC models.  Beware of eMachines' T2260 at Bestbuy.com.  This is an out-of-date PC that costs $100 more without a monitor and printer than the new bundled W2260 available at Best Buy stores this week for $449.97.  

At Best Buy stores this week, save $100-$150 with Best Buy's mail-in rebate on select computer packages.  Also receive no interest financing for 12 months on all these CPU bundles.

BestBuy.com still offers free shipping and no interest until January 2004.
  
Circuit City
Save up to $200 on select Desktop and Laptop Computer Packages and enjoy no interest for 12 months.  

Circuitcity.com continues its free shipping offer on all its online items.  Circuitcity.com is the place to go for the newest retail models in HP's A200 and A250 lines.  The A220n and A230n contain AMD Athlon processors at very attractive prices.  
   
CompUSA
CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them.  This means you can get better deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  But this can also trap you into paying almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; so, it's important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal. 

Dell
Dell currently offers free shipping and CD-RW upgrades in the second optical bay on all desktop models.  With the exception of the 2400, Dell offers mail-in rebates this week: $50 off the 4600 and 4600C and $150 off the 8300 and XPS models.  

Last week, the 2350 with a non-HT 2.4 GHz processor jumped in price to almost $1000.  As a result, we were going to stop customizing this model.  But, as Dell has replaced the 2350 with the new 2400, we thought we'd give Dell's low-end PC line one more chance.  This week, the Dimension 2400 with the same specs as last week's 2350 came to $834, which is a much better price, but still doesn't compete well with other computers in that price point.  

Customize a high-end PC with the latest Hyper-Threading Intel processor with 3.2 GHz continues to yield high prices, especially in contrast with HP's winning machine in our $1600+ category so we have not included any Dell PCs with this technology yet.

eMachines
Circuit City and Best Buy sell all four models in eMachines' new line-up and they continue to sell a few older models.  Bundled deals from these national retailers supply the best bang for your buck on eMachines desktops.  www.emachines.com is the best source for researching these PCs.

Gateway
Gateway's prices on the PCs we customize at www.gateway.com have not changed, with the exception of a slight price drop on the 500X model last week.  But we were happy to see that the 700XL, while maintaining the same price, now comes standard with the new Pentium 4 HT 3.2 GHz processor.  Shipping is always included in the prices shown at Gateway's site.
Sony
Sony.com offers a plethora of models right now, although, like eMachines, the best deals usually come from bundled retail offers.  Receive free shipping at Sony Online this week.  

Customizing a PC at Sony Online allows you to eliminate options, such as the "Giga Pocket" Personal Video Recorder, that come standard with retail models.

HP and Compaq
HP Online offers competitive prices on its retail models, although national retailers usually carry well-priced bundled deals.  HP has expanded its A250 line to resemble the S4000 series of desktops by Compaq so that choosing the grade of processor determines the model you will customize.  Along with the full release of the new line comes the disappearance of the option to customize 500, 700, or media pcs.  

We are pleasantly surprised to see that these new PCs compete directly with Compaq, unlike before when Compaq often undercut HP's prices.  Our top picks are the A250e series machine that wins in the $1200 category this week and the roughly $1700 A250y series PC that wins our most expensive category by offering the fastest Intel processor, 3.2 GHz, at the price most vendors are selling the 3.0 GHz machines.

www.hpshopping.com has some bargain prices on clearance Compaq PCs like $759.99 for the 6470NX or $507.99 for the S3200NX.  Snatch these deals up quickly because they are likely to disappear in the next week or so.

Get free shipping on all HP and Compaq PCs priced over $250.  Also, the Compaq portion of hpshopping.com offers a $50 mail-in rebate on all Compaq Presario desktops and $50 mail-in rebate on all monitors purchased with desktops.
