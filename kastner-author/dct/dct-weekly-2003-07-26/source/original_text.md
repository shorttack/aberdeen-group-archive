This Week's Analysis of PC Deals

PC Deals Trends and Commentary

As of this week, PC Deals no longer follows pricing on old eMachines, HP, and Compaq desktops because these manufacturers have released new line-ups at prices that compete with the older models.  Excellent deals can still be found on these clearance models at websites like www.hpshopping.com, www.pcconnection.com, and www.pcdeals.com.

While desktop deals haven't been overly impressive for the past few weeks, we've noticed that prices on computer accessories have been excellent.  For example, CompUSA advertises a wireless 802.11G router running at 54 mbps for $100.  And we're always talking about the amazing prices of LCD flat panel monitors.  But our newest favorite toy is HP's psc 2110 all-in-one printer/scanner/copier, the world's smallest all-in-one.  While not quite as small as today's basic inkjet printers, the psc 2110 doesn't take up any more desk surface area than a small flat bed scanner.  Set-up is easy and we're very impressed by this machine's color copy capabilities.  But the real kicker is that it only costs $100. 

This Week's Buying Tips 

BestBuy
Best Buy has revamped its website completely.  You'll find the link to its "weekly ad" at the bottom, center of the home page along with the "store locator" link.  When viewing desktop computers, the site works pretty much the same as previously, except that you have to select desktops to compare before you can sort them by brand or price.  We also noted that BestBuy.com is carrying many low-end Compaq and HP machines with skus that we can't find anywhere else, as well as older Compaq and eMachines desktops.  We recommend comparing any PCs you see at www.bestbuy.com with prices at the manufacturers' websites.

At Best Buy stores this week, save $100-$150 with Best Buy's mail-in rebate on select computer packages.  Also receive no interest financing for 12 months on all these CPU bundles.  We're impressed with the "Creation Station" HP and Compaq desktops shown on the fourth page of BestBuy's flyer; we would increase the amount of DDR memory, though.

BestBuy.com still offers free shipping and no interest until January 2004.
  
Circuit City
Save up to $200 on select Desktop and Laptop Computer Packages and enjoy no interest for 12 months.  

Circuitcity.com continues its free shipping offer on all its online items.  Circuitcity.com is the place to go for the newest retail models in all HP's lines, including the A200, A250, and Media Center 200 lines.  The A220n and A230n contain AMD Athlon processors at very attractive prices.  
   
CompUSA
CompUSA's flyer this week has an assortment of PCs, although none of their deals are the best we've seen on the given desktops.  CompUSA is the first to offer HP's M290N in a bundled deal. But at a whopping $2,800, we don't recommend this desktop as the price performer in the high-end Intel processor category. The HP 259y series PC we've customized with only slightly less impressive specs than the M290N will cost you $1,000 less.

CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them.  This means you can get better deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  But this can also trap you into paying almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; so, it's important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal. 

Dell
Dell currently offers free shipping and CD-RW upgrades in the second optical bay on all desktop models.  With the exception of the 2400, Dell offers mail-in rebates this week: $50 off the 4600 and 4600C, $100 off the 8300, and $150 XPS models.  

With free shipping and mail-in rebates we expected Dell's prices on the desktops we customize to be quite competitive this week.  But, as usual, Dell's offers are not a good indicator of final PC pricing.  This week, every Dell we configure increased dramatically in price with the 4600 and 8300 models jumping over $250.  As the "back-to-school" season draws nearer, we suspect Dell will lower its prices and offer more deals.

Customizing a high-end PC with the latest Hyper-Threading Intel processor at 3.2 GHz continues to yield high prices, especially in contrast with HP's winning machine in our $1600+ category so we have not included any Dell PCs with this technology yet.

eMachines
Circuit City and Best Buy sell all four models in eMachines' new line-up and they continue to sell a few older models.  Bundled deals from these national retailers supply the best bang for your buck on eMachines desktops.  www.emachines.com is the best source for researching these PCs.

www.circuitcity.com offers a new eMachines desktop not found at emachines.com.  The T2605 wins this week's $600 category with its Athlon 2600+ processor and 512 DDR that competes with the winning PCs in the $750 category.

Gateway
Gateway's prices on the PCs we customize at www.gateway.com have not changed, with the exception of a slight price drop on the 500X model last week.  But we were happy to see that the 700XL, while maintaining the same price, now comes standard with the new Pentium 4 HT 3.2 GHz processor.  Shipping is always included in the prices shown at Gateway's site.
Sony
Sony.com offers a plethora of models right now, although, like eMachines, the best deals usually come from bundled retail offers.  Receive free shipping at Sony Online this week.  

Customizing a PC at Sony Online allows you to eliminate options, such as the "Giga Pocket" Personal Video Recorder, that come standard with retail models.

HP and Compaq
HP Online offers competitive prices on its retail models, although national retailers usually carry well-priced bundled deals.  HP has expanded its A250 line to resemble the S4000 series of desktops by Compaq so that choosing the grade of processor determines the model you will customize.  Along with the full release of the new line comes the disappearance of the option to customize the pavilion 500 and 700, or 800 (media center) lines.  

We are pleasantly surprised to see that these new HP PCs compete directly with Compaq, unlike before when Compaq often undercut HP's prices.  Our top picks are the A250e series machine that wins in the $1200 category this week and the roughly $1700 A250y series PC that wins our most expensive category by offering the fastest Intel processor, 3.2 GHz, at the price most vendors are selling the 3.0 GHz machines.

However, now that HP has fully released its new line of media center PCs (M200 series), we're not as impressed with HP's pricing.  The media center line-up is meant to be the high-end of HP's desktops with DVD+RWs and media software standard.  But the pricing seems over-the-top when you consider that you could customize the A250y series for much less and then buy DVD editing software all for less than what HP's media center PCs cost.

Get free shipping on all HP and Compaq PCs priced over $250.  Also, the Compaq portion of hpshopping.com offers a $50 mail-in rebate on all Compaq Presario desktops and $50 mail-in rebate on all monitors purchased with desktops.
