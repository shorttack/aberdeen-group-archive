This Week's Analysis of PC Deals

PC Deals Trends and Commentary

Last week was a great time to customize a new desktop at www.dell.com.  This week, Dimension desktop prices jolted upwards in some cases over $200.  But this doesn't mean that other online retailers that allow customization have increased their prices.  HP-Compaq continue to impress with reasonable prices on all levels of desktops, and this week Compaq's prices dropped a few dollars, making these PCs an even more attractive buy.  Retailers also continued last week's abundance of bundled deals into this week.  We are particularly impressed with the customized HP Pavilion A250y series desktop (at Best Buy stores) with a 2.6 HT processor, 256 DDR, 80 GB, DVD/CD-RW, 15" LCD monitor, and HP color printer for $900.

This Week's Buying Tips 

BestBuy
Best Buy has revamped its Web site completely.  You will find the link to its weekly ad at the bottom, center of the home page along with the store locator link.  When viewing desktop computers, the site works pretty much the same as previously, except that you have to select desktops to compare before you can sort them by brand or price.  Notably, BestBuy.com is carrying many low-end Compaq and HP machines with SKUs that we can't find anywhere else, as well as older Compaq and eMachines desktops.  We recommend comparing any PCs you see at www.bestbuy.com with prices at the manufacturers' Web sites.

At Best Buy stores this week, save $150 with Best Buy's mail-in rebate on select computer packages.  In addition, receive no-interest financing for 12 months on all these CPU bundles.  We were impressed with the Creation Station HP and Compaq desktops shown on the fourth page of BestBuy's flyer; we would increase the system's amount of DDR memory, though.

BestBuy.com still offers free shipping and no interest until January 2004.
  
Circuit City
Save up to $400 on select desktop computer packages and enjoy no interest for 12 months.  

Circuitcity.com continues its free shipping offer on all of its online items.  Circuitcity.com is the place to go for the newest retail models in all of HP's lines, including the A200, A250, and Media Center 200 lines.  The A220n and A230n contain AMD Athlon processors at very attractive prices.  
   
CompUSA
No CompUSA flyer appeared in the Atlanta Journal-Constitution this week.

CompUSA.com tends to continue selling "older" PC models after BestBuy.com and CircuitCity.com have removed them.  This means you can get better deals on models that retailers want off the shelves to make room for the newer versions.  However, this can also trap you into paying almost the same amount for the older model as you would for the newest model; therefore, it is important to compare models with similar features and compare with other sites to make sure you're getting a good deal.

To save on shipping at www.compusa.com, try the in-store pick-up feature that will tell you in which stores the items you want are in stock. 

Dell
Dell currently offers free hard drive upgrades up to 80 GB and free monitor upgrades to a 19" CRT or 15" LCD flat panel.  With the exception of the 2400, Dell offers the following mail-in rebates this week: $50 off the 4600 and $100 off the 8300.  

Last week's prices at www.dell.com represented the manufacturer's back-to-school season low.  This week, prices surged over $200.  Especially in regards to the Dimension 2400, we do not recommend purchasing a Dell PC this week.

Customizing a high-end PC with the latest HT-based Intel processor with 3.2 GHz continues to yield high prices, especially in contrast with HP's winning machine in our $1,600-plus category, so we have not yet included any Dell PCs with this technology.

eMachines
Circuit City and Best Buy sell all four models in eMachines' new lineup and they continue to sell a few older models.  Bundled deals from these national retailers supply the best bang for your buck on eMachines desktops.  Emachines.com is the best source for researching these PCs.

Gateway
The prices on the PCs we customize at Gateway.com rarely change.  This means Gateway usually does not compete as well as other vendors that fluctuate their prices.  Shipping is always included in the prices shown at Gateway's site.
Sony
Sony.com offers a plethora of models right now although, as with eMachines, the best deals usually come from bundled retail offers.  The PCV-RS210 model no longer exists at any major retail websites so we have eliminated it from PC Deals.  

Customizing a PC at Sony Online allows you to eliminate options, such as the "Giga Pocket" Personal Video Recorder, that come standard with retail models.

HP and Compaq
HP Online offers competitive prices on its retail models, although national retailers usually carry well-priced bundled deals.  HP has expanded its A250 line to resemble the S4000 series of desktops by Compaq, so that choosing the grade of processor determines the model you will customize.  Along with the full release of the new line comes the disappearance of the option to customize the pavilion 500 and 700, or 800 (media center) lines.  

We are pleasantly surprised to see that these new HP PCs compete directly with Compaq, unlike before, when Compaq often undercut HP's prices.  Our top picks are the A250e series machine that wins in the $1,200 category this week and the roughly $1,700 A250y series PC that wins our most expensive category by offering the fastest Intel processor, 3.2 GHz, at a price that most vendors sell the 3.0 GHz machines.

However, now that HP has fully released its new line of media center PCs (M200 series), we're not as impressed with HP's pricing.  The media center lineup is meant to be the high end of HP's desktops, with DVD+RWs and media software standard.  But the pricing seems over-the-top when you consider that you could customize the A250y series for much less and then buy DVD editing software for less than the cost of HP's media center PC.

Get free shipping on all HP and Compaq PCs priced over $250.  Also, the Compaq portion of hpshopping.com offers a $50 mail-in rebate on all Compaq Presario desktops, free DVD-ROM upgrades, and $50 mail-in rebate on all monitors purchased with desktops.
