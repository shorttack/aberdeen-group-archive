ARS takes the hassle and guesswork out of reselling or
recycling used computer equipment – helping you recover
value for your customers, remove unneeded hardware, and
strategically replace out-of-date systems. 
ARS offers customers two disposition services:
VALUE RECOVERY For equipment that may have some
remaining value, Dell will remove and resell hardware,
then return value to the customer.
RECYCLING For older equipment, or customers who do
not want hardware resold, Dell will remove and recycle it
according to EPA guidelines.
Both services include
• Transportation
• Flexible logistics
• Data cleansing
• Detailed reporting
Dell ARS is the environmentally friendly, efficient, and
secure way to recycle or dispose of used desktops, notebooks,
servers, storage systems, networking devices, printers, and
peripherals.

Flexible Logistics and Transportation
Integrated transportation and flexible logistics allow cus-
tomers to tailor their exact level of recovery support. Asset
Recovery Services offers two options for handling the
packaging and transportation phase.
CUSTOMER PRE-PACKED
• Customer packs equipment
• Single location pickup
• No program management from Dell

ties. Dell will provide a kit with instructions and ship-
ping air bills. Customers will box their systems and
schedule a pickup at www.airborne.com or by calling 
1-800-AIRBORNE. 
Pre-Packed Pallet Logistics: For larger recovery quantities.
Dell’s freight vendor will contact the customer 3 to 5 days
after order placement to schedule a pickup. Customers
will palletize their equipment.
PROGRAM MANAGED
• Dell gathers and packs equipment
• Multiple location pickup
• Program Manager oversight
• Other special logistics on custom bid basis
Detailed Reporting
Dell addresses customers’ asset security and environmental lia-
bility concerns through a detailed and accurate reporting process.
Customers are sent two reports upon completion of the used
equipment’s sale.
SETTLEMENT REPORT: Specifically lists each piece of resold
hardware’s resale value and documents any recycled equipment.
CERTIFICATE OF DISPOSAL*: Verifies that hard disks were over-
written with an automated script that writes 0s and 1s across the
disk (inoperable disks are shredded). Also verifies that any recy-
cling was done in accordance with EPA guidelines.

Q: How does Dell’s ARS pricing define a “system”?
A: A “system” is defined as two significant pieces of computer hardware (i.e., a
CPU and monitor). Smaller peripherals such as mice and keyboards are collected
as part of an Asset Recovery Services sale, but are not considered in pricing.
A “system” can also be:
• A monitor and printer
• A CPU and printer
• Two CPUs
• Two monitors
• Two printers
Q: What information is included with ARS Reporting?
A: 1) Settlement Report: For Value Recovery, this report lists all resold hardware
and its resale value. For Recycling, it lists each piece of recycled hardware.
2) Certificate of Disposal: This certificate verifies that hard disks were overwrit-
ten with a script that writes 0s and 1s across the disk (inoperable drives are
shredded)*. It also verifies that any recycling was performed in accordance with
EPA guidelines. 
Q: Why should a customer purchase ARS from Dell?
A: This is a convenient, cost-effective solution that eliminates several hassles for
your customers:
DATA SECURITY/DATA CLEANSING – New government regulations regarding the
security of information contained on computer hard drives have caused many
companies concern and confusion over proper disposal of computer equipment.
Dell reduces this concern by removing all tags/labels on equipment (that could
identify the customer) and by using a destructive overwrite process* or by physi-
cally destroying hard drives with no remaining value.
ELIMINATES EXPENSE OF STORING EXCESS, OUTDATED, OR USED EQUIPMENT –
Customers may not realize how much excess, outdated, or used equipment they
have, nor the cost associated with its storage.
LACK OF KNOWLEDGE – Customers generally lack the knowledge to properly dis-
pose of used computer equipment in an environmentally friendly manner that also
safeguards any sensitive data remaining on old hard drives. Asset Recovery
Services is an easy-to-use, fast solution.
LACK OF LOGISITICS/DISPOSITION INFRASTRUCTURE – Customers generally lack
the in-house logistics and disposition capabilities to manage recycling used
computer equipment.  Asset Recovery Services eliminates this problem by pro-
viding a complete, cost-effective solution.  Depending on customer requirements,
Dell may either pick up pre-packed equipment or provide a fully managed solu-
tion, alleviating the concerns associated with equipment recycling and disposition.
VALUE RECOVERY– For customers with used equipment that may have some resale
value, Dell will evaluate recovered inventory to determine what value exists. Dell will
then make a fixed-price offer to the customer for that equipment based on current market
values. An additional benefit of the Value Recovery service is that the customer may
reduce storage costs or gain floor space while capturing cash from unused hardware.
RECYCLING – Many customers are not familiar with EPA guidelines regarding the
proper disposal of used computer equipment. With the Recycling option, Dell will
properly dispose of your customers’ hardware, resolving this issue for them
quickly and efficiently.
REPORTING PROCEDURES – Customers may not be aware of the proper documentation
required when recycling or disposing of used computer equipment. To eliminate this
concern, Asset Recovery Services provides detailed reports to the customer listing the
recycled or resold hardware and certifying its proper disposal.

Q: How do Asset Recovery Services contribute to my company being “environ-
mentally friendly?”
A: By purchasing Asset Recovery Services, your old or used equipment is recycled
or resold, which helps keep environmentally sensitive materials out of landfills,
enables materials to be recycled or reused, and helps conserve natural resources.
Q: How should I position these services to a customer?
A: Dell Asset Recovery Services is a service that assists customers with dispos-
ing of excess and obsolete computer hardware in a data-secure, environmentally
friendly and economically viable manner.
Q: What is “data cleansing?”
A: Cleansing removes identifying tags and overwrites data or shreds hard drives.
Q: What is the difference between “Recycling” and “Value Recovery?”
A: Our Recycling process de-manufactures and sorts hardware by commodity
type (glass, metal, and plastic).  Hardware is reused to make new products or
disposed of in an environmentally friendly manner. 
In our Value Recovery process hardware is resold if it has remaining value or
recycled if it has none. If the hardware has remaining value, current market values
are returned to the customer.
Q: What equipment can be recycled under this program?
A: Dell’s Asset Recovery Services (ARS) offers Recycling and Value Recovery pro-
grams for used computer hardware of any brand, including desktops, notebooks,
servers, storage, networking, monitors, printers, projectors, and computer periph-
erals such as keyboards and mice.
RESEARCHED OBJECTIONS. QUALIFIED RESPONSES.
OBJECTION: I can just throw the old equipment in the trash. 
ANSWER: It may be illegal to improperly dispose of your equipment. By choosing
ARS, Dell will provide a certificate that states the disposition of the old equipment
was handled in accordance with EPA guidelines.
OBJECTION: My data is too sensitive/classified to let it go out the door with a
system being recycled.
ANSWER: Dell uses an overwrite script* that writes 1s and 0s over each sector of
the hard drive. Non-operable drives will be shredded or otherwise destroyed.  A
“Certificate of Data Overwrite Process and Disposal in Accordance With EPA
Guidelines” will be provided to you. Additionally, Dell can provide custom data
security services. Check with the Asset Recovery team for details.
OBJECTION: We have locations all over the place, so this will be too hard to
manage.
ANSWER: Through the Program Managed offering, Dell will coordinate all logis-
tics services with dedicated program managers and can support multiple locations
with a single order from you.
OBJECTION: My hardware still has value, so I don’t want it to be thrown away.
ANSWER: Dell’s Value Recovery service allows you to sell your used equipment
for a predetermined price. In that service we sell the hardware at an agreed upon
rate and return the remaining value to you.
OBJECTION: How does the price compare to the competition?
ANSWER: Dell’s prices are lower than or equal to the competition, and we pro-
vide extensive documentation, logistics support, and program management to
ensure the hardware and data are disposed of in an efficient, secure, and con-
venient manner.
