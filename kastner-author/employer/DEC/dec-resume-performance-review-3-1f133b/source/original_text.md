Summary
Over nineteen years of computer industry experience covering a wide range of marketing, sales, and technical management roles. A generalist with excellent communications skills and particular expertise in marketing management, technical project management, and consulting.

Eight years of corporate marketing experience at two successful minicomputer vendors. Emphasis on market development and industry marketing, competitive analysis, sales support, and product management. Grew with a startup marketing organization from zero to $200M sales level.

Ten years experience in a consulting and software house environment. Responsible for numerous package and custom software development projects and consulting assignments. Managed three minicomputer OEM relationships.

Experience

Manager, Marketing Development, Stratus Computer, Inc., Marlboro, Mass. (Oct. 1981-Present)
As an original member of the Stratus marketing team reporting to the VP Product Marketing, he has been a key contributor in all aspects of corporate marketing for this rapidly growing manufacturer of fault tolerant transaction processing computers.

From 1981 to 1984, he was directly responsible for the strategy and management of corporate communications, sales support, marketing administration, and product introduction functions. These programs included third party software house/VAR program development, industry marketing, trade shows and seminars, advertising and sales support collateral, sales/order administration and forecasting, and product management including two product generations and an innovative PC/host connectivity product.

From 1984 to 1986, he was responsible as Manager of Business Development for the major expansion of Stratus' alternate channels of distribution. This highly successful effort culminated in agreements with Honeywell, Burroughs/SDC, and an unprecedented relationship with IBM which resulted in the IBM System/88 version of the Stratus product. Mr. Kastner was responsible for overall strategy and program management, with direct sales/business development goals.

As Manager of Marketing Development from 1986-present, he directs the competitive analysis, consultant relations, major opportunity sales planning, and product introduction activities. He innovated a sales hotline to increase sales productivity. He managed the corporate product introduction effort for the XA2000 family of products.

Manager, Business Market Planning, Prime Computer, Inc., Natick, MA, 1979-1981
Group vertical market manager for Prime's commercial markets. Managed the development and implementation of the first focused vertical marketing programs in Prime's history for the banking, general business, government, and manufacturing markets. Prepared a five year product development plan tied to the requirements of all of Prime's vertical markets.

Group Program Manager, Arthur D. Little, Inc., Cambridge, MA, 1971-1979
Progressively increasing responsibilities for the Information Systems group of ADL, a well-known consulting firm. Participated in more than thirty assignments. Managed large minicomputer systems development projects and OEM relationships with DEC, DG, and HP. Responsible for sales and business development as well as technical project management.

Senior Programmer/Analyst, PHI Computer Services, Inc., Arlington, MA, 1969-1971
Custom software development and software package development for this subsidiary of Wang Laboratories.

Education
Cornell University, School of Hotel Administration, 1969
Boston University, graduate credits in computer science, 1971-72
