Memo
To:  Tom Willmott
Cc: Chet Verceglio, Margie Koenig
Subject:  New Product Launch Status
For the past month I have been working on a number of product marketing initiatives.  As we prepare for training here next week, there is a launch training presentation of some 40 slides including extensive Frequently Asked Questions which we used successfully for a dry run in Palo Alto.
This is the status of what has been done:
Workshops
Five workshops have been defined.  These are:
    • Go-to-Market --  review market, competition, positioning, messaging
    • Market Knowledge  -- market segments, dynamics, evolution, and competition
    • Market Positioning -- positioning the company/product for maximum market advantage
    • Messaging Strategy --  development of key messages for customers, press, analysts & investors
    • Go-to-Market Readiness – review presentations and launch collateral prior to public launch.  Two variations.
In the future, we will define a sixth: Alliances – examine channel and partner opportunities.
Each Workshop has a separate set of deliverables:
    • Sales proposal template (50 pages total)
    • Product overview with selling tips (25 pages total).
Margie and Kathleen Roche are working on the Workshop methodologies.  A Workshop SLIC is in production.
Next step is to develop training on how to be an Aberdeen consultant.  I have also been identifying those analysts who will easily be given a “license to consult”, those who are questionable, and those who may not be able to do this work without significant supervision.
Retainers
We have revamped the merchant bazaar of retainer packages, created a new entry-level retainer, and agreed that an ongoing relationship will underpin retainers and other projects.  The new program name is Aberdeen Advisory Services (AAS):
    • Retainer packages are built from base relationship deliverables, some number of consulting days, and optional Pod services such as surveys.
    • Current analyst-days retainer clients will see no change in service level or pricing.  New retainers will cost less for lower-level analysts because pricing is $7,000 for access plus two days at the per diem rate.  Survey-retainer pricing is also unaffected and revenue neutral.
    • The AAS introductory runs six months, costs $6,000 up front, and includes analyst access, pubs access online, press access and a half-day of consulting.
    • Active clients, loosely defined as buying one-half day a quarter of consulting or equivalent pubs/content, continue to receive analyst access, pubs access online, and press access at no additional charge.  We have to change the culture to make sure clients actually get a relationship from us, but that due date comes in Q1-2002.
There is a Sales product overview and proposals for the new intro AAS and for traditional AAS (20+ pages).  In addition, publication proposals were updated to include a section on the AAS relationship (25 pages).  Training materials for retainers are complete.  Two SLICs are written and in production.
Pricing
The price list has been significantly rationalized and simplified, eliminating unsold services, and getting back to labor + retainers + publications + content basics.  A new price list will be issued effective September 1st.
Labor rates are changed, notably by lowering the multi-day rate so that our bids are more attractive.  There should be no revenue loss based on the current operating mix.  Consulting will be emphasized more going forward – the Workshops are a packaged example.
Retainer clients receive a modest discount on publication projects and labor per diems.
