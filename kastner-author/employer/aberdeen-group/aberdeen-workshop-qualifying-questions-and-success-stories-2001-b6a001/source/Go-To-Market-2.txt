Go-To-Market Workshop 

Overview:
Aberdeen’s Go-To-Market Workshop is targeted at companies wishing an in-depth diagnosis of their entire value proposition.  It is designed as an overall review of product strategy (completeness and competitiveness), channel strategy (mix and engagement plan), and alliance strategy (complementary partners and strategic alliances which accelerate time to market.  This workshop is structured as an interactive dialogue between senior managers and a senior Aberdeen industry analyst onsite.  The goal will be to evaluate the entire go-to-market strategy well in advance of launch planning, ideally in the product development stage.  It is designed to accelerate market acceptance and increase the impact of the product launch.
Lifecycle:
    • Development planning
    • Launch planning
Benefits:
    • In-depth diagnosis of the entire product value proposition
    • Anticipate market trends during development process
    • Accelerate market acceptance of product
    • Increase potential for successful launch
    • Focus on sustainable growth for product
Components:
    • 2 Day interactive consulting engagement
    • 4-6 page report detailing findings
    • 1 hour teleconference 
    • 1-2 page recommendations for future engagements
Pricing:
    • $50,000 
Recommended Prerequsites:
    • None
Recommended Next Steps:
    • Market Knowledge Workshop
    • Positioning Strategy
    • Messaging Strategy
    • Strategy Validation (RAMP)
    • Custom Research 
    • Retainer

Go-To-Market – Qualifying Questions

To determine whether this would be appropriate for a prospect:

    • Are they in early/mid stages of development?
Generally prospects who have been funded and are beginning to ramp up development or who have reached the alpha stage will benefit most from this type of analysis.  It is best to ask this indirectly of the client. Typical questions would be:
            ▪ How large is your staff (engineering, marketing, sales)
            ▪ Where are you looking to hire and how much will you increase the staff size?
            ▪ Have you reached an alpha stage? 

    • Do they have funding to take advantage of this?
Funding is key since the result of this engagement will typically require some re-engineering or repositioning of the product. Funding levels >2 million dollars or a pending search (such as a series B round) will be appropriate. These clients will benefit from this engagement before they seek additional venture funding beyond the seed round. 
Do not directly ask them about funding levels. Typical questions would be:
            ▪ are you seeking additional sources of funding to complete development?
            ▪ Will you be seeking additional funds before you launch?
            ▪ What level of funding will you allocate to launch activities?

    • How well have they thought out their value proposition?
It is best to find this out indirectly as well. Key things they should know (and should make sense) are (a) who will buy their product (b) how much will they pay? (c) what is their distribution strategy?  (d) what’s the end goal of the launch (market share, sales revenues, etc). If they are not clear about this, be tactful – don’t directly criticize them. Typically, they may not have any marketing expertise and only technology expertise. 

How to Propose the product:

    • Remember, our mission is to help them reach their goals. 
    • Don’t use a hard sell
    • If they say no, keep in touch with them as they move forward through the lifecycle – especially toward beta. . At the beta stage, recommend some of the other engagements. 


A sample approach might be
“One thing I might mention to you which could be helpful is our Aberdeen Go-to-Market Engagement. It is a brief consulting engagement – we can complete it in a week – where we will evaluate your product against our methodology for emerging companies. A number of our clients have found it incredibly valuable at your stage of development – just a few insights about market trends could be very helpful to you as you complete development. It is a diagnostic session where we benchmark your product against market trends, our own market forecasts, your current positioning. We recommend potential partnerships and alliances which may help in speeding your product to market, analyze where the market (and your competitors) will be when you are ready to launch, and how you can effectively position yourself for success well in advance. Given where you are right now, it can really help ensure that your product will be well received in the market and that you will be able to take advantage of future opportunities for growth. It is reasonably priced – just a ½% of your total funding – or $50,000 . “
