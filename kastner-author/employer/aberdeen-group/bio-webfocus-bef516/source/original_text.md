## Original Document Text

Peter Kastner Biography
Area of Focus
Peter Kastner manages the research business unit and is responsible for the content of Aberdeen's research products and consulting services. Using his extensive commercial Information Technology (IT) experience and the full gamut of Aberdeen Group research, Kastner actively consults with suppliers on executive level market research and positioning, industry analysis, strategic planning development, product planning, and competitive strategies. He has served as ad hoc marketing counsel to technology startups, been instrumental in the strategy and execution of billion-dollar rollouts, advised senior executives in business strategy including mergers and acquisitions, and served as a technology market expert witness.
In 2001, Kastner started Aberdeen Group's Personal IT practice area, which focuses on traditional PCs, small office/home office (SOHO) computing, and the convergence of consumer computing with Home Networking, entertainment, imaging, and messaging.
Experience
Kastner has over 30 years of IT industry experience in both user and supplier organizations. His expertise includes online transaction processing (OLTP), decision support, client/server architectures, distributed commercial systems development, e-Commerce, and commercial benchmark auditing and analysis. Prior to joining Aberdeen Group as vice president in 1988, Kastner was a marketing executive with Digital Equipment, Prime Computer, and Stratus Computer. For over a decade as a consultant and program manager with Arthur D. Little Inc., Kastner was actively involved in the management and execution of complex technology development projects spanning the manufacturing, banking and financial services, health care, and government markets and including mainframe, minicomputer, and desktop systems.
