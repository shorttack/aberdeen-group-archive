Slide 3
Time Is Your Enemy
The majority of US enterprises are acting as 
though they have 2-3 years to fix the numerous 
Y2K problems they will face
But to be Y2K safe on 1/1/2000, you must have:
• Your Y2K safe applications in place by 9/30/98 for 5 
quarters of testing-cutting over to production 
• Your 12-month renovation/replacement program started by 
9/30/97 
If you are not working on the problem 
today, you may have already failed!

Government Agency Operations Will

agencies will not be able to operate 
during 1999 and beyond as they do in

comply with oversight requirements

the World!
European enterprises are more concerned 
with the European Monetary Unit issue — 
there is a business-to-business 
requirement for 1/1/1999

not want to hear the bad Y2K news
Need to assess the impact of Y2K failures 
in non-US enterprises on your operations

Slide 7
Few Best Practices 
and Few Willing Volunteers
The normally considered prudent IT 
strategy of being a fast-second is not 
applicable to the Y2K problem
• Until 1/1/2000, nobody can show if their Y2K 
corrective actions worked — therefore, no way of 
judging best practices
Business and IT managers and staff want 
to leave the Y2K problem to anybody else
• Very few see a hero’s role
• Realistic IT managers expect at best to receive a 
“C+” for all their efforts

Business Decisions

evaluated for potential Y2K problems
Some economists are forecasting a 1999

Nobody Really Understands the 
Liabilities and Costs
Your enterprise’s president should have an 
active and monitored Y2K compliance plan in 
place — NOW!
• Directors and Officers Insurance should specify Y2K 
protection
• Note that lawyers are trying to make individuals liable 
for failures
• Y2K meetings held with corporate counsel
• Failed operations will not be brought back to life by 
litigation
The constraint to fixing the Y2K problem is 
people resources — not money

Management Options — 
Acceptance Tactics 
Discard
• The application is no longer required — put it in the 
trash can 
• The business operation is no longer necessary — sell it 
off
Repair
• Initiate and support efforts to fix existing applications
– But too often ignores underlying systems software
Replace
• New systems and applications

But Evaluating Options from a 
Management Perspective
Discard After Triage
• Much “C-level” code that was originally 
thought to be unnecessary (from using the 
A-B-C method) turns out to be required to 
support “A-level”
• Incremental gross margins of business 
operations often provide significant 
overhead contributions
Discard requires thoughtful 
management analysis


--- Slide/Page 15 ---
 
 
AberdeenGroup
Slide 15
But Evaluating Options from a 
Management Perspective
Repair
• Inter-relatedness of many undocumented mainframe 
applications creates a very high and real risk of failure
– Good luck with version control and program interface 
documentation and modification
• Undocumented, older, non-COBOL systems and 
application software may not be repairable
• Who is qualified and available to reliably repair the 
existing systems?
• Repair efforts must be expensed when incurred
– Depresses quarterly earnings per share


--- Slide/Page 16 ---
 
 
AberdeenGroup
Slide 16
But Evaluating Options from a 
Management Perspective
Replace
• If you want to replace/re-engineer business 
processes — you are now 2 years too late
• If you want to replace/replicate current 
functionality, it is possible if you move fast 
with your best executive talent
– Replace/replicate will provide a state-of-the-
art infrastructure on which to build in the 
future
• Replacement costs can be capitalized

Managing Under Uncertainty and to 
Minimize Risk
Don’t shoot the messengers
Establish a powerful Y2K Office with a 
strong, enterprise-motivated leader
Arm the Y2K Office with a 3rd-party 
Y2K methodology that is continuously 
updated
Identify all enterprise assets that may 
be at risk and assess the level of risk 
and potential impact


--- Slide/Page 18 ---
 
 
AberdeenGroup
Slide 18
Moving to Action
Separate information assets into the following 
priority:
• Customer touch
– Billing, order processing, telecommunications, customer 
service
• Internal process management
– Manufacturing scheduling, purchasing, inventory control
• Organizational effectiveness
– Office automation, records management
Repair or Replace functionality by priority
Do not delay on tool decisions — need to make 
action priority moving to testing ASAP


--- Slide/Page 19 ---
 
 
AberdeenGroup
Slide 19
Your Operational Rules May Need 
Changing
The Y2K management process now 
becomes an exercise in Triage
• Some currently automated functions may have to go 
back to manual methods temporarily
• Work directly with your customers, suppliers, and 
government agencies that oversee your operations 
to ensure that your Y2K Triage plans are all 
compatible
• You may have to delay initiating planned IT-based 
productivity improvement projects until your 
business-critical systems are Y2K stable

Take a War-Time Attitude
Move quickly — every delay only increases the 
risks of catastrophe
Select your allies to help you manage through 
this problem smartly and quickly
• Reputable suppliers are running out of capacity
Closely monitor both internal progress and that 
of members of your value chain
Be pragmatic, not theoretical
Take advantage of competitors’ mis-steps
Let the next generation of managers worry about 
paying for your efforts

can make the decisions and take the unique 
actions necessary to manage through the Y2K
