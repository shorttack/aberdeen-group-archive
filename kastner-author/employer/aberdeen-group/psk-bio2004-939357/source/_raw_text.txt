Biography of Peter S. Kastner, Chief Research Officer

Area of Focus

Peter Kastner is executive vice president of technology research and manages the technology research business.
Using his extensive commercial Information Technology (IT) experience and the full gamut of Aberdeen Group research, Kastner actively consults with suppliers on executive-level market research and positioning and industry analysis. His work with Global 5000 enterprises includes strategic technology planning centered on generating business value, and the technology selection process. He has served as ad hoc marketing counsel to technology startups, been instrumental in the strategy and execution of billion-dollar rollouts, advised senior executives in business strategy including mergers and acquisitions, and acted as a technology market expert witness.
With a deep background in information technology hardware, infrastructure software, and application software, Mr. Kastner is actively involved in Aberdeen's hardware infrastructure, business latency, mobility, retail, consumer electronics, and channel practices.

Experience

Kastner has more than 35 years of IT industry experience in both user and supplier organizations. His expertise includes online transaction processing (OLTP), decision support, client/server architectures, distributed commercial systems development, e-Business, and commercial benchmark auditing and analysis.
Prior to joining Aberdeen Group as vice president and founder in 1988, Kastner was a marketing executive with Digital Equipment, Prime Computer, and Stratus Computer. For more than a decade, Kastner was a consultant and program manager with Arthur D. Little Incorporated, where he was actively involved in the management and execution of complex technology development projects spanning the manufacturing, banking and financial services, retail, healthcare, and government markets that frequently included mainframe, midrange, and desktop systems.
