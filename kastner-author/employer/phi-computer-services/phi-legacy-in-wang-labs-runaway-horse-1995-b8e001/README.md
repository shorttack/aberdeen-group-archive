## Abstract

Excerpt from Charles C. Kenney's 1992-1995 book 'Riding the Runaway Horse: The Rise and Decline of Wang Laboratories' (Little, Brown). Documents PHI Computer Services as 'one of the best' early-1960s software pioneers; describes Wang Laboratories' 1968 PHI acquisition; identifies Bob Siegel, Harold Koplow, Dave Moros, and Bob Kolk — three of whom came from PHI — as the four men whose products yielded 'billions of dollars of revenues for Wang Laboratories' (Wang 2200 small-business computer, Wang Word Processing System, Wang VS commercial mini). Argues Wang's autobiography 'Lessons' significantly understates the PHI contribution to Wang's success.
