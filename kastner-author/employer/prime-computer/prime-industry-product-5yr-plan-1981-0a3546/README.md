## Abstract

Internal Prime Computer Inc. five-year Industry Product Requirements Plan dated July 15, 1981 (COMPANY CONFIDENTIAL), authored by Peter Kastner of Prime's Market Planning and Management Department of Marketing Development with input from Prime market managers (the 'and Others' coauthors). The plan synthesizes vertical-market plans for Construction Engineering, Power Utilities, Manufacturing Engineering, Manufacturing Production, Banking, Government, and Energy (Petroleum) into a single statement of common product requirements for Prime's first half-of-the-1980s strategy. Forecasts product revenue from planned markets growing from $117.7M in 1980 (44% of corporate goal) to $1,193.1M in 1985 (81% of corporate goal). Covers seven System Use Categories (Computational, Timesharing/Program Development, CAD/CAM, Office Automation, Transaction Processing, Query, Prime Information / Small Business) crossed against five system price bands ($30-49K Rabbit through $300K+ Prime 850). Identifies five priority near-term product gaps: programmable parallel-channel interface (HPC-4 / MPC-4 Special), floating-point precision, COBOL at full ANSI intermediate level, FORTRAN 77 compile/runtime performance, and Hyperchannel. Recommends a $10K-$30K personal computer with integral Primenet networking before mid-1980s. Identifies four 'attack' market opportunities: local-area networks of terminals, distributed processing including personal/departmental systems, office automation, and CAD/CAM. The plan defines the product strategy that Kastner — then in Prime Market Planning — used to influence Prime's 1981-1985 development cycle, and is the principal Kastner-authored artifact from his Prime Computer (1979-1981) employer era.
