TITLE: Stratus Banks on Fault-Tolerant Systems' Success — Computer Business News, 7 June 1982 (PSK extensively quoted)
AUTHOR: Peter Bochner (Computer Business News)
DATE: 1982-06-07
SOURCE: Stratus-OEM-1982-06-07-CBN-7.pdf

ABSTRACT:
Computer Business News feature article from CBN's 'CBN Visit' series in which staff writer Peter Bochner profiled Stratus Computer at its Natick MA headquarters with extensive in-person quotes from **Peter Kastner, manager of marketing development**. Article documents Stratus' channel strategy (Fortune 1000 direct + OEM + software-house agreements with discounts but no purchase requirement), the recently-signed Olivetti OEM agreement (England/France/Italy distribution; potentially $40M by 1985), early applications software (Tranpro transaction processor, Forms Management System, 3270 Terminal Support, 3270 Emulator, X.25 Networking, Fortran 77, Pascal) and competitive positioning. Kastner predicts: "By 1986, all [broad-based] computer companies will have to offer fault-tolerant systems" and "at least a half dozen or more will be shipping by the end of 1983." He explains the four-Motorola-68000 paired-CPU architecture, Stratalink networking, the high-level language software approach (only several thousand lines of code; portable across CPU generations), 7%/year vs 10-12% industry-standard maintenance pricing thanks to remote diagnostics, and centralized-storage efficiencies that may partially reverse the decentralization trend. Kastner (introduced as 'a former analyst at Arthur D. Little') frames the FT market as 'blossoming from a niche market to a cross market' as more work goes online and the cost of downtime rises. Article also captures DG President Edson de Castro's commentary on the FT-startup compatibility challenge and second-generation risk.

KEY OBSERVATIONS (from Phase 1 analysis):
- Stratus channel strategy: End-user, software-house, and two-year OEM contracts; software houses get fee per system sale and discount but no buy requirement; OEMs are 'first point of contact for customer whose computer has gone down'
- Olivetti OEM agreement: Olivetti to distribute Stratus products in England/France/Italy; OEM-integration agreement; potential $40M by 1985
- FT-market broad adoption by 1986: By 1986, all [broad-based] computer companies will have to offer fault-tolerant systems
- FT shipments by end of 1983: At least a half dozen or more FT vendors will be shipping by the end of 1983
- Stratus architecture: CPU boards use four 68000 chips paired off (OS + user); high-level language for OS keeps code small (several thousand lines) and CPU-portable via code-generator changes; pair-of-pair self-checking
- Stratus maintenance pricing: 7% per year (vs industry standard of 10-12%) thanks to remote diagnostics + customer-replaceable boards delivered by cab
- Stratus pricing positioning: Under-$200,000; positioned vs Prime 750, DEC VAX 11/750, and IBM 4341 Model 1 (against 32-bit minis on price); not directly going after Tandem on price/performance
- FT market evolution: Fault-tolerant market is blossoming from a niche market to a cross market; as more work goes on-line, computers get closer to the heart of business operations and the cost of downtime goes up
- Kastner pre-Stratus background: Kastner was a former analyst at Arthur D. Little prior to Stratus

KASTNER DIRECT QUOTES:
"any company starting out that ignores OEMs is ignoring a big opportunity"
  Context: Kastner explained Stratus' channel strategy and why OEMs (rather than only direct Fortune-1000 sales) are critical because OEMs are usually the first point of contact for a customer whose computer has gone down.
"By 1986, all [broad-based] computer companies will have to offer fault-tolerant systems. And at least a half dozen or more will be shipping by the end of 1983"
  Context: Kastner's market forecast for the FT industry's broad-vendor adoption arc, made in mid-1982.
"We're not directly going after Tandem with price/performance"
  Context: Kastner positioned Stratus against the 32-bit supermini class (Prime 750, DEC VAX 11/750, IBM 4341 Model 1) at sub-$200,000, rather than head-to-head on price with Tandem.
"Rather than Stratus sending out an engineer, who opens the system door and throws in a board, we can send the part — if it fails — over in a cab"
  Context: Kastner explained Stratus' service model — remote diagnostics plus customer-replaceable boards via courier — which enabled 7%/year maintenance pricing vs the industry standard of 10-12%.
"[the fault-tolerant market is] blossoming from a niche market to a cross market. As more work goes on-line, computers get closer to the heart of business operations. And the cost of downtime goes up"
  Context: Kastner, introduced as a former analyst at Arthur D. Little, framed the FT market's broader trajectory — from specialty to cross-industry foundation.
