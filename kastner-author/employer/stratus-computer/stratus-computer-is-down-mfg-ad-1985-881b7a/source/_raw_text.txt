TITLE: Stratus 'The Computer is Down' Manufacturing Plant-Floor Ad, 1985 — PSK as named contact
AUTHOR: Stratus Computer (corporate marketing — Kastner-era)
DATE: 1985-01-01
SOURCE: Stratus-ad-1985-6.pdf

ABSTRACT:
Full-page Stratus magazine advertisement headlined 'FOUR LITTLE WORDS THAT STRIKE FEAR ON THE PLANT FLOOR' — "The computer is down." — positioning Stratus XA400 as the fault-tolerant standard for modern manufacturing. Includes a price/performance comparison table from Computerworld, August 20 1984: **Stratus XA400** ($446,350; 125 relative performance; $3,571 per unit) vs **IBM 4381** ($707,897; 100; $7,079) vs **HP 3000/68** ($437,754; 64; $6,840) vs **DEC VAX 11/782** ($656,889; 109; $5,999) — Stratus advertised as best price/performance with fault tolerance included. Body copy frames the industry-standard 98.5% reliability as 'unthinkable' for modern manufacturing (one outage every two weeks statistical average). **For information, contact your local Stratus sales office, or call Peter Kastner in Massachusetts at (617) 460-2192, or toll-free at 1-800-752-4826.** Document is a primary-source artifact of Kastner's Stratus role as the named end-customer contact for manufacturing-vertical sales-development queries.
