## Abstract

Continuation clipping (page 14) of the 1987 fault-tolerant market article (companion to image00003 / 'Fault-tolerant market to hit $2 billion in 87'). Reports on three developments expanding the FT market beyond the traditional Stratus/Tandem high-end duopoly: (1) NCR pushing FT into its bread-and-butter POS market with $25,000-$45,000 low-cost FT POS systems — a far cry from top-of-the-line Stratus and Tandem systems costing more than $1M; (2) Tandem recently introducing a series of low-cost systems with expandable processor counts to defend the low end; (3) Santa Cruz CA-based Parallel Computers targeting Unix-based FT systems at telecom and law enforcement; (4) New entrant Tolerant Systems Inc. of San Jose ambitiously aiming low-cost systems at banking, telecommunications, manufacturing, and the federal government.
