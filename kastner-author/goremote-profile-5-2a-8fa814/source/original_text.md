Executive Summary

Computer user security, management, and cost control are well in hand
inside the enterprise firewall. Computer users outside of the firewall —
outworkers — are definitely not well under control. On any given day, more
than 30% of U.S. knowledge workers are not in the home office and not
behind the enterprise firewall’s security. They work at home, in remote
offices, at customer sites, or as domestic and international road warriors.
Telecommunications costs by outworkers are going through the roof, yet are
often hidden owing to the plethora of overlapping telecommunications
services and service providers employed by most enterprises. The key
reasons are the job demands for “always available, always on” hitting the
broadband supply of high-speed Internet at home, 802.11-enabled WiFi
laptops on the road, and fixed or wireless Ethernet in hotel rooms. Heavy
data users are likely to spend more than $5,000 a year and can have a
laptop TCO that is four to five times that of a home office desktop — at up
to $8,000 a year per worker. Aberdeen concludes that outworker cost
management is a ripe topic for IT, procurement, and financial executives to
examine as part of an overall telecommunications cost management strategy.
The task of managing and controlling outworkers has been a support-
intensive headache for information technology (IT) departments, as the
variety of problem combinations by remote users approaches infinity. IT
managers need to contract for and manage multiple service providers to
cover all of today’s “Ethernet outside the firewall” technologies. In
essence, IT organizations had to build a national or even global network to
support remote offices and mobile workers, and then manage the contracts
and network and support the users, while ensuring the managed security that
protects the enterprise. No single solution exists that allows the
outsourcing of remote worker services.
Aberdeen views GoRemote as offering the first unified solution for the cost-
effective management and control of fixed and mobile outwork-ers. Using the
GoRemote solution, enterprises will find that outsourcing user data traffic
outside of the firewall will be practical for the first time, and, thus,
worth modeling the multimillion-dollar savings that Aberdeen projects.

The Outworker Problem and Why It Demands a Solution

The knowledge worker culture has changed dramatically in the past decade or
so, such that workers are expected to be always online and always
available. This nets out for outworkers as frequent e-mail, corporate
application data, and Internet access. Outworkers can operate in both fixed
and mobile categories, and they can be in both on the same day.

Fixed Remote Workers Transition to Broadband

Typically, fixed locations are small or home offices (SOHO) and branches.
In the past, these sites were serviced, respectively, by dial-up lines and
regulated telecom services, such as ISDN and frame relay. Today, broadband
cable or DSL speeds are preferred, and the communications costs per site
can be reduced by hundreds of dollars per site per month when frame relay
is replaced with DSL or cable. Whatever communications medium is used,
fixed remote workers require the security of a VPN, personal firewalls, and
authentication services before they can safely access the enterprise
network and its data.

Mobile Workers Rack Up a la Carte Service Charges on a Daily Basis

Mobile workers represent a different cost sink. Whereas business travel is
down since September 11, 2001, mobile workers are taking advantage of the
rapidly increasing supply of high-speed Ethernet at wireless access points
and fixed Ethernet in hotels and Internet cafes. Most enterprises have dial-
up Internet service provider (ISP) contracts that do not extend well to
broadband Ethernet. There is no national single-source provider to turn to
— and do not even think about international broadband unification. Driven
to keep up with customers, coworkers, and bosses, mobile workers are
dumping dial-up service at a rapid rate. They are displacing dial-up
service with broadband Ethernet, but are paying for it through their
expense reports at a rate of $10 per coffee or airport stop times several
stops a day. As every stop can involve a different broadband service
provider, and all-you-can-drink service is $50 a month per provider, it is
hard to fault mobile knowledge workers who currently pay for service a la
carte — that is, until the CFO spots the millions in hidden a la carte
costs rolling up to the enterprise level.

Outworkers Drive Up IT Help Desk Costs

Both fixed and mobile workers typically get support from a help desk
staffed by the IT department. Because enterprises do not have a single
remote communications services supplier, outsourcing the cost and
aggravation has been problematic. Outworkers generate up to 10 times the
support calls of fixed desktop workers, and it is usually impossible to
send an IT worker to fix a problem. Because outworkers use so many ISPs and
communications technologies, the cost per call tends to be about twice as
high as a typical IT help desk call. Factor in the higher breakdown levels
of laptops, do the multiplication, and it is not hard to see why outworkers
— especially mobile ones — can have a laptop TCO that is four to five times
that of an office desktop and up to $8,000 a year per worker.

Introducing GoRemote Internet Communications

GoRemote Communications, Inc. (NASDAQ: GRIC) is a leading supplier of
managed communications services to remote and mobile knowledge workers.
Founded in 1994, the company has built the TierOne global network across
more than 150 countries totaling more than 35,000 access points, which
allow mobile professionals to seamlessly connect to the Internet or to
their enterprise networks using Ethernet over dial-up, ISDN, broadband, and
wireless technologies. With more than 9,000 access points overseas,
GoRemote has been a staple of international road warriors for years. The
company has a network run rate of more than 1.2 billion access minutes per
year.
Users view GoRemote’s TierOne network as a single global virtual network.
Yet GoRemote is an aggregator. It neither owns nor is constrained by a
private telecommunications infrastructure capex burden. Instead, GoRemote
uses more than 300 telecom service providers, including brand names such as
China Telecom, France Telecom, Level 3, MCI, Singtel, and Telstra. Many
Internet service providers, in turn, use GoRemote to extend their own
service areas.
GoRemote’s growth focus is enterprise oriented. The company already boasts
an enterprise customer list that includes Avnet, Lockheed Martin,
Matsushita, Proctor & Gamble, Saint-Gobain, Stanley Works, and VeriSign.
To better meet the enterprise opportunity for managed remote access
services, GoRemote in late 2003 acquired Axcelerant, a company with
excellent credentials at broadband fixed locations for teleworkers and
branch offices.  Axcelerant had a proven track record with Global 5000
enterprises. Combined, the two companies have the scope to provide
extensive services to enterprises looking to outsource some or all of their
secure remote access needs. Table 1 shows both Aberdeen’s model of the core
competencies of GoRemote.

GoRemote MobileOffice Universal Access Client and Services

MobileOffice is a Windows-based application that simplifies network
connection, log-in, and establishment of secure enterprise communications
through a virtual private network and a personal firewall — all with a few
clicks and regardless of the type of communications connection.
MobileOffice combines access to the Internet or corporate network, security
features for authentication and intrusion protection, and productivity
applications such as online meetings, contact management, and time and
expense reporting.
MobileOffice works the same in London on dial-up as in Los Angeles on
802.11 wireless, simplifying the user’s experience and lowering training
costs. Network access updates are downloaded automatically. IT managers can
customize the client software to automatically load specific applications,
such as Internet Explorer, Outlook or sales force automation. A portal
supports single sign-on to applications.

Total Security Protection Integrates Transparent Remote User Solution

The GoRemote Total Security Protection is a security agent integrated in
MobileOffice. TSP provides:
policy management through monitoring and reporting;
policy enforcement through desired or acceptable system configuration;
adaptive protection that is location based, and automatic remediation when
the PC comes into compliance.  Users must use authenticated, secure,
encrypted communications;
firewall and intrusion detection technology to detect, identify, and halt
intrusions. VPN end points are checked at each login.
Automatic remediation: downloads missing files, patches and applications,
and sets up the remote device before allowing access.
GoRemote’s Managed Security Service outsources firewall monitoring,
maintenance, and set-up. This service is attractive to small to midsize
enterprises that seek to offload the engineering and design up front, and
24×7 support after installation demanded by a firewall.  GoRemote will
optionally provide a fully managed VPN utilizing IP SEC or SSL configured
for remote workers, teleworkers, and especially branch offices.

Universal Remote Control: an Enterprise Management Portal for Remote
Workers

The GoRemote Universal Remote Control is a console for IT managers to
control an outsourced network of remote users. The GoRemote Dashboard
provides a web-based real-time view into critical service usage and network
management, allowing immediate problem resolution for access, security, and
billing issues:
Network monitoring — who is logged on and session data
Security Management — security violations, policy management,
User management — authorization and client download provisioning “push”
Settlement — accounting’s report on billing detail by time, person, and
account; real-time and historical reporting
Customer care — case management tools, service quality performance
measurements

GoRemote’s Enterprise Value Proposition

The GoRemote solutions present actionable benefits through increased value,
greater choice, and cost-displacing TCO reductions.
Aberdeen expects increased value to enterprise customers in GoRemote-
provided services through the following:
The reduction in the complexity, cost, and support requirements of a
growing population of outworkers as measured in growing costs to meet SLA
levels
Improved security and control by consolidating services
Potential productivity improvements for remote workers by delivering cost-
effective anyplace/anytime enterprise network access
Greater options and choices are evident from the fixed and mobile/“any
communications pipe” profile of GoRemote. Forward-looking enterprises will
consolidate and simplify with GoRemote yet actually have more options for
flexible future deployments and cost controls.
Aberdeen expects GoRemote to play well in the CFO’s office as well as the
CIO’s. GoRemote is likely to garner an increasing enterprise base with a
straightforward cost displacement proposition.

Managed Services Displace Fixed Access and Security Infrastructure Costs

For enterprises that currently provide their own remote network
infrastructure, GoRemote and other managed service providers can provide
the remote access services that displace fixed asset investments in access
servers and network management facilities, and in security infrastructure
such as VPN hardware and software, firewalls, and authentication servers.
Cost savings are on the order of 60%, making do-it-yourself an increasingly
unattractive option for many enterprises.

Aberdeen Conclusions

For enterprises still providing do-it-yourself remote user services,
Aberdeen says the genie is out of the bottle; remote outworker usage is
going to go up dramatically. Moreover, the growing need for interwoven
supply chains is driving remote enterprise network access, not just by
employees but also by partners and contractors. The situation begs for a
flexible, adaptable remote access infrastructure.
As working consultants, Aberdeen’s staff tends to be heavy-usage road
warriors. Aberdeen’s staff members are frustrated daily by the lack of any
single telecom supplier’s coverage and by the high retail costs of session
access at by-the-drink prices — plus all the
productivity-sapping headaches of multivendor access idiosyncrasies. Yet
that is the reality of coverage today for Aberdeen and for millions of
fellow outworkers.
GoRemote’s TierOne Network is a leader in managed mobile access services.
The Axcelerant acquisition added a strong fixed broadband capability, which
will add many options for incorporating home, small office, and branch
locations under the TierOne umbrella.  The Total Security Protection
service brings an integrated approach to managing a remote service which
provides a single client and a single point-of-management for any
combination of teleworkers, mobile workers, and branch offices.
Because of what GoRemote is doing, Aberdeen sees unification and
simplification of remote access services as a competitive differentiator
that will drive the telecommunications industry to offer better and more
complete solutions to enterprise customers. The days of a la carte remote
access are over, and outsourcing has become a practical alternative to
today’s managed services
jumble.
Aberdeen has used GoRemote’s international remote access for years, and it
has been a godsend. However, many enterprise buyers do not recognize the
GoRemote brand or its already wide footprint. The business case for using
GoRemote is not hard to make. In most cases, GoRemote will guarantee
service levels and commit to a full deployment in less than 90 days.
Aberdeen sees little risk and a potential multimillion-dollar reward in
engaging GoRemote Internet Communications Inc.
