GoRemote Is Unifying Secure Remote Access Services

Executive Summary

Computer user security, management, and cost control are well in hand inside the enterprise firewall. Computer users outside of the firewall -- outworkers -- are definitely not well under control. On any given day, more than 30% of U.S. knowledge workers are not in the home office and not behind the enterprise firewall's security. They work at home, in remote offices, at customer sites, or as domestic and international road warriors.

Telecommunications costs by outworkers are going through the roof, yet are often hidden owing to the plethora of overlapping telecommunications services and service providers employed by most enterprises. The key reasons are the job demands for "always available, always on" hitting the broadband supply of high-speed Internet at home, 802.11-enabled WiFi laptops on the road, and fixed or wireless Ethernet in hotel rooms. Heavy data users are likely to spend more than $5,000 a year and can have a laptop TCO that is four to five times that of a home office desktop -- at up to $8,000 a year per worker. Aberdeen concludes that outworker cost management is a ripe topic for IT, procurement, and financial executives to examine as part of an overall telecommunications cost management strategy.

The task of managing and controlling outworkers has been a support-intensive headache for information technology (IT) departments, as the variety of problem combinations by remote users approaches infinity. IT managers need to contract for and manage multiple service providers to cover all of today's "Ethernet outside the firewall" technologies. In essence, IT organizations had to build a national or even global network to support remote offices and mobile workers, and then manage the contracts and network and support the users, while ensuring the managed security that protects the enterprise. No single solution exists that allows the outsourcing of remote worker services.

Aberdeen views GoRemote as offering the first unified solution for the cost-effective management and control of fixed and mobile outworkers. Using the GoRemote solution, enterprises will find that outsourcing user data traffic outside of the firewall will be practical for the first time, and, thus, worth modeling the multimillion-dollar savings that Aberdeen projects.

The Outworker Problem and Why It Demands a Solution

The knowledge worker culture has changed dramatically in the past decade or so, such that workers are expected to be always online and always available. This nets out for outworkers as frequent e-mail, corporate application data, and Internet access. Outworkers can operate in both fixed and mobile categories, and they can be in both on the same day.

Fixed Remote Workers Transition to Broadband

Typically, fixed locations are small or home offices (SOHO) and branches. In the past, these sites were serviced, respectively, by dial-up lines and regulated telecom services, such as ISDN and frame relay. Today, broadband cable or DSL speeds are preferred, and the communications costs per site can be reduced by hundreds of dollars per site per month when frame relay is replaced with DSL or cable. Whatever communications medium is used, fixed remote workers require the security of a VPN, personal firewalls, and authentication services before they can safely access the enterprise network and its data.

Mobile Workers Rack Up a la Carte Service Charges on a Daily Basis

Mobile workers represent a different cost sink. Whereas business travel is down since September 11, 2001, mobile workers are taking advantage of the rapidly increasing supply of high-speed Ethernet at wireless access points and fixed Ethernet in hotels and Internet cafes. Most enterprises have dial-up Internet service provider (ISP) contracts that do not extend well to broadband Ethernet. There is no national single-source provider to turn to -- and do not even think about international broadband unification. Driven to keep up with customers, coworkers, and bosses, mobile workers are dumping dial-up service at a rapid rate. They are displacing dial-up service with broadband Ethernet, but are paying for it through their expense reports at a rate of $10 per coffee or airport stop times several stops a day. As every stop can involve a different broadband service provider, and all-you-can-drink service is $50 a month per provider, it is hard to fault mobile knowledge workers who currently pay for service a la carte -- that is, until the CFO spots the millions in hidden a la carte costs rolling up to the enterprise level.

Outworkers Drive Up IT Help Desk Costs

Both fixed and mobile workers typically get support from a help desk staffed by the IT department. Because enterprises do not have a single remote communications services supplier, outsourcing the cost and aggravation has been problematic. Outworkers generate up to 10 times the support calls of fixed desktop workers, and it is usually impossible to send an IT worker to fix a problem. Because outworkers use so many ISPs and communications technologies, the cost per call tends to be about twice as high as a typical IT help desk call. Factor in the higher breakdown levels of laptops, do the multiplication, and it is not hard to see why outworkers -- especially mobile ones -- can have a laptop TCO that is four to five times that of an office desktop and up to $8,000 a year per worker.

Total Security Protection Integrates Transparent Remote User Solution

The GoRemote Total Security Protection is a security agent integrated in MobileOffice. TSP provides:

* policy management through monitoring and reporting;
* policy enforcement through desired or acceptable system configuration;
* adaptive protection that is location based, and automatic remediation when the PC comes into compliance. Users must use authenticated, secure, encrypted communications;
* firewall and intrusion detection technology to detect, identify, and halt intrusions. VPN end points are checked at each login.
* Automatic remediation: downloads missing files, patches and applications, and sets up the remote device before allowing access.

GoRemote's Managed Security Service outsources firewall monitoring, maintenance, and set-up. This service is attractive to small to midsize enterprises that seek to offload the engineering and design up front, and 24x7 support after installation demanded by a firewall. GoRemote will optionally provide a fully managed VPN utilizing IPSec or SSL configured for remote workers, teleworkers, and especially branch offices.

Aberdeen Conclusions

For enterprises still providing do-it-yourself remote user services, Aberdeen says the genie is out of the bottle; remote outworker usage is going to go up dramatically. Moreover, the growing need for interwoven supply chains is driving remote enterprise network access, not just by employees but also by partners and contractors. The situation begs for a flexible, adaptable remote access infrastructure.

As working consultants, Aberdeen's staff tends to be heavy-usage road warriors. Aberdeen's staff members are frustrated daily by the lack of any single telecom supplier's coverage and by the high retail costs of session access at by-the-drink prices -- plus all the productivity-sapping headaches of multivendor access idiosyncrasies. Yet that is the reality of coverage today for Aberdeen and for millions of fellow outworkers.

GoRemote's TierOne Network is a leader in managed mobile access services. The Axcelerant acquisition added a strong fixed broadband capability, which will add many options for incorporating home, small office, and branch locations under the TierOne umbrella. The Total Security Protection service brings an integrated approach to managing a remote service which provides a single client and a single point-of-management for any combination of teleworkers, mobile workers, and branch offices.

Because of what GoRemote is doing, Aberdeen sees unification and simplification of remote access services as a competitive differentiator that will drive the telecommunications industry to offer better and more complete solutions to enterprise customers. The days of a la carte remote access are over, and outsourcing has become a practical alternative to today's managed services jumble.

Aberdeen has used GoRemote's international remote access for years, and it has been a godsend. However, many enterprise buyers do not recognize the GoRemote brand or its already wide footprint. The business case for using GoRemote is not hard to make. In most cases, GoRemote will guarantee service levels and commit to a full deployment in less than 90 days. Aberdeen sees little risk and a potential multimillion-dollar reward in engaging GoRemote Internet Communications Inc.
