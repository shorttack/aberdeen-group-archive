@SUB HEAD = IBM: Two-Pronged Attack
Competitors who thought IBM would not contend for price-performance leadership guessed wrong. IBM has dropped its long-used proprietary RAMP-C benchmark in favor of TPC benchmarks for its midrange products.

Big Blue has led the best TPC-A price-performance list with specific models several times and is always near the top. IBM is doing this with its AS/400 and RS/6000 product lines.

Figure 6 shows three models in the AS/400 E-series, announced in February 1992. The AS/400 price-performance curve is not flat; costs per transaction increase from the low-end E10 to the largest single-processor E70. However, the E70 has twice the throughput performance of the predecessor B70 at a 45 percent lower K$/TPS-A. And at under $100,000 (9.92 TPS-A times $9.95 K$/TPS-A) in five-year costs, the entry-level E10 shows attractive price-performance for low transaction-volume, distributed, replicated applications, IBM's target market.

Figure 7 illustrates RS/6000 model 550 improvements. The recent performance boost is attributable to internal improvements in version 3.2 of AIX, and indicates that the AIX operating system is maturing.

While model 550 performance jumps 59 percent from 32 to 51 TPS-A with the AIX 3.2, price-performance is only 12.4 percent better. This indicates that the RS/6000 memory and disk upgrades needed to support the greater performance potential of AIX 3.2 are relatively expensive.

A strong price-performer in 1991, the RS/6000 product line has fallen behind in price-performance in early 1992. Even with IBM's January 1992 new product introductions, the RS/6000 has failed to come close to breaking the K$10/TPS-A level.

@SUB HEAD = Where Is the System/390?
Missing from IBM's TPC lineup is its System/390 mainframe. IBM says that TPC-A is an inappropriate measure of the System/390's performance potential. Aberdeen believes that IBM has run the TPC-A benchmark on all its machines, but has chosen not to announce the results.

We have no doubts that the S/390 could obtain prodigious performance numbers using TPF or IMS/FastPath. The question buyers are asking is, "What's the S/390 price-performance running DB2?"
