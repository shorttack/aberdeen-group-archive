Desktop Platform Analyst Relations
Intel Corporation
JF3-161
2111 NE 25th Avenue
Hillsboro, OR     97124

Dear Michael,
Attached is a discussion draft of our proposal regarding consumer LaGrande
Technology market research.  As you may recall, we discussed the concepts
with Narendar Sahgal at IDF in September, and you requested this response.

Aberdeen Group may be all wet, but we believe with some passion that Intel
has a measurable opportunity to expand the current plans for LT to actively
include the consumer segment.  Moreover, we have concluded that LT is only
part of a secure and private platform decision that consumers will make.
Thus, we think the ecosystem needs careful examination, not just the
capabilities of LT.  Our early estimates are that the opportunity
represents north of $150 million in annual revenue to Intel.

As former LT Platform Marketing Manager, you are all too familiar with LT
planning.  Thus, I would ask you to distribute this proposal to the Intel
influencers and decision makers whom you believer are appropriate at this
stage.  As a next step, Aberdeen security practice VP Jim Hurley and I
would like to meet face-to-face with Intel in a week or two to discuss both
our points of view and our proposed approach to gaining the market
intelligence needed to make a sound business decision about consumer LT.
The basic agenda would be to either kill the idea at Intel or scope the
effort so that Aberdeen Group could submit an actionable proposal with
costs, schedules, and terms.  Please call me if you have another going-
forward plan to suggest.

Best regards,

Peter

Peter S. Kastner
Executive Vice President, Research

### Abstract

Letter and attached proposal from Peter S. Kastner (Aberdeen Group EVP Research) to Michael Ferron-Jones (Intel Desktop Platform Analyst Relations) dated October 5, 2003. Proposes a multi-phase market research program to evaluate consumer LaGrande Technology (LT) — Intel's hardware-based trusted computing platform. Aberdeen argues Intel is under-valuing the consumer LT opportunity, estimating >$150M annual revenue opportunity and 10M+ consumer units in 2006 at $100 retail uplift. Proposes 3-phase research (consumer qualitative, quantitative tracking, localized surveys) in US, Canada, Japan, Germany, UK, France, Italy, Brazil, Mexico. Research focuses on identity theft fear, privacy, trusted computing adoption drivers and barriers.
