It was good to talk with you last week regarding Intel's interest in the Decision Support marketplace.

As we discussed, a wide variety of analytical technologies - such as data visualization, data mining, online analytical processing (OLAP) - are creating increased demand for CPU power. However, where that CPU power should be located - at the server, at the workstation, or at the desktop - varies from tool to tool and industry to industry. By matching these decision support requirements to Intel's offerings, Intel will be able to capitalize on this market's implicit demand for CPU power.

Aberdeen is pleased to submit the following research project proposal.

Guy Creese
Senior Analyst, Decision Support Research
cc: Nancy Hill; James Gruener

PART A - MARKET CHARACTERISTICS STUDY
In order that Intel Corporation gain significant insight into how workstations are being used in the decision support market, Aberdeen proposes to take a census of which decision support applications are running on workstations. Aberdeen will conduct a telephone interview project, consisting of a total of 130 executive interviews in North America and Europe combined.

Key topics to be addressed include:
- What decision support applications (data mining, visualization, GIS, OLAP) currently run on workstations?
- What decision support applications will need to run on workstations based on future business requirements?
- Which vertical industries tend to use workstations in decision support applications?
- What other vendors offer workstations tuned for the decision support market?
- What are the key performance measures considered when evaluating a workstation for decision support?
- How is the decision support workload apportioned between desktops, workstations, and servers?
- Which hardware characteristics are most important in handling decision support tasks (CPU, memory or I/O)?
- What are the existing spending levels and numbers of users, as well as projected growth rates?

The 130 interviews will consist of:
- 60 North American companies: 30 ISV executive interviews, 30 end-user interviews
- 60 European companies: 30 ISV executive interviews, 30 end-user interviews
- First 10 interviews by Aberdeen analysts (20 minutes each); remaining 120 by third party (10 minutes each)

Cost and Duration for Part A
The work will be carried during Q3 1999 - completed within 45 days of receiving list of prospects.
Aberdeen's fee for this service is $50,000. Expenses for travel, communications, and report production will be invoiced at cost, in addition to the professional service fee.
Half of fee invoiced at start; one-quarter at completion of interview task 3; other quarter at completion of project.

PART B - MARKET POSITIONING STUDY
In order that Intel Corporation gains significant insight into how decision-makers perceive Intel's position in the decision support market, Aberdeen proposes to evaluate Intel's credibility in the decision support market and identify initiatives Intel could launch to increase the total size of the decision support market.

Aberdeen will conduct a RAMP (Rapid Accurate Market Positioning) project, consisting of 14 executive interviews in North America and Europe combined.

Key topics to be addressed include:
- Perception of Intel's high-end workstation microprocessors and corporate brand positioning.
- Position specifically of Intel's IA-64 architecture.
- Best market positioning for upcoming products with existing customers.
- Perception and value of high-performance chips in the high-end market.
- Key performance measures considered when evaluating an Intel-based workstation.
- Performance boost triggering a new system purchase.
- Technical and strategic hurdles/dilemmas facing ISVs in their current stages of IA-64 planning and development.

The 14 interviews will consist of:
- 10 North American companies: 5 ISV executive interviews, 5 end-user interviews
- 4 European companies: 2 ISV executive interviews, 2 end-user interviews

Face-to-face, on-site interviews lasting approximately 1 to 1.5 hours each.

Cost and Duration for Part B
The work will be carried during Q3 1999 - completed within 60 days of receiving list of prospects.
Aberdeen's standard fee: $3,000 per interview and $9,000 for three days of RAMP analysis. Total cost: $51,000 ($3,000 x 14 interviews + $9,000 analysis).
Expenses for travel, communications, and report production invoiced at cost.
Half of fee at start; one-quarter at completion of interview Task 4; other quarter at project completion.

OPTIONS
The number of face-to-face interviews can be amended. Aberdeen recommends no less than 8 interviews in North America and no less than 4 in Europe.

Staff Responsibility
Guy Creese, Senior Analyst, Decision Support Research, will be in supervisory charge. Joining him will be James Gruener, Managing Director of Intel Platforms Research.

This proposal is subject to acceptance within 30 days. We are prepared to initiate effort on or about July 1, 1999.

Guy Creese
Senior Analyst, Decision Support Research

Accepted for Intel (initial one):

- Both studies
