# Report of Peter S. Kastner: Intelligent Electronics Inc. v. Andersen Consulting LLP

**Study ID:** `kastner-ie-v-andersen-expert-report-3de98a`

**Date:** 1999-06-21
**Type:** Expert Report
**Domain:** IT Litigation

## Overview

This is a 124-page expert witness report prepared by Peter S. Kastner, Executive Vice President and Chief Research Officer of Aberdeen Group, for the case of Intelligent Electronics, Inc. v. Andersen Consulting LLP (Court of Common Pleas, Chester County, Pennsylvania, No. 96-04673).

The report analyzes the failed IE/2000 IT transformation project at Intelligent Electronics, a $3 billion PC distributor that went bankrupt in 1996-97. Kastner's central finding is that IE's failure was primarily strategic (pursuing a reseller model in a consolidating distributor market) rather than a result of Andersen Consulting's performance, which he deemed competent.

## Key Findings

1. **Speed imperative**: IE needed IT transformation within 12-18 months but the timeline was unrealistic
2. **Strategic misalignment**: IE pursued a value-added reseller model while the industry consolidated toward low-cost distribution
3. **IT competency gap**: IE lacked internal capability to manage a transformation of IE/2000's scope
4. **Andersen performed competently**: Given IE's constraints and changing requirements
5. **IE owned the project**: All key decisions on scope, timing, and technology were IE's
6. **Industry consolidation inevitable**: PC distribution was consolidating from 10+ players to 2-3 survivors
