--- Slide 14 ---
Decommissioning
A 3-year old Pentium III business desktop is worth about $80 when sold in Q1 2003 for recycling
Reality is, in a few months, IT will have to pay $50 to have the PC properly disposed
Conclusion: last year of a PC’s life is more expensive than generally considered when decommissioning & failure costs are considered.  
Source: Aberdeen Group
[NOTES] The opportunity to sell an IT asset in the used equipment market evaporates when the value of the asset drops below the cost of disposal.  To avoid a hard cash expense, the asset must be sold at a cost that is at least equal to the disposal cost—but Aberdeen research indicates that this moment comes much faster than IT managers or common financial depreciation tables predict. 
Consider the street value of a typical corporate desktop that is three+ years old, a Pentium III 733 megahertz PC plotted over time.  Bought in March 2000, this desktop was worth $80 in recoverable street value in March 2003—a compounded depreciation rate of 8.6% a month.  The cost to properly recycle this PC is about $50 plus transportation because it is a looming liability to dump old PCs in a landfill. 
Most companies significantly overestimate the street value of their IT assets, and therefore miss the opportunity to sell the asset while it retains value – a rapidly closing window.  There is a glut of 3-to-4 year old machines hitting ending life which will rapidly drive down street prices into negative territory.
