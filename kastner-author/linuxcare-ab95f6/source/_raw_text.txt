Linuxcare, Inc. -- Enterprise Service and Support for All Things Linux

Aberdeen Profile (November 1999)

Preface
IT management already has contracts in place to address the myriad of daily issues for supporting Unix and Windows NT. For Linux, the same "who are you going to call" question must be answered. IT management needs 24x7 global support for installation, upgrades, system development, and consulting. Aberdeen finds many companies choose selective outsourcing for best-of-breed expertise.

Executive Summary
Linuxcare was founded in 1998 as a Linux consulting company. As word of expertise spread, major OEMs sought Linuxcare for training and support. Kleiner Perkins Caulfield and Byers chose Linuxcare as its first Linux-related investment. Dell, IBM, and Sun have contracted with Linuxcare for training and back-end Linux customer support.

Aberdeen finds Linuxcare has assembled a strong product and services portfolio led by experienced management. Challenges: growing headcount and placing QA metrics in place.

Linux Market Growth Drives Demand for Linux Services
IT departments increasingly using Linux for email, file/print, Web servers. Linux increasingly used for decision support, caching, storage, DNS, Web commerce. ISVs have announced ports of major enterprise applications to Linux. Within the next year, IT managers will have a Linux-based alternative in every major application area.

Linuxcare Services: Four Centers of Excellence
1. Full range of Linux-oriented support services: 24x7 call center, on-site support, Knowledgebase (free online). All services can be private-branded (e.g., www.dell.linuxcare.com).
2. Training/education via Linuxcare University ("train the trainer"). Courses: Linux System Administration, Network Administration, Security, Linux Overview for NT Professionals, Extreme Samba, Linux for Executives.
3. Porting and migration assistance via Linuxcare Labs -- distribution-neutral.
4. Benchmarking and certification of Linux hardware and software; Dell and Compaq DeskPro already certified.

Key Industry Contracts
- Dell: back-end Linux support for OptiPlex, Dimension, Precision workstations, PowerEdge Servers
- IBM: Linux support on RS/6000 line
- Sun: support for Star Office suite (acquired from Star Division)
- TurboLinux: services and support for North American customers

Aberdeen Conclusions
Aberdeen is convinced by extensive market research that a credible enterprise Linux services market exists. Linuxcare has proven its viability as a source of Linux expertise. Should be considered by IT technical and business executives wishing to utilize Linux as an alternative enterprise OS.
