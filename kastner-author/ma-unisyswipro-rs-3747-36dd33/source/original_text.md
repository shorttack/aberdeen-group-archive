Can Unisys-Wipro Alliance Cover All Outsourcing Bases? 
Market Event 
 
Unisys Corp. and Wipro Technologies have linked up in an alliance that can service the IT 
outsourcing customer both on-site and offshore. Recent Aberdeen research shows that the typical 
outsourcing buyer can gain advantages on two fronts, rather than on one at the expense of another: 
the cost savings of offshore work coupled and the added customer care of working with someone 
close by from the outsourcing provider’s team. This partnership between two key players in the IT 
outsourcing arena has the potential to do just that and possibly more. 
 
Aberdeen Analysis 
 
Cheaper labor and high quality have combined to form the foundation of India’s rise as an 
information technology powerhouse over the last decade. So, little wonder that respondents to 
Aberdeen’s recent benchmark survey on Outsourcing Application Development and 
Maintenance whose companies outsource work to that country cited cost savings as the chief 
driver behind their application outsourcing strategies.

Market Alert 
Customers of both vendors cited cost savings and the freedom for internal IT to focus on more 
strategic work as key benefits in outsourcing ADM work. However, Wipro may bring an added 
benefit to the table: Half of its customers that were represented in the survey cited improved 
version control and lifecycle management as a benefit in outsourcing application work. 
Anything that can simplify IT operations can pay off in indirect savings and efficiency. 
Further, most Unisys customers said a key response to challenges in outsourcing relationships is 
implementing new or revised vendor management processes. By comparison, most Wipro 
clients did not cite process changes as a response to challenges. 
Most companies in the Outsourcing Application Development and Maintenance survey said 
vendor management costs and effort were higher than they had expected, highlighting the special 
operational challenges that working with third-party providers in the delivery of critical business 
functions can pose. Further, a majority of survey respondents for a previous report, The Business 
Value of IT Outsourcing, indicated they dedicated more management time and resources to help 
overcome initial challenges in their outsourcing relationships (Figure 1), while 30% tinkered 
with vendor management processes.

Aberdeen Conclusions and Recommendations 
The Wipro-Unisys alliance combines cost savings with tighter customer focus. As Wipro’s 
subcontractor, Unisys will provide Wipro clients with local support services, including desktop 
and server maintenance, desk-side support; equipment installs, moves, adds and changes 
(IMACs); and tape exchanges at data centers. Wipro, meanwhile, will handle remote services, 
including remote server management and service desk/help desk support. 
The alliance has already won a major deal with a U.S.-based Fortune 500 company, according to 
a statement from Unisys: It will be a subcontractor to Wipro in providing managed services, 
while both companies will support more than 20,000 of the company’s employees in the U.S., 
Canada, and the U.K.

An organization looking to outsource an 
IT function should first be sure what it 
wants to accomplish in sending the work 
to a third-party provider. If cost savings 
and 
operational 
efficiency 
are 
as 
important as a good working relationship, 
it may want to consider how such an 
alliance between two major players in the

those benefits. 
Above all, an IT organization with at 
least a year of experience in outsourcing 
should be able to determine what it needs to manage the relationship. It would be best to hold 
accountable one person who must know what the provider needs to deliver, how it’s performing 
its duties, and to be aware of any end-user complaints. In addition, this person should have strong 
relationship management skills.

### Abstract

Aberdeen market alert analyzing the Unisys-Wipro IT outsourcing alliance announced December 2006.
