## Description

A scanned tearsheet of the **InformationWEEK** "Quote of the Week" feature
from page 9 of the **27 February 1989** issue, citing Peter S. Kastner with
the now-classic line:

> ***"Deep down in their hearts everyone hates Unix."***
>
> — *Peter S. Kastner, a vice president and consultant at Aberdeen Group*

The original artifact carries a handwritten annotation reading
**"is this you?"** — the kind of office passalong note that confirms how
quickly the line circulated.

## Pictured / quoted

- **Peter S. Kastner** — at the time, Vice President and consultant at
  Aberdeen Group (only weeks after his December 1988 appointment to lead
  Aberdeen Transaction Services — see
  `1988-12-aberdeen-psk-photo-biz-card.md`).

## Context (cross-refs to corpus)

- Demonstrates that within ~10 weeks of joining Aberdeen as a VP,
  Kastner had become a quotable industry voice in the leading enterprise
  trade press.
- Sets up the through-line for Aberdeen's later Unix-vs.-NT-vs.-mainframe
  positioning across the 1990s research output (Open OLTP work, NT-as-OLTP
  work, RDBMS market commentary, etc.).
- The quote itself remains a frequently-cited industry-history one-liner;
  this scan is a primary-source attestation of its origin in
  InformationWEEK 27-Feb-1989 p.9.
