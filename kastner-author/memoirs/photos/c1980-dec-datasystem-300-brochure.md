**License:** CC-BY-4.0

## Description

A 14-page Digital Equipment Corporation marketing brochure for the
**DEC Datasystem 300 series** (Datasystem 322 / 324 / 354 / 357), a family
of small-business / departmental commercial computer systems running CTS-300
and DIBOL on PDP-11-class hardware. The brochure features full-color
photographs across vertical-market scenarios — Retail, Distribution,
Transportation, Banking, Insurance, Manufacturing, Service — along with
hardware shots of the Datasystem cabinets, video terminals, and removable-disk
drives. The closing pages show DEC marketing/sales meetings and a
representative customer-engagement photograph with a young DEC sales
representative reviewing materials with a senior client.

This brochure was kept by Peter S. Kastner from his **pre-Aberdeen DEC
career years** and is preserved here as a personal-archive artifact, not as a
piece of primary research output.

## Significance to PSK / Aberdeen narrative

Kastner's pre-Aberdeen background includes deep familiarity with DEC's
commercial-computing portfolio, which directly shaped his Aberdeen-era
analyst voice on:

- **Open OLTP / departmental computing** (Aberdeen 1991–1992 *Open OLTP for
  Enterprise Managers* white paper, multiple batches in this corpus)
- **Client-server transition narratives** (Aberdeen mid-1990s research)
- **Database platform evolution** (RDBMS sales training decks; see
  `PSK-misc-speech-agendas-7.pdf` IBM Power Academy 1996 entry)

The Datasystem 300 family represents the *prior generation* of departmental
business computing that Aberdeen's later Open-OLTP and client-server theses
explicitly displaced.
