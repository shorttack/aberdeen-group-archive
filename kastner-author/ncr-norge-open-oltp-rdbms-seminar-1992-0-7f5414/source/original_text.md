What a manager needs to know about transaction processing in an open environment
Traditional On Line Transaction Processing (OLTP) technology was characterized by a central computer - often a mainframe, unintelligent terminals, and a proprietary transaction processing software development and runtime environment. Open OLTP is a computer mechanism that changes the state of ±e enterprise in real-time while using industry standards which support interoperability across a mixture of computing systems. Aberdeen Group believes that Open OLTP is now a mature enough technology that it should be seriously considered by enterprise management. The business issues that support this conclusion include the advantages of downsizing, lower cost of ownership, and distributed computing.
The presentation examines why OLTP is a powerful technology tool for business. The migration from traditional OLTP to OPEN OLTP, and the benefits it can provide. What buyers should look for in evaluating suppliers and products is also discussed.
Lecturer: Peter S. Kastner, Vice President, Aberdeen Group, Inc. Pause
Synergy effects between transaction processing and relational databases in an open environment
The advent of multiple-vendor, multiple-machine, multiple-operating system environment opened the floodgate of choice for users of computer systems. Users were free to develop their applications on hardware provided by one vendor, while keeping their options to switch to hardware provided by another vendor. Availability of such technology caused a shift to Client/Server computing with multiple server hardware and operating system platforms. This approach, while permitting downsizing, introduced problems in the areas of administration, maintenance, and redundant data.
Oracle Corporation believes that doing business in the 1990s requires a re-examination of this philosophy. The trend will be towards Open OLTP systems as the backbone of critical business functions. This will require large massively parallel open hardware as well as an industry compliant, open relation database management system. In order to bring critical enterprise data on-line, the hardware end RDMS must provide high system availability, cost effectiveness, data integrity, performance scalability, and interoperability with other systems.
The presentation describes how an organization can take advantage of Open OLTP and Open relation database technologies to enhance their competitive position through the 1990's and into the next century.
Lecturer: Sven Aamodt, Technical Director, Oracle Norge AS

The future success factor of transaction processing under UNIX System V
In the 1990s, business leaders are facing unprecedented challenges faced with shortened product and service life cycles and intensified global competition, toda/s managers must have instant access to information in order to successfully compete. It is no longer possible, or affordable, to re-invent information processing tools as user needs grow or expand. The definition and adoption of IT architectural standards is mandatory to release the goals of minimizing training and retraining concentrating on evolving business applications to provide real-world competitive advantages and maximizing information compatibility with other intemal and extemal organization through interoperable IT systems. Under the umbrella of the UNIX International AT LAS architecture the Corporate Hub Environment defines the computing environment that provides business critical commercial processing capabilities centered on transaction processing under UNIX System V. Characteristically this environment will include large data bases, high availability, fast and predictable response times, a large user base and a reflection of business priorities.
The presentation describes the scope of these characteristics, the technology enablers, and the products available now which provide the benefits of open, and interoperable systems.
