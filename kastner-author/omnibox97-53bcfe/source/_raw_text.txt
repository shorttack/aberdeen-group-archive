1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
OmniBox International (the "Company") is a Delaware holding company which retains the exclusive world-wide rights to patents and technology for electronic ordering/payment, digital compression, and interactive broadcasting. Seeking $10 million in funding for global roll-out.

Timeline:
1987-1995: OmniBox's founder develops and proves the technology.
1995-1997: Market validated; signed contracts and letters of intent.
1997-1999: With customer base, management team and proven technology, OmniBox turns on its network and revenue stream.

Industry
Frost & Sullivan 1996 report: market for interactive TV and advertising to reach $1 billion by 2002. Intel: 26 million US homes had both PC and cable TV in 1994.

OmniBox Products/Services
- OmniBox Interactive Broadcasting Network
- Satellite JukeBox: digital music album delivery ($8.00/album)
- MailBox: interactive home shopping (12% commission)
- VideoBox: pay-per-view ($3.00/buy)
- OmniBox Digital Set-Top-Box: under $200 base; $50-75 accessories
- DVC Digital Compression: 10 digital channels per analog; 100:1 compression; superior to MPEG-2
- Inferno OS (Lucent Technologies): underlies OmniBox software
- MPEG II DVB-Compliant PC Boards

Company Background
Incorporated Delaware 1987. Stamford, CT. Founded by Thomas A. Bush, CEO. $2M raised since inception. 78 shareholders; Bush controls 70.67%. Total assets: $1,439,032 (1994).

Relationships
- Time/Warner Cable: field test partner, NYC -- verified high-quality digitized video delivery
- Bank One: transaction processing (real-time PPV, two-step purchases, mail-order)
- NMPA: music industry preferred delivery system
- VCOM: compression technology supplier
- Lucent Technologies: Inferno OS; co-marketing agreement
- Group W Satellite Communications: proposed broadcast origination/transmission facilities

Financial Summary

Subscribers: 500K (Y1) -> 1.25M (Y2) -> 2.1M (Y3) -> 3.0M (Y4).
ARS: $403 (Y1) -> $390 (Y4).

Joint Ventures: First JV covers 53 countries (Middle East, Asia, Africa); finalized.
Financing sought: $10 million equity investment.
