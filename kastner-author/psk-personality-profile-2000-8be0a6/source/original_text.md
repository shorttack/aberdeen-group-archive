## Original Document Text

16 PF PERSONALITY ASSESSMENT REPORT
On: Peter Kastner
Date: 17th July 2001

OVERVIEW
Being rather extraverted Peter Kastner tends to be sociable and good at making and maintaining contacts with people. Being a well adjusted individual, he find life generally satisfying and he is apparently able to achieve those things which he considers to be most important. As a decision maker, he probably keeps his feelings under control when coming to a balanced judgement on the action to be taken. Neither fiercely independent in outlook nor strongly conformist, he is happy to get help from people if he cannot manage on his own.

INTERPERSONAL RELATIONSHIPS
With people, Peter Kastner is likely to be fairly good natured and inclined to be cooperative. He prefers to be direct, open and forthright in expressing his views, but may not always be acceptable to others unless he is suitably tactful. Nevertheless he is more likely to be trusted than less outspoken people. Enjoying the company of people in working groups, he often gets warmly involved with them. But being natural and spontaneous, and tending to take life as it comes, he may not be very good at coolly and objectively analysing people's motives. And being a dominant individual, he is apt to be stern and authoritarian. Being prepared to stand up for his views when opposed, he is assertive and to a degree independent-minded. Probably rather talkative and lively, he tends to be an active communicator. Being somewhat adventurous and inclined to be sociable, he rather likes meeting people. He prefers to work and to make decisions with other people, thereby obtaining social approval and group support, although sometimes at the cost of having to conform with their wishes. Generally he likes to take people at their face value, without feeling suspicious about them or being over-trusting.

COPING WITH PRESSURE
Being a resilient person, Peter Kastner possesses a mature, unanxious confidence in his ability to deal with any kind of situation. So if he is talked about by others, he does not care what they say. Most of the time he tends to give the impression of being relaxed, composed and generally satisfied. As a consequence he may seem to others to be somewhat laid-back and lacking in motivation. Although he may not over-react or gets disorganised, his extremely relaxed style could lead to low performance unless he is kept under pressure to achieve results. Being emotionally mature, he appears able to cope well with frustrating circumstances. And since he does not allow his feelings to interfere with seeing things as they are, he can adjusts to the realities of a situation. He tends to lack will-power and self-control.

DECISION MAKING AND PROBLEM SOLVING
Although Peter Kastner attends to most moral standards and rules, he is probably prepared to ignore a regulation when it no longer seems to serve its purpose. It is likely that he will be happier thinking about practical and concrete matters rather than about abstract problems. Often absorbed in his work, he tends to be rather unconventional. He is likely to be fascinated by ideas, theories and beliefs and show concern for essentials. Like many others, he is probably rather interested in new ideas and changes, and occasionally inclined to experiment with the ways in which he does things. Generally unfussy, and rarely demanding attention and assistance from others, he rather likes to manage his own affairs if possible.

TEAM BEHAVIOUR
To be effective as a Resource Investigator Peter Kastner may need to widen his circle of personal contacts even more in order to find out what goes on elsewhere. Fascinated by new ideas and developments, he has the imagination to recognize which of these could be of value to his colleagues, customers, clients or friends. But being emotionally mature and realistic he adjusts to facts and is well equipped to deal with any difficulties which arise, generally performing most effectively when kept under steady pressure.
Peter Kastner possesses the qualities of the Coordinator, the person who has a capacity for controlling the way in which members of a team or group work together in pursuit of common objectives. And since he knows his own mind he is probably prepared to push forward ideas to his superiors in order to gain their support. An imaginative person, he will probably benefit from having a second-in-command who checks that his bright ideas can be made to work in practice. Despite his managerial attributes, however, he may lacks the determination to see things through to completion.

Peter Kastner : End of report
