Sequent Server Product Overview
! Symmetry, WinServer
– Deskside to 30-processor symmetric multiprocessing
– All servers 486/Pentium-based; no RISC
– Low-end WinServer runs Windows NT
– High-availability Sequent Clusters up to 4 systems
– RAID 0, 1, 2
– DYNIX/ptx OS just moved up to UNIX V.4
– 10K packet/sec Ethernet, 240MB/sec VMEbus on new 5000s
– 840GB SCSI-2 disks

Sequent Server Products
! Symmetry ELS, 2000, 5000
– ELS Desktop, Deskside, II Deskside
– 2000 midrange
  » Entry price $85,000 (290), $155,000 (490), $351,000 (790)
  » Benchmarks 2000/250 183.34 tps-A, 2000/750 2-cluster 1002.37 tps-A
– New 5000 high-end
  » Entry price $171,100 (SE20), $469,800 (SE60)
  » Fully configured $1,030,400 SE20, $2,625,700 (SE60)
  » No benchmarks yet for 5000
! WinServer 500, 1000, 1500, 3000, 5000
– Windows NT
– 500 deskside (1-2 CPUs), 1000/1500 up to 50 fulltime users (1-2 CPUs),
  3000 hundreds of users (1-6 CPUs), 5000 high-end (2-30 CPUs)
– Entry prices $13,200 (500), $29,900 (1000), $39,300 (1500), $79,100 (3000), $246,900 (5000)

Sequent Sales Messages
! Open systems (early into high-end Unix)
! Open-system scalability (SMP, clustering, RDBMS parallelism, broad product/performance range)
! Development relationships with Oracle, Informix (parallelism 6-month exclusive on Informix DSA)
! Best-in-class SMP parallelism (up to 30 SMP processors)
! Windows NT on Intel for early adopters

Sequent Strengths/Concerns
! Strengths:
– High-end Unix-system market share
– SMP plus clustering scalability
– Good RDBMS performance
– Entrepreneurial. Good at closing prospects
! Customer Concerns:
– High pricing . . . mediocre price/performance
– No focus on low-end server, workstation
– Fewer support resources/experience
– Under-$500-million size
– Financial losses. Lack of long-term profitability

Sequent Profile
! Annual Sales: $354 million fiscal '93
! Distribution: direct
! In The News
– New service division with large number of consultant hires
– Symmetry 5000

Data General Server Product Overview
! Aviion
– Deskside to 16-processor symmetric multiprocessing
– All servers based on Motorola 88K RISC microprocessors
– High-availability clustering up to 4 systems
– RAID 0, 1, 3, 5, up to 2 TB external storage
– Sold as commercial server, strong in medical market
– Aggressive pricing

Data General Server Products
! Aviion 4500, 5500, 8500, 9500
– Low-end branch-office to 16-CPU SMP
– DG/UX is UNIX SVR4
– 2 TB max. external storage for 9500
– Offers Oracle Parallel Server 2-4-system clustering
– RAID 0, 1, 3, 5; SCSI-2 drives
– CA-UNICENTER marketing/service and support
– Entry price $14,490 (5500), $40,995 (8500), $79,995 (9500)
  » 5500 130.9 tps-A, $5,780 $/tps-A
  » 9500 523.64 tps-A, $5,357 tps-A

Data General Prioritized Sales Messages
! Open systems
! High availability (RAID for disks, tape drives)
! Price-performance
! Alternate choice for MIS shops wanting to downsize the data center

Data General Strengths/Concerns
! Strengths:
– Technology: early into SMP, RAID; extended security; NetWare support; LAN/WAN networking
  (Token Ring, Netbios, NetWare, SNA, DECnet, LAN Manager)
– Aggressive pricing/price-performance
! Customer Concerns:
– DG financial problems
– Future of chip choice (Motorola 88K chipset end of life)

Data General Profile
! Annual Sales: $1 billion fiscal '93
! Strong in medical market via VARs
! Focus on government, health-care, finance, retail, and telecomm. markets
! 50/50 international/US sales; strictly commercial server (no technical/workstation sales)
! Distribution: 50/50 direct/VAR
! In The News – loss in 1Q94
