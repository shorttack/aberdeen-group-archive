Tandem versus Stratus
price/performance
When comparing the price/performance of a
Tandem system to a Stratus system, the
measurement should be based on the cost per
transactions per second (tps). The ET1
benchmark, which is called "TP1" by
Stratus, is a well-documented benchmark
(see "A Measure of Transaction Processing
Power,** Datamation, April 1, 1985). Both
Tandem and Stratus have released their
benchmark results to the public. Therefore,
without running an actual benchmark of a
customer’s application, the "ET1/TP1"
benchmark is the most valid comparison of
the price/performance of either vendor’s
system.
The "ET1/TP1" numbers for both vendors
are as follows:

Using the ET1 tps figures, the cost/tps is
listed below for equivalently configured
Tandem and Stratus systems (only hardware
and software costs were included in figuring
the cost/tps). The pricing information and the
makeup of a "typically" configured Stratus
system are taken from a May 1985 report
published by the Management Information
Corporation (MIC) and a December 1984
report by Data Decisions.
A typical XA600 configuration consists of:
• Six duplexed processors
• 24 MB of duplexed memory
• 1.8 GB of disc storage
• Communications subsystem
• Six terminals
• Operating system
Figure 1 shows the total cost of the sys­
tems and the cost/tps. The Tandem system
costs 50% less than Stratus in the cost/tps.
Though hardware and software maintenance
costs are not part of the cost/tps figures, they
are given for comparison purposes. Stratus
offers a "participatory" maintenance option,
which is represented by the second figure in
the maintenance column.
Figures 2 and 3 show the cost of the
systems at a specified ET1 transaction rate.
In order to achieve 10 tps (granting Stratus
linearity), Stratus must add another process­
ing module at the approximate cost of
$280,000.
Figure 4 shows the cost/tps and the dif­
ference at various ET1 transaction rates.
Figure 5 compares the cost/tps between
an XA400 and a comparably configured
NonStop II system. A typical XA400 config­
uration consists of:
• Four duplexed processors
• 8'MB of duplexed memory
• 896 MB of disc storage
• Communications subsystem
• Six terminals
• Operating system
An FT250 and a comparably configured
NonStop EXT are compared in Figure 6.
Maintenance costs were not included because
maintenance figures for the FT250 are
unavailable at this time. According to MIC a
typically configured FT250 consists of:
• Two duplexed processors
• 4 MB of duplexed memory
• 286 MB of disc storage
• Communications subsystem
• Six terminals
• Operating system
Figure 7 compares a large EXT system to
the XA400 MIC configuration. The EXT
configuration is:
• Four NonStop II processors
• 8 MB of memory
• 1.5 GB of disc storage
