## Original Document Text

﻿nVidia GeForce FX

Brian Burke
Todd Reddick

Vary modular: mobile, low cost $99

March 6th.  NV31 and NV34.    Ship in April.  Full DirectX 9 support.
NV35 high end future.  May 11 NDA
    • .13 m  256 bit memory.  DDR2 and DDR1
    • 2nd generation compression and caching
    • 2nd generation pixel shader
    • AGP 8x
    • Clocks no less than NV30  -- 20GB/second  = 2x MV30

NV31 replaces 4200 midrange
    • 2x Ti4200
    • 4 pipe engine
    • .13 micron TSMC
    • lossless compression continues
    • nView: TMDA 16x12” panels
    • Full MPEG2 in hardware
    • AGP 8x
    • DDR1 
    • 1.5x ATI 9500
    • Prices $179 128MB pretty aggressive
    • No OEMs to announce yet – PR later
    • Production silicon with rev A01



NV34 replaces  GeForce Ti 4200 low end and MX
    • Full cinematix.  Full 128 bit, vertex and pixel shaders
    • 2x GeForce 4 MX 440 64MB
    • Retains dual ramdacs, 350MHz 2048x1356
    • MPEG decoder
    • DDR1 .15 m TSMC  process.  Clock not yet locked down
    • 128MB RAM standard
    • Lacks compression and other high end features
    • $99 price point


NV30 ships end Feb.  More than a 4600 at $399 = 5800
Power, precision, programmability
Vertex and pixel shaders across the line as is 128 bit precision
Get hardware pixel shaders and vertex into mainstream -> Dawn and Doom III
