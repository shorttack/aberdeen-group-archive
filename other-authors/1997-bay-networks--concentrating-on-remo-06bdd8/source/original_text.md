Abstract: Announcement Proﬁle
Bay Networks
Bay Networks: Concentrating on Remote Access
Earlier this year, Bay Networks unveiled two signiﬁcant
additions to broaden its remote access portfolio. The 5399
Remote Access Concentrator is designed for users requiring
high density access, such as service providers, telcos, carriers
and enterprise networks. It is modular in form and installs in
Bay's 5000 Multi-Service Access Switch (MSX). The Model
8000 Remote Access Concentrator is a stand-alone box
targeted at smaller ISPs and enterprise users. Both offerings
feature BayDSP™ modem technology, the fruit of Bay's
acquisition of Penril.
In this Announcement Proﬁle, Aberdeen highlights the
requirements and challenges of next-generation remote access,
examines the issues surrounding the newly-emerged 56K
modem technologies and shows how, with its high-end remote
access solutions, Bay Networks is meeting the needs of the
high-density remote access market, both through innovative
technology and support.
Virginia Brooks

### Abstract

This Aberdeen Announcement Profile examines Bay Networks' two new remote access concentrator products: the 5399 (high-density, modular, for service providers and large enterprises) and the Model 8000 (stand-alone, for smaller ISPs and enterprises). Both feature BayDSP modem technology from Bay's acquisition of Penril Datability Networks. Aberdeen evaluates the emerging 56K modem market and how Bay's solutions address high-density remote access requirements.
