Telecommunications companies, world-class ﬁnancial services ﬁrms, and Web

The BroadVision One-to-One dynamic Web application platform provides a
means of matching people with digital content, any digital content. In this
Product Viewpoint, Aberdeen deﬁnes dynamic Web application platforms and

corporate relationship management — relationships with employees, business
partners, suppliers, and customers — while simultaneously satisfying
demanding performance criteria.
Executive Summary
BroadVision's One-to-One Application System is a platform for creating and
managing complex Web-based applications in critical operational
environments. Unlike other platforms, BroadVision offers end-to-end
application solutions and domain expertise for Self-service customer care,
Knowledge Management, Commerce (Internet Sales), and Financial Services.
Ultimately the platform and speciﬁc Web applications will help businesses
compete for a greater share of customer loyalty and improve business processes
internally and externally.
The One-to-One Application System uses multiple technologies to tailor digital
content to individual end-users with far greater ﬂexibility than simple product
cross-selling and up-selling. By better matching content to people, One-to-One
can better manage human / machine interaction via the Web. When applied
across Web sites for consumers (Internet), employees (Intranets) or suppliers
and partners (extranets), the One-to-One system can show a rapid return on
investment because of a combination of cost savings, incremental revenue and
improved business processes.
Custom interaction is not technically simple. BroadVision designed One-to-
One to realistically match the skills of the people who will use it, to perform
acceptably to end-users and consumers, and to allay MIS skepticism by scaling
to a mass-transaction environment.
What is a Dynamic Web Application?
Dynamic Web application platforms are an emerging class of software that
combine Internet technologies, database, middleware, messaging, and
transaction processing to create sophisticated resource-leveraging corporate
Web sites.
At the most basic level, database-driven Web sites use proprietary query
languages and HTML templates to format database content for the Web.
Database-driven (dynamic) Web pages become catalog servers by adding a
shopping cart to aggregate orders, a Cybercash or Verifone module for
payment, and application modules to handle simple tax and shipping.
There is, however, an enormous difference between a basic catalog server and a
full blown industrial-strength dynamic Web application. Database-driven
commerce application servers can be divided into the following three general
categories:
Low-end Dynamic Servers use common gateway interface (CGI) scripts to
gain database access. They do not scale or perform well with a large
number of users, heavy transaction volumes, or large databases and they
offer little ﬂexibility or integration with existing systems.
Mid-range Dynamic Servers offer improved database access, generally
with ODBC or native drivers and an SQL-like query language. Midrange
servers also let users write some basic business rules to cross-sell and up-
sell products.

High-end Dynamic Servers maximize performance, scalability, and
systems integration with a middleware-like object-oriented foundation.
Object-oriented technology facilitates existing business system integration
and, when combined with TP-monitor technology, effectively manages
intense Internet loads. A notable exception to this category are high-end
products that scale by separating all transaction processing from catalog
presentation.
Progressing Beyond Catalog-based Electronic
Commerce
Fortune-class companies hope to use the Internet for more than catalogs. They
aim to improve the productivity of their relationships with their employees,
customers, suppliers, and business partners and they do not want to develop
several Internet-based systems in parallel.
Several catalog servers at the low-end and mid-range meet the catalog-speciﬁc
requirements of smaller companies but fail to meet the production criteria for
large intranet and extranet applications. These criteria include scalability,
performance, extensibility, and systems integration.
Today, the vast majority of large commercial sites are built in-house. The
challenge for third party suppliers of high-end Dynamic Web Applications is to
add value to what is otherwise a potpourri of software technology and
applications — many of which are commodities.
BroadVision does so on three fronts. First, the One-to-One environment ties
several software technologies into a single secure and well oiled system that
meets the demands of the most intense processing environments. Second, it
provides the management and development tools to realistically match the
skills of the people who use them. And third, One-to-One is the industry-
leading system for managing business relationships with personalized,
dynamic, content and applications.
Relationship Management: Avoiding A Technology
Morass
The BroadVision system is valuable because of its unique ability to combine
complex user data and multiple technologies in order to better manage human
interaction with computer systems. There is no easy way to ensure that people
get the digital content they need, and only the digital content they need.
Personalized digital content includes strategically placed ad banners and
targeted promotions, customer service for consumers, research and business
applications for employees, a unique interface and pricing for suppliers and
partners, and advice for all.
Independent Software Vendors (ISVs) recently introduced "push" technologies
and promised effortless information aggregation. In reality, most of these
systems contain client software that automates user requests (pull). True push
technologies are essentially repackaged and specialized email systems which
are frequently used to automate manufacturing and procurement processes.
Automated pull Internet Broadcasting Services (IBS), such as Pointcast,
logically categorize information into "push" channels to add some degree of
custom interaction. But just as new highways are jammed with trafﬁc, channels
are soon crammed with unmanageable content. Menu-based "push" merely lets
users choose with which data they will be swamped. To avoid meltdown, end-
users unsubscribe from entire categories of information although some of it is
relevant. Automated pull effectively delivers the e-news and software upgrades
but becomes more and more cumbersome when customized for individual
needs because the number of categories that have to be managed approaches
inﬁnity.
Whether pushed, pulled, or automatically pulled, end-users still need to weed
through gobs of information to ﬁnd relevant data nuggets. Therefore, push and
pull technologies alone merely shift where information resides and do not
signiﬁcantly improve worker productivity or personalize end-user interaction.

In order to achieve ﬁne-granularity personalized interaction, a mix of people
and application software must make qualitative judgments about digital content
and match that content with speciﬁc user behavior, preferences, categories /
demographics, etc.
BroadVision's One-to-One application platform integrates multiple digital
delivery technologies and provides the tools and interfaces necessary for
developers, content managers, and businesspeople to manage human
relationships via the Web. And while none of today's high-end dynamic Web
application platforms are strictly point-and-click environments, One-to-One
simpliﬁes custom interaction by distributing the workload to:
Content Managers: Content managers index, categorize and update site
content with the Content Management Center. Human Resources or
departmental managers can assign qualitative value to content in order to
avoid information overload or irrelevancy for employees, suppliers,
partners, or customers. Business rules determine how digital information
is promulgated throughout the organization and may restrict access to
sensitive documents or narrowcast to individuals.
Business managers: BroadVision's Dynamic Command Center allows
non-technical business managers to design business rules, promotional
campaigns, and workﬂow with a simple visual interface. These business
rules trigger personalized content by establishing conditions that link
content categories with user proﬁle attributes. The result is a customized
experience for each user session.
Developers build and modify One-to-One applications with the Visual
Development Center, a visual interface that exposes One-to-One object
classes. Note: One-to-One developers, and only the developers, should
have some object-oriented programming training and experience.
End users: One-to-One integrates conventional self-service menu systems,
text searches, and feature (attribute) searches.
When practicable, The One-to-One Application System also automates
interaction by employing:
Agents: One-to-One agents are specialized matching applications that link
rich user proﬁle data with speciﬁc content types. For example, given a
high level of risk tolerance, an agent will recommend a high-growth but
volatile security.
Feedback and Learning: The One-to-One system can also learn from
users' online behavior and tailor interaction accordingly. For example, an
individual user who always checks their 401K balance ﬁrst will see their
balance displayed ﬁrst upon subsequent visits.
Other users: In some niche applications, collaborative user feedback helps
end-users make decisions. BroadVision partners supply this collaborative
ﬁltering software.
Own The Customer!
One long-term goal of custom interaction is to own as much customer share as
possible. The online battle for customer share is already apparent in ﬁnancial
services and travel services. Aberdeen expects the trend to expand to CDs,
books, and computer hardware and software.
In many industries, the cost of securing a new customer is ten times as great as
selling to an existing customer. The practice of cross-selling and up-selling to
existing customers will rise proportionally to the availability of detailed
customer information. For example, over the last decade, Merrill Lynch

garnered signiﬁcant bank customer share by offering a single cash management
account (CMA) to customers for all of their banking, brokerage and retirement
accounts.
But CMA accounts are only the beginning. Afﬁnity marketing programs, smart
cards, data mining, and direct marketing will all yield tremendous data about
consumers. This impending customer information explosion will require a new
breed of applications that can turn customer information into customer share.
Considering the advent of smart cards, BroadVision's One-to-One application
system is ideally positioned to exploit these trends. Smart cards are essentially
a computer that is the size of a credit card. These chip-based cards will allow
ﬁnancial institutions and other retail industries to breach the chasm between
Internet-based storefronts and brick-and-mortar stores.
Consumer purchasing history and identity can be stored on the card and will
enable companies to efﬁciently and conveniently cross-pollinate promotional
campaigns and target advertisements with laser accuracy. For example, by
leveraging customer information, Barnes & Nobles will be able to offer their
physical store consumers discounts at their Web site and vice versa. New,
complex afﬁnity marketing programs could grant conventional brick-and-
mortar companies tremendous leverage over Internet startups such as
Amazon.com that have no physical store real estate investments.

they choose for conducting business.

Short-term One-to-One ROI
Seasoned chief ﬁnancial ofﬁcers want to know exactly how and when large-
scale Web site development projects will show a return on investment (ROI). In
general, Dynamic Web application platforms make a good case for fast ROI
because they offer:
Incremental customers: The volume of new online customers varies
signiﬁcantly by industry. In most large conventional catalog industries,
new Internet customers account for between one and two percent of total
sales;
Channel shift: companies can reduce order processing costs by an order of
magnitude by moving existing customers to the Internet. Channel shift is

the most pronounced in largest Internet industries, ﬁnancial services and
computer hardware;
Reduced customer service costs: UPS, Federal Express and other Fortune-
class companies are realizing millions of dollars in cost savings because
Web-based customer service may cost an order of magnitude less than call
center-based customer service at $2 per call.
Reduced Marketing Costs: paper catalog printing and distribution costs
alone justify some large business-to-business dynamic Web sites.
One-to-One applications beneﬁt from all of the above and, beyond standard
Web application platforms, they offer:
Increased share of customer: most high-end Web application systems offer
some simple rule-based cross-selling and up-selling but can not
graphically manage complex matching of content with users;
Enhanced employee productivity: Broad-Vision's Knowledge Management
Web application improves employee efﬁciency through reengineered
business processes and accurate information promulgation;
Improved partner management: just as bank ATM machines helped turn
customers into employees, extending corporate systems to the Internet (an
extranet) can help convert suppliers and partners into employees. One-to-
One partner management streamlines interbusiness processes with custom
knowledge sharing, pricing, and other business systems access.
In the short-term, BroadVision customers are just as attracted to Web
applications that improve employee productivity and interbusiness
communication as they are to BroadVision's unique consumer targeting and
catalog features.
Large Internet-based computer catalog vendors tell Aberdeen that a return on
investment can be realized in as little as six months with third party solutions.
Aberdeen believes that the ROI may be signiﬁcantly faster with One-to-One
because of the improved knowledge management, increased share of customer,
improved business processes and vertical One-to-One applications such as
One-to-One Finance.
One-to-One Extranets
Aberdeen interviewed USWest, a major BroadVision customer, to ﬁnd out how
well One-to-One exposes business systems to the Internet for other companies
(an extranet).
USWest's atypical extranet implementation is not designed to pamper partners.
Rather, the system opens USWest to competitive long distance service
providers, regional bell operating companies (RBOCs), and even power
companies in order to meet the requirements of the FCC's 1996

The USWest extranet implementation is of impressive scale. The system
interfaces with approximately 30 mainframe systems, customer and job
scheduling applications, inventory management systems, and telephone and
billing systems in order to provide a Web interface for organizations that want
to buy local phone service.
USWest ofﬁcials tell Aberdeen that, by purchasing One-to-One, they bought at
least six months of development time as well as linear scalability over
developing a similar system in-house. Longer-term, the company plans to
exploit One-to-One features for customer service (800 number replacement),
increased customer share, and knowledge management — all various ﬂavors of
BroadVision's core focus, relationship management.
Sure, But is it FAST?
Long ago, IBM graphical user interface (GUI) research found that most users
will only tolerate delays of a few seconds before getting extremely frustrated.
Before selecting any dynamic high-end Web application platform, Aberdeen
cautions all potential buyers to carefully determine the expected database sizes,
number of users, number of concurrent users, and complexity of business rules,
and evaluate suppliers with respect to these requirements.
Direct comparison of dynamic Web application performance remains extremely
difﬁcult because there are no industry-accepted benchmarks. Web server
benchmarks are of little use because dynamic Web application performance
depends upon the operation and interaction of all of the following subsystems:
The Web server;
The application server;

The database server;
External commerce systems.
Typically, the primary bottlenecks in a dynamic Web application are due to
opening and closing database connections as well as performing database
queries. Each database operation costs (in time and system overhead) about ten
times as much as all other dynamic page creation operations combined. To
reduce database interaction, BroadVision's interaction manager maintains a
persistent connection with users via HTML or Java and caches connections and
data about user proﬁles, content, and categories. These techniques allow One-
to-One to amortize the database cost over multiple user sessions and
transactions.
The One-to-One system is both multithreaded and multiprocessing to improve
parallelism, performance, scalability and error tolerance. One-to-One
selectively applies parallelism to minimize serial bottlenecks in CPU-intensive
operations, such as payment authorization, dynamic content generation,
database access, and database queries.

High-end dynamic Web applications should also scale to hundreds of thousands
of users, thousands of simultaneous users, and they should exploit existing
corporate databases and applications. Object-oriented middleware-based
platforms deliver both.
The BroadVision One-to-One Application System is compliant with the object
request broker architecture (CORBA). Corba-based systems scale well and may
be integrated into existing systems with relative ease.
The One-to-One Corba-based system can scale by replacing servers with more
powerful machines or by adding additional servers to the system. It can
communicate with existing business applications by screen scraping and
wrapping them or by interacting with other Corba-based systems, such as those
from IBM and Oracle, via lower level objects.
Competition
As a dynamic Web server for standard Internet commerce, BroadVision faces
stiff competition from IBM, Oracle, Microsoft, and Interworld. However,
BroadVision customers are not buying a standard dynamic Web server.
BroadVision customers are investing in a personalized dynamic Web
application system to better manage digital information and human interaction.
None of the competition offers the single gateway for humans to efﬁciently
interact with corporate resources. Only BroadVision fully appreciates the value
and power of end-user and consumer data availability, and the corresponding
implications for MIS.
Conclusions

BroadVision's competition characterize custom interaction and relationship
management as an electronic commerce niche.
Aberdeen believes this characterization is remarkably short sighted. The
continued expansion of computing from PCs to lap tops and ultimately to smart
cards will dramatically increase customer and end-user information. The real
question is, "is BroadVision too early for the market?"
Aberdeen believes the answer is no. Serious Internet businesses in ﬁnancial
services and computer-related sales already recognize the that consumer (end-
user) data is the competitive advantage. Further, there are several applications
of custom interaction well beyond Internet marketing.
Better end-user communication also translates into improved Intranet
knowledge management, superior customer service, and improved
interbusiness communication with partners.
One-to-One has been optimized to eliminate major performance sinks related to
database access, database queries, and session management. It runs large scale
ﬁnancial services sites at Liberty Financial, JP Morgan and Citibank to manage
relationships with customers, partners and employees and it serves as the
cornerstone of Internet commerce for the Internet Shopping Network's First
Auction Site and Eastman Kodak's visionary Kodak Picture Network.
Aberdeen strongly recommends that all potential buyers of software for
Fortune-1000 corporate Web sites evaluate BroadVision's software. One-to-One
represents a shift toward managing business relationships internally and
externally and Aberdeen believes that, ultimately, the rest of the industry will
choose a similar strategy.
