CA’s Unicenter TNG™ Framework: Entry-Level for the

Preface
On July 14, Computer Associates International (CA) announces a new product
in its family of Unicenter enterprise management software, the Unicenter
TNG™ Framework. This new packaging provides Independent Software
Vendors (ISVs) and end-user customers with access to Unicenter’s APIs and to
a group of highly useful, out-of-the-box enterprise management applications.
In 1994, Aberdeen called Unicenter 1.0 "the overlord of systems managers", an
apt description for the integrated, Swiss-army-knife approach that enables
Unicenter to provide superb, coordinated, cross-platform management of
computer systems operations. In 1996, CA delivered Unicenter/The Next
Generation (TNG) with even more extensive management capabilities and,
hands down, the best enterprise management user interface available anywhere.
The Unicenter software business in FY1997 ran at an industry-leading, one
billion dollar run-rate with numerous more-than-satisﬁed customers. So why is
CA giving away 20,000 CD-ROMs containing the new Unicenter TNG
Framework software for free to customers at its CA World user conference?
This Aberdeen Proﬁle explains why giving away millions of dollars worth of
software will beneﬁt customers, the industry — and CA.
Executive Summary
Unicenter TNG has been enormously successful, with revenues growing in
three years to over a billion dollars — a fast start on the same ﬁnancial order of
success as Microsoft’s heralded NT Server. This has resulted in 7,000-plus
customers, with support from over 200 ISV partners, and products running on
37 different operating system platforms to date. Unicenter TNG has both
breadth and depth — and that is what spawned the need for Unicenter TNG
Framework (hereafter, the Framework).
The solution that is Unicenter TNG comes with numerous services, but it is a
tightly integrated suite that, like its competitors, requires serious effort to
deploy. Some customers and lots of ISVs have needed the ability to provide
custom application management or services — including access to Unicenter
APIs — but with selected components from the Unicenter portfolio and/or
other best-of-breed solutions. Moreover, CA realized that the mass market for
enterprise management is at small-to-medium businesses who have few
Information Technology (IT) resources to analyze and deploy all the Unicenter
services at once. Lastly, CA wants to repackage Unicenter so that it can be
more pervasive and open — and win a greater share of an expanding market.
Thus, the idea for the Framework.
The Framework serves two purposes. First and foremost, the Framework
provides an easy to use, self-installing, automatic-network-discovering subset
of the full Unicenter TNG. Second, it provides customers and ISVs who want
to custom-build their systems management infrastructure with base
functionality for using the Unicenter Software Development Kit (SDK) to craft
unique solutions.
The Framework contains complete, uncrippled software for The Next
Generation two- and three-dimensional user interface, an object repository,
event monitoring and reporting, operations scheduling and virus detection. It
took Aberdeen Group just ten minutes to install the software on an NT server
and have the Framework detect all of the devices in our enterprise, including
desktops, hubs, routers, and Internet gateways — even the Network Computers
we recently installed. Unix versions of the Framework will be available later
this summer.
With that ten minutes of work (drinking coffee, actually), Aberdeen gained the
ability to graphically see its internal network and to have a fully populated
object repository with all of the network and node information. We could then
set up event monitoring to look for conditions such as "log full", and funnel all
the operational and event messages from our four servers back to a single
console — a big improvement in hands-free operations. In short, Aberdeen is a
small business that immediately beneﬁted from CA’s Framework. We expect
thousands of large and small enterprises to gain a similar out-of-the-box
experience. The Framework will introduce them to cross-platform enterprise
management quickly — with a rapid ROI. Later, most will want to add on

Unicenter TNG’s full services and Unicenter options. But they can schedule
this upgrade at their convenience.
In combination with the SDK, sophisticated IT organizations and ISVs can use
the Framework as a toolbox to build custom application-management solutions
— with or without speciﬁc Unicenter TNG options. This approach gives
customers more options for the breadth and depth of application and systems
management.
Importantly, CA has nine, household-name server hardware partners who will
ship the Framework bundled with market-leading Unix and NT server
hardware, and is actively lining up ISVs including SAP AG to use the
Framework as their application management base. By providing ISVs with a
ubiquitous, open management infrastructure, Aberdeen believes the Framework
will help widen CA’s enterprise management market leadership over the next 2-
3 years. This is an aggressive move by Computer Associates International!
Our conclusion is that CA’s aggressive merchandising of the Framework will
result in competitive consolidation. Having fewer but stronger choices actually
makes the IT and ISV decision-making job easier because the remaining
choices must, by deﬁnition, have strong industry backing. The Unicenter
TNG™ Framework, along with Unicenter TNG, will earn an industry
leadership position and competitor’s respect.
What is a Management Framework?
A framework manages application interactions with other applications or
system resources while providing a graphical view of the environment. The
Unicenter Framework (see Figure 1) is a common means of integrating and
simplifying the complexity of managing heterogeneous, networked computing
environments. By providing an easy to use facility for plugging applications
into the Framework, IT managers can select and deploy the enterprise
management solutions of their choice while retaining the advantages of an
integrated package. With its Framework, CA is now providing users the ability
to select and deploy the management environment they need now, with the
ability to plug-in management solutions from CA and its partners — to meet
evolving needs of the enterprise.

Why the Framework Meets the Needs of the Market
IS decision makers charged with cost-effectively charting the course of IT for
the enterprise insist on investing in common information technologies
operating across a wide range of platforms and application environments. The
reasons cited to Aberdeen by sage decision makers include:
Developing common management processes across a heterogeneous
technology environment with multiple platforms and operating systems;
Avoiding technology lock-in and obsolescence; and,
Being able to take full advantage of rapid technology and business change
— such as network computing and electronic commerce.
By de-coupling and releasing the underlying core services of Unicenter as the
Framework, CA is providing users and ISV’s with the critical baseline
functionality of Unicenter. Moreover, by including the core services and APIs
of Unicenter TNG through numerous platform suppliers, CA intends to make
the Framework a pervasive, de-facto market standard. And with the Framework
shipped to most enterprise users at no additional cost, its simple presence
promises to change the shape of supplier alliances and options for users.
As a result, CA and its business partners are now in a position to better deliver
more timely solutions to users. Instead of being constrained by long product
release-cycles and supplier lock-ins, ISVs and users can now begin to depend

upon greater market opportunity, choice, and freedom of selection — in short,
open.
Where the Framework is Best Suited
Initially operating on Windows NT Server and with support soon for the most
popular Unix operating environments, the Framework is highly suited for
multi-tiered and network-enabled enterprises. And, as the number of platform
environments that must be managed across the network by the IT department
increases, the viability of the Framework becoming the agent-of-choice for
cost-effective enterprise IT management processes grows dramatically.
Secondly, the Framework is well suited for enterprises that until now have
rolled their own management solutions across multiple platforms. Instead of
spending signiﬁcant portions of the IT budget on building, then maintaining,
home-grown management solutions, the Framework promises to provide a
signiﬁcant core functionality to IT. The Framework should prove more
economical, reliable, and labor-effective than home-grown solutions.
The vast majority of organizations that are now distributing computing
throughout their enterprises on Unix and NT servers should consider the
Framework as their "already there; no extra charge" management solution as
they deploy new, name-brand servers and take advantage of the Framework’s
functionality.
Most importantly, for enterprises depending on multiple software packages and
custom software applications for operating core business functions, the ability
to rely on the Framework promises salvation from the growing complexity of
multi-layered software components that can otherwise rapidly stall the
deployment of important applications.

solution of Unicenter TNG. Users looking for management solutions and

Framework a solution for mainframe MVS, Digital Equipment VMS and other
legacy operating system environments. While CA intends to deliver Framework
releases for these and other platforms based on customer need, users must
currently look to Unicenter TNG for support of these other platforms.

Competitive Positioning
Market dynamics have caused a shift in momentum for several of the major
suppliers of Systems, Operations and Network (aka Enterprise) management. In
general, after about a decade of signiﬁcant investment, hardware vendors’ own
enterprise management products are no longer on the cutting edge due to now-
lower investment rates and slower product introductions. For example, after its
technology alliance with HP lost luster, IBM acquired Tivoli and began to
aggressively pursue market-share growth on its own terms with its acquired
technology. Sun Microsystems and its SunSoft division continue to capably
support their Solstice customers — but appear to be more interested in
partnering for future technology than building their own new products.
Aberdeen believes that Microsoft is quietly assessing its supplier alignment
options while continuing to create new products for the desktop and NT Server
segments. And after a difﬁcult start-up phase, OpenVision was sold earlier this
year.
HP OpenView remains a serious competitor in enterprise management with a
large installed base of customers, but in Aberdeen Group’s opinion HP has
clearly lost momentum with customers as a leading supplier in this market
segment. HP does not appear to have decided whether to reinvigorate its
OpenView product development and beef up its marketing and sales activities
— and is likely to reserve that decision until later in 1997.
IBM has had some time to integrate Tivoli into its sales, marketing and
technical planning. Customers seemed to be comfortable that IBM is
committed to continuing investments in its broad portfolio of management
software products. Thus, IBM appeared to have become CA’s most obvious
competitor. Now, CA’s Framework provides the strengths that go beyond what
Tivoli’s TME promises — namely event management and distributed systems
management. The CA Framework automatically discovers and populates an
underlying object-based repository. Instead of Tivoli’s remote-network-
supplied conﬁguration estimates and days spent in network and asset
evaluation, CA’s Framework now makes it possible for typical IS
administrators to conﬁgure the enterprise management infrastructure in minutes
— on-site — from a CD. Moreover, CA is upping the ante by integrating
Unicenter’s Real World Interface and renowned scheduling capabilities. Instead
of a "me-too" framework story, CA has listened to its users and business
partners and provided a production-ready framework that can empower IS to
bridge the gap of managing distributed, networked and host-based operations
— quickly and cost-effectively.
Computer Associates has moved aggressively to retain momentum as the
market leader. This not only means maintaining Unicenter TNG as the de facto
standard for managing existing installed systems — as CA demonstrated clearly
with its acquisition of Digital Equipment’s Polycenter products and the tight
integration of those with its own management console — but also staking a
claim to leadership in the rapidly growing market for next-generation
management applications.
The ﬁrst indication customers had of this important strategic shift was with the
announcement of a radically new 2D/3D virtual reality-oriented user interface
and an object repository in Unicenter TNG. Now, with the introduction of the
Unicenter TNG Framework, customers and ISVs have even more evidence of
CA’s clear ﬁnancial and technical commitments.
The Framework — and the access to underlying Unicenter APIs that are a part
of the Framework package — will provide CA with an opportunity to build
strong ties with industry leading ISVs and to an expanding blue-chip customer
list who look to CA not only for cost-efﬁcient management products for the
installed base, but also advanced technology for the next generation of
distributed applications.
Aberdeen Conclusions
With this important announcement, CA moves from selling only the shave (the
Unicenter TNG solution) to selling or giving away the mass-market razor (i.e.,
the Framework) while selling razor-blades (i.e., Framework upgrades to
Unicenter TNG) to a broader, mass market. This will reward CA handsomely.
CA is creating a compelling value proposition for the mass IT market which
has not yet committed to an enterprise management solution.
And this value is increased geometrically, we believe, by CA’s astute decision
to break with its traditional direct-sales model and have name-brand server
hardware suppliers bundle the Framework along with operating software. And
ISVs will rally around the Framework as the de facto, volume market-leader —
they truly want to write once and deploy anywhere. That means you’ll see the
Framework just about everywhere important in the IT infrastructure business.

The Framework provides excellent out-of-the-box value with its base-level but
full-functionality management services. Those deploying distributed systems
at, say, 1000 bank branches, will cry for joy at the Framework’s easy
installation and self-discovery of networked resources — enhanced by a single
event management console to control all the branches. The repository is an
under-appreciated storehouse of network asset and management process
information.
The Aberdeen conclusion is that the Framework is a bold and visionary step by
CA to: extend the market for Unicenter by offering more choices; more rapid
installation and implementation yielding fast ROI; a clear migration path to full
Unicenter TNG enterprise management; excellent out-of-the-box value and
introduction to harried or unsophisticated IT users; and, the widely available,
open infrastructure that ISVs will depend on for their application’s
management. Aberdeen positions the Framework as the enterprise management
entry-level user choice and de facto ISV platform choice. And the added
extensions and value in the Framework make Unicenter TNG Aberdeen’s best
choice for enterprise management.
