## Overview

Aberdeen Group's comprehensive profile of Cisco Systems at the moment it was consolidating dominant enterprise networking market leadership. The report documents Cisco's strategic evolution from router-only vendor to comprehensive end-to-end solutions provider, analyzing: IOS as the de facto internetworking standard, the 1996 StrataCom acquisition ($4.67B) and Stratm Technology integration, NetFlow Switching (per-flow statistics and QoS), Tag Switching (MPLS precursor submitted to IETF), the new Gigabit Switch Router (GSR 12000 Series), and Cisco's ISP market targeting.

Aberdeen's core thesis — that Cisco would maintain market dominance by assimilating switching technology rather than being displaced by it — proved entirely correct. All major predictions validated: Cisco maintained dominant position through 2000s; Tag Switching became MPLS; GSR sold 1,000 units in first year; NetFlow became an industry monitoring standard.
