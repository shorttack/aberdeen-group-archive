**Source:** [Aberdeen Group Product Viewpoint Vol.10 No.2, March 14, 1997](https://web.archive.org/web/19970604112242/http://www.aberdeen.com:80/secure/viewpnts/1997/v10n2/body.htm)

## Overview

Aberdeen Group Product Viewpoint evaluating InterWorld Technology Ventures' Commerce eXchange 2.0 at the birth of commercial e-commerce. Aberdeen documents the $520M internet goods market (1996), argues that high-volume commercial sites need integrated solutions (not point products), and endorses Commerce eXchange for its CORBA/DCOM-neutral architecture (Oasis platform), comprehensive application portfolio, and proven deployments at Broderbund, MicroWarehouse, Multiple Zones International, Comdisco, and Scholastic.

InterWorld ultimately failed (bankrupt 2004 under eB2B Commerce successor), but Aberdeen's market framework, architectural prescriptions, and market timing predictions proved largely accurate.
