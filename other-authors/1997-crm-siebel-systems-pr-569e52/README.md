## Overview

This Aberdeen Group strategy profile evaluates Siebel Systems as a major and rapidly growing force in the Customer Interaction Software (CIS) market. The study covers Siebel's organizational structure, core product line (Siebel Sales Enterprise and Siebel Service Enterprise), technology architecture, partnership strategy, and competitive positioning.

Key claims include:
- Siebel's rapid growth is driven by N-tiered client-server architecture, a leveraged partnership business model, and rapid application development methodology
- The company has over 214 employees and a growing roster of major implementation partners (Andersen Consulting, KPMG, Cambridge Technology Partners, Price Waterhouse)
- Aberdeen expects 1997 to be another successful year as Siebel capitalizes on CRM market growth
- Siebel's Microsoft alliance (Visual C++, MS Office, MS BackOffice) and flexible internet-capable deployment will drive adoption

## Notes

Siebel Systems was acquired by Oracle Corporation in January 2006 for $5.85 billion. The Siebel brand continued as Oracle's on-premises CRM platform. Siebel peaked at ~45% CRM market share in 2002. The company that Aberdeen described as having 214 employees in 1997 grew to $1B+ revenue by 2000. This is one of the most prescient Aberdeen profiles in the archive.
