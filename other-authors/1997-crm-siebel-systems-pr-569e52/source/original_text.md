### Market Position

Siebel Systems supplies advanced customer interaction software (CIS) to large end-user organizations. Siebel's best customers are those companies that are striving for excellence across the entire customer-prospect life cycle. The company's core product line, Siebel Enterprise Applications, includes Siebel Sales Enterprise, the company's sophisticated sales and marketing solution that allows organizations to quickly identify opportunities with new and existing customers and sell on a more sophisticated level than competitors. Also included is a customer support offering that helps ensure that customer satisfaction levels remain high on a consistent basis. Siebel's solutions are appropriate for firms that sell to and support their customers directly, as well as those that use indirect channels.

Aberdeen believes Siebel's rapid growth and market acceptance are the result of three elements. First, the company's robust products are built on top of a modern, N-tiered client-server architecture that provides rich functionality. Second, Siebel's business model enables it to leverage the extensive resources of its partners and maintain rapid growth. Finally, Siebel's rapid application development methodology allows its customers to move quickly into production — this has resulted in many large-scale, successful production implementations for Siebel in the past 12 months.

### Core Products

Siebel Systems' family of software solutions, Siebel Enterprise Applications, consists of two primary applications. Siebel Sales Enterprise, the company's flagship product, is a robust sales force automation and marketing product that dramatically shortens sales cycles, while improving top-line revenue results. It offers comprehensive sales opportunity management tools including a contact manager, activity manager, scheduler, calendar, as well as correspondence, quotes and forecasting facilities. A built-in, three-dimensional, graphical executive information system (EIS) provides enhanced reporting and tracking capabilities.

Siebel Service Enterprise completes the customer information loop by providing the functionality needed to support and service customers after the sale is made. Features include service request logging and management, assignment and notification, escalation, contract/service agreements, and on-line knowledge bases.

Aberdeen believes three important design points of Siebel's product line have been a major factor in its rapid acceptance by the market. They are:

**Intuitive Graphical User Interface** — Siebel has concentrated on making it extremely easy for end-users to navigate the application and find the right information. The company's anticipation of the need for pen-based computing is another example of its advantage in this area.

**Application Customizability** — Siebel offers a powerful suite of software tools to configure the application to unique user requirements. Its modular, N-tiered architecture and use of configurable business objects (BusObjects, which define how the application's components work together) let users configure Siebel Enterprise Applications without altering the source code — ensuring a smooth adoption of future upgrades.

**Support for Global Operations** — Siebel Enterprise Applications are ideally suited for large multi-national firms that employ a variety of distribution channels. They are architected to support very large data volumes and high transaction rates and enable detached mobile clients to be easily resynchronized with database servers. Additionally, multiple languages are supported, including English, French, German, Japanese and Spanish.

Siebel Enterprise Applications are written in Microsoft Visual C++ and Microsoft Foundation Class Libraries, with an object-oriented design. The application runs on Windows, Windows for Workgroups, Windows 95, and Windows NT clients and Windows NT and Unix servers. Databases include Oracle, Sybase, Informix and Microsoft SQL Server.

In January 1997, the company announced Version 3.0 of Siebel Enterprise Applications. This latest release includes several important enhancements:

- **Enhanced customization capabilities** — With the introduction of Siebel Tools, Siebel allows its solutions to be modified graphically, making it much easier to configure Siebel Enterprise Applications to meet rapidly evolving business conditions. Additionally, Siebel VB allows the use of Microsoft's popular development tool.
- **Easier and more rapid deployment and upgrades** — Siebel now supports remote software distribution. Additionally, the Application Upgrader is unique in the market in that it enables new releases of Siebel's software to be seamlessly merged with previous versions, as well as user-specific customizations, by a single command.
- **Integration with complementary software** — Siebel provides bi-directional data transfer to sales configuration offerings from Calico, Concentra, and Trilogy, allowing sales professionals to automatically engineer customized solutions at the point-of-sale. In addition, the company has built interfaces to computer-telephony integration (CTI) middleware with Prospect and Genesys and provides support for automatic call distributors (ACDs) from Aspect, AT&T and Nortel.
- **Tighter integration with sales and service modules** — Additional capabilities for sharing data between different Siebel Enterprise Applications means end-users always have the information they need to satisfy customers.
- **Internetwork Computing Capabilities** — Taking advantage of its N-tiered architecture, Siebel allows flexible and dynamic deployment of its applications to make optimal use of hardware, systems software, and network resources. Using Internet-based technologies, Siebel Enterprise Applications can be deployed in a personal computing (client centric), client-server, or virtual computing (network/server centric) topology.

### Partnerships

Realizing the need to efficiently install, implement and support Siebel Sales Enterprise, the company has formed several important partnerships with systems integration and consulting firms. This is unique in that while Siebel has internal people dedicated to software development, marketing and sales, the company uses the much larger resources of its many partners for installation and support. This virtual professional services organization enables Siebel Systems to provide a sales force automation solution, not just software. Perhaps more important, Siebel's growth is not constrained by its ability to hire personnel for installing and maintaining its software.

Vital partnerships include a Global Strategic Alliance with Andersen Consulting. Andersen provides a full range of services, including business process reengineering, Siebel software configuration, installation, and enterprise-wide integration. Other partners include KPMG Peat Marwick, Cambridge Technology Partners, and Price Waterhouse. In March 1997, Siebel and Compaq announced an alliance under which they mutually recognize each other as the preferred provider in the CIS market.

Siebel's relationship with Microsoft is perhaps most significant. The two companies have forged a strong alliance, with Siebel providing full support for Microsoft's Application Development Tools, MS Office and MS BackOffice. Siebel's advanced client-server product is a perfect example of the type of customer-focused solution Microsoft needs to become a leader in the enterprise computing space. Aberdeen expects this relationship will increase in value for both Siebel and Microsoft throughout 1997.

### Aberdeen Conclusions

Siebel Enterprise Applications are an advanced suite of CIS applications that deserve careful consideration. The products' rich functionality, ease of use, and flexible deployment options are a winning combination. Siebel's choice to optimize its own strengths by using the service and support capabilities of third parties is a sound business decision.

Aberdeen is impressed with Siebel Systems. The company is positioned as a major and rapidly growing force in the CIS market. The company has the right mix of management talent and product technology to succeed. Aberdeen expects 1997 will be another successful year for the company as Siebel capitalizes on its ability to help forward-looking organizations increase revenues while driving down costs.

### Prescience Detail

**Key Prediction 1:** Aberdeen expects 1997 will be another successful year for Siebel.  
**Outcome:** CORRECT. Siebel's 1997 revenue rose to $120 million. The company continued explosive growth through 1999-2000.  
Source: https://www.company-histories.com/Siebel-Systems-Inc-Company-History.html

**Key Prediction 2:** Siebel positioned as a major and rapidly growing force in CIS market.  
**Outcome:** CORRECT. Siebel became the dominant CRM vendor, reaching 45% market share by 2002, $1B+ revenue by 2000, and was acquired by Oracle for $5.85 billion in 2006.  
Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siebel_Systems

**Key Prediction 3:** Siebel-Microsoft relationship will increase in value for both companies throughout 1997.  
**Outcome:** PARTIALLY CORRECT. The relationship was valuable in the late 1990s, but Microsoft eventually developed its own Dynamics CRM in 2003, making the two companies competitors.

**Key Prediction 4:** Siebel's virtual professional services model (using partner resources) would not constrain growth.  
**Outcome:** CORRECT. Siebel had 400+ consulting partners by 2000, and the virtual services model was widely credited as a key growth enabler. Source: https://www.company-histories.com

**Key Prediction 5:** Pen-based computing anticipation indicates field sales advantage.  
**Outcome:** DIRECTIONALLY CORRECT. Mobile CRM became standard by 2010s. However, Salesforce.com (founded 1999) led mobile/cloud CRM rather than Siebel.
