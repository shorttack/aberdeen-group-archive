Data Integration Management
in Heterogeneous Computing Environments
Preface
IS is now ﬁnding that today’s one-off data-migration projects are becoming
more frequent, more strategic (involving mission critical data, data
warehousing, and Year 2000 conversions) and more complex (involving
increasingly heterogeneous platforms and environments).
In this Proﬁle, Aberdeen examines one supplier, Evolutionary Technologies
International (ETI), whose objective is to provide a scalable and comprehensive
data integration management toolset for: data warehousing, client/server
migration, Year 2000 conversion, heterogeneous-platform integration,
application implementation such as SAP, and data migration and conversion.
ETI can be particularly effective for an organization’s data extraction and
conversion needs, such as large-scale migration and/or consolidation projects,
and the building of bridges and interfaces between systems.
Executive Summary
ETI’s core product, the ETI•EXTRACT® Tool Suite, focuses on the problem of
data access and bridging between disparate hardware and software systems in
heterogeneous computing environments. ETI•EXTRACT generates programs
that transform and move data to and from virtually any data storage format or
environment — including proprietary systems and languages—enabling new
technology implementation while maintaining legacy systems (see Figure 1).
ETI•EXTRACT is particularly effective in situations in which IS needs to
generate necessary data extraction and conversion programs rapidly and
especially for complex migrations where the production of scripts aids in
moving the data and executing the conversion programs. Users are able to
graphically depict how they want the data to be moved and ETI•EXTRACT
seamlessly generates and executes the programs and keeps track of all
transformations and inter-database relationships.
ETI•EXTRACT supports a robust Metadata Facility that provides access to
schema deﬁnitions, source-to-target mapping, and support for business rules
and/or data transformation logic. The tool has exceptional strengths in the
power of its scripting engines, its metadata reporting capability and the
ﬂexibility of its data cleansing. These strengths deliver the beneﬁts of
scalability, ﬂexibility, broad functionality, reuse and extensibility.
ETI has a unique strength with its access to legacy data. This knowledge has
been built from many years of practical work with its customers. This
experience can also be seen in the methodology that ETI employs, Success
First™, which ensures that customers will be successful with their entire
project, a part of which is the data integration management.
ETI is a fast-growing but stable supplier with signiﬁcant representation in
Fortune 1000 corporations such as Procter & Gamble, Hallmark, PHH and
Neodata Services Inc. ETI differentiates its offering from the competition with
its focus on the data integration management market, its powerful scripting
engines and the broad range of metadata generated.

Aberdeen believes that ETI•EXTRACT can be particularly helpful as part of an
ongoing strategy for data integration management as part of producing an
overall middleware infrastructure architecture. Users in this situation should
give this toolset serious attention.
A Key IS Infrastructure Tool
Data integration management tools such as ETI•EXTRACT automate the
process of retrieving, cleansing, transforming and moving data. The data-
integration functions these tools automate are important especially when IS is:
Implementing and maintaining data warehouses;
Exchanging metadata;
Reengineering processes and implementing new applications; or
Migrating to new environments.
However, to fully reap the beneﬁts of automation, Aberdeen believes the
following top-line considerations are key:
Power and ﬂexibility. The more powerful the scripting engine, the greater
the degree of automation, ﬂexibility and customization that us-ers can
derive from the tool.
Ease of use. The data transformation tool should have a graphical point
and click interface that hides unnecessary details and reduces frustration
and the learning curve.
Functional depth with broad enterprise application. By choosing a tool that
delivers broad functionality, enterprise scalability, and effective inte-
gration with related tools, users can aggregate its beneﬁts over many
projects and organizations within their enterprise and avoid the home-
grown ﬁx that creates long-term problems.
Technology Overview
ETI’s core offering, the ETI•EXTRACT Tool Suite, enables users to:
Populate and maintain data warehouses;
Implement such applications as SAP, Baan, Oracle and Peoplesoft;
Migrate to new architectures;
Integrate incompatible systems; and
Migrate data to new databases, platforms and applications.
The ETI•EXTRACT Tool Suite consists of two sets of productivity tools, the
Master Toolset and the Data Conversion Toolset, both of which include the
Conversion Editor, the Executive, the Workset Browser and the Metadata
Facility. In addition, the tool suite offers advanced administration functionality
that augments a user’s control over heterogeneous environments as well as a
GUI that enables the process of specifying data collection and transformation
instructions and generating the necessary programs as effortlessly as point-and-
click. The tool is able to create programs in virtually any programming
language, including C, COBOL, RPG, and even proprietary languages such as
SAP’s ABAP/4 language (see Figure 2).

Master Toolset
The Master Toolset is a set of interactive editors that empower a systems
programmer (a Master User) to deﬁne to the MetaStore database everything
required to be able to read and write to any database management system or ﬁle
system in the environment—data that is also referred to as a ETI•EXTRACT
Data System Library™ (DSL). It includes powerful Editors with the following
features:
The Environment Editor speciﬁes the different platforms and system
operating environments to be accessed;
The Schema Editor provides browsing or updating access to schema
information;
The Grammar Editor deﬁnes customized conditional retrieval,
transformation, and target populate logic to meet environment-speciﬁc
needs; and
The Template Editor allows for the speciﬁcation of programming rules.
Data Conversion Toolset
The Data Conversion Toolset allows a user to deﬁne the mapping of data
between the diversiﬁed source data systems and target data systems. A user is
able to specify the instructions for selectively collecting, transforming and
moving data. The Conversion Editor includes a conﬁgurable screen with a
single window for data mapping functions. Since the Conversion Editor
provides all of the necessary information about the data and the options for
selecting and transforming it, a user is able to:
Selectively retrieve data from one or multiple DBMS or ﬁle formats;
Create programs that test and transform data;
Merge data from various systems to create new databases; and
Populate numerous DBMSs or ﬁle formats.
Once the conversion parameters have been speciﬁed, the Conversion Editor is
able to take this information (along with information already contained in its
internal database) and generate the programs required to complete the
conversion, allowing information to be captured, stored as metadata and placed
in the Meta- Store database.
ETI•EXTRACT Executive
The Executive provides process control and automatic job execution
capabilities. It physically transfers the generated programs to the right source
and target computer systems. The Executive also initiates the compiling of the
programs, controls the execution of the compiled programs, and monitors the
entire process, reporting errors and warnings.
ETI•EXTRACT Workset Browser
The Workset Browser provides a user-customizable desktop metaphor with
intuitive graphical icons that designate the processes and objects that can be
selected and manipulated, thus providing a single point for controlling user
interactions and tool access.
Using the Workset Browser, administrators are able to create a workset where
database views and access privileges are deﬁned for a single or multiple user.
Administrators can also deﬁne conversions at a project or enterprise level.
Thus, an administrator is able to provide users with access to only the databases
or subsets of the environment that are necessary for them. The Workset
Browser also provides a checkin/checkout feature that allows multiple
concurrent users to utilize the same databases or ﬁles without jeopardizing the
integrity of the data.

ETI•EXTRACT Metadata Facility
The Metadata Facility includes the MetaStore database, the Metadata Exchange
Library™ (MDX), a browsing mechanism, and a report generation capability
that provide comprehensive metadata capture, access, reporting and integration.
The MetaStore Database
The MetaStore database is an object-oriented database that provides concurrent
multi-user support, impact analysis reporting, and versioning capabilities. This
database provides a centralized location for capturing and storing the metadata
used by the Conversion Editor as well as metadata from other sources. This
capability is effective in building data warehouses but also beneﬁcial in
application implementation and database migration efforts.
The Metadata Exchange Library™
The Metadata Exchange Library permits metadata to be managed just like any
other type of enterprise data. Users employ the Conversion Editor graphically
to show how to export the metadata from the MetaStore database to any other
enterprise repository or catalogue, to import metadata from other sources into
the MetaStore database, and even to merge metadata from multiple sources.
Where ETI•EXTRACT Is Most Effective
The strengths of ETI•EXTRACT — its bi-directional movement of data, its
ability to generate programs in the user’s preferred programming language for
an exceptionally broad range of platforms, its ability to transform and reformat
data at the data-item level, and its capture of conversion metadata to support
change man-agement, maintenance and auditing — are well-suited for such IS
initiatives as:
Building and maintaining a data warehouse;
Implementing a new suite of enterprise applications;
Combining IT functions as a result of acquisitions or mergers; and
Integrating a client/server or network computing architecture into the
environment.
Organizations undertaking any one of these projects will want to assess
ETI•EXTRACT not only for the job at hand but as a long-term integral part of
the IT infrastructure.
Competition
Because of its broad set of features, ETI•EXTRACT can be viewed as
competing with an extensive range of data access, data migration, and
application integration products from Prism, Constellar, V Mark, Reliant, TSI,
and others.
ETI differentiates ETI•EXTRACT from the competition through support for a
bi-directional approach that allows data to be moved between RDBMSs (and
warehouses), or from relational systems to host DB2 or ﬂat ﬁle systems—a
capability that can be used in a variety of ways. In addition, ETI•EXTRACT
physically moves the ﬁles containing the generated programs, allowing it to
execute the data transformation on the most appropriate platform. And
ETI•EXTRACT clearly differentiates itself with powerful data extraction
capabilities, broad support for the main types of mainframe data storage, and
beneﬁts from an extensible architecture that allows business-speciﬁc logic to be
supported in the generated programs.
In fact, many of these suppliers’ offerings should be viewed as complementary
rather than competitive—and ETI shows clear indications of striking up
partnerships with some of these suppliers.
Alliances/Partnerships
ETI takes a synergistic approach to formulating relationships through their
Strategic Alliance Partners Program. The program includes joint marketing
relationships, certiﬁed consultants, integrators and VARs. An example of this is
the reseller/joint development agreement with IBM. This agreement allows
IBM to resell ETI•EXTRACT. In addition, ETI•EXTRACT will be the data
extraction/transformation tool in IBM warehouse solutions—both suppliers are
collaborating on advanced, sophisticated changed-data-capture features.
Financial Outlook
ETI has sustained growth of 50 to 100% per year, with ﬁscal 1997 (ending June
30) revenues of $25.5 million—and over $13 million has been invested in R&D
alone. In addition, ETI has 12 ofﬁces in cities throughout the U.S. and Canada
— with four wholly owned subsidiaries in Europe.
Aberdeen Conclusions

ETI’s ETI•EXTRACT is a powerful, ﬂexible data integration management tool
that can handle bi-directional conversion and migration between a multitude of
computing environments. It is especially useful in downsizing legacy databases
to new applications and platforms, in integrating and coordinating fast-
changing databases in heterogeneous computing environments, in merging the
databases of organizations that are growing by acquisition or merger, and where
IS needs to regularly import or export new data ﬁles.
Aberdeen believes that as programming, administration and knowledge worker
functions become increasingly distributed across an enterprise, data-migration
and other tools will need to support the new environments. ETI•EXTRACT’s
point-and-click interface, enabling non-programmer analysts, and its metadata
facilities targeting data administration needs are particularly effective in this
direction.
IS buyers should evaluate data transformation tools not only according to their
usefulness for the project at hand but also for their ability to provide persistent
value to the enterprise. Aberdeen ﬁnds that ETI delivers a well-integrated
combination of technology, tools, process, administrative and support that
large-scale data transformation efforts require. Users undertaking these data
transformation and migration projects should take a close look at ETI today.

### Prescience Detail

**Correct Predictions:**
- Data integration management tools became increasingly strategic as Aberdeen predicted — the ETL market grew substantially through the 2000s
- Metadata management became a critical enterprise discipline (modern data catalogs confirm this)
- Y2K conversion was indeed a major driver for data migration tools in 1998-2000
- The need for tools supporting distributed/heterogeneous environments proved accurate
- Bi-directional data movement capability proved important in the market

**Incorrect or Partially Correct Predictions:**
- ETI did not become a long-term IT infrastructure standard as implied; acquired during late-1990s/early-2000s market consolidation
- The market consolidated around larger vendors (Informatica, IBM DataStage) rather than specialist tools
- Enterprise data integration market moved toward cloud services rather than on-premises ETL tools

**Long-Term Outcomes:**
- ETI was acquired; the data integration market consolidated around Informatica, IBM DataStage (from Ardent/Ascential), Ab Initio
- Data warehousing became mainstream and evolved into cloud data warehouses (Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift) by the 2020s
- ETL pattern evolved to ELT in the cloud era; modern tools include dbt, Fivetran, Airbyte
- Prism Solutions (competitor) was acquired by Ardent Software in 1998
- Constellar (competitor) was acquired by IBM in 1998
- Y2K completed without major catastrophic failures; significant migration projects executed 1998-2000
