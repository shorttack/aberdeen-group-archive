Abstract: Profile
DataReach: Finding Business Value in
Extracting Data
Global 2000 enterprises are under continual pressure to
increase revenues, lower costs, and improve margins. By first
unlocking its mainframe data, an enterprise can extract these
and other business benefits through better decision making.
Unlocking this data for decision making typically occurs in
data warehouses and data marts running in open systems
environments.

However, mainframes do not always yield their data easily to
open systems environments. A variety of scheduling and
operational constraints typically limit the time available to
move this data. If the data cannot get there on time, business
value may be lost. Obtaining data through the use of
traditional propagation methods may cost more than expected.

This Profile examines DataReach, a unique combination of
intelligent storage and data movement software that bypasses
the mainframe and speeds up the delivery of critical data to an
open system. EMC Corporation and BMC Software, Inc.
developed DataReach in a partnership. Aberdeen believes that
DataReach will topple mainframe-infrastructure barriers to the
free flow of data and help many large enterprises to capture
business value that might otherwise be lost.

David Hill

### Abstract

Aberdeen Group vendor profile of DataReach, a joint product by EMC Corporation and BMC Software that bypasses mainframe scheduling constraints to accelerate delivery of critical data to open systems environments. Aberdeen predicts the product will help Global 2000 enterprises unlock mainframe data for data warehouses and data marts. ### Prescience Detail

**Prediction:** Aberdeen believed DataReach would "topple mainframe-infrastructure barriers" and enable Global 2000 enterprises to move mainframe data to data warehouses and data marts more efficiently than traditional propagation methods.

**Outcome Assessment:** The underlying problem Aberdeen identified — mainframe data as a bottleneck for analytics — proved prescient and durable. However, the specific solution (a purpose-built joint hardware/software product from EMC and BMC) did not become the dominant approach. The market evolved toward general-purpose ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) platforms (Informatica, IBM DataStage, SAS), and later cloud-based data integration. EMC was eventually acquired by Dell in 2016, and BMC Software went private in 2013. There is no evidence of DataReach achieving significant long-term commercial success as a standalone product. The prediction scores a neutral 5/10: the problem was real, but the specific solution was wrong.
