## Overview

This Aberdeen Group strategy profile evaluates Hewlett-Packard's commitment to the Desktop Management Interface (DMI) standard as a competitive differentiator in the enterprise PC market. Written in March 1997, the profile examines HP's Vectra Corporate PC line and its TopTOOLS management application suite, arguing that HP's open-standards DMI approach gives it a strategic advantage over Compaq's proprietary Intelligent Manageability system.

Key claims include:
- PC desktop management costs are 2x–3x the purchase price of the PC itself
- HP's DMI strategy could reduce total cost of PC ownership by 50% or more
- DMI certification would become an increasingly important competitive differentiator in 1997
- HP's open approach (vs. Compaq's proprietary model) would help it overtake Compaq in enterprise PC sales

## Notes

DMI (Desktop Management Interface) reached end-of-life on March 31, 2005, superseded by CIM (Common Information Model). HP Vectra was discontinued in 2002 following the HP-Compaq merger, replaced by the HP Evo line. HP OpenView was rebranded in 2007 as HP Software Division products; eventually sold to MicroFocus. The open vs. proprietary debate Aberdeen documented played out differently than predicted — Microsoft's WMI (Windows Management Instrumentation) became the dominant desktop management standard, not DMI per se.
