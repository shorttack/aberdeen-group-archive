Executive Summary
To compete effectively in the dynamic telecommunications marketplace,
industry leaders must offer customized solutions, attractive price points, and
proactive customer service — all while continuously responding to evolving
business conditions. Increasingly, use of advanced information technology and
next-generation software applications is seen as a key component for meeting
these market demands. Many leading telecommunications ﬁrms view DIGITAL
as the strategic partner to help them use information systems as a competitive
weapon.
Through its telecommunications industry practice, DIGITAL helps its clients
achieve four critical goals — the development of new services based on the
innovative use of emerging technologies and communications devices; the
ability to deliver ﬂexible broadband services; the effective management of
intelligent networks, and the ability to provide client-focused billing and
customer support.
Aberdeen believes DIGITAL is positioned to help telecommunications ﬁrms for
the following reasons:
Advanced network management platform — DIGITAL’s TeMiPs product
can be used to issue automatic network alerts and identify faults before
they become visible to the customer.
Partnerships with best-of-breed suppliers — Recognizing that it is often
better to use existing solutions than build from the ground up, DIGITAL
has established several key alliances with complementary ISVs so that it
can deliver complete solutions to its customers.
Leverage existing systems — DIGITAL’s strategy is to extend current
systems by adding new functionality and services on top of what already
works.
Multi-platform capabilities — DIGITAL’s 64-bit Alpha platform gives
clients the choice of using Unix, VMS, or Microsoft’s NT operating
systems.
Proven industry track record — DIGITAL has worked with several
leading telecommunications ﬁrms, including Nortel and Alcatel.
DIGITAL’s philosophy of exhaustively testing a new solution before
deployment is a major reason for the high satisfaction levels across its
client base.
This Proﬁle discusses in more detail DIGITAL’s offerings, highlights its most
important partnerships, and shows how DIGITAL and its partners combine
forces to solve the unique needs of each of its clients.
DIGITAL’s Market Expansion Model
DIGITAL’s strategy is threefold: use partners to expand product and service
options, use partners to shorten the customers’ learning curve and introduction
of new technology, and leverage its strength in systems engineering and
integration.
By working together to offer cutting edge solutions, DIGITAL and its partners
save on research and development costs, and shorten time to market for new
solutions, while simultaneously providing a superior product to the customer.
Similarly, this permits DIGITAL and its partners to compete in new areas
which would otherwise be closed to them based on cost, lack of resources, or
limited market potential.
Most importantly, DIGITAL provides the most critical element in the total
solutions package: integration. Without successful integration, the net result
would be a regionalization of service operations for the customer, precisely
when most customers seek to consolidate systems into a common framework.
Interoperability issues continue to face customers as a serious problem in
keeping up with new technology, in being responsive to customers, and in
avoiding early obsolescence of costly investments. By building upon existing
platforms and legacy systems, DIGITAL saves the customer both time, effort,

money and headaches, while also enabling the company to offer its customers
enhanced services, integrated billing and single point of contact for
provisioning the service.
Market Approach
The telecommunications industry must respond to demand for innovative,
ﬂexible services that match the convergence of computing, communications,
and new media. DIGITAL’s strategy targets the traditional telcos, cable,
wireless, and satellite operators, the telecommunications equipment
manufacturers, and new entrants in high-speed communications. The DIGITAL
solution set consists of the following:
Intelligent Network Solutions;
Telecommunications Management Network solutions (TMN);
Customer Care and Billing (CC&B);
Broadband Services solutions (BBS).
DIGITAL actively recruited partners in these areas, expanding the range of
solutions it brings to market. In addition to independent software vendors
(ISVs), DIGITAL also partners with telecommunications equipment
manufacturers.
Intelligent Network technology trends point toward increasing use of smart
cards. In particular, smart cards are driving the remote, wireless, cellular market
by providing more options to the end user in the form of prepaid phone cards,
enhanced cellular capability through larger roaming areas, and even full phone
personalities maintained in the smart card itself. As an integrator, DIGITAL
combines the various components from multiple partners into a whole solution,
and plugs the entire package into the telco’s existing platform, thus simplifying
both the design and implementation phases for the customer.
Telecommunications network management monitors overall systems and
network performance, and alerts the systems administrator in advance of any
potential network failures or downtime. Another integral solution set includes
customer care and billing, which involves processing incoming phone calls or
media transactions, rating them according to the customer’s account plan, and
billing the customer for services provided. Broadband services are technology
"enablers", that permit the telco service provider to transmit multimedia (voice,
data, fax) signals concurrently.
The Roles of the Partners
The DIGITAL name and reputation as a global service provider increases and
strengthens brand leverage and credibility for the smaller, more geographically
focused vendor. By combining products, both companies can address a greater
range of telco operator needs, and offer more possibilities for customization.
Because of DIGITAL’s successful integration track record, telco’s can acquire
software from DIGITAL’s partners, conﬁdent that these products can be
integrated to meet the most demanding applications. Partnering also permits
taking advantage of technology updates by "borrowing" new features into
existing product lines without spending a fortune on research and development.
Consequently, partnering also shortens time to market for those involved,
offering a strategic advantage that may be passed on to the telco operator and
its end users.

Forming a Total Solutions Set via Partnering
The following section details ﬁve partner’s product and service offerings within
the four application segments DIGITAL has targeted.
CGI
Since 1968, Computer Generation Incorporated (CGI) has designed and
developed cost-effective, technically advanced, pre-billing communication and
support systems. CGI provides three distinct solutions: collection modules,
generalized data conversions, and rating modules.

The collection modules retrieve customer record data from any object in the
network. Once the objects have been polled and the data collected, it is
processed to databases or productized. The rating modules are used in
categorizing customer proﬁles and billing rate plans to determine appropriate
service charges.
CGI provides exceptional 7x24 service worldwide, with a guaranteed two hour
response time. Remote tie-in to its installed base allows CGI to access their
customers’ systems to determine and resolve problems regardless of geographic
location.
Why DIGITAL chose CGI
CGI has a worldwide reputation for the highest quality business solutions and
services in the telecommunications market. This reputation, coupled with its
superior research and development team, make CGI an ideal partner. All new
product ideas come directly from their customer base. As competition stiffens
between telcos, strong customer support and ﬂexible solutions become key
differentiators.
CGI maintains close ongoing relationships with its installed base. Most of
CGI’s new business development efforts rely on their referencable customer
base. The company also relies on direct mail, targeted to its customers to keep
them up-to-date on product and technology innovations. Last, CGI is so
conﬁdent in its customer satisfaction and support levels that the company
reports "anyone can reference any of our accounts at any time". Its successful
track record continues to lead CGI to increased growth and proﬁtability since
delivering its ﬁrst turnkey telephony system in 1982.
Why CGI chose DIGITAL
CGI believes DIGITAL has the "best hardware and the best engineering" in the
market. Also, DIGITAL’s worldwide ﬁeld service organization aligns well with
CGI’s worldwide customer base. The company has a strong business
relationship with DIGITAL and ﬁnds DIGITAL to be extremely ﬂexible. In
conclusion, DIGITAL’s strengths complement the CGI solution offerings.
CMG
CMG targets the high-end text messaging market for DIGITAL wireless
networks, such as PCS 1900 in the US. Text messaging functionality allows
DIGITAL mobile telephones to be used as a pager. CMG provides a short
message service center, which is a store-and-forward system for text messages
with interfaces to numerous networks, and a value-added services platform,
which allows numerous enhanced services to be deployed based on the
messaging system and intelligent networking technology. Examples of these
services include on-demand stock quotes, news, sports and weather, trafﬁc
information, e-mail to and from mobile telephones, interactive voice response
systems, etc.
CMG is the European market leader for the messaging systems industry. The
company entered the US in 1995, initially through a partnership with switch
vendor, Ericsson. CMG’s current US strategy focuses on: supporting its US
customers; expanding its US market share; building partnerships; and
consultancy services.
Why DIGITAL chose CMG
CMG is a technology leader in the short messaging segment, and conforms to
the European GSM standards, in addition to the recently introduced PCS 1900
standards. Adoption of PCS 1900 technology is slow because many potential
customers still rely on the legacy analog technology, which is incompatible
with the new standards. Because DIGITAL data transmission is superior in
quality, and because short message store and forwarding technology cannot be
fully utilized without PCS 1900 compliance, an attractive growth opportunity
exists within this market.
CMG’s service platform is soon to be certiﬁed by Ericsson, and become an
embedded part of Ericsson’s network, making CMG technology more visible in
the marketplace. CMG is well-established within Europe, where it offers full
consultancy, systems design, and integration services.
Why CMG chose DIGITAL
Partnering with DIGITAL opens up additional sales channels and sales
opportunities for CMG. The future opportunity for CMG to act as a licensed
distributor for DIGITAL is another attraction. As a new entrant to the US
market, the DIGITAL alliance adds an aura of prestige to its marketing efforts.
Another factor for partnering with DIGITAL was based on customer request.
The speed and throughput capacity of the Alpha processor offers the perfect ﬁt
for short messaging technology, which is designed to fully exploit the 64-bit
processor.
DIALOGIC

The DIGITAL/Dialogic relationship was established in 1994. Essentially a
component company, Dialogic partners with a wide variety of OEMs, resellers,
ISVs, telecommunications equipment manufacturers, and platform providers,
who deliver solutions to end users. The DIGITAL partnership addresses
virtually all areas of computer telephony, including messaging, local
connectivity, wide area connectivity, and information access. Over the past 15
years, DIGITAL ventured into computer telephony to a limited extent; its
partnership with Dialogic expands the breadth and substance of its offerings
worldwide.
Dialogic sells to the application development community, with over 2 million
installed ports worldwide. Dialogic has a broad selection of interoperable
components — all based on open standards. The company’s core competencies
originated in signal computing and network interfaces. Dialogic has expanded
its product line via its acquisition of Gammalink (fax), Spectron Microsystems
(realtime DSP), and Dianatel (network interface).
Dialogic projects a uniﬁed marketing message worldwide, and sells the same
product components globally. In addition to its technology-based partnership,
the two companies pursue joint marketing efforts, attend trade shows together
and advertise cooperatively. Together, this partnership educates its customers
on the ease, simplicity, and robustness of the total solutions package concept.
Why DIGITAL chose Dialogic
In a market traditionally populated with proprietary architecture, Dialogic
excels in standards leadership with its efforts to grow the demand for open
technology applications. Leading the computer telephony industry in both sales
and technological innovation, the widely recognized Dialogic name strengthens
DIGITAL bid potential. The Dialogic product line contains a complete
spectrum of media components, and the broadest selection of interoperable
components.
During the execution phase of customer implementation, DIGITAL handles
system design and integration issues, while Dialogic maintains a strong
technical support network. Dialogic has over 2000 application partners
successfully deployed on its products worldwide. The Dialogic partnership aids
DIGITAL in further recruiting computer telephony applications partners.
Dialogic products conform to worldwide telecommunications standards, with
proven deployment in 74 countries.
Why Dialogic chose DIGITAL
DIGITAL is already well-represented within the network carrier market. The
Alpha server’s scale and performance capabilities open up the high-end telco
segment for deploying computer telephony applications. The combination of
Dialogic products in the Alpha server platform offers what telcos require to
deploy computer telephony applications: scale, performance, manageability,
open standards, wireless and wireline services. Furthermore, DIGITAL
addresses the breadth of computer telephony market potential, serving the
traditional telco, the corporate enterprise, Internet and intranet telephony
applications. In conclusion, DIGITAL’s global market reach and extensive
support network provide the ideal strategic ﬁt with Dialogic’s worldwide focus.
KENAN SYSTEMS
Kenan Systems competes in the customer care & billing (CC&B) segment of
the telco industry. The major business units Kenan excels in are processing and
rating telephone calls and consolidated billing, which enable the
telecommunications ﬁrm to be more responsive to its customers.
Kenan completed the port to the Alpha platform in December 1996, and
competitive benchmarking efforts showed that hourly bill processing rates were
50% faster using the Alpha platform compared to other platforms. The
symmetric multi-processing and additional CPU capacity of Alpha enables data
to be computed much faster. The Alpha platform functions as a technology
enabler, further leveraging the robustness and speed of Kenan’s CC&B
software — the same telephone call processing and rating speed can not be
done with a 32-bit Intel platform. Kenan permits the end user to view multiple
billing processes at once, as opposed to scrolling through multiple screens to
view activity within each discipline.
Why DIGITAL chose Kenan
First, Kenan’s Arbor/BP product allows the user of multiple
telecommunications services to receive a single bill through a centralized
mechanism which ﬁlters data from various telephony switches and mediation
devices. Second, the Arbor product allows the telco to analyze proﬁtability
quickly and accurately by mining transactional data through ﬁlters into a
decision support system. Subsequent productivity enhancement may be
achieved by way of inventory management, which categorizes and assigns
blocks of phone numbers. The billing, processing, and rating functions
combined enable the telco to improve its customer service by being more
responsive to the customer.

Why Kenan chose DIGITAL
The Alpha 64-bit platform maximizes Arbor/BP performance. Additional
considerations were DIGITAL’s strong installed base with PTTs, RBOCs and
IXCs worldwide, and its established presence in wireless communications. The
DIGITAL alliance is integral in expanding Kenan’s presence within the
DIGITAL and wireless communications segments due to DIGITAL’s strength
in the GSM standards.
As a specialized telco applications manufacturer, partnering with DIGITAL
expands Kenan’s market reach in a much broader solutions package. Kenan
regards the DIGITAL sales force as having "a great presence with customers in
key accounts." Kenan’s afﬁliation with DIGITAL also extends the scope of
Kenan’s current installation and integration capabilities, which previously
cornered only the CC&B segments of a telco network.
VIENNA SYSTEMS
Vienna targets the corporate customer’s bottom line by reducing or eliminating
toll charges by rerouting phone calls over the Internet, Intranet, or private IP
network. Vienna’s services are simple, scalable, and cost-effective for the
customer. Furthermore, the company offers the option of consolidating Internet
and telephone billing into a single statement.
Vienna’s products are compatible with legacy systems, and its target date for
shipping to DIGITAL customers is set for Spring 1997. Vienna products port to
Alpha and NT. As customers adopt and incorporate NT technology into their
networks, DIGITAL offers the ﬂexibility of porting to multiple operating
systems.
Why DIGITAL chose Vienna
This Newbridge afﬁliate offers IP voice and IP fax. Its software supports
multiple gateways, and an open set of APIs. Vienna also supports TAPI
applications — instrumental in integrating Windows NT into the IP solution.
Vienna offers web- based customer support, increasing the accessibility of
technical assistance staff.
Why Vienna chose DIGITAL
For a small, start-up company, DIGITAL’s name lends prestige and adds
credibility. Prior to the DIGITAL alliance, integration efforts were outsourced;
the alliance adds an additional service option to Vienna customers. While
Vienna is capable of porting to any Intel-based server, it views Alpha as more
"futuristic," with more raw capacity and greater capability. As a point solution,
Vienna’s afﬁliation with a total solution set provider enables them to compete
on a higher level than would otherwise be possible solely as a point solution
provider.
Conclusions
DIGITAL is well-positioned to expand its presence in key telco market
segments with many "best in class" technology partners. The strength of the
Alpha 64-bit chip continues to differentiate it from mainstream 32-bit
processors. In a market where hardware is becoming commoditized, service
becomes the new focal point. DIGITAL achieves this through ﬂexibility and
customization options in systems integration, which in turn enable telco
operators to enhance their services cost effectively.
To be competitive, telecommunications ﬁrms must be market-driven, offer
customized solutions, responsive customer service, and deploy new technology
solutions rapidly — all at a competitive price. To help its customers attain these
goals, DIGITAL has capitalized on strategic alliances that enable it to offer a
full product and services portfolio. Just as important as the individual
components is the ability to integrate these new solutions into the customer’s
pre-existing systems successfully: a proven capability of DIGITAL’s. This
integration capability is derived through equal parts engineering talent, and the
strength of the TeMIPs network management architecture combined with the
Alpha microprocessor. In conclusion, the DIGITAL package should provide the
customer with superior performance, ﬂexibility, and convenience —thus, the
total solutions package provides a whole greater than the sum of the individual
parts.

### Abstract

Aberdeen Group profiled Digital Equipment Corporation's telecom industry strategy in May 1997, assessing DIGITAL's four solution areas and five key ISV partners: CGI, CMG, Dialogic, Kenan Systems, and Vienna Systems. Aberdeen concluded DIGITAL was well-positioned for telecom expansion via its Alpha platform strengths and integration capabilities.

### Prescience Detail

Aberdeen's assessment of DIGITAL's 1997 telecom positioning was largely accurate for the short term. The partner outcomes were broadly positive and validated Aberdeen's partner selection: Kenan was acquired by Lucent for $1.48B (1999), Vienna by Nokia for ~$90M (1998), CMG survived via Logica merger (2002), and Dialogic was acquired by Intel for $780M (1999). These acquisitions at premium valuations broadly validated Aberdeen's view of the ecosystem quality. The long-term DEC narrative failed however: Compaq acquired DEC for $9.6B in June 1998 (just 13 months after this study), and the Alpha architecture was discontinued in 2001. Aberdeen's emphasis on Alpha's 64-bit advantage as a lasting differentiator proved incorrect; x86 caught up and surpassed Alpha before the decade ended.
