## Overview

This Technology Viewpoint examines distributed object technology (DOT) middleware in 1997, at the height of the CORBA/DCOM standards war. Aberdeen analyst Jeanine Fournier argues that DOT is simultaneously the era's biggest example of oversold hype and a genuinely strategic foundation for future enterprise computing. The report identifies three key barriers: product immaturity/integration gaps, standards fragmentation (CORBA vs. DCOM), and the absence of a clear business case.

## Key Findings

- DOT characterized as simultaneously over-hyped and strategically essential
- Three barriers identified: product immaturity, standards conflict (CORBA vs DCOM), no clear business case
- Aberdeen recommends pragmatic DOT adoption for Internet competitive-advantage applications
- **Prescience outcome:** Both CORBA and DCOM were superseded by web services (SOAP/REST) and later microservices — the "live wire" thesis proved correct but via different standards than anticipated
