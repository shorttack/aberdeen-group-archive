## Overview

Aberdeen Group vendor profile of DynaFlo Systems and its PushMail product. Aberdeen field research found ISPs growing sales while losing money. The report argues ISPs must target non-early-adopter businesses (9 out of 10 businesses not yet on the Internet in 1997) with enhanced services like PushMail to survive. Endorses PushMail as the best price/performance Internet email solution for ISPs.

## Key Finding: ISP Market Dynamics (1997)

- ISPs growing at "rapid pace" — barely filling orders
- Many ISPs growing sales and *losing money simultaneously*
- For every 1 business on Internet, 9 had not yet connected
- Aberdeen: ISPs must address broader market beyond early adopters

## Outcome

The ISP market consolidated dramatically by 2000. DynaFlo and its PushMail product did not survive. Free web-based email (Hotmail acquired by Microsoft 1997, Yahoo Mail 1997) eliminated the paid ISP email value proposition.
