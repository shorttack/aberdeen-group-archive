DynaSoft’s BoKS Family:
A Pragmatic Choice for Single Sign-On
Preface
Information Systems (IS) decision makers charged with planning and
deploying Single Sign-On (SSO) for the enterprise are ﬁnding slide-ware
instead of demonstrable product and reference accounts. Looking for key
suppliers to assist their efforts, IS decision makers tell Aberdeen that SSO
appears to be out of reach.
But, as the enterprise becomes more geographically dispersed for responding to
local business conditions, make-the-money managers are demanding that IS
eliminate the complexity of multiple passwords and credentials that users must
deal with — and simplify access to information. Already vexed by high
expenses for managing non-interoperable user proﬁle databases across unlike
computing platforms with complex layered applications, veteran IS managers
are looking for SSO to simplify IS procedures while reducing IS operations
expense.
In this Proﬁle, Aberdeen provides an overview of a key supplier that early
adopters are now relying on for a pragmatic SSO backbone-framework with
multiple end-point security solutions that simply plug-in. This Proﬁle provides
decision makers with an overview of DynaSoft’s:
• Approach to single sign-on;
• Business partnerships with two market-leading suppliers;
• BoKS products; and,
• Uncovers the experiences of its users.
Avoiding Mistakes — And Moving Forward
In theory, SSO is supposed to provide the ability for employees, and hopefully
customers, partners and suppliers, to use a single credential for logon and
access to authorized applications, networks and computing platforms.
Resolute about ﬁnding information when needed, users and business managers
have little understanding or empathy for IS when the IS infrastructure is not
working — especially when users cannot logon. Smart IS managers are
insisting on reliability and fault-resilience for their SSO deployments in order
to avoid business disruption. And, experienced IS managers state that they must
avoid non-interoperable user proﬁles and registry databases — if the IS
operations budget is to be brought under control.
Looking to avoid mistakes of the past, veterans are demanding that suppliers
provide common authentication, authorization and auditing functions operating
across multiple technology domains for:
• Providing reasonable assurance that critical information is protected;
• Providing a reusable framework for evolving business needs; and
• Without requiring a cadre of highly-skilled IS staff to implement and
maintain.
Market Positioning
DynaSoft has been delivering well-regarded security solutions since 1984 when
the company was focused on providing solutions for Unix multi-user
environments. Expanding its product capabilities the company delivered
security solutions for client-server environments, most notably between Unix

servers and PC client desktops. Now, DynaSoft is delivering SSO through its
BoKS product solutions (see Figure 1) for meeting users needs.
Operating throughout the world, the company is small enough to work directly
and closely with its users in order to ensure successful deployments. Moreover,
the company integrates lessons it learns from each customer engagement into
subsequent product releases. As a result, DynaSoft and its BoKS solutions are
providing users with pragmatic insight into best-practices instead of projections
based solely on engineering laboratory analysis.
Recognizing the value that BoKS provides users, Hewlett Packard and Sun
Microsystems recently incorporated DynaSoft’s security technology and
products into their own security offerings. Hewlett Packard chose BoKS
technology for use with its Praesidium Single Sign-On solution. And, Sun
Microsystems chose DynaSoft as a strategic partner for both technology and
products for its Sun Security Manager offerings.
The result of these alliances for IS decision makers is that DynaSoft’s
technology and product solutions are gaining wider market acceptance, larger
channels of distribution and the professional services needed for larger-scale
support and deployment.

BoKS and SSO
DynaSoft implements two approaches to Single Sign-On, one called Secure
SSO (SSSO) and the other called Single Sign-On (SSO). The major difference
between the two includes the computing environments supported and the extent
of protection for information in-transit over network connections.
Secure Single Sign-On (SSSO)
SSSO is well suited for deployment for on-line interactions with suppliers,
partners and customers over the Internet, or anywhere on the enterprise network
where critical business processes and information protection require stronger
security. SSSO is supported for all computing environments that its products
support.
SSSO and SSO currently implement a common approach for authentication
credentials through a Personal Security Device (PSD) on desktops. Used for all
initial logon operations, BoKS PSDs are independent of speciﬁc credential
implementations. For example, credential types that can be used with BoKS
PSDs could include RSA private-keys, smart cards, digital signatures, X.509
certiﬁcates and any other end-point authentication solution.
SSSO uses RSA-based cryptography engines by default for protecting
information in-transit over the network. But, any cryptographic-engine desired
or required by national import and export control laws can be snapped into the
BoKS framework for SSSO by DynaSoft using its ToolBoKS product.
A light weight Certiﬁcate Authority (CA) function is provided with the standard
BoKS Manager solution. In addition, large enterprises that require multi-level
intra-company authorizations with suppliers, partners and customers are using
CA services that can integrate with BoKS, including Entrust Technologies’
products.
Single Sign-On (SSO)
SSO is well suited for integrating legacy computing environments and for
deployment on enterprise networks where less critical business processes and
information do not require cryptography for protecting information in-transit.
Also, SSO provides IS with the ability to integrate diverse computing
environments into one manageable whole, including: 1) IBM’s MVS-based
environments using PassTickets, 2) Novell’s IPX/SPX environments, and 3)
Microsoft’s LAN Manager and Netbui connected environments. Further,
support for kerberos and DCE based authentication deployments is planned for
a future release.

SSO does not use cryptography for protecting information in-ﬂight, but does
rely on crypto-protected software-containers (PSDs) operated on desktop
clients for ensuring the integrity and uniqueness of user credentials. The same
tokens used for SSSO can be deployed with SSO for minimizing IS expenses.
Core Products
DynaSoft’s products include a framework designed for distributed computing
environments, end-point security solutions that can be operated seamlessly with
packaged applications and tools needed for integrating custom applications.
Packaged as BoKS Desktop, BoKS Connect, BoKS Manager and ToolBoKS,
these products are deployed as a classic networked authorization-server
architecture (see Figure 2) for providing reliable, scalable and secure Single
Sign-On.
BoKS Desktop
BoKS Desktop provides assurance that users can be uniquely identiﬁed in order
to appropriately segregate classes of people from sensitive information and
applications — as deemed appropriate by empowered and prudent managers.
This desktop module is transparent to applications and users, making security
for the desktop easier for IS to implement and without disgruntling users.
BoKS Desktop currently operates on the following:
• Microsoft’s MS DOS, Windows 3.1 and Windows for Work Groups; and
• Microsoft’s Windows 95 and Windows NT Desktop.
In addition to Single Sign-On, the security services provided by BoKS Desktop
include: 1) transparent protection of information on local disks through user-
selected encryption, 2) event-logging for monitoring and audit, ﬁle access
controls, integrity checks on executable ﬁles and Dynamic Link Libraries
(DLLs), 3) automatic locking of the desktop due to user inactivity, and 4) the
elimination of boot devices and boot-paths from compromising local security
controls.
Employing multiple options for authenticating users, BoKS Desktop currently
supports passwords, challenge-response solutions, ISO smart cards,
cryptographically-protected software-only credentials (PSDs) and digital
signatures conforming to standards including X.509.
BoKS Connect
BoKS Connect provides IS with pre-tested applications that operate seamlessly
with DynaSoft’s SSO and SSSO solutions. Unlike most security
implementations requiring close cooperation between independent software
suppliers, BoKS Connect is produced by DynaSoft using off-the-shelf binaries
from other suppliers. The result for IS decision makers is that BoKS Connect
promises consistent, timely and secured application projects can become the
norm — not the exception.

BoKS Connect can be operated on the same servers that BoKS Manager
supports. But, the additional value delivered by BoKS Connect is that when
installed on desired application servers on the network, BoKS Manager does
not have to be deployed, maintained and managed for every platform on the
network.
BoKS Connect modules for Sybase, Oracle, Unix connectivity, networking
tools and other software packages are already deployed by users. DynaSoft
anticipates providing support for SQL Server, WWW software integration and
other popular packaged application environments in coming releases of BoKS
Connect.
BoKS Manager
BoKS Manager provides all the services of a distributed authentication,
authorization and auditing server and the tools for cost-effectively managing

security services across the enterprise. This includes managing a variety of user
credentials, platforms, applications, networks and certiﬁcate and key
management services. This product currently operates on the popular Unix-
based platforms, including:
• Digital’s Unix; • Hewlett Packard’s HP-UX;
• IBM’s AIX; and • Sun’s Solaris.
The registry services of BoKs Manager implement a light-weight, replicated
and fault-resilient directory of security subjects and objects that are easily
managed across the enterprise from a central location. Protected by strong
encryption, this database is seamlessly populated from standard Unix NIS user-
registries on the network.
Currently in ﬁeld test, the version of BoKS Manager supporting Microsoft’s NT
Server is planned to ensure interoperability with NT-based user accounts. This
product will populate the BoKS security-directory from standard NT
application servers and enable IS to consistently manage authorizations for user
accounts deﬁned on NT Servers.
Authorization services provided by BoKs Manager implement class, role and
rule-based access to applications, systems and networks by users, other
applications and even systems. By providing authorization for users based upon
identity, time, location, logon-services, network-routes and applications, BoKs
Manager ensures that classes of dislike users and systems can be appropriately
segregated without impacting business operations. Using a standard web-
browser as the administrative interface, the product provides IS with the means
of lowering training and operating expenses while ensuring consistency across
a wide variety of unlike operating environments.
As an additional bonus, BoKS Manager integrates with HP’s OpenView, Sun’s
SunNet Manager and with Tivoli’s TME in order to provide IS with the ability
to manage security through existing management framework solutions.
Tool BoKS
This product provides the entire set of application programming interfaces
(APIs) and libraries needed for integrating applications with the BoKS family
of products. Initially intended for adapting low-level networking code to
operate with BoKs, ToolBoKS is now used by DynaSoft and its partners for
producing server-side middleware that is then embedded in BoKS Connect
modules.
And to meet demand from its users, DynaSoft plans to introduce ToolBoKS as
a supported product in order that custom and legacy applications can join
Single Sign-On deployments as easily as packaged applications are being
integrated.
What BoKS Users Say
The most compelling reasons to consider evaluating DynaSoft comes from its
users. DynaSoft’s BoKS solutions are already deployed by major enterprises,
including: BankBoston; BP Oil; Citibank, Chase Manhattan Bank, Indian
Telecom, Mannesmann Mobilfunk, Merrill Lynch, SmithKline Beecham,
Telecom Italia, Telmex and Telstra among many others.
According to users, BoKS is reliable, seasoned and improving with age. The
common ﬁndings encountered by Aberdeen, include:
• Full project deployments from start to ﬁnish in less than sixty days;
• Moderate and low-skilled administrators managing hundreds to
thousands of networked nodes and tens of thousands of users; and
• No impact on existing applications and networks.
The most consistent praise from users is that DynaSoft works doggedly to
ensure their success. Decision makers consistently give DynaSoft high-marks
for helping them deploy important projects ahead of schedule. More
importantly, decision makers say that BoKS is a framework solution they can
rely on, across platforms and applications, and through technology and business
change — over time.
Conclusions
Based on its architecture alone, BoKS would be capable of being deployed out-
of-the-box for distributed IS operations across multiple client-server
environments. But, capabilities do not make for success. Execution and
customer-servicing that is then folded into subsequent product releases counts
for more with hard-nosed IS decision makers. According to its users, DynaSoft
delivers consistently.
However, no product can meet all user expectations and BoKS is no exception.
There are always improvements that can be made, including the forthcoming
support for NT application servers. Despite its limitations, users consistently
recommend DynaSoft and BoKs to other users.

And, by endorsing BoKS technology and DynaSoft’s products, HP and Sun are
consolidating support for one of the few security solutions that users willingly
and consistently praise and recommend. By harvesting BoKS for their own
security programs and solutions, HP and Sun are integrating DynaSoft’s user
successes into their SSO solutions and security frameworks.
For IS decision makers charged with implementing SSO with reasonable
security controls that can cost-effectively reduce the risk of integrating
suppliers, partners, customers and Web-enabled applications, DynaSoft’s users
are among the best and most reliable testament.
DynaSoft’s users will happily tell you that you cannot go wrong by
standardizing on DynaSoft and BoKS.
