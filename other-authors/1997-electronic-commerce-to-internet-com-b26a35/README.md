## Overview

Aberdeen Group Executive White Paper declaring in November 1997 that organizations are "on the threshold of a revolution in Electronic Commerce." Introduces "Internet Commerce (IC)" as the fusion of EC and Internet technologies. Provides an analytical framework to: (1) understand virtual channel impacts on business equilibrium, (2) assess EC/IC supplier technologies, and (3) map organizational requirements to supplier functionality. Also explores the EDI-to-Internet transition.

## Key Prediction Outcomes

- **E-commerce growth:** Aberdeen said it *will* grow; it grew from ~$500M (1997) to $6.38 trillion (2024 projected)
- **Virtual channels:** Confirmed as primary disruptors of retail, travel, media, financial services
- **EDI transition:** Partially correct — EDI persists in legacy supply chains but web-based B2B dominant for new implementations
