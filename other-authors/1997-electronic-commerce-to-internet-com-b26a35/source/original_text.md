Abstract: White Paper
Electronic Commerce to Internet Commerce:
The Evolution of The InterNetworked Enterprise
Recent Aberdeen research has concluded that we are currently
on the threshold of a revolution in Electronic Commerce (EC).
The real question is not: "Will it grow?" It is "How fast will it
grow?" An advancing wave of EC automation, enabled by the
rapid adoption of Internet technologies, simultaneously creates
tremendous opportunities and risks for organizations. Internet
Commerce (IC) is a term used to describe the fusion of EC
and Internet technologies.
This Executive White Paper explores the benefits of Internet
Commerce, its relationship to Electronic Data Interchange
(EDI), and the impacts on the business processes which are
both internal and external to the organization. An analytic
framework is provided to: understand the impact of virtual
channels on the business equilibrium: assess and evaluate EC
and IC supplier technologies; and map organizational
requirements to supplier product functionality.
David Alschuler

### Prescience Detail

Aberdeen's November 1997 analysis proved exceptionally prescient:

1. **E-commerce growth confirmed:** Global e-commerce grew from approximately $500M in 1997 to a projected $6.38 trillion by 2024 — arguably the most accurate technology growth prediction of the era. (Source: [The Fulfillment Lab](https://www.thefulfillmentlab.com/blog/history-of-ecommerce); [42 Signals](https://www.42signals.com/blog/the-evolution-of-e-commerce-analysis-from-its-origins-to-today/))

2. **"How fast" framing correct:** Aberdeen's framing that the question was growth rate, not growth itself, was exactly right. Growth was explosive and ongoing.

3. **Virtual channels disrupted business equilibrium:** Aberdeen's prediction that virtual channels would create new business equilibria proved correct — Amazon disrupted retail, Expedia disrupted travel, digital distribution disrupted music and film.

4. **EDI transition:** EDI persists in legacy supply chain contexts (Walmart, automotive) but web-based B2B integration and API commerce replaced it for new implementations. Partially correct.

5. **Internet Commerce as EC+Internet fusion:** The term "Internet Commerce" was accurate — modern e-commerce is precisely the fusion Aberdeen described.

**Assessment:** Prescience 5/5. This may be one of the most accurately predictive Aberdeen studies of the era.
