## Abstract
Aberdeen Group examines Forte Software's Forte Release 3.0, Forte Express, and Forte Conductor — a second-generation High-end Application Development Environment (HADE) suite. The profile evaluates Forte's capabilities in application partitioning, multitier client-server development, Internet/Intranet support, and workflow automation. Aberdeen concludes that Forte delivers significant long-term value for enterprise-critical applications and rates it a leader in second-generation HADE capabilities.

## Historical Significance
Forte Software (NASDAQ: FRTE) was acquired by Sun Microsystems in August 1999 for approximately $540 million in stock — one of the largest enterprise software acquisitions of that era. Sun valued Forte specifically for its Enterprise Application Integration capabilities and Java/web tooling, precisely the areas Aberdeen highlighted as strategic differentiators in this profile. Sun itself was acquired by Oracle in 2010.

## Prescience Assessment: 4/5
Aberdeen's assessment was highly prescient. The identification of Forte's Internet integration strategy and EAI capabilities as long-term differentiators was validated by the 1999 Sun acquisition. The report correctly predicted that CORBA/IIOP and Java integration would be critical competitive factors.

## Key Findings
- Forte Software revenue grew 130% YoY to $43.5M (9-month FY1997)
- Aberdeen rated Forte as the leader in second-generation HADE capabilities
- Key differentiators: application partitioning, workflow integration, full lifecycle support
- Competitors: Uniface and Dynasty Technologies (only other second-gen HADE vendors)
- Distribution: direct sales; Digital Equipment as strategic reseller
