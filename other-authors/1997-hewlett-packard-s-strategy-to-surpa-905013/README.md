## Abstract
Aberdeen Group examines HP's Simplified Enterprise Computing (SEC) strategy, a joint HP/Microsoft initiative to capture the enterprise NT market from Compaq. The paper analyzes HP's enterprise advantages (service infrastructure, Microsoft partnership, VPA pricing, IA-64 co-development) and Compaq's weaknesses (channel-centric, limited enterprise service). Aberdeen predicts HP could achieve NT market leadership by late 1998, and analyzes the Compaq/Tandem acquisition as Compaq's strategic response.

## Historical Significance
Aberdeen's prediction of HP surpassing Compaq in NT did not materialize independently; instead, HP acquired Compaq for ~$25B in May 2002. Aberdeen correctly predicted Dell's disruption of Compaq (Dell surpassed Compaq by 2001), the Tandem acquisition dynamics, and the Merced/IA-64 importance — though Itanium arrived in 2001 (delayed) and ultimately failed commercially.

## Prescience Assessment: 3/5
- CORRECT: Dell pressure on Compaq (confirmed by 2001 Dell market leadership)
- CORRECT: Tandem acquisition analysis and synergy assessment
- CORRECT: IBM competitive-alternative NT strategy (Notes vs Exchange, etc.)
- INCORRECT: HP achieving NT market leadership by late 1998
- INCORRECT: Merced timeline expectations
