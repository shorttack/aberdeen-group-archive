## Abstract
Aberdeen Group evaluates HP's newly announced V-Class HP 9000 enterprise SMP servers. Based on Convex supercomputing crossbar technology, the V2200 promises ~50% OLTP improvement. Aberdeen examines HP's roadmap through 1999, competitive positioning vs Sun/IBM/DEC/NT, and recommends enterprise IS executives evaluate V-Class for high-end UNIX production workloads and mainframe replacement.

## Historical Significance
The HP V-Class used the HyperPlane crossbar backplane derived from HP's 1995 Convex acquisition. The V2200 shipped on schedule in November 1997. The HP 9000 line was discontinued in 2003, succeeded by HP Integrity (Itanium) servers. HP merged with Compaq in 2002 for ~$25B. The Itanium roadmap proved unsuccessful commercially; Intel discontinued Itanium in 2021.

## Prescience Assessment: 3/5
Aberdeen was correct on V2200 delivery schedule and mainframe replacement trend. Aberdeen's dismissal of NT for enterprise production proved wrong — Windows 2000/Server 2003 captured significant enterprise workloads by 2001-2003. The Merced/IA-64 prediction for 1999 was delayed to 2001.
