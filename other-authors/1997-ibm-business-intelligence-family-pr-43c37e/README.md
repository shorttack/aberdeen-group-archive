## Abstract
Aberdeen Group profiles IBM's Business Intelligence Family: Information Warehouse (enterprise DW), Visual Warehouse (10-100GB data marts), Intelligent Decision Server (OLAP, evolved from Metaphor DIS), and Intelligent Miner (data mining), all built on DB2 with Internet-enablement via Net.data. Aberdeen evaluates IBM's strategy to expand decision support from corporate analysts to line-of-business and remote users via network computing.

## Historical Significance
This profile captures IBM's BI strategy at a pivotal moment. Key historical outcomes:
- IBM acquired Cognos (cited as third-party tool) for ~$5B in 2008; Cognos replaced most IBM BI tools
- IBM acquired SPSS in 2009 for $1.2B; SPSS Modeler replaced Intelligent Miner
- IBM Db2 remains active in 2026
- The prediction of BI democratization "to the masses" was validated by Tableau (2003), Power BI (2015), and cloud data warehouses (Redshift, Snowflake)

## Prescience Assessment: 4/5
Highly prescient on the direction of BI democratization and the importance of data mining growth. Aberdeen's framework of expanding decision support "to the masses" precisely describes the BI market trajectory over the following 25+ years.
