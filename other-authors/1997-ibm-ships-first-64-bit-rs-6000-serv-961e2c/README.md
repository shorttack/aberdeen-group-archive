## Overview

This data package archives Aberdeen Group analyst Tom Willmott's brief **Announcement Profile** documenting IBM's October 6, 1997 general availability of the **RS/6000 S70** (code-named **Raven**) — IBM's first 64-bit RS/6000 server.

> **Note:** This is an abstract/announcement brief (1,139 characters), significantly shorter than full Aberdeen profiles. The piece references a fuller review of IBM's Unix market position and strategy.

**Key facts at announcement:**
- Model: RS/6000 S70 (Model 7017)
- Processor: IBM RS64 (Apache) — IBM's first 64-bit commercial RISC processor
- Configuration: 4-to-12-way SMP with PCI bus
- Performance: Estimated 18,000 Transactions Per Minute (TPC benchmark)
- Aberdeen had correctly forecast the Phase 1 (PowerPC 604e F50 in May 1997) and Phase 2 (64-bit S70 in October 1997) product strategy

## Key Findings

- IBM S70 (Raven) delivered 18,000 estimated TPC TPM with RS64 processor in a 4-12 way SMP configuration
- Aberdeen correctly predicted the two-phase 1997 RS/6000 product rollout (604e F50 in May, RS64 S70 in October)
- RS64 line: discontinued 2001 when POWER4 merged PowerPC-AS and PowerPC instruction sets
- AIX/Power Systems: AIX outlasted all major Unix competitors; AIX 7.3 supported through 2033
- Sun Microsystems (primary competitor): acquired by Oracle 2010; Solaris wind-down followed
