Abstract: Proﬁle
Informix Software
MetaCube 4.0: Relational OLAP and RDBMS
Create an Integrated DSS Environment
Emerging customer-centric applications will demand far more
of today's data warehouses and data marts.
Information systems executives and line-of-business managers
already face a need to build data warehouses and data marts on
an RDBMS foundation and to thoroughly analyze the contents
of these on-line repositories of corporate information. Routine,
large-scale decision-support access will require more: a
foundation of greater database speed and scalability, a linked
and RDBMS-leveraging decision-support environment, and a
critically important "surround" strategy that combines
decision-support systems with RDBMS administration for
greater manageability. Be-cause Relational OLAP implies a
growing, dynamic, decision-support environment, enterprise
planners must determine whether the Relational OLAP tool is
able to adapt quickly and semi-automatically to numerous
changes, particularly to increases in user populations and data
vol-umes, and to alterations of data models and business
models. These requirements, Aberdeen believes, will become
more important as enterprises increasingly adopt intranet
strategies.
This Proﬁle describes the forthcoming release of Informix
MetaCube 4.0, a Relational OLAP envi-ronment that leverages
the power and performance of the Informix Dynamic Scalable
Architec-ture (DSA) by intelligently sharing critical and
potentially resource intensive tasks between the RDBMS and
the MetaCube Relational OLAP environment.
Bob Moran

## Prescience Detail

### Assessment: 6/10 — Moderately Prescient

This brief product profile makes several market predictions that proved correct, though the specific vendor (Informix/MetaCube) did not survive.

**Accurate Predictions:**

1. **Intranet as ROLAP driver** — Aberdeen's prediction that intranet adoption would accelerate ROLAP requirements proved exactly correct. Web-based BI delivery became the dominant model by 2000–2003, with every major BI vendor (Business Objects, Cognos, MicroStrategy) releasing web/intranet-native reporting by 2001.

2. **RDBMS-native ROLAP as winning approach** — Aberdeen's implicit preference for RDBMS-leveraging ROLAP (vs. standalone MOLAP) was validated by the market. In-database analytics embedded in SQL Server, Oracle, and DB2 became the dominant enterprise BI architecture by 2010.

3. **User population scalability as key criterion** — Aberdeen's prediction that scalability to large user populations would be a critical ROLAP requirement proved accurate; cloud BI platforms (Tableau Server, Looker, Power BI) now serve millions of concurrent users.

4. **Customer-centric app demands on data infrastructure** — The rise of CRM analytics, e-commerce personalization, and 360-degree customer views (all driven by "customer-centric applications") confirmed Aberdeen's prediction about data warehouse demand growth.

**What Didn't Materialize:**

- Informix MetaCube itself was discontinued after IBM acquired Informix's database division in 2001 for $1 billion. The product was not carried forward in the IBM BI portfolio, which instead relied on Cognos (acquired 2008) and DB2 OLAP Server (Essbase).
- The "surround strategy" as a specific product category didn't emerge; instead, unified BI platforms absorbed both DSS and RDBMS administration functions.

**Overall:** Good directional accuracy on market trends; poor outcome for the specific vendor/product profiled.
