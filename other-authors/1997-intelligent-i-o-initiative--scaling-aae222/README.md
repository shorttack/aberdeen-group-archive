## Abstract

Aberdeen Group examines the I2O (Intelligent I/O) initiative, an industry specification led by the I2O Special Interest Group (SIG). The paper argues that I2O will be an important server capability requiring "I2O-Ready" labeling to become a procurement requirement for IS planners upgrading to new OS releases (Microsoft, Novell, SCO) or new Intel architecture servers. Aberdeen examines the performance and scalability benefits of I2O, describes how the I2O SIG members are working to create a new industry standard, and analyzes the impact IS executives should expect.

## Context & Outcome

The I2O specification, despite backing from Intel, Compaq, HP, Microsoft, Novell, and many others, never achieved the industry adoption Aberdeen predicted. I2O suffered from implementation complexity, performance overhead concerns, and limited OS support outside of NetWare. The I2O SIG dissolved in the early 2000s. The I/O bottleneck problem was ultimately addressed through different means: PCI-Express (PCIe) for bus bandwidth, multicore CPUs absorbing I/O processing, and modern driver frameworks (Windows Driver Model, Linux device drivers).
