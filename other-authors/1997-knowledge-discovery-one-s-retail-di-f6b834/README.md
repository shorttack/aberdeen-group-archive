## Abstract

Aberdeen Group profiles Knowledge Discovery One (KD1) and its Retail Discovery Suite (RDS), a data mining platform targeting retail professionals. The RDS architecture eliminates much of the labor traditionally required for retail data mining by providing the analytical depth of data mining with automated parallel processing that scales up with more data. Unlike competitors that require dedicated data mining specialists, RDS is designed to deliver insights directly to daily retail decision-makers. The profile highlights KD1's differentiation in reducing implementation burden for retail point-of-sale (POS) data analysis.

## Context & Outcome

Knowledge Discovery One (KD1) was a small vendor targeting the retail data mining market with its Retail Discovery Suite. The company did not survive as an independent entity — no record of it operating past the late 1990s exists. However, the market opportunity Aberdeen identified was accurate: retail data mining and customer analytics became a multi-billion-dollar sector. The broader data mining software market grew from approximately $50 million in 1996 to $800 million by 2000, validating Aberdeen's directional view. Modern successors include SAS Retail Analytics, Oracle Retail, and AI-driven tools from companies like Salesforce and SAP.
