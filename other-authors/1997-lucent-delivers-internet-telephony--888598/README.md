## Abstract

Aberdeen Group evaluates Lucent Technologies' Internet Telephony Server SP, described as the first scalable, carrier-grade, turnkey gateway platform for routing voice and fax traffic over IP networks. The study positions Lucent as uniquely capable of addressing service providers' needs for high-quality, large-scale internet telephony by leveraging Lucent's call processing expertise and elemedia's voice compression technology. Aberdeen highlights the product's ability to enable POTS-to-POTS calling without behavior change for end users, support for both ISPs and traditional telcos (RBOCs, CLECs, IXCs), and scalability starting from 24 ports. The brief identifies internet telephony as an emerging market with significant cost reduction potential for long distance and international calls.

## Context & Outcome

Aberdeen's assessment proved remarkably prescient on the macro trend. Internet telephony did become mass-market — Skype (2003), Vonage (2004), and eventually built-in VoIP in mobile phones made IP-based voice universal. Lucent's specific trajectory was more complex: Lucent merged with Alcatel in 2006 to form Alcatel-Lucent, which was subsequently acquired by Nokia in 2016. The elemedia subsidiary was eventually absorbed. The Internet Telephony Server SP product line was superseded by next-generation softswitch architectures. Xedia Corporation (covered in Study 5) was acquired by Lucent itself in 1999 for $246 million, reflecting Lucent's aggressive internet infrastructure buildout. Aberdeen's prediction that service providers would be key VoIP adopters was correct — all major carriers eventually deployed IP-based switching.
