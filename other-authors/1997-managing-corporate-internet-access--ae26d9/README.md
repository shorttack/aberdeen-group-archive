## Abstract

Aberdeen Group examines the challenge of managing corporate broadband Internet access and evaluates Xedia Corporation's Access Point solution. The paper argues that raw bandwidth investment alone is insufficient — IT administrators need tools to prioritize, allocate, and dynamically manage bandwidth across users and applications. Aberdeen presents Xedia's Access Point as addressing this gap with Class Based Queuing, Intelligent Queuing (IQ) software, and deep buffering at 50% below cost of connectivity-only solutions. The paper covers bandwidth management requirements (scalability, guaranteed access, flexibility, traffic monitoring), TCP/IP's inability to prioritize applications, and Xedia's Frame Relay support with planned ATM support.

## Context & Outcome

Xedia Corporation was acquired by Lucent Technologies for $246 million in August 1999 — a strong validation of Aberdeen's positive assessment. Lucent acquired Xedia to complement technology it had inherited from Ascend Communications. The Access Point product line was absorbed into Lucent's portfolio. The broader bandwidth management market Aberdeen identified evolved into the QoS and SD-WAN sectors; Cisco, Juniper, and later SD-WAN vendors (VeloCloud, Silver Peak, Meraki) captured this market. Frame Relay itself declined as MPLS and broadband internet replaced it. Aberdeen's prediction that internet-centric business strategy would require managed bandwidth access was fully validated.
