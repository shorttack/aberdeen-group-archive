**Preface**

Dun & Bradstreet Software (D&B Software) has created a best practices-driven enterprise-application environment that can readily adapt to meet an enterprise's existing business model, yet is flexible enough to permit ongoing change and continuous process improvement. The underlying technology and architectural design of SmartStream Distributed Enterprise (SmartStream DE) is meant to support a computing and business model that closely reflects how numerous Fortune-class enterprises seek to align and distribute their business and information technology (IT) infrastructures. This vision and model could well be the unique and differentiating market imperative that will propel D&B Software into broader high-end Client-Server Solution (CSS) opportunities.

D&B Software has aggressively acknowledged the customer- and market-driven requirements for open systems client-server computing. Aberdeen believes that D&B Software has taken a unique approach with its fully distributed application architecture for enterprise-worthy business solutions. Currently, the company is delivering SmartStream DE, a full suite of line-of-business client-server applications for finance, manufacturing/distribution, procurement, human resources and decision support.

**Executive Summary**

Starting with a clean slate and armed with the knowledge of twenty-five successful years of enterprise applications experience, D&B Software began the task of creating a complete new architecture for their SmartStream DE product suite. Design goals included: full exploitation of distributed database technologies; incorporation of best business practices; adaptable workflow integration; deployment flexibility; transactional application performance; reduction in network traffic; and ease of installation and customization to the customer's business model.

D&B Software's vision for distributed client-server computing is to provide software solutions and services that enable an enterprise to maximize the business benefits of timely, responsive, adaptable access to information and changing business requirements. The SmartStream distributed architecture is a cooperative client-server design in which business-application logic and transactions are primarily server based. D&B Software has taken an open-systems approach to the development of their CSS. Moreover, the company's single database focus has allowed it to exploit optimized utilization of critical database features and functions. The SmartStream environment integrates several components: workflow, common services, enabling technologies and interactive support services.

**Market Position**

Dun & Bradstreet Software is a market-leading global supplier of packaged business applications that are installed at more than 10,000 customer sites. The company was originally formed by the merger and acquisition of the top two application suppliers of the 1970's and 1980's, McCormack & Dodge and Management Science America. D&B Software continues to maintain a sizable mainframe and VAX installed base, which operates the Millennium (M) and Expert (E) series of applications. Contrary to the popular market perception that suppliers are abandoning mainframes and their product sets that run on them, D&B Software has resolutely committed to supporting and enhancing their mainframe M-series and E-series products as long as there is demand to do so from their customers. Aberdeen notes, in this regard, that install-based maintenance renewals continue at an impressive rate of over 80% per year.

Since deploying the first SmartStream applications in 1992, D&B Software has steadily expanded functionality, as well as seen increases in customer and market acceptance of its CSS. Competing today with a fully integrated suite of applications, D&B Software has been able to garner over $100 Million in SmartStream revenues during the past fiscal year. In Aberdeen's view, a product ramp-up performance of that magnitude qualifies the SmartStream portion of the total business to be counted among the top tier suppliers of CSS applications available today.

**Key Technologies/Products**

D&B Software has taken an open system approach to the development of their CSS. Development is done with PowerSoft's PowerBuilder, C++ and SQL Server (Sybase & Microsoft) stored procedures; Microsoft's Access and Cognos' PowerPlay are used to handle decision support; with Visual Basic employed for user interface tasks. The company's single database focus has allowed it to exploit optimized utilization of critical database features and functions. Among these are: native call-level interfaces; stored procedures and triggers; and replication technology.

**The SmartStream Environment**

SmartStream DE v4.0 is comprised of: Sybase System v11.1, Microsoft SQL Server v6.5, Sybase PowerBuilder v4.02, Cognos PowerPlay v4.1, and Microsoft Access v2.0.

Supported platforms:
- Hardware: Intel PCs (Windows, Win95, WindowsNT), Data General AViiON (DG/UX), Digital AlphaServer (Digital UNIX, WindowsNT), HP9000 (HP/UX), IBM RS6000 (AIX), Intel (WindowsNT), SUN Sparc (Solaris)
- Database: Sybase System 11.1, Microsoft SQL Server 6.5
- Network: TCP/IP

The CSS environment consists of four integrated components:
1. **Workflow** — Production Workflow (Activity Manager), Administrative Workflow (Intelligent Agents + Activity Manager), Ad Hoc Workflow
2. **Common services** — Query/Reporter (MS Access + Cognos PowerPlay), security, VIM/MAPI email, job scheduler, transaction distribution
3. **Enabling technologies** — Windows-based GUI and SQL Server (Sybase/Microsoft) including replication services
4. **Support services** — SmartStream Assistant for distributed network systems management

**A Unique Distributed Architectural Model**

Contemporary businesses are already functioning in a distributed manner. SmartStream's architecture takes this into account and provides for the distribution of any or all business processes across the enterprise IT landscape. This design supports local autonomy of transactional work while still maintaining cooperative links to the central corporate structure for consolidation reporting and decision support.

Sybase's Replication Server technology is employed to ensure that enterprise critical business process and security information are consistently propagated to all distributed locations.

**Competition**

D&B Software's SmartStream DE competes with Oracle Applications, PeopleSoft, SAP and Systems Software Associates (SSA).

Aberdeen field experience indicates that while other suppliers have had difficulty with their Sybase implementations, D&B Software has not and has become the largest supplier in the Sybase SQL Server ISV market. The company was the first to field an enterprise-worthy deployment of a competitive CSS.

Several competitors offer multiplatform RDBMS options and this could present an advantage to suppliers where supporting other RDBMS products is a requirement.

Aberdeen believes that SmartStream DE is best suited for dynamic enterprises — those faced with continual, competition-driven requirements to change and adapt their business processes.

**Financials**

Dun & Bradstreet Software (D&B Software), formed by the merger and acquisition of McCormack & Dodge and Management Science America in 1990, is a wholly owned subsidiary of The Dun & Bradstreet Corporation.

D&B Software CEO Doug MacIntyre reports revenues for fiscal year ending November 30 were more than $350 Million — 30% from outside the United States. SmartStream CSS products brought in revenues of over $100 Million, with 40% from new customers or 150% growth over 1994 results.

This 1995 resurgence of D&B Software places them on a strong footing as an independent software developer and supplier as Dun & Bradstreet Corporation prepares to divest themselves of the software unit.

**Aberdeen Conclusions**

Aberdeen finds that D&B Software has decisively responded to customer and market demands for application architectures that are based on open systems and client-server oriented technologies with SmartStream DE. In the no-holds-barred ERP solutions market, SmartStream DE is proving to be a very competitive CSS offering.

The company maintains that support of SQL Server, from Sybase and Microsoft, is sufficient to satisfy the majority of its target market. However, prospective customers that have made strategic commitments to other RDBMS solutions may find it difficult to rationalize and justify adding yet another database to their IT infrastructure in order to take advantage of SmartStream DE benefits. Aberdeen believes this issue will continue to confront D&B Software as long as it primary competitors offer multiplatform RDBMS options.

Aberdeen encourages D&B Software to provide a more straight-forward approach to legacy-system interoperability. Little is provided to support either concurrent operation or real-time integration with host-based systems — even D&B Software's own Millennium Series applications.

The SmartStream DE product and D&B Software are positioned to take a leadership position in the growing market for fully distributed business-process-driven ERP application suites.

Aberdeen believes that organizations planning to acquire and deploy client-server based application solutions as strategic initiatives in their competitive business arsenal would be well served by seriously considering D&B Software SmartStream DE in their evaluation process.
