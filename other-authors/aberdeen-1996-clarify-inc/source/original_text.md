## Original Document Text

### Market Position

Customer interaction software (CIS) is a class of front-line business applications used by leading organizations in all industries to acquire and retain long-term, profitable customers. CIS covers a wide range of applications, including customer service, sales force automation, telemarketing/telesales, helpdesk and quality assurance. What makes CIS so attractive is its ability to help companies increase revenues — organizations using CIS are pulling ahead of their rivals in terms of customer satisfaction and retention, new business development, and profitability.

Clarify Inc. provides enterprise-scale customer interaction software (CIS) applications that give user organizations a competitive edge in the areas of customer service and field support; sales and marketing; and product development and improvement. In the highly competitive CIS market, Clarify has emerged as a leader with revenues increasing over 100% on an annualized basis.

Clarify has secured this enviable position for the following reasons:

- **Integrated, best-of-breed CIS suite** — Clarify has a wide range of CIS applications including customer support, internal helpdesk, quality control, logistics and SFA. Clarify's decision to acquire Metropolis Software for its highly-respected SFA products — rather than develop mediocre SFA software internally — demonstrates this strategy.
- **A safe, reliable solution** — Aberdeen research indicates that the company has achieved 99% uptime with its customer base.
- **Linchpin accounts** — In IT and telecommunications, Clarify clients include Microsoft, Cisco and HP, as well as MCI and Sprint Spectrum. The influence of these firms gives Clarify the opportunity to be viewed as the de facto CIS standard for their business partners and resellers.
- **Out-of-the-box functionality** — Clarify has customizable applications for immediate use. No months of development before deriving benefits.
- **Scalability and performance** — Products are architected for enterprise-wide deployment.
- **Heavy R&D investments** — 20% of Clarify's revenues poured back into R&D.

### CIS Industry Trends and Evolving User Requirements

CIS helps organizations be more effective in acquiring new customers and growing new buyers into long-term, profitable clients. The industry is experiencing growth nearing 50% per year. The market is quickly consolidating from hundreds of software suppliers to a few leaders with resources to meet Fortune 1000 requirements. Clarify is one of these leaders.

Aberdeen's ongoing CIS practice interviews identify five key requirements and evaluation criteria:

1. **Speed and Scalability** — Response time is paramount; rapid deployment; global scale for thousands of users across distributed computing environments.
2. **Customizability** — Must adapt to company's unique policies/processes; end-user modifiable without IS staff; upgrades must preserve customizations.
3. **Expertise and product richness** — Supplier must understand best practices in sales and support; each application must stand on its own right.
4. **Integration** — Three levels: (1) cross-CIS app data sharing; (2) enterprise back-office integration (SAP, Oracle Financials); (3) cross-organizational boundaries.
5. **Communications** — Remote/mobile access, Internet availability, security, data replication and synchronization for mobile users and customers.

Clarify gets high marks in all five areas.

### Clarify's Complete CIS Solution

Clarify's enterprise solutions address five key areas of the customer interaction lifecycle: customer support; field service management; quality assurance; sales and marketing; and internal helpdesk.

Clarify has adopted an **integrated best-of-breed strategy** — each application delivers the full range of capabilities demanded by users, with integration concentrated at the data level rather than the application level.

#### Customer Service and Support (ClearSupport)

ClearSupport includes call and problem tracking, service contract verification, customer call histories, workflow management and report generation. Clarify's **Diagnosis Engine** provides advanced problem resolution and dynamic search of a solutions database. Clarify has also enabled customer self-service via Web and email.

#### Field Service Management (ClearLogistics)

ClearLogistics provides dispatching field technicians and managing spare parts and inventory. Tightly integrated with ClearSupport. Service technicians exchange parts, update customer configurations, log time and expenses. Also manages repair center operations, reducing parts misallocation. Integrates with customers' MRP and financials applications.

#### Quality Control (ClearQuality)

ClearQuality links customer feedback — problems, suggestions, demands — with development and QA organizations. Manages change requests with escalation and advanced query capabilities.

#### Sales and Marketing (ClearSales)

Clarify's acquisition of Metropolis enhanced its SFA capability. ClearSales includes an opportunity management system (OMS) for tracking opportunities, accounts, contacts, activities, proposals, quotes, and forecasts. **ClearEnterprise Traveler** enables remote sales professionals to upload/download data as needed — Aberdeen believes this is an important differentiator.

ClearTelesales (expected mid-1997) enables closed-loop sales from campaign management to lead generation, quotation, closure, and forecasting.

#### Internal Helpdesk (ClearHelpdesk)

ClearHelpdesk began shipping early 1995. Approximately 80% of functionality is the same as ClearSupport, plus back-end links to network and systems management platforms (Microsoft's SMS, Tivoli). Includes prepackaged interfaces to legacy helpdesks (Infoman, Netman). Flagship customers include Microsoft, Hewlett-Packard, Amoco, and Corning Clinical Labs.

Aberdeen believes Clarify's vision of connecting helpdesks across company boundaries is on-track with market realities.

#### Vertical Industry Focus (ClearSupport CommCenter)

In July 1996, Clarify announced CommCenter for telecommunications. It provides simultaneous access to both customer accounts and communications network infrastructure. Current customers: MCI, Sprint, TeleNordia.

### The Metropolis Acquisition — A Perfect Fit

Clarify acquired Metropolis Software in early 1996. Metropolis brought: rich SFA functionality, robust data synchronization and mobile computing capabilities, and deep expertise in sales process methodology. A common data model for ClearSupport and ClearSales is expected by end of 1996.

Cross-pollination is leading to ClearTelesales (sales expertise in local environment) and enhanced ClearLogistics (support expertise in mobile environment).

### Key Technologies

Clarify's products adhere to four technology tenets:

1. Performance and scalability for enterprise-wide implementations;
2. Continual user modifications and future product upgrades;
3. Access by remote/mobile users and ability to integrate with third-party applications;
4. Ease-of-use.

Clarify's applications subscribe to a **three-tier architecture**, with presentation, application logic, and database layers partitioned into separate modules dynamically deployed on different hardware platforms.

**Metadata architecture**: Central to modification is metadata, which sits on top of application logic and contains all user customizations (modified using ClearCustomize toolset). Metadata and application logic are independent — new versions of an application can be deployed without corrupting customizations. This contrasts sharply with vendors that give source code to clients — causing lock-in to outdated versions.

**Data replication and synchronization** (from Metropolis) is critical for detached mobile users and will be incorporated throughout the product line.

**ClearExpress WebSupport** provides Internet support, making Clarify's applications available to anyone with a PC and browser.

### Important Partnerships

Three categories of partners:

- **Systems Integrators**: KPMG Peat Marwick, Cambridge Technology Partners, The Bentley Company, ETCI, Ernst & Young, ENTEX, BSG/Medaphis, Indigo, Quicksilver;
- **Value-added Application Providers**: Databases (Oracle, Sybase), network management (HP OpenView, Tivoli, SunNet Manager, Microsoft SMS), knowledge bases (KnowledgeBroker, ServiceWare), problem resolution (Inference), CTI (Aspect, AT&T, Nortel, Prospect), sales configuration (Trilogy), back-office (SAP, Oracle);
- **Platform Providers**: Microsoft Windows NT, HP, Sun, IBM, Silicon Graphics, Apple.

### Professional Services and Support

Clarify will not allow a deal to proceed unless it believes an implementation will succeed — hence 99% uptime. A Technical Account Manager (TAM) is assigned to every customer as part of a standard support contract.

### Sales and Marketing Strategies

Clarify sells primarily through its direct sales force targeting senior support, sales, and IS executives. Approximately 10% of revenues from overseas. **Linchpin account strategy**: Microsoft, HP, Cisco, Bay Networks, MCI, Sprint are all Clarify customers, creating natural pull-through to their channel partners, resellers, and OEMs.

### Organizational Structure

Headquartered in San Jose, CA. For 1H 1996, revenues exceeded $20.1M. Aberdeen expects this to more than double in 2H 1996. Traded on NASDAQ under symbol CLFY.

### Aberdeen Conclusions

With over 200 customers and 43,000 individuals using its solutions, Clarify is a leader in the CIS market. By following a best-of-breed strategy and investing heavily in R&D, the company has the functional excellence and scalability demanded by senior support and sales executives.

Aberdeen is highly encouraged by the progress of the Metropolis acquisition. Aberdeen expects Clarify will maintain its rapid growth, particularly in the information technology and telecommunications markets. **Quite simply, Clarify is a CIS winner and will continue to be so in the future.**
