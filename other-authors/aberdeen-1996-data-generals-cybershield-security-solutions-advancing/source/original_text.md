Preface
Information Systems (IS) executives openly state they are more worried than ever about an increasing lack of control over valuable information assets, trade secrets, financial data and
competitive information that is deployed throughout their networked enterprise. Many IS executives now say that Intranet, remote access and electronic commerce applications are being
primed to support mainstream business processes. But, they also say that their networks, applications, data and important business operations are becoming less secure — faster.
In this Profile, Aberdeen describes what veteran IS managers say about their experiences with security for high-profile Internet applications, Data General’s (DG’s) Cybershield solutions and
technologies for the Internet, and why seasoned IS managers have chosen DG as a trusted supplier.
Executive Summary
Data General is providing integrated security solutions and services for responsible and empowered IS decision makers to cost-effectively protect enterprise information assets for new
Internet-based business systems. DG’s packaged security solutions enable IS executives to eliminate the effort and expense of integrating multi-faceted, often non-interoperable security
controls from multiple suppliers. More importantly, DG’s well designed commercially oriented security products now make it possible for IS executives to cost effectively segregate
business-line operations, employees, customers, partners and suppliers.
Packaged with comprehensive host, network, and application security services, DG’s Cybershield solutions turn off-the-shelf DG AViiON servers into off-the-shelf high-strength Internet
security solutions for IS executives to fast-track Intranet and Electronic Commerce application projects.
Aberdeen has interviewed IS executives and Independent Software Vendors (ISV) who depend on DG’s core security solutions. IS managers responsible for information security programs
give DG high praise for Cybershield’s flexible and comprehensive security controls. And, ISV’s state that DG’s security foundation is integral for protecting their own operations. More
importantly, IS managers stated that users are not aware that DG’s security controls were operating — indicating that Cybershield has little impact on applications and IS operations.
Aberdeen suggests that as IS organizations are building Intranet applications to ex-change information of value with suppliers, business partners and customers, IS decision makers would be
well served to evaluate the use of DG’s Cybershield security solutions for protecting these enterprise Intranets, data and applications.
Search for the Internet-Security Grail
IS executives, looking for security solutions for their Internet-based business applications are finding numerous point-solutions — almost all centered on network security. Experienced IS
managers tell Aberdeen that the fundamental security control for networked applications involves encryption for providing privacy and integrity of information en-route over the Internet.
And, IS managers say that closely linked with protecting information on the Net is a confusing array of options they must select from for authenticating users over the Net.
IS managers say that encryption and strong authentication controls are definitely in-plan for new Net-based projects, but that without secured foundations on either side of the Net, security
controls for protecting information in-transit are useless.
Recognizing that they cannot cost-effectively improve the security of Wintel-based clients, pragmatic decision makers are simply looking for inexpensive and reasonable solutions for
authenticating users, including: smart cards, one-time challenge-response systems, MD5 digests, SET and digital signatures.
The first action that these astute IS managers are taking to protect information assets on their internal networks is to ensure that critical enterprise applications and data do not become
publicly available.
Firewalls or Fireways?
Concerned that firewalls implemented on unsecured operating environments are inappropriate for high-profile Intranet and electronic commerce applications, security-savvy IS managers tell
Aberdeen that firewalls are not designed for:
• protecting complex web-enabled applications operating on commerce servers;
• adequately limiting access to Intranets and valuable enterprise information;
• segregating suppliers, customers, and business partners from one another; and
• preventing administrators from acquiring very sensitive information.
More importantly, veteran IS security managers tell Aberdeen that integrated off-the-shelf security solutions are no where to be found. Forced to address security issues, these IS managers
are spending time and budget monies to unite component security solutions. This is why determined IS executives are looking for suppliers that can provide more integrated solutions for
protecting vital information and Internet-enabled business operations.

Integrating numerous security components required to safely conduct business over the Internet, DG’s Cybershield packages are positioned to provide IS executives greater confidence in
delivering applications for business on the Internet.

Cybershield — the foundation for secure Intranets
Cybershield turns an off-the-shelf AViiON server into an off-the-shelf solution for securing enterprise Intranets. Integrating many component security point solutions into a single whole,
Cybershield provides IS decision makers with security solutions normally implemented by separate discrete servers, including: secured applications servers, authorization servers,
application-level firewalls, statefull message content inspection application servers, strong anti-virus controls, strong authentication services, secured web-servers and secured routing
gateways.
As a result, Cybershield can consolidate numerous Internet-related application and security platforms (see Figure 2) onto one manageable secure-commerce-server, saving IS executives time
and money for integrating and deploying production-grade safe Intranet applications. Unlike traditional firewalls, Cybershield is integrated into a highly secured operating system with
embedded network security controls that can actually provide the assurances needed for protecting applications and data. And for must-be-up-and-running production applications, tandem
Cybershield servers can be configured for continuous operations.
An array of standard, optional and public domain component solutions from Apache, Bellcore, BDM, CERN, DG, NCSA, IRE, Racal Data, Security Dynamics and ViaCrypt are currently
available for Cybershield. These include: 1) trusted application-level firewall proxies for anonymous and authenticated access (ftp, telnet, http, etc.), 2) secured public domain web servers
including CERN, Apache and NCSA, 3) optional encryption for dial-up and wide-area X.25 links for transparently protecting user credentials and Internet messages, 4) optional PGP
message and electronic mail encryption, 5) secured public domain electronic mail clients supporting SMTP, POP3 and MIME-enabled mail applications, 6) secured public domain network
news services through PINE and INN news servers, 7) numerous optional authentication systems (kerberos, SecurID, WatchWord, Fortezza and S/Key, etcetera.), 8) optional anti-viral
filtering to intercept and neutralize imported threats, and 9) key-word filtering to prevent critical enterprise information from being disseminated over the Internet.

Cybershield’s security foundation — B2SO
The security technology at the heart of Cybershield, DG’s B2 Security Option (B2SO), is an option in name only. This core technology is a fundamental part of DG’s Unix operating
environment and turned on only when the option is ordered. Implementing the flexible commercial security controls IS managers say they are looking for, DG’s B2SO goes far beyond the
protections afforded by such mainframe controls as RACF, ACF2 and TOP SECRET.
B2SO employs three regions (see figure 3) for transparently segregating users and administrators from networks, applications and data. These regions ensure that: viruses cannot be
introduced into production applications and data; administrators cannot acquire access to valued production applications and data; and applications and data are protected by the underlying
foundation. This design construct ensures that unauthorized access to customer records, business partners transactions and credit card information stored on-line can be eliminated.
Moreover, within the application and data region, security controls can be configured for simple RACF or Windows NT style information access controls, or for comprehensive segregation
of customers, partners, customers and employees. But, whether applications and data operate in a single domain (RACF’s and Windows NT) or in multiple network-enabled compartments,
the configured information access policies established through B2SO by empowered IS managers prevent users from wandering outside authorized networks, applications and data.
In addition, very high-assurance monitors in B2SO regulate access between these containment zones in order to assure that users’ access to networks, applications and data is based on
configured policy. And, the monitors of B2SO ensure that users and administrators actions are audited according to policy.
B2SO also provides IS executives enormous value for Internet application deployment with Capability Access Controls. Capability controls in B2SO are placed on enterprise applications
and data to provide for role-based access controls and iron-clad guarantees that critical information assets cannot be acquired or accessed except through authorized roles. This role-based
approach ensures cost-effective implementations for IS managers while also providing assurances for partners, customers and suppliers that the integrity and privacy of their sensitive
applications and data cannot be compromised.
Electronic Commerce Applications on Cybershield
Operating standard Internet client and server software, Cybershield is capable of isolating each Internet-connected user and application in containment zones because of the underlying
features of B2SO. The result for users is that standard Windows, Macintosh and Unix workstation applications transparently interoperate with Cybershield-based electronic commerce
applications.
Electronic commerce on a Cybershield foundation provides decision makers the ability to consolidate separate application and security servers (see Figures 2) for customers, suppliers and
partners, saving IS executives time and money for deploying safe production-grade Internet commerce business operations.
Users and ISV’s Stand-Up for Cybershield
IS managers now deploying Cybershield and B2SO say they have consolidated firewall and secure application servers with Wide Area Network (WAN) connections. And, some IS decision
makers are already deploying Cybershield for appropriately isolating multiple virtual business-lines.
According to these IS managers, the usual degradation in performance and service levels noticeable with security products is negligible using DG’s B2SO. Moreover, IS managers reported
that users did not notice the security controls because applications and business operations were unaffected.
ISV’s told Aberdeen that the libraries they used to build their products were the same on DG’s B2SO and standard Unix products. According to ISV’s, DG has implemented B2SO as an
integral part of its Unix product, the B2SO package
just turned-on what was already underneath.

Summary Observations and Conclusion
The reason Cybershield makes sense for IS executives is that it eliminates the integration headaches and delayed project schedules that are inherent with components-based point solutions.
And, because Cybershield can actually segregate unlike classes of users and applications from unauthorized enterprise networks, applications and data, it provides the security services
needed to confidently and cost-effectively deploy safe Intranet and electronic trading operations.
Without the integrated solutions that DG delivers with its Cybershield family, IS executives are forced to rely on a mixture of non-integrated and untested components. And, without
Cybershield’s ability to safely segregate customers, partners, suppliers and employees, IS decision makers would be forced to spend more IS budget monies on acquiring, managing and
integrating many separate network firewalls and authentication servers — with less confidence that vital information was adequately protected.
But, what makes DG’s Cybershield solutions most compelling for decision makers is the ability to simply reuse existing applications. Using Cybershield, smart IS executives can reuse
existing applications and data, and safely deploy new web-enabled applications using Java or ActiveX clients wherever appropriate.
The positive assessment of experienced IS executives regarding DG’s Cybershield products and services provides the best recommendation for the Cybershield product suite, and should
encourage other IS executives to evaluate DG’s Cybershield closely.

### Abstract

Aberdeen Group profiles Data General's Cybershield security platform, an integrated suite built on the B2-level secure DG/UX operating system (B2SO), designed to protect enterprise Intranets and electronic commerce applications. Aberdeen recommends Cybershield as a superior alternative to component-based point solutions for IS executives deploying Internet-facing business applications.
