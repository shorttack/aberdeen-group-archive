## Abstract

Aberdeen Group argues that the Year 2000 (Y2K) problem is fundamentally a competitive strategy issue, not merely a technical one, and that senior executives must take leadership roles. The study contends that non-Y2K-compliant enterprises will suffer competitive disadvantage and prescribes a proactive executive response with trusted external assistance.
