Preface
Founded in 1991, FlexiInternational Software (Flexi) is one of the emerging growth companies battling for market share and share of mind in the highly competitive market for financial
Client-Server Solutions (CSS). The company was formed to deliver products that were: focused on meeting the demanding core-business requirements of finance and accounting; based on a
commitment to open standards; built with "state-of-the-art" development methods and tools; and designed with a particular focus on real world performance requirements.
These early-on strategic choices -- combined with savvy business development and marketing activities -- continue to yield dramatic results as FlexiInternational has rapidly expanded its
financial functional capabilities and has moved to integrate key value-added technologies to meet demanding enterprise requirements. Aberdeen maintains its assessment that
FlexiInternational is well positioned both technologically and from the perspective of key business alliances to challenge for CSS market leadership in the rapidly expanding market for best-
of-breed, object-oriented enterprise-class financial systems.
Executive Summary
Aberdeen research shows that IT executives and their financial management peers are demanding the benefits deliverable by client-server computing -- making best use of personal
workstations and distributed computing resources to provide greater productivity, flexibility and responsiveness to demanding business processing requirements. Over the past year, market
skepticism has quickly dissipated concerning how the application of object-oriented methods and implementation of object-oriented technology (OOT) could translate into real-world
financial management business advantages. This is primarily due to advances in corporate understanding of the potential for component-based, message-oriented solution architectures and
moves by major technology suppliers -- like Microsoft, Sun and others -- providing the infrastructure for making distributed object-oriented computing possible.
As a result, key suppliers of core business applications find that those among them that have adopted object-oriented technologies are the best positioned to meet next-century distributed
client-server requirements as well as rapidly deploy their solutions over the Internet, Intranet and World Wide Web. In this light, Aberdeen advises its clients that the key to assessing OOT-
based solutions is to examine the depth and breadth of the object-oriented technologies utilization within the broader context of the development, deployment and potential for distributed
interoperability of the CSS.
First and foremost, FlexiFinancials is a CSS driven by the definition of business rules and data models. It is the union of rules and data that produce the business objects. Business objects
combine "what is it" with "what and how does it do it" to create a high productivity environment for the CSS developer and customer alike. Both can now concentrate and operate on
business information, events and processes -- e.g., generate purchase order, process invoice, or perform trial balance -- rather than manipulating data, codes and tables. When the general
ledger, word processor, spreadsheet and graphs lose their distinct application identities and become an integrated resource, the power of an object-oriented approach on the financial desktop
is inescapable.
Today, astute financial executives have determined how to integrate their favorite spreadsheet models with their financial system's information. As requirements and competitive pressures to
implement best business practices catch up to what is possible with today's advanced CSS technology, those same executives will expect to manage the reengineered distributed financial
enterprise with the same level of business integration as is common today when transferring data between the ledger and Excel. Aberdeen believes that forward-looking IT buyers and
financial managers will be well served by looking closely at object-oriented and
-capable CSS today, such as FlexiFinancials -- to prepare to meet those pressing future requirements and avoid entrapment in technologically incapable backwaters.
Market Position
FlexiInternational’s target client profile is one made up of enterprises with complex accounting and/or advanced technological requirements, and those migrating their financial systems from
proprietary to open, client-server environments. By definition, young companies need be both opportunistic and aggressive to fuel growth and expansion and FlexiInternational scores on
both counts. While taking aim at the high-end of the enterprise financials market, the company’s early growth has come from a spectrum of clients of diverse sizes, revenue categories and
industries.
Of note, Flexi has recently been achieving increasing success and establishing market presence within the financial services and banking sector. Aberdeen expects FlexiInternational to
continue adding key Fortune-class enterprises to its roster of customer accounts as its value proposition continues to expand to meet increasingly demanding enterprise requirements.
Key Technologies/Products
FlexiFinancials is an Application-centric CSS -- or one designed to operate with multiple RDBMS -- with a Database-centric approach to underlying RDBMS integration. The company has
chosen to implement the native call-level interfaces to each of the supported databases. While this approach does require more ongoing R&D attention and investment than the ODBC
approach favored by other suppliers, it provides a more robust and higher-performing level of integration to the expanding list of supported databases. Coupled to the high-performance
database implementation is an array of potential deployment systems that allow FlexiInternational to scale from desktop to mainframe environments. See Table 1 for a complete list of
available FlexiFinancials modules and Table 2 for details on supported hardware and database platforms.
FlexiFinancials is designed and developed to be fully object-oriented. Refer to Figure 1 for a depiction of FlexiInternational’s object architecture. Flexi uses Popkin's System Architect CASE
product to design and document the business rules, application logic, and FlexiObjects. The results of the design effort are input to FlexiDB that maintains the database structures and models
in a graphically-accessible format and populates the underlying database with the appropriate definitions and information. The resultant applications are highly portable, easily deployable
and are developed with underlying technology components that include:

• Microsoft Foundation Class Libraries;
• Visual C++ development environment;
• Microsoft standard interoperability technologies (i.e., OLE/OCX, ODBC, DDE).
The company has also developed a unique application-level, real-time heterogenous database replication technology as a component of FlexiDB. With this feature users can import Flexi
financial database structures from one platform, like Oracle, and export the data -- in the native format of the target database -- to any of the FlexiFinancials supported RDBMs. For those
enterprises seeking effective ways to deploy multi-site financials in a mixed hardware and database environment, this feature can add an effective and powerful administrative capability to
their arsenal.
The distributed object-oriented approach provides flexibility to the IS implementer and the enterprise in several key areas. First, configurability and application partitioning is extremely
flexible both across the network and within a multi-tier architecture, Second, the capability to customize the CSS increases, either through IS developed extensions to existing object classes
or the creation of new ones. Thirdly, with open APIs and standards-based means of interprocess communication, the ability to create a comprehensive CSS infrastructure is limited more by
the decision process of which other line-of-business functions or products to integrate than by the ability to integrate them.

If You’re Blue, Flexi Has Something For You
Aberdeen’s Fortune-class clients are seeking technologies capable of supporting their evolutionary migration to open, client-server financial systems. To meet this requirement, successful
suppliers will need to acknowledge and implement solutions capable of exploiting the changing role of a client’s IBM enterprise servers (ES9000 and AS/400) as well as integration of Unix,
Windows NT and OS/2 midrange servers. Of all the Financial CSS suppliers that Aberdeen monitors, FlexiInternational is uniquely positioned with the completeness and depth of its IBM
Integration Strategy. The cornerstone of Flexi’s approach is optimized DB2 access -- regardless of hardware platform. To meet this goal the company is using key IBM integration
technologies, including;
• Distributed Relational Database Architecture (DRDA) -- database interoperation protocols and multisupplier data replication;
• Client Application Enabler (CAE) -- client-based API’s;
• Distributed Database Connectivity Services (DDCS) -- network protocol converter for legacy-based DB2 cross-system integration; and
• Database Application Remote Interfaces (DARIs) -- bound, triggerable stored procedures.
FlexiInternational has wisely provided for support of older versions of DB2 -- those that do not support DARIs -- by implementing pre-compiled, callable procedures that mirror the
functionality provided in a DARI stored procedure. The company reports that its implementation approach is yielding performances advantages of between 300% to 500% over applications
employing an ODBC course. Aberdeen understands that the company will be ready, later in 1996, with updated and DB2-Common-Server-ready FlexiFinancials for IBM’s Common Server
enhanced DB2 services and interfaces.
Value-Added Client-Server Support.

operating systems, across a network of client workstations and network or enterprise servers presents a challenge when it comes to problem-source identification. Compounding the technical
issues are ones of problem ownership and responsibility for resolution in a multi-supplier integrated environment. Aberdeen's clients continue to tell us that suppliers have to address more in
terms of support than only their own product. Suppliers tell us that untangling client-server problems can often be budget and expense busters -- especially when one supplier provides the
time and expertise to resolve another supplier's issue.

site at the customer -- that may be used for comparative as well as diagnostic activities. The FlexiCenter system is installed within the existing FlexiFinancials network and includes all the
