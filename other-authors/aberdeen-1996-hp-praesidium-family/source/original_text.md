Hewlett-Packard's Praesidium:
A Family of Products To make Business on the Net A Reality
September 9, 1996 — As more enterprises eye the use of the Internet to conduct business transaction, IS (Information System) executives have found themselves stymied by the lack of
integrated and comprehensive security solutions for secure end-to-end financial transactions that can ensure privacy, integrity, and audit requirements are met.
Despite the worry, business-line managers are actively leading their enterprise to the forefront of electronic commerce — before competitors can establish a presence. IS executives and IT
(Information Technology) planners charged with advancing the enterprise into transaction applications for Internet deployment have already been testing pilot-projects — typically involving
small-scale tests between trading-partners. However, these early-pioneers have found that architecting, debugging, and testing security for the pilot-projects to be too complicated for
production-grade field deployment. These IS executives have been relying on a hodge-podge of security point-product solutions and highly-skilled third-party security contractors for proof-
of-concept projects. And early results have led many to conclude that point-solutions will not work if the enterprise is to deploy critical make-the-money applications into production.
To help leading enterprises take advantage of the opportunity to conduct secure financial transactions for Intra/Inter Net business applications, Hewlett-Packard (HP) has unveiled its
Praesidum security framework and several Praesidium security products that enable an enterprise to safely and cost-effectively conduct transactions with business partners, suppliers, and
customers.
HP’s Praesidium — A Family of Products
HP’s Praesidium security family of products (see Figure 1) have been designed to: solve critical security-problems for Intra/Inter net transactions; allow IS to deploy the security services
needed; and work with existing IT infrastructures and applications so that cost-effective deployment is possible. HP’s Praesidium provides IT planners the blueprint for architecting and
deploying secure-networked applications, with fiduciary responsibility. And, to deliver on its Praesidium family of products, HP has invested in compelling frameworks and technology
solutions that will help IS executives and planners meet their security requirements — to move fast-charging business-line managers beyond small-scale pilot-projects, and into production-
grade field deployment.
Figure 1: HP’s Praesidium Family
Authenticating credentials — securely initiating a transaction
To operate a secured network transaction environment, IT planners need to authenticate and protect the front-end (transaction initiators) credentials that can uniquely identify an employee,
customer, supplier, or partner. The credentials could range from something a user knows (passwords), to items a person carries (smart cards, digital certificates, X.509 certificates, smart
cards, debit cards, etcetera); and to an indelible mark of who the person is (a biometric such as thumb-prints or retinal scans). And, it is common practice for enterprises to support several
different types of credentials to authenticate users — thereby separating access to information assets based on credential-type.
To seamlessly handle different authentication requirements while ensuring uniqueness of identities, HP, Gemplus and Informix plan to deliver the ImagineCard. ImagineCard is designed to
provide strong authentication for businesses and consumers. Unlike other solutions now being planned or tested, the ImagineCard allows its users to control how their personal and corporate
information can be used. And, for businesses, the ImagineCard will empower employees to access and distribute vital information assets via Intranets and private networks, as well as
interoperating with physically secured building access controls systems. ImagineCard may provide businesses and consumers the features needed for universal adoption.
And, HP recognizes the enterprise has to integrate a diverse range of credential-types, from passwords to smart cards, and from private- to public-key systems. HP plans to make it easy for
the enterprise to interoperate other front-end authentication systems including: the emerging Secure Electronic Transfer (SET) plan; X.509-based certificates; and other public-key based
authentication systems. And, being able to support multiple forms of credentials is only a portion of the front-end security problems that must be solved. Other requirements such as the non-
repudiation of customers, business-partners, and suppliers and the need to protect information in-transit over the network — all have to be planned for, accommodated, and delivered. HP’s
Praesidium security framework, HP’s Praesidium products, and its family of Independent Software Vendor (ISV) products promises to solve these subtle but critical security requirements.
ICF network security — delivering security for global needs
Cryptographic ciphers protect the identities and credentials of employees, customers, suppliers, and partners, as well as the information they may transact with the enterprise over network
connections. Unfortunately, not many enterprises have taken the necessary steps to protect credentials and message traffic flowing over wire and wireless networks. Enterprises have found
themselves subject to national laws governing encryption technology in every country they operate, due to national concern over security interests. The continued controls over the import
and export of cipher technologies has largely prevented commercial enterprises from achieving the protection they need — from the very same elements governments are worried about. HP

introduced the International Cryptography Framework (ICF) as a standard to resolve the impasse between the needs of national government control of its security interests and those of
businesses intent on protecting electronic information assets in-transit around the world. HP’s ICF is designed to enable enterprises to use and deploy the cryptography they deem necessary,
and that is approved by each political boundary. The ICF promises to resolve the impasse over national regulations governing cipher technology.
The Intranet back-end — securely fulfilling the transaction
The lack of integrated and cost-effective security solution for the back-end Intranet has convinced most early leaders testing electronic commerce applications that without more modular,
integrated, and cost-effective security controls on Intranet transactions the electronic-web that integrates employees, suppliers, partners, and customers — may remain a dream, and not
become the desired reality. After all, the back-end (transaction processing) security requirements are more comprehensive than the front-end. The back-end security services must:
authenticate employees, partners, suppliers, and customers while protecting and keeping private authorized profiles and credentials; route transaction requests to only authorized internal
network services, platforms, and applications; provide adequate service levels for authorized transactions; and maintain the integrity of the security controls, transactions, and transaction
audit records. For some enterprises, the possibility of introducing access from the insecure Internet into their networks has simply prevented them from introducing access to the Internet for
employees. And, continued worry about the safety and privacy of sensitive information on their internal networks continues to haunt now-experienced early leaders using web-servers for a
range of applications.
While firewalls have been an integral part of early pilot-projects, the actual test-projects have been running on internal-commerce-networks, networks that are physically disconnected from
the corporate network. These internal-commerce-networks have been implemented to eliminate internal and external threats to valuable information assets and resources. The puzzle now
facing IS executives that need to charge ahead, is how to effectively secure information on their Intranets — while also providing ready access to the most up-to-date information. IS
executives that have tested firewalls in low-profile and disconnected-network pilot-projects, say the need for high-profile make-the-money trading now demands that the internal-commerce-
networks be rejoined to corporate Intranets. Further, the commingling of employees, partners, customers, and suppliers makes most IS executives uneasy! All of these reasons have convinced
IS executives of the need for greater assurance than firewalls were designed to provide, and a security-service that connects authorized transaction requests to the correct Intranet resource.
And probably most important, IS executives do not want to find themselves with an elegant technology solution that requires an army of administrators to implement.
HP’s Praesidium Back-End Intranet Products
The strategy guiding HP’s Praesidium family of products is that business logic provides the ability for the enterprise to securely, efficiently and cost-effectively manage information-flow
according to business opportunity — while not being constrained by the limitations of the security technology. HP’s Praesidium products for the back-end Intranet have been designed to
provide a modular, yet integrated approach to deploying security services for commercial business-transactions. To provide prudent, cost-effective, and useable information security controls
that meet fiduciary responsibility-levels for commercial public-network transactions, HP has developed two of its own Presidium products, the Praesidium VirtualVault and the Praesidium
Authorization Server.
HP’s Praesidium VirtualVault: high assurance, high-profile Intranet applications
The Praesidium VirtualVault is a trusted gateway designed to do what firewalls are designed for, to ensure only authorized transactions are allowed access to Intranet resources. But the
VirtualVault resists external threats more effectively than standard firewalls were designed for while providing much greater protection from internally authorized employees. The
VirtualVault, already deployed commercially by several Federally chartered banks to conduct banking transactions over the Internet, uses a trusted operating environment with a protected
reference monitor that: isolates insecure web-server applications from the vital resources on the enterprises Intranet; and isolates and protects applications, security policies, and data on the
VirtualVault. And, the VirtualVault also protects audit logs of security-relevant events, including those of the trusted operating environment, web-server and transaction-applications. The
audit capabilities on the VirtualVault can also be configured for real-time monitoring and notification if desired. IS managers suspicious of trusted operating environments will find the
VirtualVault implements a simplified browser interface to provide cost-effective configuration and administration (note: the VirtualVault is based on B1 class SecureWare technology
evaluated by the National Computer Security Center that HP purchased to further its ability to facilitate secure Internet commerce).
And, HP’s VirtualVault provides protection against: human error, tampering by internally authorized users; and from administrators from acquiring privileges beyond those authorized, —
functions firewalls are not designed for, nor capable of providing. Eliminating the all-powerful role of superuser, the VirtualVault implements a roles-based approach that allows
administrative duties to be performed without administrators having full control of the environment, access to security tables and policies, access to web-servers and transaction applications,
or access to the critical audit trails.
Praesidium Authorization Server: the Intranet business-logic service
The Praesidium Authorization Server contains the business-logic a class of transactions or users have authorization for, including access by: application, application-servers, locations, time
of day, day of week, roles, etcetera. And because validation of identities and authorizations are separated from the actual back-end repositories, networks, and transaction engines, it leaves
empowered and responsible decision makers free to make whatever changes they desire on the back-end networks and applications to meet dynamically evolving business opportunity. The
HP Praesidium Authorization Server enables IS to easily add classes of users, modify classes of user rights. Supporting the ability to rapidly add or modify existing IT investments is a
design-point often missed by most security solutions providers — not so with HP’s Authorization Server. It does this by using a role-and-rules- based approach instead of resource-access
controls. By eliminating the cumbersome, hard to program, and more difficult to manage access control lists found in networked operating systems, most operating environments, and host
resource access control solutions, the Authorization Server transforms what would otherwise be mechanical security controls into business-logic. And, for demanding must-be-up-and-
running production environments the Authorization Server supplies a secured replication service. The secured replication service ensures that if a network failure occurs, or if service is
denied, then authorized users and transactions can be serviced from a local or remote site, including one that is half way around the world.
The Praesidium whole is greater than the parts
The VirtualVault and Authorization Server enable an enterprise to secure its valuable information assets from the insecure Internet, while securing the Intranet information infrastructure.
More importantly, these two solutions form the basis upon which the enterprise can build and deploy business-flexible security services for high-assurance, high-profile, networked
transaction applications without having to change applications and back-end infrastructure — every time a new application module or data base is updated. By focusing on business-logic
instead of mechanical security controls, the enterprise can flexibly yet securely combine the Authorization Server with transaction engines to provide transparent access to authorized users no
matter where data and applications are located on internal networks, and from wherever employees, partners, customers, and suppliers are located on the Internet. The Praesidium
Authorization Server and VirtualVault are the foundation network security services needed to provide the capability of flexibly reusing existing networked applications and platforms for
enterprise Intranets, including: workgroup PC LANs, Unix/RDBMS servers, System/390 data legacy data repositories, and even modern RDBMS-based data warehouses and data marts. It is
the Authorization Server, VirtualVault, and the Praesidium family of products that combined go well beyond the capability of Unix, standard C2 class solutions (Unix and NT Server), most B
class Unix, and even RACF on MVS!
The Key to Success — Frameworks, Business and Software Partnerships

A framework manages application interactions with other applications or system resources, as well a providing a graphical view of the environment (see Figure 2). Perhaps the most
successful framework in the industry is HP OpenView which has provided enterprises the ability to cost-effectively manage the complexity of their IT environment. HP’s Praesidium
framework is an umbrella that provides a common means of integrating and managing security policies, credentials, and attributes across the wide array of IT resources on internal networks,
including databases, application servers, networks, certificate servers, authorization servers, and network gateways according to management policy and disciplined IS procedures.
Figure 2: HP’s Security Framework
The key to long-term success is partnerships. The more Independent Software Vendor (ISV) partners that support a given framework, the greater its chances of being adopted to solve real
business problems. HP knows the importance of having a rich suite of application solutions available under its frameworks. HP aggressively recruited key system/network management
software partners necessary to ensure the user acceptance of its highly successful OpenView family — HP has "been here before". And with Praesidium, HP recognizes that a very strong
partnership program is important for recruiting and testing leading-edge, state-of-the market security solutions — to rapidly bring them to market. Among the more significant partnerships
with leading security-solution providers HP has entered into are those including Axent, BEA, Checkpoint, Dynasoft, Gemplus, Gradient Technologies, Informix, Memco, Netscape, Nortel,
Open Horizon, Oracle, Raptor, Red Brick, Schuma AG, Sybase, and Verifone. And, HP has a long list of potential candidates for future partnerships under the Praesidium framework.
Conclusion
HP has already established a reputation as a trusted partner that can deliver IT solutions for business advantage. HP has been investing in the necessary products and programs to allow IS
executives to move beyond electronic commerce pilot programs — and begin deploying production-grade secured electronic commerce. HP has recognized that it is on the back-end where
goods and service suppliers, financial transfer agents, credit clearinghouses, and even certificate authorities live or die, and where the information assets that are exchanged for cash — must
be secured. HP’s Praesidium provides IT executives and CFO’s the tools to now secure all of their Intranet information assets, and the peace of mind to take advantage of new market
opportunity presented by commercial transactions over the Internet. If you are a commercial Internet user, such as a seller looking to go beyond advertising, or a cash-transfer-agent looking
to meet audit requirements, or a clearinghouse ready to deploy services for goods and service providers — then you must look to the new Praesidium offerings from HP.
Praesidium provides the security features required to safely operate as a public-key certificate-authority with a high degree of confidence — and the opportunity for an enterprise to become a
certificate-authority by taking advantage of Praesidium ISV products. Cash transfer agents, credit-clearinghouses, and even large service and goods providers can now reduce or eliminate the
$300,000 up-front expense to establish an account with a third-party. Enterprises with large transaction volumes will now be able to run their own secured back-end transaction operations,
and keep the margin on each transaction.
HP’s Praesidium strategy and products provide the business-logic approach IS executives and CFO’s are already familiar with on the mainframe, but go well beyond the control and
protection afforded by the mainframe. Praesidium has the potential to make the Net as safe as humanly possible. HP’s Praesidium could do for commercial cyber-transactions — what vaults
did for banks.
