Managing Network Computing
IT (Information Technology) executives deploying new client-server applications have found managing these environments to be more costly than anticipated. To hold the line on costs,
many IT executives have told Aberdeen they are standardizing on the fewest application platforms as possible. Aberdeen’s research also indicates that IT planners and decision makers are:
outsourcing legacy IT environments; looking for breadth of coverage for client-server management tools, under pressure to more rapidly deploy and manage make-the-money client-server
applications; trying to unify fragmented client-server, legacy mainframe, and mid-range database application environments to further reduce costs; and under pressure from business-lines to
deliver better levels of service.
Tivoli Systems provides IT executives with the networked management solutions needed by the enterprise to:
• boldly push forward to rapidly deploy client-server application environments instead of holding-back;
• reduce the cost of administration by extending the reach of centralized resources — throughout the enterprise;
• unify the management of applications, operating environments, platforms and networks, — from the mainframe to the desktop; and
• manage the evolving IT environment using policies and procedures, instead of administrative skill or whim.
This Proﬁle describes Tivoli Systems approach to managing network computing, and provides an overview of how Tivoli beneﬁts users, line organizations, and IS (Information Systems)
managers and empowers IS executives to more cost-effectively manage their evolving network computing IT infrastructure.
Executive Summary
Tivoli provides IT planners a common framework (TMF, the Tivoli Management Framework) that enables the enterprise to manage mainstream client-server application environments
including; Unix, Windows NT Server, Novell NetWare, DOS, Windows, NT Workstation, and Windows 95. And, Tivoli will add the capability for IS administrators to manage OS/400 and
OS/390 (MVS) production environments using the TMF. The TMF and Tivoli-based management applications make it possible for the enterprise to cost-effectively manage production-
critical (RDBMS) environments by allowing IS to plug into the TMF the applications needed — to manage production environments. IT executives have told Aberdeen that Tivoli’s wide
choice of ISV (Independent Software Vendors) management applications allays their worries over ﬁnding cost-effective management tools — enabling IS to deploy make-the-money client-
server applications faster.
Savvy IT executives have learned from hard experience that cost-of-ownership for production networked application environments includes hidden costs for operations staff to conﬁgure and
manage the networked IT infrastructure. And as many IT executives have found, some production networked operating environments are much more costly to operate than others. Tivoli
provides IT executives the ability to bring operational costs under control by: centralizing the operations staff; extending the effective reach of a highly-skilled centralized staff; and by
unifying the IT environments the IS staff manages. And for enterprises that need to manage remote regions or small branches, the ability to employ consistent policies and procedures from a
single corporate location promises not only to reduce operational costs, but also provide control and consistency for key enterprise business activities.
A Digression — What is the TMF (Tivoli Management Framework)?
A framework manages application interactions with other applications or system resources while providing a graphical view of the environment (see Figure 1). The TMF is an umbrella
providing a common means of integrating and managing client-server environments. By providing an easy means of plugging management applications into the Tivoli framework, IT
planners are able to select best-in-class management solutions while retaining the advantages that come with integrated packages. Tivoli allows IT planners to select and deploy the
management environment they need now, and the ability to add the management solutions required as their IT infrastructure grows.
And the TMF is well designed to scale as the IT environment itself grows because it employs managers and agents that carry out management application policies and procedures. Tivoli’s
TMF-based managers and agents are smart objects that simplify managing client-server environments — and which carry out the policies and procedures of the enterprise.

Positioning
Tivoli is an enterprise-grade management solution with its own core management applications and a wide assortment of ISV applications. Tivoli operates across a wide array of commonly
deployed platforms, from the desktop to mainstream production-oriented data repositories.
IBM wanted Tivoli to lead its future IT management strategies for many sound reasons. First, IBM’s Karat project promised to duplicate many of the features already deployed by users of
Tivoli’s products — but would have been delivered years after Tivoli. Second, the Karat project would manage IBM-only platforms and even IBM’s users said Karat had to help them
manage non-IBM products. Third, production-critical application suppliers (RDBMSs) and leading-edge management applications providers were already running on and committed to
Tivoli — not Karat. And ﬁnally, its largest users told IBM that they were already deploying the real Karat, and it was Tivoli.
IBM could have replaced Tivoli with Karat, or augmented its Karat project by adding Tivoli technology to jump-start Karat — and exclusively focused on IBM-only products. However, IBM
chose to replace it’s Karat project with Tivoli, and has positioned IBM’s venerable SystemView and NetView solutions to become well-behaved Tivoli management applications. For IT
executives and planners, Tivoli’s partners, and ISVs, these actions are important because they:
• validate users investments in Tivoli, SystemView and NetView;
• extend the reach of IS by unifying complex IT environments — from the desktop to the mainframe;
• sustain the winning Tivoli programs, strategies and technologies;
• assure application suppliers and management application providers, that winning with Tivoli will continue; and
• provide IBM’s global consulting and services for Tivoli-based solutions.
Tivoli competes with HP OpenView and CA Unicenter TNG. Of the two, Tivoli is more like HP OpenView because it combines the freedom for IT decision makers to choose best-of-breed
management applications from among leading-edge suppliers. CA Unicenter TNG is a comprehensive offering that must be purchased in its entirety, with less ﬂexible options to integrate
other management applications, should they be needed. Other notable differences between these major enterprise-grade management solutions include:
• CA-Unicenter TNG: CA’s users possess the ability to visually integrate the management of their networked resources using a pre-packaged solution whereas Tivoli’s users possess the
ability to functionally integrate their networked resources using best-in-class management solutions; and
• HP OpenView: HP’s users are provided a bottoms-up view of their enterprise, whereas Tivoli’s users are provided a top-down view of their resources based on their IT management
policies and procedures.
Tivoli — Core Products
The coupling of Tivoli’s exciting ﬁeld-proven products with IBM’s System/390 and AS/400 platforms, SystemView, and NetView now provides IT planners with the ability to manage their
production operations — from end to end. Tivoli makes it easy to bring a disciplined and ordered approach to managing the entire networked computing environment. Tivoli enables IS
administrators to distill order from complex operating environments by: operating across multiple platforms; providing core management applications; allowing IT planners to select from
special-purpose management applications; providing decision makers the freedom to choose management applications to rapidly deploy applications; while managing critical production
applications.

Tivoli Management Applications
Tivoli’s management applications provide a basis for IS managers to more effectively control their diverse client-server production environments. TME 10 User Administration offers
efﬁcient and centralized control over user accounts and system platforms (from OS/390 to Windows desktops) for production-grade environments across a distributed enterprise. TME 10
Software Distribution provides IS the ability to control distributing software patches, updates, and upgrades from a central location to any managed Tivoli system (from OS/390 to desktops).
TME 10 Print Manager allows administrators to assign and queue print jobs throughout the network. TME 10 Distributed Monitoring monitors conditions on managed Tivoli resources,


providing alerts to an administrator at a TME 10 Enterprise Console. TME 10 Distributed Monitoring enables IS to set thresholds for a wide variety of conditions, to take automatic
corrective actions, or await corrective action by an administrator. TME 10 Enterprise Console provides a rules-based management application console to collect, process, and automatically
initiate corrective actions for system, network and application (i.e., database, mail, groupware) events. And TME 10 Enterprise Console can also provide network management tools already
integrated to provide a top-down uniﬁed view of networks — instead of the cacophony of alerts that network-management-only products are known for.

TME 10 Enterprise Console — Uniﬁes Fragmented Management Domains
The TME 10 Enterprise Console (TME 10 EC — see Figure 2) provides IT planners the ability to centrally locate administrators far from actual application platforms and networks.
Removing administrators from the details of the underlying technology, the TME 10 EC provides the capability of managing according to enterprise policy. The TME 10 EC provides IS
managers with an integrated and uniﬁed view of IT resources, with access to authorized management applications, to care and feed networked information environments.
Figure 2: TME 10 Enterprise Console
IS management commonly deploys whatever tools and utilities are embedded with their applications and platforms. The bad news is that there are so many different platform and application
solutions, each with its own management tools that managing the enterprise IT infrastructure is fragmented resulting in a signiﬁcant portion of the IT budget managing the infrastructure —
especially client desktops. And, the reality for most IS managers is that most networked production environments remain islands, separated by administrative skill-sets and overhead as much
as by technology. And compounding the high cost of managing diverse networked production environments is the cost of managing branch and regional sites that the enterprise cannot afford
to staff. The good news is suppliers attempt to provide management utilities within their applications. The better news is that Tivoli’s Application Management Speciﬁcation (AMS) and
10/Plus partners allow IS executives to bring order, discipline, and cost-savings to managing a complex
networked production environment by making the critical must-be-managed production applications, manageable. The TME 10 Enterprise Console allows IT executives to unify managing
the production applications that business-line executives depend on for make-the-money operations. And by unifying the management function with Tivoli’s AMS and TME 10 Enterprise
Console, IT executives can ensure that remote branch sites are provided with the same consistent policies and procedures corporate-located IS sites receive — without bloated staff expense.
Conclusion
Instead of constraining Tivoli to a Karat strategy users said they could not purchase, IBM has done an about-face and charged ahead — with Tivoli Systems leading the charge. Tivoli at IBM
means that business-line executives, IT executives and IS managers looking to more cost-effectively manage their networked production environments can expect to ﬁnd their needs realized.
And, probably most compelling, Tivoli has the minds and hearts of the ISV’s, system platform suppliers, best-in-class packaged application suppliers, and leading-edge custom application
development suppliers — and where the ISV’s go, so goes the enterprise. The good news is that IBM acquired Tivoli to acquire the Karat of the industry while providing IS executives a
larger consulting and support team to call on if needed. The better news for IS executives is that the Tivoli strategies and products that have been so successful to date, are now IBM’s. Tivoli
Systems offers IT planners and executives the opportunity to take control of their networked enterprise information resources and manage them as business assets — and in the process, gain
competitive advantage.
