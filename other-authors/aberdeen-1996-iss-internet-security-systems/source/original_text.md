Preface
Information Systems (IS) executives openly state they are more worried than ever about an increasing lack of control over valuable information assets, trade secrets, ﬁnancial data, and
competitive information distributed throughout their networked enterprise. And, many IS executives now personally worry that with Intranet and remote access applications being primed to
support mainstream business processes, their networks are becoming less secure — faster. This is why IS decision makers have been yearning for anything that can identify genuine
vulnerabilities in their networked-fabric. And once correctly identiﬁed, IS staff can rapidly and effectively deploy pragmatic security solutions.
In this Proﬁle, Aberdeen describes what veteran IS managers say about their experience with security and networked computing and why they have selected Internet Security Systems as their
trusted supplier for products they will use to build an empirically grounded, enterprise-wide, effective security program.
Executive Summary
To empower responsible IS managers to cost-effectively protect networked applications and information assets, Internet Security Systems (ISS) provides products that identify, measure,
analyze and prioritize the security risks of networked computing. ISS’s products, packaged together as SAFEsuite, empower responsible IS managers by identifying actual holes throughout
the network, and prioritizing the risks posed to the enterprise by the voids. And, all the SAFEsuite products have ISS’s advanced technology built into them to provide a comprehensive view
of the enterprise network as intruders and attackers see it, so that IS executives can fast-track the enterprise into a more secure networked computing proﬁle.
Way back in 1994, a technology called SATAN created an uproar in the IS security community. Hackers and IS executives charged with maintaining their enterprises’ networks could
download it for free and then modify it to attempt to attack or ﬁnd the weak points in any network. In comparison, SAFEsuite has been designed to assist responsible IS organizations to
implement and continuously test the security levels of their own enterprise’s networks. More to the point, ISS’s Firewall Scanner has been found to be so successful at performing this task it
is used by the National Computer Security Association to certify independently developed ﬁrewall products.
Aberdeen has interviewed IS executives who have used both SAFEsuite and alternative networked attack simulators. These respondents have praised the ease-of-use, more comprehensive
coverage and accuracy of SAFEsuite.
Aberdeen recommends that as IS organizations are building web-based electronic commerce applications and placing a greater reliance on web technology for internal business processes,
they would be well served to evaluate the use of ISS’s SAFEsuite as the foundation for a comprehensive and professional security program.
Network Computing Security — A Fragile Egg!
Widely deployed by IS, integrated into all major platform operating environments, and used as the glue binding critical business applications together — the TCP/IP protocol has become the
common alphabet of the modern information age. Reinforcing its ubiquity and utility, it is the backbone for Internet, Intranet and remote access applications. And, it has become the common
communication pipe used by enterprises for rapidly exchanging information between trading partners, suppliers, and customers.
To control access to networked applications and data, knowledgeable IS managers are installing ﬁrewalls, and some are now installing Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) and authentication
mechanisms stronger than passwords, such as smartcards, retinal scans, etc.. IS managers conversant in these solutions rate all these security controls as essential, but not foolproof. More
disturbing, these IS executives say that incorrectly conﬁgured routers, web servers, applications, operating environments, and even modems on internal sub-networks can actually shatter
protective safeguards without warning — thus creating a porous network.
For experienced IS managers, networked application security appears like an egg, hard on the outside and soft on the inside. And, while IS decision makers are correct on insisting on such
tools as ﬁrewalls, VPNs and stronger authentication, they also know that once the shell is broken or a new way to the yolk has been discovered, security perimeters are useless.
ISS’s Products — Moles That Root-Out Actual Exposure
ISS’s products have been designed to uncover the holes in the networked-fabric and prioritize the risks posed to the enterprise. ISS’s mission is to develop and release products that empower
IS executives charged with maintaining networked security to continuously monitor, identify, and close both the internal and external holes in their information infrastructure — systems,
applications, and networks.
By using TCP/IP as the common glue, ISS ensures that all IP-based networks, sub-networks, and attached systems or devices (see Table 1) can be probed for vulnerabilities and analyzed for
threats and risks posed to the enterprise. As a result, ISS’s products have the capability to provide comprehensive detection of almost everything throughout an enterprise’s networks. The
enormous beneﬁt of this is that responsible decision makers and IS staff have the tools they need to safely lead the enterprise into production Intranet applications.
Aberdeen found that as a strong endorsement of ISS, even enterprises with security-knowledgeable staff and established information security programs are now using ISS’s products to
identify networked breaches, so that effective responses and procedures can be put in place. And, these security-experienced enterprises have found ISS’s products capable of providing an
early-warning system that identiﬁes new threats as their networks evolve to support new protocols, more users, additional interconnects with other enterprises, etc.

Key to all of ISS’s products is the company’s industry-leading scanning and analysis technology. This technology is the foundation of SAFEsuite. ISS’s core scanning and analysis modules
are built into every ISS product. These modules are able to probe and scan an almost inﬁnite set of target environments connected across any TCP/IP network connection.
ISS’s scanning module unleashes probes over the network to gather data from every selected IP address belonging to the enterprise. Information gathered by ISS’s scanning-probes is
returned to the platform from which they were launched. The analysis module sifts through the information returned from the scanning probes to produce reports identifying and prioritizing
the vulnerabilities found.
SAFEsuite is capable of probing in-situ the defenses of the underlying operating environment, the ﬁrewall’s security applications, web-servers, network service connections, and even
common application services in the environment. (See Figure 1). And it does all this in an easy to install and operate package.
To make SAFEsuite continuously even more valuable for users, ISS provides its customers with timely updates of new found exposures and methods of analyzing vulnerabilities. Further,
SAFEsuite’s design enables users to add additional packaged and custom applications required by the enterprise.
The SAFEsuite products operate on the most popular midrange application servers, including: Microsoft’s Windows NT, Hewlett-Packard’s HP-UX, IBM’s AIX, Sun Microsystem’s SunOS
and Solaris, and Intel-based Linux platforms.
Auditing How Safe Your Firewalls Really Are
Many IS decision makers ﬁnd themselves deploying ﬁrewalls based upon the limited information available to them, including the differences between circuit-ﬁlter and proxy-server ﬁrewalls,
and the cost of acquiring and managing competing ﬁrewall products. And, while this is important information, IS decision makers also yearn for more knowledge about the efﬁcacy of the
protective controls implemented by suppliers. IS managers who have deployed ﬁrewalls know from experience that conﬁguring and maintaining an effective ﬁrewall depends on complex
interactions between the underlying operating environment, the conﬁgured ﬁrewall services, and any application or data added to these network policemen.
ISS’s products are able to probe and analyze the total ﬁrewall environment, as conﬁgured in the lab or deployed in the production network, providing a true assessment of the safety afforded
valuable information resources on the network.
Ensuring That Electronic Commerce Web Servers Are Secure
Suppliers providing solutions for Internet-based application are operating on Internet-time, where product-life cycles are very short — often less than three months. Software suppliers are
only able to juggle three factors that determine the released characteristics of their products — quality, functionality, or schedule. Optimizing any one of these factors can cause a deﬁciency
in another. Given the focus on releasing products with new functionality once every three months to stay ahead of competitors, the quality-level of an ofﬁcial release often suffers. In fact,
many Internet suppliers depend on customers downloading the real version, with quality tested-in — after the ofﬁcial release. Security is closely related to quality. The less time spent ﬁnding
and evaluating spurious interactions in complex client-server code means less security. And, unable to test for real-world use in beta tests because of three month product life-cycles, it is not
surprising that Web-enabled applications are vulnerable.
ISS’s products enable prudent IS managers to greatly accelerate testing of web-based applications for high-proﬁle business requirements, and provide a competitive time-to-market advantage
that makes it safe to deploy.

Operating Environment Security — Assessing The Realities
The most overlooked security vulnerabilities are those posed by operating environments. While some suppliers are dedicated to providing secured versions of their operating environments,
the reality is that most only provide an optional installable security service. And, with most ﬁrewalls and the Internet operating on standard unsecured Unix operating environments, it is little
wonder there are security risks.

For IS decision makers planning on using Windows NT and hoping to avoid the security problems inherent in most standard versions of Unix — there is good news and bad news. The good
news is that NT’s security services are part of the standard release. The bad news is that the host-based access-controls only apply on the NT server. NT’s security policies are not enforced
across the network. And note that Microsoft is no longer shipping the security controls that were available in Windows NT Server release 3.50 in 3.51 or 4.0 for the new Internet and network
functionality available in these most recent releases.
ISS’s products enable IS staff to uncover the common vulnerabilities inherent to components-based operating environments — both Unix and NT — and applications from becoming Trojan-
horses in the networked enterprise.
Intrusion Monitoring — A Watchdog Against the Vandals
The best defense against hackers and others launching attacks on networks is to ﬁnd out who they are and how they are trying to get in before they are successful. Continuous monitoring is a
key component of any security scheme.
RealSecure is an intrusion monitoring, detection and warning solution that can be deployed to provide round-the-clock service. Operating in real-time, RealSecure recognizes attacks and
responds as conﬁgured. Leveraging ISS’s in-depth understanding of how systems are attacked, it monitors the packet-ﬂow over a network and recognizes attack patterns. Responses can be
conﬁgured, from electronic mail notiﬁcation services to denial of service by terminating connections.
Competitive Positioning
Prior to ISS’s release of its commercial network scanning products, SATAN (Security Administrators Tool for Analyzing Networks) had captured the headlines in 1994 as the industry-
leading product in this area. Unlike SATAN, where a systems programmer or skilled IS administrator has to actually build the product from a free download, SAFEsuite is commercially
packaged, ready to install and use. And, unlike SATAN, ISS’s products do not require an in-depth knowledge of Unix or TCP/IP to be used effectively. According to its users, ISS provides
the most comprehensive coverage available, by scanning and probing more networked entry-points, with more being added monthly. And, ISS’s products go beyond detection — they also
probe and identify inherently weak service types with known bugs, providing IS professionals with the comprehensive knowledge that skilled intruders also possess. If your enterprise needs
1994’s coverage for 1996’s networked vulnerabilities and can wait for Unix-literate staff to build the shareware application, then proceed to deploy SATAN.
NetProbe is just one of many products of an eclectic mix that is marketed by the Qualix Group. Its users report the product is easy to conﬁgure but provides rather skimpy coverage for
networked vulnerabilities — and some report it misses common vulnerabilities altogether.
Users of Bellcore’s PingWare report the product is easy to use, but that it misses some common vulnerabilities. And, its users also say it requires a network-savvy administrator to be truly
effective.
According to users that have evaluated all three available commercial alternatives, SAFEsuite is comparable to the others in ease of use, installation and conﬁguration. But SAFEsuite really
outshines the competition with more comprehensive coverage of vulnerabilities, prioritized risk analysis, and frequency of updates.
Business Partnerships — The Key to Success
ISS has expanded its distribution channels to include more than 130 distributors world-wide, and several signiﬁcant partnerships during the last year. Among these are partnerships with IT

Known By the Company One Keeps
Most signiﬁcantly, the National Computer Security Association (NCSA) uses ISS’s Firewall Scanner as a core part of its ﬁrewall accreditation program that certiﬁes ﬁrewall products. The
introduction of NCSA’s certiﬁcation programs have led several insurance carriers to underwrite liability coverage for NCSA-certiﬁed web sites. This changes the complexion of Internet-
security from one of total fright — to one of manageable ﬁnancial risk. And, while liability insurance for certiﬁed ﬁrewalls is an important new tool for ﬁnancial executives, ISS’s tools
forming the foundation of this risk underwriting will provide IS executives the underpinnings for long-term, on-going protection.
Perhaps the best indicator of the capabilities of a small ﬁrm are the enterprises already using and committed to its products and services. For ISS, this list includes organizations such as:

Conclusions and Observations
The simple reason that SAFEsuite can be the foundation for an effective, comprehensive security program is that until IS knows where the potential problems are in their networked
infrastructure, they will not have a pragmatic, empirically grounded basis for protecting the enterprise. Without a tool like SAFEsuite, it is nearly impossible for any IS department to
realistically protect an enterprise’s information assets or assure the integrity of electronic commerce operations, especially with an increasing number of networked interconnects with
customers, suppliers, other business partners and government agencies. Spending IS budget moneys on security controls without understanding the vulnerabilities or continuously monitoring
for actual attacks is blindly throwing it away.
ISS’s technology appears to be leading the industry at this time. In addition, ISS is continuously developing new capabilities for its products. After all, it must — security is still viewed by
too many vandals as a game between responsible IS managers building stronger armor and hackers creating more devious projectiles.
Instead of deploying Intranet applications without security controls and waiting to see what problems occur, prudent IS executives can now start to assess their own networked vulnerabilities
— and make decisions about how to close them. ISS’s products allow responsible and empowered IS managers to continuously measure vulnerabilities and close them before the vandals ﬁnd

them. This becomes an extremely important capability in today’s enterprise computing environment of quickly changing and evolving Intranet applications.
Finally, IS executives say ISS’s products are providing real value to advance the enterprise into a more secure networked computing proﬁle. This bodes well for IS decision makers charged
with deploying Internet-based technologies for business advantage. And experienced IS executives’ positive assessment of ISS’s products are the best recommendation they can receive to
encourage other IS managers to evaluate them closely.
