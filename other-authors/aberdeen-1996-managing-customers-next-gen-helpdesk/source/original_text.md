## Original Document Text

Growth and profit-oriented companies are gaining market share and stealing their rivals' business through coordinated sales tactics and superior customer service. These leaders are finding that new software applications, what Aberdeen has called **Customer Interaction Software (CIS)**, hold the key to building long term, profitable relationships with their customers.

*Managing Customers with Next-Generation Software Applications: 1996 Edition* provides the reader with a clear understanding of the forces influencing the market coupled with Aberdeen's insight into the future directions of Customer Interaction Software. In addition to explaining the basic CIS modules and important related technologies, Aberdeen also discusses how organizations have successfully installed CIS systems and highlights some of the pitfalls that can be avoided. Finally, the study includes profiles of over **40 CIS suppliers**, allowing executives to quickly identify the major players and understand their market position, as well as the unique value each brings to the table.

This is Aberdeen's second report on the CIS market and includes several important additions over our first study, which was published in May 1995. Some of the more significant enhancements include:

- **Revised findings** — Aberdeen's latest findings for both user organizations and suppliers of CIS-related products and services.
- **Perspective on the Internet** — Aberdeen explains some of the unexpected implications of Internet-enabled CIS.

*Managing Customers with Next-Generation Software Applications: 1996 Edition* is intended for three major audiences. The first is the **business executive** who needs to understand what CIS can do and which products are best suited to meet a particular objective. The second is the **IS executive** responsible for implementing CIS applications and integrating them into an enterprise's information systems infrastructure. Finally, it is a valuable market analysis and planning document for **CIS suppliers** who want to better understand key industry dynamics and user buying patterns in order to improve market position.

Companies rushing to install CIS systems cannot afford to make mistakes. The market for CIS software is filled with **literally hundreds of suppliers** -- and **only a few will be around over the long term** to support users. Line-of-business and information systems executives need to choose right the first time.

### Key CIS Module Definitions

**Customer Management Applications:**
- *Customer Support / Internal Helpdesk* — Issue tracking, escalation management, knowledge base for internal IT and external customer support
- *Field Service Management* — Dispatch, scheduling, parts management, on-site service tracking
- *Quality Control and Assurance* — Defect tracking, return management, warranty administration

**Sales and Marketing Applications:**
- *Sales Force Automation (SFA)* — Contact management, opportunity tracking, forecasting, sales territory management
- *Telemarketing/Telesales* — Outbound/inbound calling campaigns, lead management, scripting
- *Order Entry and Reservation Systems* — Transaction-level customer-facing order processing

**Important Related Technologies:**
- Computer-Telephony Integration (CTI)
- Problem Resolution / Knowledge Base Systems
- Workflow
- Remote Access and Data Replication/Synchronization
