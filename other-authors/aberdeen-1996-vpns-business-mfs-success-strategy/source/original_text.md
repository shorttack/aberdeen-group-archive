VPNs for Business: The MFS Success Strategy
Executive Summary
Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) are a trend on the rise, creating strong demand for skillful network integrators and global VPN communications service providers. Aberdeen recognizes that
digital network integration is redefining regional and global telecommunications.
Change is being driven by advances in digital and fiber technology coupled with an unprecedented level of global re-regulation. For the first time, local, regional and national common
carriers face turbulence and a paradigm shift in the way they do business. Significant competition is emerging from innovative suppliers who are capable of seizing opportunity in
telecommunications network infrastructure, services and telemanagement markets.
MFS Communications Company, Inc. is a public company listed (as MFST) on the NASDAQ. MFS' vision is to achieve a world leadership position in telecommunications infrastructure and
services. To date the company has delivered on this vision by meeting customer needs, in concentrated urban metro centers, with highly reliable, integrated LAN/WAN (Local Area
Network/Wide Area Network) access and co-carrier implementations, providing integrated voice and data services. MFS has demonstrated leadership by building one of the first national
SONET/ATM (Synchronous Optical Network/Asynchronous Transmission Mode) fiber network platforms. MFS supplies the network integration and communication service offerings to
create complete solutions for user organizations.
In this Company Profile, Aberdeen examines the vision, methodology and technology base successfully propelling MFS into position as a world leader in business network communication
solutions.
Market Strategy
MFS' market strategy is focused on meeting the communication needs of business users clustered in metro areas. It takes aggressive advantage of regulatory change coupled with the rapid
deployment of advanced SONET/ATM technology. MFS provides local exchange service and end-to-end services across the wide area.
MFS' original plan, announced in the late 1980s, was to deploy in 65 US metro centers by the year 2000. Due to MFS' success, this plan has been accelerated to be completed by the end of
1998. Additionally, company plans now call for expansion into financial centers in 25 countries.
To provide customers with complete solutions delivered by highly focused sales teams, MFS has organized itself into five distinct innovative operating companies. Significantly, these units
are well integrated, resulting in a development and innovation model yielding rapid network deployment capability and very high levels of system availability, 99. 99% for MFS users in
1994.
The Operating Companies
MFS' five operating units are highly coordinated, market driven businesses.
MFS Telecom Companies
The MFS Telecom Companies are made up of two organizations, MFS Telecom and MFS Data Services. Each organization is focused on selling its respective services to large business and
government customers.
1. MFS Telecom
MFS Telecom is a CAP (Competitive Access Provider) headquartered in Oakbrook Terrace, Illinois. MFS owns and operates metropolitan SONET-based fiber optic networks linking
over 4400 buildings in over 45 major US cities. Telecom service offerings include local private lines, special access digital services and switched services. The Telecom unit's market
advantage derives from its price/performance ratio coupled with high quality transmission services, network capacity and reliability, delivered by a responsive sales and support
organization. The network is ideal for heavy users. With an "uptime" availability of 99. 99% throughout 1994 and 1995, it is understandable that the Telecom unit boasts New York
Stock Exchange members, large government agencies and many global corporations as customers.
2. MFS Data Services
Based in San Jose, CA, MFS Data Services (formerly MFS Datanet) is a leader in SONET/ATM networked data services. In 1993 Data Services introduced the first national ATM data
service in the United States and in 1994 was the first company to do so internationally. Offering business users guaranteed transmission rates up to 100Mbps with its FDDI HLI Service,
MFS Data Services is quickly becoming a significant player in the financial data services arena.
In 1994 MFS Datanet became the first carrier to provide FR (Frame Relay) services over an ATM network and the first to offer FR at speeds over 1. 5 Mbps. FR is rapidly being adopted
by users because it is 30% faster than conventional packet switched data and more cost effective. Additionally, today's FR customer will be tomorrow's full ATM client as upgrade
implementations take place at customer sites.
MFS Intelenet Companies
The largest and fastest growing operating company, MFS Intelenet serves medium and small business users with full service, facilities-based local and long distance telecommunications and
telemanagement services. Intelenet utilizes both the MFS network and has co-carrier agreements with the RBOCs (Regional Bell Operating Company) in a growing number of states. The

unit is actively seeking co-carrier status in some 20 additional states.
In 1994 Intelenet completed two important acquisitions, Atlanta-based RealCom Office Communications, Inc. and Centrex Telemanagement, Inc. of San Francisco. RealCom was the second
largest shared tenant PBX (Private Branch exchange) service provider in the United States. Centrex has been a leader in telemanage-ment services. Together the acquisitions made up 75% of
Intelenet's 1994 sales, serving 20,000 business customers.
Current plans call for the deployment of switched services in 50 metro areas across the US. MFS Intelenet, Inc. is an independent operating company based in Jersey City, New Jersey.
MFS International
MFS International provides the company with its global market presence. Formed in 1993 to meet the needs of MFS' multinational client base, the unit continues to develop license
opportunities for infrastructure and services outside US markets. The unit is successfully leading MFS' efforts overseas and has won licenses in Germany, France, Sweden, the United
Kingdom, Switzerland and China (Hong Kong). In May of 1994, MFS International made history when it launched its SONET/ATM network connecting London, England via trans-oceanic
fiber connections to the United States. This event marked the world's first international ATM service offered by a single company. MFS International, Inc. is an independent operating
company based in Vienna, Virginia.
MFS Network Technologies
MFS Network Technologies, located in Omaha, Nebraska, is a network systems integrator serving telecommunications operators, cable operators, toll authorities, government agencies and
corporations worldwide. The unit designs, develops and manages networks for its clients as well as other MFS units. This work includes engineering, project management, construction and
network support. To date the unit has installed 125,000 miles of fiber optic cable. Skilled in copper, fiber, coax, wireless and hybrid technologies, MFS Network Technologies has significant
experience in sophisticated compatibility and interoperability issues.
MFS Network Technologies builds discreet and integrated digital networks with the highest availability characteristics and security. An example of this is the network developed for BART
(Bay Area Regional Transportation) in San Francisco. This system features a 75 mile SONET fiber backbone and a wireless communication network for police, firefighting and medical
emergency command. In post earth-quake San Francisco, this network goes beyond mission critical, it is life saving.
MFS Global Network Services
The Global Network organization is responsible for strategic and technical planning, development, expansion and operation of the MFS international network platform, over which a wide
range of MFS Communication's voice, data and other enhanced services are delivered. As of September 1995, the international fiber optic network incorporates 2,559, 526 circuits, 165,999
miles of fiber, and connects over 4458 buildings in 49 cities using a variety of switching equipment including integrated local and long distance switching platforms in 13 cities and 26 ATM
switches in 25 cities. GNS is focused on network operations, engineering, provisioning and support systems, as well as future network requirements planing.
Currently, GNS is working on MFS' three to five year expansion plan into 75 new metro centers worldwide and is based in Westmont, Illinois.
The MFS Method
A leading factor in MFS' success is its network development methodology. While common sense dictates a coordinated market driven approach, many common carriers have followed
models driven by regulatory or geographic constraint and, monopoly containment models. MFS utilizes a multi-phased process driven by customer needs and market forces.
Phase 1 -Development
Identifying a specific market opportunity supersedes all other aspects of a MFS construction project and network deployment. Once a market opportunity is identified, MFS conducts a
detailed analysis. In its analysis MFS will:
Assess the legal and regulatory environment;
Develop a network design;
Prepare a business plan and financial analysis;
Identify new construction vs. acquisition characteristics; and
Secure sites, licenses, rights-of-way and franchises for construction.
Phase 2 - Construction and Implementation
MFS Network Technologies handles network construction projects. The development unit coordinates with Network Technologies engineers who will build the fiber optic network or
upgrade the acquisition's infrastructure. Project management, engineering, design and construction is performed by Network Technologies.
Phase 3 - Telemanagement and Services
Once constructed, the network is made available to various operating units to offer services directly to the marketplace.

Innovative New Services
MFS has been a world leader in new service offerings over SONET/ATM networks. MFS was the first company to offer national ATM service in the US (1993) and international (1994)
markets. In addition, in 1994 MFS was the first provider to offer commercial frame relay services over an ATM.
In 1995 MFS introduced its new WAVE (Wide Area Voice Exchange) service. This integrated voice and data service is delivered over MFS' network and is billed as a single offering. For
users this is an important development. Now, customers pay for a single connection instead of two separate connections with one for voice and the other for data. In a typical installation of
two T-1 lines, WAVE service can save users 20% to 25%. For new installations, the startup costs are lower as well.
MFS offers its WAVE service using VBR (Variable Bit Rate) voice transmission. Previously, CBR (Constant Bit Rate) and TDM (Time Division Multiplexing) have been the standard for
voice in integrated networks because of sound quality issues. TDM offers full integration of voice, video and data with dedicated time slots for voice transmission regardless of traffic even if
there is no voice content. CBR utilizes circuit emulation to send fixed bandwidth voice and video. By definition CBR is not efficient. VBR takes full advantage of the speed of ATM, thus
maximizing MFS' availability to handle more traffic. MFS was first to offer VBR voice service with toll quality fidelity. This efficiency translates directly into revenue potential.
In December 1995 CNNfn (CNN Financial News) began service using MFS Datanet to link a "virtual television studio" to its base in Atlanta. Using desktop computers running Intel's
Proshare videoconferencing solution, financial news makers are being interviewed on CNNfn from their offices. Not only is it an innovative way to use MFS facilities and a natural evolution
for existing MFS customers — it is a big time savings for participants in the interviews. No longer does the show participant have to travel to a TV studio nor is the news organization
required to send a broadcast truck and crew to a distant site. Instead, the participant just logs on from the office saving time and money.
Market Explosion
Worldwide re-regulation and changes in telecommunications policy are being driven by global economic competition. This creates new license opportunities for MFS. In turn, customers with
networking needs, particularly those with data intensive requirements such as the financial industry, are creating heavy demand for MFS' services. The MFS network development method is
allowing the company to take full advantage of the situation, exceeding MFS' own original forecast.
MFS' history of providing network quality and technology innovation give the company an advantage with regulators. For example, in Germany MFS is the first non-government licensee to
be permitted to build a telecom infrastructure. MFS recently began construction in Paris after it received permission in France.
In the United Kingdom MFS has built a fiber network in London out to Westminster and the Docklands. Over the next 3 to 5 years MFS plans to locate in 25 financial centers, primarily in
Western Europe. This represents an additional 15 metro centers over the 10 metros originally planned. New networks under development include Stockholm and Zurich.
In the United States, MFS continues its success in linking metro areas and in winning co-carrier status on a state by state basis. MFS Data Services is a market leader in high speed data
transmission services. It is well positioned to expand its customer base with an array of data related services including Frame Relay, Ethernet, Token Ring and FDDI interconnections. User
organizations with light, moderate and heavy usage are accommodated with data rate availability of 56Kbps to over 100Mbps.
Rallied on by he passage of the US Telecommunications Act of 1996, MFS immediately issued detailed letters to regional telephone companies in all fifty states requesting mutually
agreeable terms for co-carrier status. Aberdeen acknowledges that in some cases co-carrier agreements will be contested at the state and federal level, however the Telecom Act itself provides
incentives for regional telcos to foster competition in-region as a trigger allowing the regionals to compete out-of-region. Ameritech has been aggressive in opening up their territory to MFS
so that they can offer long distance and other services. Aberdeen sees this as beneficial to both parties.
As MFS achieves greater penetration at the regional level in the US, these new customers will add to domestic revenue while fueling development in Europe and Asia. MFS Intelenet has
grown from 100 employees at the end of 1993 to more than 1500 employees and 20,000 business customers nationwide today.
Financial
MFS was organized in 1987 as Metro Fiber Services, Inc. , a wholly owned subsidiary of Kiewit Diversified Group, Inc. , which is a wholly owned subsidiary of Peter Kiewit Sons', Inc. of
Omaha, Nebraska. A related core competency of the founders included successful experience in coax and fiber cable construction for a telco and cable TV client base.
MFS became a public company in 1993 and is traded on the NASDAQ under the symbol MFST. To date the company has raised approximately $1 billion in equity funding and debt
financing for the purpose of growing an on-going revenue base through its expanding network platform and service offerings.
The initial public offering of 12,650,000 shares of common stock in May of 1993 was followed by a secondary offering of 4,600,000 shares of common stock in September of that year. In
January 1994 the company completed a $500 million debt offering of 9 3/8% Senior Discount Notes due in 2004.
Sales in 1994 were $228.7 million in telecommunications services and $58 million in network systems integration. 1994 sales were more than double the previous period with a sales total of
$286.7. In 1995 revenues more than doubled again to $583.2 million with $498.2 million in telecom services and $85 million in network systems integration respectively.
Conclusion
As an established network integrator, fiber plant construction operator, facilities based telecommunications provider, communications service and management company, MFS is leveraging
all of its assets. Significantly, MFS has succeeded in developing advanced networks for its own use in addition to working for third parties.
As an innovator, MFS has proven to be a world leader in technology and market responsiveness. Customers have become reliant not only on the high reliability and quality of service but on
the innovation that a corporate vendor/partner brings to business issues. While most customers are trying to figure out how to move from leased lines and slow data rates, MFS is providing
comprehensive solutions to small business and some of the largest corporations and government agencies alike.
At a time when the behemoths are announcing endless alliances and broad stroke moves, MFS is delivering coordinated development and comprehensive new services. As a result, the
outlook is very promising for MFS and its customers, who yield the benefits of strong leadership, a great strategy and aggressive execution.
