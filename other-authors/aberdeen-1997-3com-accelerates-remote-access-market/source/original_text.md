3Com Accelerates into Remote Access Market
Executive Summary
3Com Corporation, one of the industry’s premier networking companies, is
intent on extending its leadership to the remote access market. While 3Com
currently owns less market share than some of its competitors, over the past
year the company has demonstrated its serious commitment to the remote
access market through both the development and acquisition of remote access
technologies. Recognizing that the network is, indeed, a business lifeline that
can provide a real competitive advantage, 3Com has applied its traditional
systems approach to its remote access expansion efforts. Focusing on lowering
the cost of ownership, both from a line cost and hidden cost approach, 3Com
today offers a broad range of solutions capable of meeting the varied
requirements of the booming remote access market.
Changes in Network Buying Patterns
The acquisition, delivery and sound management of information is becoming a
core business competency and a key to success. Aberdeen has observed that as
the true value of the corporate network becomes clearer and more strategic, a
new class of buying influencer is emerging in the networking market. Top level
decisions regarding the implementation and expansion of networking and
remote access solutions are more and more frequently being made by CIOs or
MIS executives who come from a financial rather than technical background.
Their decision-making process focuses on the corporate network’s capability to
deliver competitive, end-to-end business solutions. They appreciate the
strategic value of remote access to their networks because it enables employees
to obtain critical business data and to respond as quickly as possible to
customer requests and demands.
CIOs and MIS executives consider their information network to be a capital
investment. They are therefore willing to invest in solutions that can provide
the breadth and depth needed to encompass the entire corporation’s remote
access requirements, which can range from the relative simplicity of the single
remote user to the growing complexity of large enterprise connectivity.
In addition to lowering capital costs, today’s MIS are anxious to trim
administrative as well as wide-area networking costs. Investment protection is a
major purchase decision-driver. Vendors who demonstrate an understanding of
the underlying business value of the network, both from a philosophical and a
product perspective, who possess a clear sense of the particular demands of
remote access, and who recognize the importance of low cost-of-network
ownership are at a definite advantage when it comes to making the corporate
"short list" of solutions providers.
Meeting the Demands of Remote Access
3Com has a well-established reputation for being acutely aware of the business
value that networks and the information they carry hold today. The company
realizes that, as a premier networking vendor, it must provide a full range of
business-enhancing solutions, including remote access. In order to incorporate
remote access as an integral piece of its enterprise networking strategy, 3Com
has spent the past year listening to its existing customer base, observing the
remote access market, ascertaining where improvements could be made and
developing end-to-end solution suites that address end-user needs head-on.
A solid "steeped-in-the-tradition-of-the-LAN" heritage gives 3Com a good
understanding of both the needs of the remote access user and the demands of
the central site, a key point, because these two remote access components

possess fundamentally different sets of requirements. Ease-of use is the
hallmark of the remote site; connectivity integration and management are the
primary challenges of the central site. 3Com recognizes the strategic
importance of tying the LAN to the WAN. Furthermore, the company clearly
understands that every site has unique requirements that can only be fully
addressed through a wide product mix.
As ISDN availability became more widespread and its tariffing became more
reasonable, 3Com clearly recognized the growing global importance that this
technology would play in the remote access market. With its acquisition of
Access Works and Sonix in 1995, the company took the necessary steps to
ensure it possessed the expertise needed to establish and maintain a leadership
position in the ISDN segment of the remote access market.
A mainstay of 3Com’s overall strategy has been a consistent focus on lowering
the cost of ownership for users, both through technology optimization and
reduction of hidden costs. This focus is extended to the remote access market,
where 30 - 35% of the cost of ownership is assigned to line costs and a
significant 40 - 45% to support requirements.
Remote Access Market Dynamics
Remote access has grown into a billion-dollar-plus market that today is
populated by thousands of individual users and remote networks needing to
send and retrieve data to and from a central enterprise site. Propelled by ever-
evolving technologies, improved portable computer processing power, a
growing library of applications and a ground swell in demand for Internet
connection, the remote access market is growing in excess of 150% per year
and has become the hottest area in internetworking.
As the remote access market evolves, four clearly identifiable market segments
have emerged, each with its own particular needs. Nothing in the information
technology world is clean-cut, however, and these segments frequently
interlace, requiring a variety of solutions that can scale to users’ needs. For
precisely these reasons, 3Com's strategy addresses all segments of the remote
access market.
The Enterprise Customer
The first group, which consists of the enterprise customer, has roots in a data-
centric, mainframe-orientation, but has extended its reach to both
telecommuters and branch offices needing to access the enterprise network. A
subset of this segment consists of independent branches that communicate peer-
to-peer among themselves or several satellite offices which are attached to a
larger branch office. Requirements of enterprise remote access include: lower
cost of ownership, support for legacy environments, enhanced security, the
ability to protect networking investments and lower administrative costs. 3Com
has targeted this market segment with its high-end AccessBuilder high-
performance remote access servers, NetBuilder and Transcend Management
solutions.
The Small Office/Home Office Market Opportunity
The second and fastest growing remote access segment, and the one in which
Aberdeen considers 3Com to be best-established and most competitive, consists
of the small office/home office market opportunity. This group requires dial-up
connectivity to public networks such as the Internet, both for economic and
competitive reasons. Users in this category may also desire connection to a
virtual private network for information exchange. This area will continue to
experience rapid growth as access devices improve in both functionality and
scale and pricing levels decline. 3Com’s NetBuilder Remote Office, Office
Connect and low-end AccessBuilder solutions will continue to sell
competitively here.
The Individual Remote User
The third segment, the individual remote user, has been the foundation of the
remote access market and continues to play a significant role in its growth and
development. Advancements in modem technologies and the increasing
availability of faster connectivity options such as ISDN have helped to keep
this market segment vital. Because they are often the least technically oriented
and have no support resources, remote end-users depend upon solutions that
include ease-of-use, good performance, interoperability, scalability and
security. 3Com has targeted this market segment with its Impact ISDN digital
modems and adapters, AccessBuilder Internet and Etherlink III solutions.
Internet and On-line Service Providers
The unprecedented and unexpected demand for Internet access has spurred on a
fourth market classification — the Internet and On-line Service Providers.
While enterprise network extension can be categorized as private remote
network access, the Internet and On-line Service Providers are building very
large scale, homogeneous public remote access networks. These networks,

based on new technologies and new applications, are attracting a new user
group that is looking for more than mere access. Requirements for solution
success in this market include: robustness and reliability that insure availability
of service, scalability, connectivity choices, and solid management capabilities.
The most recent additions to 3Com’s remote access strategy, the AccessBuilder
5000 and AccessBuilder 8000 are designed with these criteria in mind and
should enable the company to gain good penetration in this market segment.
3Com’s On-Going Access Investment
3Com has demonstrated the depth of its commitment to all segments of the
remote access market not only through its own technology efforts, but also
though the considerable investments it has made in acquiring additional
technologies that enable a full range of solutions capable of extending the
network well beyond the enterprise. In February 1994, 3Com made its first
significant acquisition in the remote access arena with the purchase of Centrum
Communications, Inc. and its AccessBuilder line of remote access servers. In
January 1995, the company announced the acquisition of AccessWorks
Communications, its ISDN development partner. This acquisition
complemented 3Com’s own portfolio of ISDN solutions, which have proved
key to its success in the emerging small and home office environments. In June
1995, 3Com acquired Primary Access Corporation, a dominant provider of
integrated remote access systems to network service providers worldwide, and
Sonix Communications Limited, the leading U.K. ISDN internetworking
company, which gave 3Com ISDN dial-up router technology for the small
office/ home office market.
Three Families of Remote Access Solutions
The results of 3Com’s acquisitions have been incorporated into three families
of remote access solutions that comprise 3Com’s remote access strategy today.
These include the AccessBuilder, NetBuilder and Impact families, which
together offer a full range of remote access solutions from the individual user to
the carrier class/ISP provider (See Figure 1).

Spotlight on the AccessBuilder 5000
With the release of the AccessBuilder 5000, 3Com has demonstrated the depth
of its commitment to the remote access marketplace by adding a significant
piece to its remote access solutions set. A high-end LAN/WAN switch, the
AccessBuilder 5000 is designed to meet the requirements of mission-critical
networks needing fault-tolerant support and non-blocking architectures for a
high concentration of remote users. Today, the scalable, 7 and 17-slot chassis
platforms can support up to 256 WAN ports, multiple LAN segments and can
accommodate either Ethernet or Token-Ring media. High performance
distributed RISC processor and several options for high-speed LAN media
modules provide the processing capacity to handle new media technologies.
The AccessBuilder 5000 treats remote end-users like local network nodes,
dynamically switching incoming calls to different enterprise LAN segments.
Built-in bridging and routing functionality allows users to run multiple
concurrent sessions and exchange information among them.
Three elements are especially important both to managers and remote users:
comprehensive fault tolerance, network management and enhanced security.
Any remote access platform that is designed for use in mission-critical
networks must include these elements. The AccessBuilder 5000 design
provides significant value-add in these areas (see Table 1).

Transcend Networking: Extending the Reach
The consistent embodiment of 3Com’s networking philosophy is Transcend
Networking, which incorporates integrated management and comprehensive
LAN and WAN solutions in order to extend Transcend benefits to 3Com remote
access systems and products. This framework for building and managing
networks encompasses products and systems and is designed to provide
simplicity, performance and reliability in order to ensure universal network
access. The principle behind the Transcend framework is that networks are
dynamic and that users need the capability to incorporate new technologies into
their networks while leveraging their existing platforms and configurations.
Transcend springs from three basic tenets: scaling performance, growth
management and extension of the network reach from a central core to remote
offices, small businesses and mobile users.
Commitment Through Customer Support
3Com also extends its remote access philosophy to its approach to customer
support. With the advent of remote networking, new challenges have arisen for
network managers and end-users alike. A decentralized employee base has
moved many users beyond the immediate physical reach of the network
manager. Moreover, because of their size and financial resources, branch, small
and home offices do not usually have MIS personnel on-site. Many remote
users are not knowledgeable in areas such as line provisioning, nor do they
have significant amounts of time to spend on networking issues and still be able
to fulfill their normal work requirements. And, because of the increasingly
mission-critical nature of information access, users of all sizes are demanding
improved levels of service and efficiency.
Being sensitive to the increasing importance of networks for business strategy
and understanding that remote access creates certain unique challenges both for
network managers and for end-users, 3Com has designed a support program
that Aberdeen views as specifically addressing the needs of remote networkers.
Its components include:
• Technical support and on-site service;
• Hardware replacement/spare parts;
• Professional services;
• ISDN line provisioning and
• Remote Access Gold Seal Resellers.

3Com’s offer of free ISDN line ordering and provisioning has proved to be
especially successful. Customers can make a single phone call to an 800
number, where they gain access to 3Com’s team of ISDN professionals to
deploy ISDN in homes, small offices and branch offices. This specially trained
team efficiently orders and correctly provisions ISDN lines for 3Com’s broad
product range.
3Com’s Channels
Aberdeen contends that a diversified channel strategy is the most effective
approach for successfully penetrating the remote access market, which cuts
horizontally across all internetworking market segments. 3Com has invested
heavily in developing this diversity. The company’s strong, long-standing,
multi-tiered channel strategy, one of the more successful in internetworking,
has been complemented by its acquisition in 1995 of a worldwide network of
single-tier, higher-end VARs and network integrators, providing much needed
market penetration for the company at the Fortune 500 level.
Recently restructured, 3Com has established two separate global business units,
one to address the needs of low-and-middle-tier customers, the other the needs
of high-end enterprise, ISP and telecommunications customers. This move
places the company in a solid position to address the requirements of remote
access implementation across the internetworking spectrum.
Conclusions
Building on its LAN heritage, 3Com views remote access as an integral
component of its overall corporate networking strategy. The company fully
understands both ends of the remote access equation — the remote client and
the central site — and the critical role that each plays in creating successful
remote access solutions. While not yet a market leader in the remote access
arena, 3Com has put in place a remote access strategy that Aberdeen considers
logical and reasonable. The company is now executing that strategy by
delivering the technology and solution sets that are required.
Aberdeen is impressed with the breadth of 3Com’s remote access solutions,
which range from terminal adapters that can be installed in fifteen minutes to
complex integrated access switching platforms that offer the robustness and
redundancy required of the ISP and carrier marketplace. We believe that this
product range is necessary for any vendor to achieve leadership of the remote
access marketplace. Because of its established strength in the small-office/home
office market, which has been built on reliable, plug-and-play products, and its
efforts to lower cost of ownership both from a line cost and a hidden cost
perspective, Aberdeen expects 3Com to continue to enjoy increasing success in
this market. The challenge for 3Com will come as it tries to wrest marketshare
in the enterprise and ISP/on-line provider market segments, both of which have
become increasingly competitive. Perhaps 3Com’s greatest asset, and the one
its competitors are most covetous of, is its channels. The most important
element in 3Com’s remote access play will be its ability to energize its channels
to view the network both from a LAN and a WAN perspective and to convince
both channels and end-users that the company can, indeed, translate its LAN
strength into a solid LAN/WAN remote access networking story.

### Abstract

Aberdeen Group assesses 3Com Corporation's strategic expansion into the remote access market as of early 1997, following a year of acquisitions (Centrum, AccessWorks, Primary Access, Sonix) and product launches. The study describes the four market segments of remote access (enterprise, SOHO, individual user, ISP/carrier), evaluates 3Com's three solution families (AccessBuilder, NetBuilder, Impact), and concludes that while 3Com is not yet the market leader it has a logical and executable strategy, with its greatest strength in the SOHO segment and its channels as its most valuable competitive asset.
