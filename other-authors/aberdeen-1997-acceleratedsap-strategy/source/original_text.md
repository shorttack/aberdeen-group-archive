# AcceleratedSAP: Fast Implementation, Rapid Realization of SAP Benefits

### Preface

Business executives want to obtain all the benefits of R/3 — but at an even faster rate. This is the message from customers in the course of implementing SAP R/3 or currently evaluating the use of R/3 for their business. SAP's response, AcceleratedSAP, is a rapid implementation option specifically designed for enterprises that are prepared to exploit the tremendous value of R/3 quickly while laying the technology foundation for continued business improvement.

### Executive Overview

ROI — both return on information and return on investment — continues to be the overriding goal for any enterprise implementing SAP's very popular, integrated, client-server software solution, R/3. And as with any business investment, achieving or exceeding the expected return on this investment is always welcome as soon as possible.

AcceleratedSAP — an implementation program for SAP's software, R/3 — was conceived, designed, and tested at customer sites by SAP to meet the objective of faster returns for companies implementing R/3. The team behind its development captured and analyzed cumulative learning from years of R/3 implementations, identified successful accelerators in process methodologies, eliminated redundant implementation efforts, and leveraged the ever-increasing automated configuration capabilities in R/3's Business Engineer. The result is an efficient R/3 implementation option for customers whose primary objective is achieving critical business functionality from an R/3 investment in four to ten months.

Initially designed with the requirements of North American customers in mind — specifically those with annual revenues between $200 million and $2.5 billion — AcceleratedSAP is now being evaluated and used both on a global basis and by multi-billion dollar customers to achieve more rapid returns on an SAP investment. Offered both by SAP and many of SAP's implementation partners, AcceleratedSAP is also an option for enterprises that require a start-today, finish-in-1998 Year 2000 transition off legacy applications.

It is important to note that AcceleratedSAP as an offering is not feasible or desirable for all R/3 implementations. Enterprises that need significant operational improvement through large-scale reengineering would be better served by a more traditional R/3 implementation effort that emphasizes process improvement over rapid deployment. However, the principles of acceleration embodied in AcceleratedSAP are universally applicable and available across R/3 implementations.

### The AcceleratedSAP Difference

What differentiates AcceleratedSAP is that it:

- is the first branded implementation solution to be developed and directly supported by SAP;
- offers an up-front SAP-derived pre-planning estimation process;
- provides standard questionnaires aligned to the Business Engineer;
- creates a framework for expectation alignment between customer and consulting teams.

Customers are well positioned to benefit from SAP's continued and substantial R&D efforts incorporated in version upgrades.

In addition, continued innovations in R/3's Business Engineer — a configuration, modeling, implementation, and continuous improvement tool — offer real speed advantages to companies who intend to match specific business requirements to R/3's capabilities. The Business Engineer is the underlying tool for AcceleratedSAP that enables rapid implementation and continuous process improvement.

AcceleratedSAP also differs from more traditional software implementation efforts because executives must be prepared for rapid software deployment. This applies to any enterprise resource planning (ERP) package implementation — including R/3. This preparedness requires irrefutable project motivation, an effective decision-making process, clear business objectives, low institutionalized cultural resistance to change, and an agreement to banish scope creep.

Executives also must be prepared to estimate the degree to which their business can adopt R/3's best practice processes versus the potential modification required for their own operational needs. For executives who are confident that their enterprises do not have substantive change management, reengineering or R/3 modification requirements, AcceleratedSAP offers significant time and resource savings while laying the groundwork for continued operational improvements.

### The Six Steps of AcceleratedSAP

Before a business chooses AcceleratedSAP as an implementation path, SAP consultants help gauge the appropriateness of this offering for a particular situation. These consultants use an SAP Project Estimator kit that includes pre-defined questionnaires to interview senior executives and operating managers about: 1) their expectations of the use of R/3 for their business and 2) the required speed of deployment. They match the responses against a database of historical implementation information and derive potential scope, resource, and cost estimates.

The resulting pre-planning estimates will be rough yet powerful because the numbers will offer the basis for discussion, disagreement, and improvements at the start of an R/3 implementation. Too often, R/3 implementations begin with implicit and mismatched expectations both within the customer organization and between the customer and its independent professional services advisors. This two day process aligns expectations and allows SAP consultants to recommend an AcceleratedSAP approach if a customer is prepared to implement R/3 quickly with minimal system changes.

#### Step 1: Project Preparation — Organizational Readiness

A company that chooses AcceleratedSAP as an implementation path is ready to discover business solutions within R/3. By this first phase, the company's decision-makers have agreed that R/3's best practice processes will support the bulk of their own business requirements and that major modifications to R/3 would be unlikely. This acceptance of R/3 embedded processes will allow months to be shaved from the implementation time.

In addition, executives preparing for the use of AcceleratedSAP should feel secure that there is a disciplined and active decision-making process in place, clear project objectives, minimal cultural resistance to change, and an agreement to banish scope creep. Once project guidelines are established, an implementation team of managers from across the business is assembled and the Preparation phase moves to the project start.

#### Step 2: Business Blueprint — Define the Business Requirements

The second phase of AcceleratedSAP is possibly the most challenging. Through a series of very detailed questionnaires used in one-on-one interviews, group discussions and executive sessions, SAP consultants learn the customer's core business and document exactly the processes that a company uses today. In a six month implementation, for example, this iterative phase will require three to four weeks of very concentrated effort. The result is a comprehensive blueprint of the customer's business.

#### Step 3: Simulation — R/3 Knowledge Transfer To Customers

Once a Business Blueprint is agreed upon, customer team members on the AcceleratedSAP team begin concentrated training on the R/3 system. In parallel, AcceleratedSAP consultants start the configuration of the R/3 system based on the Blueprint. Because an AcceleratedSAP customer starts the project with a willingness to accept best practice processes, the configuration can proceed very rapidly.

#### Step 4: Validation — Configuration Finalized and Knowledge Transfer Continued

While the Business Blueprint allows the configuration of most of a customer's R/3 system, there will always be specific and unique business scenarios that need to be addressed. This is the real detail work where the collective AcceleratedSAP team must ensure that the final R/3 configuration best supports the enterprise's business processes.

#### Step 5: Final Preparation — Finish Testing and End-user Training

Because AcceleratedSAP is designed to test the system throughout the implementation, efforts in this fifth phase focus on testing interfaces and end-user acceptance. AcceleratedSAP customers must work with their SAP consultants to ensure that training is comprehensive and timely — an understandable goal that technology managers tell Aberdeen is often surprisingly complex to coordinate.

#### Step 6: Go Live and Support — Measuring the Benefits of the Investment

This is the point where an enterprise's own employees are self-sufficient to run their own R/3 system. While SAP consultants will be available for specific additional work — for example, an upcoming month-end financial close — the AcceleratedSAP program should have provided the enterprise with the system, tools and skills to both operate and measure the benefits of its R/3 investment.

### Key Benefits of AcceleratedSAP

**Allows Rapid R/3 Deployment for Critical Year 2000 Transitions**

The Year 2000 problem is real, looming, and demands action today. One option to counter Year 2000 vulnerability is to migrate from legacy applications and replace many non-compliant and older systems. AcceleratedSAP enables any size enterprise to rapidly replicate existing functionality.

**Facilitates a Standardized Global Roll-out**

AcceleratedSAP offers a common methodology that can be used by several different consulting firms across geographic or line of business boundaries in parallel implementations.

**Offers Re-usable Approach for R/3 Deployment**

Once learned, either as a pilot or a first phase R/3 roll-out, the knowledge of AcceleratedSAP and R/3 resides in the client organization. Customers planning a second phase using an AcceleratedSAP implementation tell Aberdeen that they will need to use far fewer external resources for their next implementation.

**Provides Optimal Foundation for R/3 Upgrades**

Because SAP spends hundreds of millions of R&D dollars annually to improve R/3, customers that minimize modifications will find it easier to migrate from one version to another. AcceleratedSAP helps customers find solutions within R/3.

### Abstract

This 1997 Aberdeen Group white paper profiles SAP's AcceleratedSAP (ASAP) rapid-implementation methodology, designed to deploy R/3 in four to ten months for mid-market enterprises. It describes the six-step ASAP process (Project Preparation, Business Blueprint, Simulation, Validation, Final Preparation, Go Live), the TeamSAP partner ecosystem, and positions ASAP as the preferred path for Year 2000 legacy replacement. Aberdeen assesses ASAP as a credible differentiator that reduces implementation redundancy and creates a reusable knowledge foundation.
