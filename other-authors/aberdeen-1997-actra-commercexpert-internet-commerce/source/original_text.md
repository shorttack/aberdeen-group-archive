**Abstract: Profile**

**Actra Corp.**

**No Longer an Internet Commerce Paper Tiger, Actra's CommerceXpert is Now in the Hunt**

This spring Aberdeen questioned whether Actra could deliver on what we believed was an exceptionally ambitious product plan. Our general opinion was: "Runs great on the overhead projector." Over the last five months the company has generally delivered on these plans: releasing the Internet EDI and structured messaging product, ECXpert, to Beta and GA status; delivering a new version of its Knowledge Publishing product, PublishingXpert to General Availability (GA); bringing the sales-to-order product, SellerXpert to Beta and GA status; and delivering its procurement product, BuyerXpert, to early customers -- all on or near the release times promised in April meetings. These products are individually competent competitors in the Internet and Electronic Commerce markets; they each could merit consideration for best-of-breed in their respective area of business process automation.

Although this product family is still a "work in process," taken together it represents a formidable offering. Combine these products with the distribution capability of the parents -- Netscape Communications and General Electric Information Services -- and you understand why other suppliers in this market are now looking over their shoulders towards Sunnyvale.

David Alschuler

The combined CommerceXpert product family, backed by the distribution power of Netscape and GEIS, positions Actra as a formidable competitor in the emerging business-to-business Internet commerce market.
