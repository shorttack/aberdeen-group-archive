Testing SOA and Web Services Applications: 
How Different Can It Be? 
 
Testing and QA pose unique challenges to organizations deploying SOA and 
web-services based applications. Unit and functional testing no longer are 
enough; integration and orchestration testing are critical new pieces of the 
overall testing strategy. Add performance and versioning testing to the mix 
and you have the ingredients for significant change in the QA department.  
Best in Class PACE Hypothesis 
Testing and QA have consistently been at the top of the list of concerns for 
survey respondents. In one study (ESB/Middleware Benchmark, July 2006), 
43% of the overall response pool indicated that testing and deployment 
were key challenges. In June of 2007, 48% of the participants in the SOA 
Middleware study reported that they planned to implement new strategies in 
testing SOA applications. Also in June 2007, the Modernizing Legacy 
Applications report showed that 28% of all respondents felt that changing 
existing processes, including test, was a key challenge. 
Current testing tools and methodologies can only go so far. New tools and 
techniques, developed specifically for the component and messaging 
software environment, should allow organizations to pull together design, 
development, and test into one integrated process and to address the 
unique requirements of a distributed application architecture. 
Aberdeen’s Hypothesis 
We believe that organizations that have deployed specialized SOA and web 
services quality management tools outperform those that are using tools 
designed for more traditional software. We also believe that organizations 
which have significantly modified their QA and test processes to 
accommodate composite applications enjoy Best in Class performance.
