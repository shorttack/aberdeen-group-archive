### Abstract

Boston Globe (May 1994) reports DEC CFO William M. Steul, 51, resigned a month after DEC's stock plummeted on a $183M unexpected Q3 loss. Second top-rank departure since the April 15 announcement (Edward E. Lucente, worldwide sales chief and #2 exec, left days after). Vincent J. Mullarkey, 46, corporate controller (Bentley College 1971, joined DEC same year), promoted to CFO. Quote from Franc Romano of Aberdeen Group (former DEC employee) criticizes Mullarkey: 'The only financial experience Vin has ever had was with Digital. I don't believe he brings the broad perspective that is necessary there or the cash management skills the company is going to need.' Counter-quote from Richard Buchanan of Forrester Research: DEC's accounting system 'is so arcane and quite specific to the history that Digital has passed through. To bring in an outsider to decipher that would be tough to do' but 'is broken in many ways and needs to be fixed.' Steul was 22-year DEC veteran, two years as CFO; predecessor was James Osterhoff (Ford Motor recruit, highly regarded for financial discipline). Mullarkey was Robert Palmer's controller in DEC semiconductor group.
