TECH GROUP RESEARCH / MORGAN, KEEGAN & COMPANY, INC.

HYPERGROWTH BALANCE SHEETS - NEGLECTED STEPCHILDREN?

In EVP 1-3 we reviewed the structure of hypergrowth; in EVP 4 we saw that poor controls and 
questionable practices are difficult to detect in the P&L. In this EVP we analyze a few aspects 
of hypergrowth balance sheets and find they are more neglected than they should be.

MAKING THE EPS GOAL IN SPITE OF LOOSE CONTROLS
FTI at end of fiscal 82 (August 31st year): 56% revenue gain vs unpublished 50% plan. FTI was 
in a Cost Push mode of operation.

COST-PUSH DISTORTS STATED RECEIVABLES
No change in terms, delinquencies, or returns from plan to actual.
Average monthly receivables < quarter-end statements.
Stated results distorted by heavy weighting of final two weeks of quarterly shipments.
Conclusion: apparent receivables increase is symptom of underlying cost control problem.

WHEN IS A DOLLAR ONLY WORTH FORTY CENTS? WHEN IT IS CALLED "INVENTORY"
FTI gross margins 60%; inventory at cost = 40 cents on sales dollar.
Three inventory categories vs one receivables category; 10-Q required for breakout.
