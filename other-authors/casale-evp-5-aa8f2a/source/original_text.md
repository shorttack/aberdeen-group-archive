EXECUTIVE VIEWPOINT - EVP 5
Charles T. Casale
January 7, 1983
TECH GROUP RESEARCH / MORGAN, KEEGAN & COMPANY, INC.

HYPERGROWTH BALANCE SHEETS - NEGLECTED STEPCHILDREN?

In EVP 1-3 we reviewed the structure of hypergrowth; in EVP 4 we saw that poor controls and 
questionable practices are difficult to detect in the P&L. In this EVP we analyze a few aspects 
of hypergrowth balance sheets and find they are more neglected than they should be.

MAKING THE EPS GOAL IN SPITE OF LOOSE CONTROLS
FTI at end of fiscal 82 (August 31st year): 56% revenue gain vs unpublished 50% plan. FTI was 
in a Cost Push mode of operation.

BAM PREACHES THE BALANCE SHEET GOSPEL
BAM (Better Asset Management) reviews FTI's FY82 balance sheet.

TABLE 1 - FTI BALANCE SHEET - FISCAL YEAR 1982 ($M)
ASSETS: Cash 30.0 | Receivables 190.0 | Inventories 155.0 | Prepaid 15.0 | TOTAL CURRENT 390.0
Plants & Fixtures 60.0 | Equipment (net) 110.0 | TOTAL ASSETS 500.0
LIABILITIES: Notes to Banks 3.0 | AP 47.0 | Accrued 40.0 | TOTAL CURRENT 90.0 
Deferred Taxes 35.0 | LT Debt 55.0 | TOTAL LIABILITIES 180.0
Common Stock 200.0 | Retained Earnings 120.0 | SHAREHOLDERS EQUITY 320.0

CFO: 90 days DSO is norm; FTI planned 100 days FY82 for high-interest environment.
IBM: considerably less than 100 days. DEC: under 90 days.

TABLE 2 - FTI QUARTERLY RECEIVABLES
FY81 Q4: Revenue 90.0 | Receivables 89.0 | Days 90
FY82 Q1: Revenue 100.0 | Receivables 112.0 | Days 102
FY82 Q2: Revenue 112.0 | Receivables 132.0 | Days 107
FY82 Q3: Revenue 125.0 | Receivables 155.0 | Days 113
FY82 Q4: Revenue 141.0 | Receivables 190.0 | Days 123

TABLE 3 - FTI FY82 SUMMARY PLAN
Revenue: Plan 460 | Actual 478 | Diff +18 (+4%)
EPS: Plan 2.75 | Actual 2.75 | Diff 0
Receivables: Plan 146 | Actual 190 | Diff +44 (+30%)

TABLE 4 - FTI FY82 MONTHLY OPERATING PLAN
[25/35/40 production allocation per quarter; actual results show increasing skew to final month]
Steady-state plan DSO: 102 days. Actual Q4 DSO: 123 days.

COST-PUSH DISTORTS STATED RECEIVABLES
No change in terms, delinquencies, or returns from plan to actual.
Average monthly receivables < quarter-end statements.
Stated results distorted by heavy weighting of final two weeks of quarterly shipments.
Conclusion: apparent receivables increase is symptom of underlying cost control problem.

WHEN IS A DOLLAR ONLY WORTH FORTY CENTS? WHEN IT IS CALLED "INVENTORY"
FTI gross margins 60%; inventory at cost = 40 cents on sales dollar.
Three inventory categories vs one receivables category; 10-Q required for breakout.

INVESTMENT RELEVANCE
Numbers useful but not to precision degree some wish. Compare original vs restated figures.

Next: EVP series to be aperiodic, issued in response to current investor interest.

### Abstract

EVP #5 in the Executive Viewpoint series analyzes balance sheet dynamics of a composite hypergrowth tech company (FTI), focusing on accounts receivable DSO expansion, inventory valuation asymmetry, and the distorting effect of end-of-quarter shipment concentration. Casale argues that apparent receivables deterioration is a symptom of loose operational cost control rather than credit quality issues, and that balance sheets are systematically underanalyzed by institutional investors focused on P/E ratios.
