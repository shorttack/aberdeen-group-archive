## Abstract

June 7, 1982 Computer Business News page-8 cover-class feature by Peter Bochner on Stratus Computer's fault-tolerant systems strategy. Pete Kastner — quoted as 'Stratus' manager of marketing development' — outlines the most extensive publicly-recorded Stratus strategy of the era: (1) OEM channel: 'any company starting out that ignores OEMs is ignoring a big opportunity'; software houses earn a fee on each Stratus system sold incorporating their software; (2) competitive positioning: Stratus/32 pits against the Prime 750, DEC VAX 11/750 and IBM 4341 Model 1 on price/performance, NOT directly head-to-head with Tandem; (3) second-generation plans: at least a year away, will maintain back-compatibility, may use a different CPU via code-generator change to protect user software investment; (4) industry forecast: by 1986 'all broad-based computer companies will have to offer fault-tolerant systems' and at least half a dozen will be shipping by end of 1983; (5) market thesis: fault-tolerant 'blossoming from a niche market to a cross market' as more work goes online and downtime cost rises. Bochner notes Kastner is 'a former analyst at Arthur D. Little' — the FIRST appearance of Kastner's ADL background in the Aberdeen archive. The article also covers Stratus 3270 emulator, X.25 Networking, four-68000-chip CPU pairing, 7%/yr maintenance vs industry 10-12%/yr, remote diagnostics, and Edson de Castro of DG warning startups have problems shipping second-gen products.
