## Abstract

June 2002 Computerworld feature contrasting Intel's then-imminent Itanium 2 (McKinley) with Sun Microsystems' UltraSPARC server line. Peter Kastner — quoted as 'executive VP of the Aberdeen Group in Boston' — characterizes Itanium 2 as putting 'more pressure on Sun earlier than we had anticipated', cautions readers to 'wait until [Itanium 2] are announced by the OEMs' for real-world performance and frames Intel's positioning: 'Clearly Intel wants to compare the Itanium with the best-in-breed of the RISC processors where Sun has the broadest market share.' Kastner notes Itanium 2 has 'trumped even Moore's Law in some performance tests' but that the benchmarks that really matter will be those tested on shipping OEM systems. Critically, Kastner observes that the 1-2 performance benchmarks are 'probably a little too close for comfort for Sun's liking' in 1-way to 4-way boxes, but that Sun's UltraSPARC chips 'can really strut their stuff in systems with up to 100 processors operating simultaneously'. This is a clean Aberdeen-EVP Kastner quote on the Intel-vs-RISC server-architecture debate that defined the early 2000s enterprise server market.
