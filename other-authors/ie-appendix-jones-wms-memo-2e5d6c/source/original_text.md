Subject: 
Availability of Client/Server WMS in 1994 
 
This memo is in response to your research question, “What client-server warehouse 
management software (WMS) packages were available in the marketplace in the U.S. in 
mid-1994?” 
While the technology supporting client/server was available in 1994 and before, my 
research has not uncovered commercially available Warehouse Management Programs 
(WMS) that were client/server based in the time frame in question. 
The two modes of communicating warehouse management information at that time were 
radio-frequency handhelds and Digital Equipment Corp. VT 100 or VT 200 terminals in 
emulation mode, neither of which is client/server.  Suppliers of software applications often 
referred to the handhelds as "clients;" however, this does not correspond to Aberdeen's 
definition of client/server ⎯ an application cleaved such that the portion interfacing with 
the user is running locally and the business logic of the application itself runs on another 
computer. 
The first instance of commercially released client/server WMS I uncovered was PkMS by 
Manhattan Associates in 1995.  Catalyst, one of the very first WMS providers used 
Windows and Vermont Views on RF handhelds in 1993-94, but did not offer true 
client/server replacements for VT 200s prior to their GUI desktop program, shipped in 
1996.
