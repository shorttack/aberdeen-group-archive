Victorious Incumbents Vow Change For CA

Computer Associates vowed today to improve service and communication in the wake of a failed attempt to unseat chairman Charles Wang. 

By David Joachim

InternetWeek

August 29, 2001 12:18 PM

Hauppague, NY--Computer Associates vowed today to improve service and communication in the wake of a failed attempt to unseat chairman Charles Wang. 

Wang and three other incumbent directors garnered 75 percent of shareholders votes at CA's annual meeting, according to unofficial results. Certified results will be released in mid September. 

CA executives acknowledged complaints about customer service and support that were among Texas investor Sam Wyly's grounds for the challenge. But they pointed to changes under way to improve CA'sstanding, including subscription pricing options and a new 650-employee customer support organization.

"We have to get better at communicating [with customers] and we need to make sure that no customer walks away because we didn't service them properly," Wang said. 

CA exeuctives also denied that the changes were related to the proxy challenge, noting that many of them predated the summer-long battle. 

"If people want to say that Mr. Wyly urged us to do all this, let them," said President and CEO Sanjay Kumar. Wyly initially wanted to replace the entire board and split the company in four. 

Analysts agreed that changes would have occured anyway. "It's fair to say that CA has been trying with a fair amount of desperation to rid itself of the long standing prejorative that 'CA screws it customers,'" said Aberdeen Group analyst Peter Kastner. "But CA continually tweaks its sales and support organization and I certainly don't consider it to be a result of the Wyly thing at all." 

At least one CA customer was relieved by the outcome. "I'm very pleased," said Andrew Winer, CIO of plastics maker Myers Industries. "The idea of splitting up the company was absurd." 

Myers uses CA's Neugents predictive analysis software and BizWorks process management software, both of which are part of CA's network management security and supply chain products group. "The benefits that are coming into focus on business intelligence technology requires collaboration between different pieces and parts to make it work. If Wyly had pulled the company apart, CA would have become just a network management company."
