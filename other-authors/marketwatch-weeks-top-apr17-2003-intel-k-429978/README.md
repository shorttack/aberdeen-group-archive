## Abstract

MarketWatch 'week's top stories' roundup (Apr 17 2003, 6:06 p.m. EST) covering April 14-17, 2003 — the week that included Intel's Q1 2003 earnings (reported Tuesday Apr 15 2003) and Apple's earnings. Peter Kastner, chief research officer at Aberdeen Group, provides the Intel commentary: 'Given the global economic uncertainty, Intel has executed with some dexterity and deserves a lot of credit for their numbers.' Intel stock rose ~$2 to $18.66 by week-end. Article frame is the dot-com-bust-plus-Iraq-war-economic-uncertainty backdrop. Apple posted a 65% YoY earnings decline and denied rumors of a Universal Music bid. Other stories covered: Bush $550B tax cut, weekly market wrap-up. Kastner's Intel quote captures the 'Intel-as-defensive-holding' framing characteristic of spring-2003 analyst commentary.
