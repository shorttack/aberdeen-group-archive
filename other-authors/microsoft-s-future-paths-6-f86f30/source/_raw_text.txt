Program Description
Jackson Shaw, lead product manager for the Windows .NET Server Solutions Group at Microsoft, along 
with chief research officer Peter Kastner and research analyst Isaac Ro of the Aberdeen Group, discuss the 
future directions of Microsoft enterprise products. The program describes Microsoft's next generation of 
operating systems - code-named Longhorn and Blackcomb; the emerging Tablet PC; and advances in 
speech recognition, knowledge management and storage. In addition, the program looks at how .NET will 
tie together all these advances. In outlining the ways these initiatives will change computing, this 
presentation will help managers plot their upgrade paths and strategies over the next few years.

Viewers of the CD and online versions of the program will have instant access to numerous Web pages 
concerning Microsoft's current and future products and research, as well as the texts of several of Bill 
Gates' keynote addresses detailing Microsoft's plans for the future. Other Web resources include links to: 
Directions on Microsoft, a high-tech newsletter that gives up-to-date, unbiased information on where the 
software giant is headed; Pen Computing Magazine; Pocket PC Magazine; and Web Services Journal. 
Articles include: 'Analysts See Bumpy Road Ahead for .NET'; 'Microsoft Rethinks Exchange Storage 
Architecture'; 'MS Poised to Switch Windows File Systems With Blackcomb'; and 'Smooth Sailing for 
Microsoft's .NET?.' White papers include: 'Bells and Whistler'; 'Introducing Microsoft .NET'; 'Microsoft Aims 
at the Enterprise' and 'J2EE vs. Microsoft .NET.'

Biography
Kathleen Koch is a television news correspondent based in Washington, D.C., where she has worked for a 
variety of news organizations since the mid-1980s. Since 2001, her beat has been technology/consumer 
issues and aviation. She has received a variety of print and broadcast news honors, and has been an 
invited speaker at Harvard's Women in Public Policy Forum and the annual meeting of the Society of 
Professional Journalists. Kathleen majored in journalism at the University of Southern Mississippi, with a 
double minor in political science and French. She was a Rotary Foundation International Graduate Scholar, 
studying at the University of Dijon in France. 

Interviewee
Peter Kastner
Biography
Peter Kastner is chief research officer of Aberdeen Group. He manages the research business unit and is 
responsible for the content of Aberdeen's research products and consulting services. Using his extensive 
commercial information technology experience and the full gamut of Aberdeen Group research, Kastner 
actively consults with suppliers on executive-level market research and positioning, industry analysis, 
strategic planning development, product planning and competitive strategies. He has served as ad hoc 
marketing counsel to technology startups, been instrumental in the strategy and execution of billion-dollar 
rollouts, advised senior executives in business strategy, including mergers and acquisitions, and served as 
a technology market expert witness. In 2001, Kastner initiated Aberdeen Group's Personal IT practice 
area, which focuses on traditional PCs, small office/home office (SOHO) computing, and the convergence 
of consumer computing with home networking, entertainment, imaging and messaging. Kastner has more 
than 30 years of IT industry experience in both user and supplier organizations. His expertise includes 
online transaction processing (OLTP), decision support, client/server architectures, distributed commercial 
systems development, e-commerce, and commercial benchmark auditing and analysis. Prior to joining 
Aberdeen Group as vice president in 1988, Kastner was a marketing executive with Digital Equipment, 
Prime Computer, and Stratus Computer. For more than a decade, Kastner was a consultant and program 
manager with Arthur D. Little, Inc., where he was actively involved in the management and execution of 
complex technology development projects spanning the manufacturing, banking and financial services, 
health care and government markets that frequently included mainframe, minicomputer and desktop 
systems. 

Interviewee
Isaac Ro
Biography
Isaac Ro is a research associate in Aberdeen Group's Personal IT Research Practice, which covers personal 
technologies from a hardware, software, and services perspective. He focuses on the handheld computing 
industry: researching, analyzing, and reporting on the business and technical impact of handhelds in the 
supplier and end user communities. Ro is currently researching the market for mobile middleware vendors 
and tracking the continuing diversification of mobile devices. Additionally, he continues to track and 
publish research on the evolution of the handheld market - as larger independent software vendors deliver 
mobile enterprise solutions, and wireless functionalities become pervasive. His research has been featured 
in publications such as Investors Business Daily, Information Week, internet.com, and CRM Daily. Ro holds 
a B.A. with honors in History from Middlebury College.

Interviewee
Jackson Shaw
Biography
Jackson Shaw is lead product manager, Windows .NET Server Marketing. He has been working in the
directory services area for more than ten years and has been with Microsoft since 1999. Prior to joining
Microsoft to work in Active Directory and Metadirectory product planning, he was a vice president at
ZOOMIT Corporation. While at ZOOMIT, Jackson participated in the creation, development, marketing and
sales of the world's first metadirectory product. His areas of focus include directory, security, PKI, and
encryption technologies.
