 What's Happening in High-Tech Supply Chains

 Staff -- Purchasing, 11/20/2003

 Just as the $40 billion market for mobile-phone networks seems to be stabilizing, a new study says the industry may be facing a steep revenue decline because of the spread of more-efficient third-generation (3G) technology. 

 Recent improvement in ground delivery business points to an imminent commercial rebound, says John Beystehner, senior vice president of marketing for United Parcel Service. He expects 2004 ground business to expand at least at the same rate as the overall economy. 

 Government reports say spending on software and equipment jumped in the third quarter of 2003 at an annual rate of 15.4%, an increase not seen since before 2000 when the technology bubble burst. Most analysts reject the data, however, citing corporate reports of only single-digit revenue growth over the previous year. "That 15.4% rise is not being reflected in IT (information technology) budgets or the revenues of IT companies," says analyst Peter Kastner at Aberdeen Research. 

 Lighter, thinner notebook personal computers are pushing laptop sales ahead of desktop sales. To support these new laptop PCs, Intel has a new Centrino chip bundle that includes Pentium M, a new processor that conserves energy better than earlier notebook chips, and a wireless module, a new product category for the Santa Clara, Calif.-based chipmaker.

 A global semiconductor trade group has raised its 2003 forecast for worldwide chip sales after three years of flat or falling sales—and sees even stronger growth in 2004. The world chip market will reach $161 billion in 2003, an increase of 14.2% over 2002 when $141 billion worth were sold around the world, the World Semiconductor Trade Statistics forecasts. The new forecast is higher than the outlook released in early 2003, when WSTS expected 11.5% growth this year.

 Laptops will represent an increasing percentage of personal computer sales in coming years. Lower prices, longer battery life, and integrated wireless networking will help spur sales of notebook computers. Sales will continue to rise as a result of improvements in technology.
