Sybase told software and securities analysts last October, on the eve of a major new release of its flagship database system, SQL Server System 11, that SAP would support the database with its R/3 software someti me this year. "We think it's very important for the customers [to have R/3 running on Sybase]," says Mike Regan, general manager of applications support at Sybase in Emeryville, Calif. Now it looks as if that support won't happen until next year at the earliest. "The only people who can determine when are [at] SAP," says Regan. The problem has to do with a relational database feature known as row-level locking. Sybase's System 11, like its predecessor System 10, does not support row-level locking. SAP depends on the database server locking at a particular row of data, rather than a full page of data, for satisfactory performance. "The ball is totally in Sybase's court at this point," says Paul Wahl, executive VP and international marketing director at SAP headquarters in Walldorf, Germany. Adds an SAP spokeswoman: "They have not proven to SAP that Sybase 11 can perform to the level we would need for R/3 to be made available on Sybase." Analysts say Sybase needs to resolve the problem as soon as possible. ### Abstract

InformationWeek news story (Jan 29 1996, Issue #564) on a feud between SAP AG and Sybase Inc. over database support for SAP's R/3 ERP system. Sybase had promised in October 1995 that SAP R/3 would support its SQL Server System 11 database, but the support does not appear imminent: Mike Regan (GM applications support, Sybase Emeryville) says SAP decides the timing; Paul Wahl (EVP international marketing, SAP Walldorf) says the ball is in Sybase's court. The dispute centers on row-level locking, which Sybase System 10/11 does not support; SAP R/3 requires it for satisfactory multi-user performance. An anonymous former Sybase source describes row-level locking as 'a deal-breaker' for SAP. Peter Kastner, VP and analyst at Aberdeen Group Inc. in Boston, comments: 'There is no doubt Sybase is missing a huge amount of business with SAP. Just look at what a great business SAP has been for Informix.'
