Other PDAs > News > SD Memory Cards to Drive Market for Digital Content --> SD Memory Cards to Drive Market for Digital Content SD Memory Cards to Drive Market for Digital Content By Dan Muse October 28, 2003 The future of digital content is a memory card about the size of a postage stamp, according to research conducted by Aberdeen Group for the Internet Home Alliance. The Boston-based research firm and cross-industry network of companies — which boasts a roster of members ranging from Cisco and Microsoft to Sears and Whirlpool — will announce on Wednesday research findings that indicate consumer adoption of memory cards will continue to grow and that SD (Secure Digital) memory cards are becoming the leading format for storing and transporting digital content. The small memory cards are already the expansion media of choice for PDAs and other types of handheld devices. The postage-stamp size SD Memory Cards hold from 8MB to 4GB (expected by end of 2004) of data and are ideally suited for transferring and storing images, video, music and other types of digital data . According to Panasonic, a founding member of the IHA and a member of the SD Association, a 512MB SD Card can store up to 7,000 JPG images, up to three hours of MPEG4 Video and up to 11 hours of digital music. "SD memory cards are likely to continue becoming a widespread means of storing and transporting digital content," said Peter Kastner, executive vice president and chief research officer, Aberdeen Group. "This is particularly true given the combination of technology and widespread industry support that the SD Memory Card enjoys." According to Consumer Adoption of Memory Cards: A Qualitative Study, SD memory cards lead all other formats both in terms of number of products and number of product categories, with over 1,300 SD-enabled products available worldwide. Aberdeen reports that the format held a 25 percent U.S. market share in the first half of 2003.
