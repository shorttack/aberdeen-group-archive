## Abstract

MarketWatch pre-market news story (Jul 23 2003, 9:16 a.m. EST) by Mike Tarsala reporting Sun Microsystems' fiscal Q4 2003 profit fell 80% as disclosed the prior afternoon. Pre-market shares down ~10% from $4.77 close to $4.30 in euro trading. Q4 profit $12M (breakeven EPS) vs $61M (2c EPS) year ago; revenue $2.98B down 13% from $3.42B; missed consensus of 2c/$3.07B. Peter Kastner, computer-hardware analyst with Aberdeen Group in Boston, provided analyst context with the identical two quotes later re-used by Rex Crum in the end-of-day wrap: 'investors are likely to interpret Sun's results as being on the edge of respectability' and 'anyone who gives up on Sun would be making a big mistake.' The pre-market stub is shorter than the closing-bell piece (Tarsala vs Crum) but stands as an independent MarketWatch article with different byline, URL, and time-stamp — not a republication.
