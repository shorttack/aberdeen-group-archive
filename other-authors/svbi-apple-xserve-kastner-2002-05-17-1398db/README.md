## Abstract

Silicon Valley Business Ink (May 17, 2002) reports on Apple Computer's May 14, 2002 launch of the rack-mounted Xserve server, positioned against revamped G4s and iMacs used as servers in offices and schools. Entry model: single-G4 processor, $2,999; two-processor: $3,999. Quotes Dean Nelson (Allegro Networks test engineer running 184 G4s and 23 racks — 'I could literally put that in four racks [of the new Apple servers]'), Mike Hathaway (Fremont Union HS adult education, excited about redundancy), Tim Deal (Technology Business Research — 'historical moment for this incarnation of Apple'), Steve Jobs ('we're approaching this very carefully and have a lot to learn'), and Peter Kastner (Aberdeen Group Chief Research Officer): Kastner 'likes the price and the technology, but isn't confident Apple can dent the enterprise server market. It's too late for Apple to get back into the bread-and-butter enterprise market for desktops and servers.' Mentions Genentech purchasing 1,000 iMacs for staff.
