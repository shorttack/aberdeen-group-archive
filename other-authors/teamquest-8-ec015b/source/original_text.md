Using TeamQuest Model to Align IT As mentioned earlier, TeamQuest Model provides capacity planning capability. Capacity planning is crucial, because “planning” is probably the most important ingredient for having a mature IT organization. You want to be ready for the future with systems optimally configured to fulfill the requirements of overall corporate strategy, providing value by demonstrably contributing to the bottom line. The alternative is to wallow in firefighting mode, constantly struggling to justify the cost of your operation. There are at least three ways to perform capacity planning: 1. Capacity benchmarking 2. Capacity trending 3. Capacity modeling Benchmarking or load testing is perhaps the most common, but also the most expensive. The idea is, you set up a configuration and then throw traffic at it to see how it performs. Actually setting up the systems to test can make this method impractical, to say the least. Linear trend analysis and statistical approaches to trending can provide a quick and dirty way to predict when you will need to do something about performance, but they don’t tell you what you should do to optimally respond. Trending does not provide a way to evaluate alternative solutions to an impending problem, nor to understand problems unrelated to trending, like server consolidation or adding an application, for example. That leaves modeling, which comes in a couple flavors: simulation and analytic modeling. Simulation modeling can be very versatile and accurate, but requires a great deal of set up and time. Analytic modeling is fast and is potentially very accurate as well. The beauty of modeling is that you can “test” various proposed solutions to a problem without actually implementing them. This can save a lot of time and money. TeamQuest’s capacity modeling tool, TeamQuest Model, supports both simulation and analytic modeling. Most people choose to use the speedy analytic capability, because it is possible to evaluate a number of alternative solutions in a short period of time. With TeamQuest Model you can: • Find underutilized capacity • Implement just-in-time upgrades • Optimally provision new services • Plan and validate service level agreements • Consolidate servers and applications (while maintaining service levels) While all of the above-listed uses can result in significant contributions to the profitability of a company, consolidation has been a particularly hot topic for the last few years. It can optimize the efficiency of your IT operations while helping to put information consistently in the hands of the business managers who need it. “For a large corporation, it’s fairly easy to get to millions of dollars in savings through recentralization of servers and expensive IT support,” says Peter Kastner, chief research officer at Aberdeen Group Inc.2 The experiences of TeamQuest Model users confirms Kastner’s observation.
