## Abstract

TechNewsWorld/E-Commerce Times article (Jun 8 2004, Elizabeth Millard) covering Microsoft's Jun 7 2004 filing with the European Court of Justice to annul the EC's Mar 24 2004 antitrust ruling. The ruling required Windows-Media-Player unbundling, interoperability disclosures to competitors, and a €497M ($612M) fine. Aberdeen chief research officer Peter Kastner framed the appeal as a broader sovereignty dispute: Microsoft has 'silent friends' among global firms, and 'for the EU to set a totally different standard for business conduct threatens not only Microsoft, but every other global company as well.' Yankee Group's Laura DiDio notes Microsoft will pay the fine 'at the last minute.' Sun Microsystems was the lead EC complainant; Microsoft's Apr 2004 $2B Sun settlement raised questions about the appeal's strength.
