## Abstract

Wall Street Journal article (January 1989) reports Honeywell Bull Inc. of Billerica, Mass. will reduce U.S. workforce by 15% (~1,600 employees) primarily in manufacturing and administration; sales force untouched. CEO Roland Pampel (ex-Apollo Computer president, joined July 1988) said R&D spending will be trimmed to ~10.5% of revenue. 1988 revenue expected to rise 7%+ from $2.06B (1987 — first year as separate venture, $17.4M profit). Honeywell Bull is the former Honeywell computer operations: 65% controlled by Groupe Bull (France), with Honeywell Inc. and NEC owning the rest. Quote from Peter Kastner (Aberdeen Group): 'Honeywell Bull had to bite the bullet... The computer industry now is much like the railroad business was 30 years ago. You either consolidate and clearly execute your strategies, or there's a good chance that you'll disappear. Right now, it's unclear that Honeywell [Bull] will be a long-term player.' Kastner believes Pampel was brought in 'to clean up.' Important early national-press appearance for Aberdeen Group (founded December 1988); appears within weeks of the firm's founding.
