# v1.5 Phase 1 Anomalies — for v1.6 investigation (v1)

**Date logged**: 2026-05-26
**Source**: Output of `01_load_csvs_v2.py` on archive_masters/ on Pete's Mac
**Status**: Non-blocking for v1.5 build. Track for v1.6 cleanup.

These are numbers surfaced by the new derived-column logic in
`01_load_csvs_v2.py`. They aren't bugs in the script — the script's
diagnostics are exposing pre-existing gaps in the masters that earlier
ingest passes never measured.

---

## Anomaly inventory

### 1. 350 / 1,434 studies (24%) have no `pub_year`

Phase 1 derived `pub_year` from `studies.date` (ISO `YYYY-MM-DD`); 350 studies
had blank or non-standard date strings.

**Suspected source**: kastner-author / video-transcript / DCT / memoir
collections where ingest never captured a structured publication date —
or where the only date available is a year-range or "circa".

**Impact in v1.5**: those 350 studies still get their own pages, but they
are excluded from:
- `wiki/decades/*.md` rollups
- DuckDB views that group by decade (`v_studies_by_decade`,
  `v_prescience_by_decade`)
- Phase 4 `_decades.md` index

**Investigation for v1.6**:
- Bucket by `studies.type` to see which collections are date-poor
- Sample 20 rows: are dates missing in source PDFs, or just not extracted?
- Build a one-shot date-backfill pass (regex over abstract +
  source_file metadata + Pass 1 date-extraction outputs)
- Goal: lift coverage to ≥95%

### 2. 1,890 / 3,207 entities (59%) have `occurrence_count = 0`

These entities live in `_master_entities.csv` but never appear in
`_master_entity_studies.csv`. They were registered (likely via
`_known_entities.csv` source_studies parsing) but never attached to an
observation.

**Impact in v1.5**: render as stub pages with no observation list.
Still navigable, still searchable, but light on content.

**Investigation for v1.6**:
- Cross-reference `_known_entities.csv.source_studies` against
  `_master_entity_studies.csv` — are the join-table rows just missing?
- Could be a join-table population bug in an earlier ingest pass
- Or these are entities mentioned in prose but not coded as observations
- Decide: backfill the join table, or accept and mark them as
  "mentioned-only" with a frontmatter flag (`provenance: known-only`)

### 3. 1,323 / 4,312 techs (31%) have `occurrence_count = 0`

Same pattern as entities. Same diagnosis path.

### 4. 1 / 2,723 prescience scores didn't join to observations

One row in `_master_prescience_scores.csv` has an `obs_id` that doesn't
exist in `_master_observations.csv`. Tiny — almost certainly an obs that
was dropped during a normalization pass after Pass C scored it, or a
typo in the obs_id.

**Investigation for v1.6**:
- `python3 -c "import pandas as pd; p=pd.read_csv('_master_prescience_scores.csv'); o=pd.read_csv('_master_observations.csv'); print(set(p.obs_id) - set(o.obs_id))"`
- One-line fix once the orphan obs_id is identified

### 5. Only 6 unique collections (expected ~14)

The wiki-builder skill assumed ~14 collections based on the Volume 1
memoir chapters. Reality: `_collection_stats.csv` has 6 actual
collections (we had a guess from before — listing them via Phase 2
view `v_collection_overview` will reveal which).

**Investigation for v1.6**:
- Is the "collection" axis vs "thematic" axis being conflated?
- Volume 1 memoir chapters may be a separate organizing concept
  (themes/) rather than collections — confirm with Pete
- Update wiki-builder skill v2 to match reality

### 6. 308 / 1,434 studies have ≥1 scored obs (21%)

Expected — Bucket A+B Pass C scope was 309 studies. The math:
- 309 Bucket A+B studies − 1 orphan score = 308 studies with scored obs

**No action needed** — this confirms Pass C ran on the intended scope.
Buckets C/D/E (1,126 studies) remain unscored by design. Future pass
work can extend coverage if/when desired.
